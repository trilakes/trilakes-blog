About Elk Creek Highlands

Elk Creek Highlands is a picturesque, mountainous neighborhood located just off Highway 67 near Divide, Colorado. Nestled at an elevation of approximately 8,500 feet, this area offers stunning views of the surrounding landscapes, including the majestic Pikes Peak and the sprawling San Isabel National Forest. The neighborhood is characterized by its serene atmosphere, abundant wildlife, and a strong sense of community, making it an attractive location for families and retirees alike. With a mix of single-family homes, cabins, and lots ready for custom builds, Elk Creek Highlands appeals to a wide range of buyers looking to immerse themselves in Colorado‚Äôs natural beauty.

Water & Utilities

In Elk Creek Highlands, the water infrastructure primarily relies on wells and cisterns rather than municipal services. Homeowners typically drill private wells to access water, and it is essential to ensure the well is properly permitted and tested for quality. According to ColoradoWell.com, many wells in this area yield adequate water, but it is crucial to conduct thorough research regarding water table levels and flow rates. Cisterns are also common, especially for those who may need additional water storage for seasonal use or emergencies. Residents can expect to manage their water needs independently, which emphasizes the importance of understanding well maintenance and water conservation practices.
