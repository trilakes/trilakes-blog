Septic System Permits (OWTS)

In Fremont County, septic system permits, also known as On-Site Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS), are separate from standard building permits. If your property is not connected to a municipal sewer system, you'll need to obtain an OWTS permit from the Fremont County Health Department. This process begins with a site evaluation to determine soil suitability and ensure that your proposed system will meet health and safety regulations. The Health Department requires detailed plans that outline the type of septic system you intend to install, alongside a fee for the permit application. It's essential to be aware that any project involving a septic system can take longer due to the need for additional inspections and approvals. Engaging with the health department early in your planning phase can help you navigate these requirements efficiently.
