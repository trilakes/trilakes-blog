Case Study: Mountain Driveway on 25% Grade (Cripple Creek)

Project by Tri-Lakes Contracting
```html

Project Overview

Located in the picturesque Cripple Creek area, this project involved a challenging driveway with a steep 25% grade extending from the road to the building site. The unique topography of the lot provided breathtaking views, but it also posed significant accessibility challenges, particularly concerning winter access. To overcome this issue, our team designed an engineered switchback driveway that navigated the steep incline while ensuring safety and ease of use for all vehicles. The solution required meticulous planning and execution to create a functional and aesthetically pleasing entryway to the property.

The Challenge

The steep lot where the home was to be built offered stunning panoramic views but came with a series of significant challenges, most notably the 25% grade that made direct access impossible. This steep incline not only complicated the construction process but also raised concerns about accessibility, especially during the harsh winter months when snow and ice could render the road impassable. Residents needed reliable year-round access for daily activities and emergency situations. The challenge was to create a viable solution that would allow for safe travel to and from the property without compromising the natural beauty of the surrounding area.

Our Solution

To address the steep grade, we devised a switchback driveway design that would allow vehicles to navigate the incline safely. This design involved creating a series of gentle turns that reduced the steepness of the approach while providing a visually appealing pathway. Proper drainage was a critical component of our solution, as it would prevent water accumulation and erosion during heavy rains or snowmelt. We used high-quality gravel for the surface, ensuring that it was well-compacted to provide a sturdy foundation. The switchback design not only met the accessibility requirements but also integrated seamlessly with the natural landscape, maintaining the aesthetic integrity of the property.

Construction

The construction process required careful planning and the use of specialized equipment to manage the steep terrain. We began by clearing the area and marking the layout of the switchback driveway. Heavy machinery, including excavators and compactors, was employed to create the necessary slopes and turns while ensuring that the earth was properly graded for drainage. Our team worked diligently to compact the gravel surface, creating a solid foundation that could withstand the elements and heavy vehicle traffic. Throughout the construction process, we prioritized safety, implementing measures to protect workers and the surrounding environment, while also adhering to local regulations. This phase of the project was completed on time and within budget, setting the stage for the successful completion of the home.

Results

The completion of the switchback driveway transformed the steep lot into a fully accessible property, allowing for year-round access regardless of weather conditions. Emergency vehicles can now reach the home without difficulty, providing peace of mind for the residents. The newly constructed home is now nestled comfortably within the landscape, benefiting from the stunning views while ensuring the safety and accessibility of its inhabitants. The project not only met the functional requirements but also enhanced the overall value of the property, making it a desirable location for future homeowners.
```
