Lake County Building Permits: Complete Guide

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed Contractor Updated January 2026
Building Permit Guide for Lake County, Colorado

Overview of Lake County Building Department

The Lake County Building Department is dedicated to ensuring the safety, accessibility, and sustainability of construction projects throughout the county. The department is responsible for enforcing building codes, zoning regulations, and ensuring that construction meets the standards set forth by state and local laws. Whether you are building a new home, renovating an existing structure, or undertaking any construction project, understanding the permitting process is crucial.

Types of Permits Needed

There are several types of permits that may be required for construction projects in Lake County. These include:

  • Building Permit: Required for new constructions, additions, alterations, and certain repairs.
  • Septic Permit: Necessary for installations or modifications to septic systems.
  • Well Permit: Needed for drilling new wells or making significant modifications to existing ones.
  • Electrical Permit: Required for electrical installations, upgrades, or repairs.

How to Apply for Permits

The application process for permits in Lake County typically involves the following steps:

  1. Determine the Required Permits: Before applying, assess your project to identify which permits are needed.
  2. Gather Required Documentation: This may include site plans, construction drawings, and any relevant surveys.
  3. Complete the Application Forms: Applications can be obtained from the Lake County Building Department's office or website.
  4. Submit Your Application: Submit your completed application along with the required documentation and fees to the Building Department.
  5. Wait for Review: The department will review your application for completeness and compliance with local codes.

Typical Fees

Fees for permits can vary based on the type of permit and the scope of the project. Typical fees include:

  • Building Permit: Fees can range from $100 to several thousand dollars depending on the project size and complexity.
  • Septic Permit: Usually between $225 and $450, depending on the system type.
  • Well Permit: Typically around $250.
  • Electrical Permit: Ranges from $50 to $200 based on the extent of electrical work.

It is advisable to check with the Lake County Building Department for the most current fee schedule.

Timeline Expectations

Timelines for obtaining permits can vary significantly depending on the type of project and the volume of applications the department is processing. Generally, you can expect the following timelines:

  • Building Permits: Initial review may take 2-4 weeks, with additional time for revisions if necessary.
  • Septic and Well Permits: Typically processed within 1-2 weeks if all documentation is complete.
  • Electrical Permits: Usually processed within a week, but can take longer if additional inspections are needed.

Inspection Process

After receiving your permits, you will need to schedule inspections at various stages of your project. Inspections ensure that construction complies with all applicable codes and standards. The inspection process generally includes:

  1. Scheduling Inspections: Contact the Lake County Building Department to schedule inspections at designated phases of your project.
  2. Preparing for Inspections: Ensure that the work is accessible and that all required documentation is available for the inspector.
  3. Conducting Inspections: An inspector will visit your site to assess the work completed. They will provide feedback and note any required corrections.
  4. Final Inspection: A final inspection is necessary before a Certificate of Occupancy can be issued, confirming that the project is safe for use.

Tips for Smooth Permitting

To ensure a smooth permitting experience, consider the following tips:

  • Be Thorough: Ensure all documentation is complete and accurate before submission to avoid delays.
  • Stay Informed: Familiarize yourself with local building codes and requirements that may affect your project.
  • Communicate: Maintain open lines of communication with the Building Department. Do not hesitate to ask questions or seek clarification.
  • Plan for Inspections: Schedule inspections in advance and be prepared for them to avoid unnecessary holdups.
  • Keep Records: Maintain copies of all permits, correspondence, and inspection reports for your records.
