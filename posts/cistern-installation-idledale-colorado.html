Why Idledale Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Idledale, Colorado, comes with its unique charms and challenges, especially when it comes to water supply. With an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties here lack access to reliable groundwater. This is where cistern installation becomes essential. Unlike traditional homes, mountain properties often require innovative solutions like underground water storage tanks to ensure a continuous supply of potable water. Professional installation not only guarantees compliance with local regulations but also ensures that your system is installed with proper frost protection, which is crucial for winter survival in this region.

Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Idledale's geographical features pose several challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate excavation, often increasing costs significantly. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep, making water hauling a necessary consideration for many residents. The frost line in this area can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, requiring specialized techniques for buried water tanks to prevent freezing during harsh winters. Moreover, the steep driveways and limited winter access can pose logistical challenges for installations, underscoring the need for a local expert who understands these unique conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Idledale

The cost of cistern installation varies significantly based on several factors. For a residential system, the size of your potable water storage tank is typically determined by daily water usage, which is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. As a rule of thumb, assume 2 people per bedroom and 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require around 600 gallons per day, leading to a tank size of at least 18,000 gallons if you prefer monthly refills.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of potential costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: starting from $13,000 to $15,000.
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: starting from $22,000 to $28,000.
  • Excavation costs: between $3,000 to $8,000+, depending on the rocky terrain and access issues.
  • Pump system and pressure tank: approximately $2,500 to $5,000.
  • Overall installed costs for a 5,000 gallon system could range from $25,000 to $35,000 or more.

When considering water storage tank installation, keep in mind that any rocky or difficult terrain may increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Investing in a larger tank can save you from frequent refills, which is especially beneficial given the limited access to properties during winter months.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation complexity. For a basic system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank, while larger systems can cost significantly more, ranging up to $120,000 for larger capacity installations.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your household's daily water usage, calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home (up to 6 people) would typically require a tank of at least 18,000 gallons if you want to refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but generally, it takes several days to a few weeks. Factors such as site preparation, permitting processes, and weather conditions can influence the duration of the project.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your location and water needs. If the water table is deep or unreliable, a cistern can provide a more stable solution. Conversely, if you have access to a reliable well, it may be more convenient, but a cistern often serves as a great backup and can be essential for properties with limited access to groundwater.
