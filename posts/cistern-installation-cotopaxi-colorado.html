Why Cotopaxi Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Cotopaxi, a stunning mountain community at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges when it comes to securing a reliable water supply. Many properties here lack direct access to a water source, making cistern installation an essential service for homeowners. A professional cistern installer like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, providing you with a dependable source of potable water storage that meets local regulations. With the extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall typical of this region, a well-executed installation is crucial to prevent issues like freezing and water contamination.

Local Challenges in Fremont County

In Fremont County, the rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate water storage tank installation. The water table is often significantly deep, necessitating the use of an underground cistern for many mountain properties. If your land has challenging access due to steep driveways or limited winter accessibility, these factors can drive up excavation costs. Additionally, the frost line, which can reach 36-48 inches, requires specialized planning to ensure that your buried water tank is adequately protected against freezing. With the building season generally limited to May through October, timely planning and execution are paramount.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Cotopaxi

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for effective budgeting. Our pricing reflects the high-quality materials and expert labor necessary for installations in the rugged terrain of Cotopaxi. Here‚Äôs a breakdown:

  • Tank Costs: For a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank, expect to pay between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank typically ranges from $22,000 to $28,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on several factors, including tank size and site conditions. Generally, for a 5,000-gallon cistern, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically costs between $40,000 and $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, which is calculated by the number of bedrooms in your home multiplied by 2 occupants per bedroom and 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a tank capable of holding at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The time frame for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions and the complexity of the project. Generally, the installation process can take anywhere from one week to several weeks, including permitting and excavation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends largely on your specific property conditions. In areas like Cotopaxi, where the water table may be deep or unreliable, a cistern often serves as a better alternative, offering reliable storage for potable water, whereas wells can be expensive to drill and maintain.
