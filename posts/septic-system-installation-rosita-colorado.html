Local Challenges in Custer County County

Installing a septic system in Rosita brings unique local challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate the installation process, often necessitating engineered systems to ensure effective treatment of onsite wastewater. Moreover, many properties face deeper water tables, which may require additional solutions like cisterns for water supply.

The frost line in this mountainous region typically ranges from 36 to 48 inches, meaning that any septic installation must be designed with freeze protection in mind. These factors, combined with extreme weather conditions such as heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, make it essential to work with professionals who understand the terrain and local climate.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and designed to meet the unique needs of your Rosita property. First, we perform detailed soil testing to evaluate the specific conditions of your land. This involves digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather samples that are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis.

Next, we develop a customized septic design based on the soils report. Depending on the findings, you may require a conventional gravity system, a pressure-dosed system, or an advanced treatment unit (ATU). Once the design is approved, we will secure the necessary septic permit through the Custer County Building Department.

Installation typically occurs between May and October, when access to higher elevations is easiest. Our team carefully excavates the site, installs the septic tank and leach field, and ensures that all components are properly connected. We also conduct a final septic inspection to confirm that everything meets state regulations. This entire process generally takes a few weeks, depending on site conditions and weather.

When planning for winter, consider additional insulation around your septic components to prevent freezing. Regular maintenance ensures a long-lasting system.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Rosita

The cost of septic installation in Rosita can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, system type, and local regulations. For standard engineered systems, you can expect costs starting from $35,000 to $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, the total installed price generally ranges from $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can set you back between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can start from $35,000 and go up to $80,000 or more, depending on the type of system required, site conditions, and local regulations. For example, engineered systems typically range from $35,000 to $50,000, while advanced treatment units can cost between $55,000 to $80,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The septic installation process usually takes a few weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. It's crucial to schedule the installation during the building season from May to October to ensure accessibility in the mountainous terrain.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Custer County require engineered systems due to the challenging soil conditions and higher elevation. These systems are designed to effectively handle onsite wastewater treatment in areas where conventional systems would fail.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests to assess soil conditions. This process involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the proper type of septic system that will function effectively on your property.
