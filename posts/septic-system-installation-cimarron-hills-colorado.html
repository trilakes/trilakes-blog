Why Cimarron Hills Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

When it comes to owning property in Cimarron Hills, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, having a reliable septic system is essential for your property's functionality and safety. The mountainous terrain and unique soil conditions require expertise beyond basic septic installation. Improper installation can lead to costly repairs, health hazards, and environmental contamination. That's why hiring a professional septic installer near you, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, is crucial. Our licensed and insured team understands the specific requirements of the region, ensuring your septic system meets all local regulations and operates efficiently for many years.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Cimarron Hills presents several challenges for septic system installation due to its rocky granite and clay soil, which can impede proper drainage. The local water table is often deep, meaning many properties may require cisterns or regular water hauling for everyday use. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating specialized installation techniques to avoid damage during the winter months. The steep driveways and limited access during inclement weather are other factors that can complicate septic installation in this mountain community. Understanding these challenges is vital for homeowners seeking to install a reliable onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS).

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a structured process for septic installation tailored to the unique needs of Cimarron Hills homeowners. First, we conduct a thorough soils test to determine the appropriate system based on your property's specific conditions. This involves digging two test holes, each eight feet deep, and analyzing soil samples to create a detailed soils report.

Once the report is available, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the El Paso County Building Department. After securing the permit, we can schedule the excavation, installation of the septic tank, and connection to the leach field or drain field. The entire process typically takes about one to two weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and permitting timelines. Our team's expertise ensures your septic system is installed efficiently and complies with all local regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Cimarron Hills

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Cimarron Hills is crucial for homeowners. The cost varies based on several factors, but here are the typical ranges:

  • Soils test + engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000
  • Excavation for system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank + components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000-$4,000

The total cost of a basic engineered system typically starts from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, while a sand filter system ranges from $45,000 to $65,000. If you require an ATU system, costs can range from $55,000 to $80,000. Challenging site conditions such as rocky terrain may add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall cost.

For cistern installations, the size is calculated based on the number of bedrooms, using the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. Planning your system effectively can save you money and ensure compliance with local regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on site conditions and system type. For a basic engineered system, homeowners can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000 on average. More advanced systems, like sand filter or ATU systems, can range from $45,000 to over $80,000 depending on the specific needs of the property.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeframe for septic installation generally ranges from one to two weeks. This includes time for soils testing, obtaining the necessary permits, and the actual installation process. Variations may occur due to weather conditions and site accessibility.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Cimarron Hills require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions and state regulations. An engineered system ensures optimal performance and longevity, making it a necessary investment for homeowners in mountainous areas.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with soil testing to assess the suitability of the site for septic systems. This involves digging test holes and analyzing the soil's characteristics to determine the appropriate type of system required for effective onsite wastewater treatment.
