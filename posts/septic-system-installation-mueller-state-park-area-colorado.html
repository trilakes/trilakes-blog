Local Challenges in Teller County

Installing a septic system in Teller County, especially in the Mueller State Park area, presents several unique challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate installation, often necessitating engineered systems that are specifically designed to handle such conditions. Additionally, the deep water table means that many properties require cisterns for water supply, further complicating the overall site planning. The frost line, which reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches or more at higher elevations, must also be accounted for during installation to prevent freeze damage. Moreover, steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder equipment transport and access, making the selection of a skilled septic installer near me crucial for a successful project. Furthermore, ensuring compliance with Teller County's building department for septic permits is necessary to avoid delays and ensure the long-term viability of your system.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is comprehensive and designed to meet the unique needs of each property. First, we conduct a thorough soil testing analysis to determine the most suitable septic system for your land. This includes digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to collect soil samples at varying depths, which are then sent to a licensed engineer for evaluation. Once we receive the soils report, we can begin the septic design phase, determining the appropriate system type‚Äîmost commonly engineered systems as per Regulation 43.

After securing the necessary septic permit from the Teller County building department, our experienced team will schedule the installation. The typical timeline for an installation ranges from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions and permits. During installation, we focus on ensuring that the system meets all regulatory requirements, including proper setbacks from wells and property lines. Once installed, we provide ongoing maintenance and support to ensure your system continues to function optimally.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Mueller State Park Area

The cost of septic installation in the Mueller State Park area can vary significantly based on several factors. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we estimate the total cost for an engineered system typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000, while a sand filter system might cost between $45,000 and $65,000. If you require an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) for higher treatment levels, expect a total installed cost of $55,000 to $80,000. Rocky terrain and difficult access can add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to these estimates.

For homes requiring cisterns, the size is based on the number of bedrooms (two people per bedroom, multiplied by 100 gallons per day), which further influences the overall costs. The initial soil testing and engineering report typically costs between $2,000 and $4,000, while permits may range from $500 to $2,000. Excavation costs for the system can vary from $8,000 to $20,000 or more, depending on site conditions. Remember, while these costs can seem daunting, investing in a properly installed and engineered system not only protects your property but also enhances its value in this stunning mountain community.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado generally ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system required. For instance, engineered systems can cost between $35,000 and $50,000, while advanced treatment units may start from $55,000 and can exceed $80,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system typically takes several days to a week, depending on factors such as site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the system design. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting works efficiently to minimize disruption while ensuring compliance with all regulations.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Mueller State Park area require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and elevation. These systems are designed to handle site-specific issues, ensuring long-term functionality and compliance with Colorado's OWTS regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps determine the most suitable septic system for your property. A thorough understanding of the soil is essential for effective septic design.
