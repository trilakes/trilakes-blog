Local Challenges in Chaffee County

In Chaffee County, homeowners face a variety of challenges when it comes to septic system installation. The soil conditions, including rocky granite and decomposed granite, can complicate the septic installation process, requiring many properties to utilize engineered septic systems. Additionally, the deep water table often necessitates the use of cisterns or water hauling, further complicating the design of effective on-site wastewater solutions. The frost line in this region is between 36 to 48 inches, meaning that systems must be installed deeper, which can add to excavation costs. As access to many properties can be limited during the winter months due to steep driveways and heavy snowfall, planning for winterization of your septic system is crucial. Homeowners must also consider fire mitigation strategies, especially in forested areas, making it vital to work with a knowledgeable contractor who understands these local conditions.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is designed to ensure you receive a reliable and compliant system that meets your needs. We start with a thorough soil testing procedure to determine the best system for your property. This involves bringing a mini excavator to your site to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, from which we gather soil samples. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis, helping us understand the soil conditions and select the appropriate system type.

Once the soils report is complete, we will guide you through the septic design tailored specifically for your property. Following this, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permits through the Chaffee County Building Department, which is crucial for compliance with local regulations. Our installation timeline typically spans from late spring to early fall, given the region's high elevation and the challenges presented by winter weather. After installation, we provide a comprehensive septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly and that your new system is ready for use. Our goal is to provide you with peace of mind and a system that lasts for decades.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Buena Vista

When considering septic installation in Buena Vista, several factors influence the overall costs. Typically, homeowners can expect to pay between $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs), which are often necessary in our challenging mountain terrain, can cost between $55,000 and $80,000. The total cost includes various elements such as excavation, tanks, and pumps, which can vary significantly based on site conditions.

For those requiring cisterns, the size is based on the formula of: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. This is crucial for ensuring you have an adequate water supply. Challenging access, rocky terrain, and the need for deeper installations due to frost lines can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your costs. Always remember, the specifics of your property and the soil conditions will ultimately drive the pricing, which is why estimating at the high end is advisable.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on site conditions, but homeowners can typically expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000, depending on the type of system needed.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of septic installation can vary, but the process typically takes a few days to a week, depending on the complexity of the system and weather conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Many properties in mountain regions like Buena Vista require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. It's essential to have a soils report to determine the best system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not perform traditional perc tests; instead, we conduct soil tests to analyze soil conditions. This involves digging test holes to collect samples for a soils report that guides the design of your septic system.
