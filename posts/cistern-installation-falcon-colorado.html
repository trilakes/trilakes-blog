Why Falcon Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In Falcon, Colorado, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, accessing a reliable water supply can be a challenge. Many homes are situated in areas with deep water tables or rocky terrain, making traditional well drilling impractical. This is where cistern installation comes into play as a vital water storage solution for both residential and agricultural needs.

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our expertise in cistern installation ensures that you have a reliable source of water for your home. Whether for personal use, irrigation, or fire suppression, our skilled team understands the unique requirements of mountain properties. Additionally, our professional installation services ensure compliance with El Paso County permit regulations, giving you peace of mind in your investment.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Falcon's terrain presents several challenges for water storage solutions. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation for your underground water storage tank. With an average frost line of 36-48 inches, achieving proper burial depth for a buried water tank is critical to avoid freezing, especially during harsh winters.

Moreover, many properties in this mountainous region experience limited access, particularly during winter months when steep driveways can become impassable. This can affect not only the installation timeline but also future water hauling and maintenance needs. Understanding these local challenges is crucial when considering a water cistern as part of your off-grid water system or backup water supply.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Falcon

The cost of installing a water cistern in Falcon can vary significantly based on several factors, including the size of the tank, site conditions, and additional features. For instance, a typical installation of a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank can start from $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon tank can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

When calculating your cistern size, consider that a 3-bedroom home (assumed occupancy of 6 people) would ideally need a tank that supports at least 600 gallons per day. If you want a system that can get you through winter, you may need a tank that holds upwards of 72,000 gallons. This can mean installing multiple tanks, significantly increasing the total project cost.

Additional costs include excavation, which can typically range from $3,000 to $8,000+, depending on the rocky nature of the terrain and site access. Other necessary components such as pumps, electrical work, and water filtration can add another $5,000 to $10,000 to your total. Be prepared for potential complications, as rocky soil can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Therefore, it‚Äôs advisable to budget for the high end of these ranges to avoid surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and installation challenges. A 5,000-gallon system typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system ranges from $40,000 to $55,000. Remember, these prices can increase significantly due to factors like rocky terrain and difficult access.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage and how often you plan to refill. For a 3-bedroom home, a minimum of 36,000 gallons may be needed to ensure an adequate supply over winter, which is calculated at 600 gallons per day.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 2-3 days, depending on site conditions. Factors like rocky soil, access difficulties, and the complexity of the plumbing system can influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better choice in areas like Falcon where water tables are deep or unreliable. Unlike wells, cisterns provide a consistent water supply and can be sized to meet peak demand, especially in off-grid settings. For more information, check out our Cistern vs Well Comparison.
