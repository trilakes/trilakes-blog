Local Challenges in Park County

Como, known as a historic railroad town, poses distinctive challenges for custom home builders. The soil here consists of rocky granite and decomposed granite, which can complicate foundation engineering. Additionally, the water table is often deep, necessitating the use of cisterns for water storage or even water hauling, especially during the harsh winters when access may be limited.

The frost line in Como extends between 36 to 48 inches, demanding careful foundation planning. Heavy snowfall and extreme temperature fluctuations further complicate new construction, necessitating fire-resistant construction and proper insulation to ensure the home remains warm and safe. Furthermore, properties often feature steep driveways that require careful consideration in site planning for both aesthetics and functionality. For these reasons, hiring a knowledgeable contractor familiar with Como's local conditions is essential for a successful build.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Como

Understanding the financial implications of building a custom home in Como is essential for achieving your dream home without budgetary surprises. The cost per square foot typically ranges from $550 to $700 for standard quality construction, while luxury builds may exceed $1,000 per square foot. For example, a 2,000 square foot home could cost from $1,100,000 to $1,500,000, depending on the complexity of the design and site conditions.

Additionally, when considering water needs, a cistern's size is typically calculated as two people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. Thus, for a three-bedroom home, you would need a cistern capable of holding at least 6,000 gallons, which could cost between $25,000 to $75,000 to install. Other factors, such as rocky terrain access and engineered septic systems, can further increase costs. Given these complexities, it‚Äôs wise to plan for the high end of all construction estimates to cushion any unexpected expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies widely based on location and design. In Como, you can expect to pay between $550 to $700 per square foot for standard construction, with luxury builds potentially exceeding $1,000 per square foot.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: Typically, the entire construction process for a custom home can take between 10 to 18 months. However, factors like site conditions and weather may extend this timeline, particularly in higher elevations like Como.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When choosing a custom home builder, consider their experience in mountain construction, understanding of local regulations, and ability to provide a comprehensive service from design to completion. Checking references and past projects can also provide insights into their work quality.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a build feasibility report before purchasing land is highly recommended. This report assesses site conditions, setback requirements, and overall buildability, ensuring you make an informed decision before committing to a property.
