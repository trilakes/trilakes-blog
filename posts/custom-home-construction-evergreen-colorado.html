Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Evergreen's terrain is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which can complicate foundation engineering and require specialized approaches to site planning. Many properties in this area will require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions, adding another layer of complexity to new construction. Additionally, the frost line here can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning and execution to ensure your home is built to withstand the region's harsh winters.

Access to many properties is often limited, with steep driveways that can pose challenges, especially during winter months when snow accumulation can hinder entry. Weather conditions in Evergreen can also be extreme, with heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, making it essential to incorporate fire resistant construction and energy efficient homes into your design. Understanding these local challenges upfront will help you avoid costly mistakes and delays in your home building journey.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive step-by-step process for custom home construction that ensures your project is executed efficiently and to the highest standards. Our process begins with a pre-purchase build feasibility report, where we assess the land and determine its viability for construction. This step is crucial as it includes a site visit, soil analysis, utility availability, and a setback assessment.

After confirming the land is suitable, we help you file for a well permit or set up a cistern for water supply. This is often a crucial step in Colorado, as many counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit. Once the water situation is settled, we move forward with creating a detailed site plan that outlines the layout of your home, driveway, and utilities.

Next, we develop architectural plans, which can be designed by your chosen architect or selected from our partners, including ArchitecturalDesigns.com. These plans guide the building phase, which includes engineered foundation and framing plans, ensuring your home meets all safety and structural requirements. After compiling all necessary documents, we submit for your building permit to the Jefferson County Building Department.

Once we receive the permit, the construction phase begins, typically from May to October due to weather considerations at higher elevations. Throughout the build process, we ensure that all inspections are passed and that the project remains on schedule. Finally, upon successful completion and final inspection, we provide you with a certificate of occupancy, allowing you to move into your new dream home.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Evergreen

Understanding the costs involved in building a custom home in Evergreen is critical for budgeting effectively. The costs can vary significantly based on several factors, including terrain, design complexity, and materials used. On average, the cost per square foot for home construction ranges from $550 to $700 for custom builds and can exceed $1,000 for luxury homes.

For example, a 2,500 square foot home could cost between $1,375,000 and $1,750,000, depending on the quality of materials and finishes selected. Additionally, you must factor in separate costs for land acquisition, which is not included in the above estimates. If you plan to install a cistern, remember to size it correctly based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day), which can add $25,000 to $75,000 to your total expenditures.

Other potential costs include septic installation, driveways, and utility extensions. Complexities such as rocky terrain and the need for engineered systems can drive prices higher, making it essential to consult a general contractor who understands the specifics of building in this mountainous region. As you consider your custom build, be sure to budget for unexpected challenges that may arise throughout the process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $550 to $700 per square foot for custom builds, with luxury options exceeding $1,000 per square foot. For a 2,500 square foot home, this can total between $1,375,000 and $1,750,000, excluding land costs.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house in Evergreen can vary, but you should expect pre-construction planning to take 2-6 months, followed by 10-18 months for construction. For larger or more complex homes, the timeline can extend to 24 months or more, particularly given the region‚Äôs weather challenges.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, look for experience in mountain construction, knowledge of local building codes, and a solid reputation. It's also beneficial to review past projects and ensure that the company is licensed and insured, like Tri-Lakes Contracting.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study before purchasing land is crucial. It assesses the land's suitability for building, including factors like soil conditions, water access, and local zoning regulations, helping you avoid costly mistakes down the line.
