Local Challenges in Teller County

Chipita Park presents a unique set of challenges for cistern installation. The region is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which can complicate excavation and increase installation costs. As the frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, particularly in the higher elevations, it is crucial to ensure that your potable water storage tank is properly insulated and protected against freezing temperatures.

Moreover, access to many properties is restricted, especially during winter months when heavy snowfall and steep driveways create obstacles. The typical building season runs from May to October, limiting the window for installation. Homeowners must also navigate local regulations, as all permits for cisterns must be obtained through the Teller County Building Department. Understanding these challenges is essential to ensure a successful water storage tank installation.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Chipita Park

The cost of cistern installation in Chipita Park can vary significantly based on several factors. For standard installations, you can expect to pay as follows:

  • 5,000 gallon system: Starting from $25,000 to $35,000
  • 10,000 gallon system: Starting from $40,000 to $55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): Starting from $50,000 to $75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): Starting from $75,000 to $120,000+

When determining the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank, a general formula is to calculate daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires around 600 gallons per day. If you want to refill your system every two months, you would then need a minimum of 36,000 gallons.

Keep in mind that rocky terrain or difficult access can increase excavation costs by 20-40%, and additional expenses for pumps, electrical hookups, and trenching can also add up quickly. It‚Äôs crucial to approach this investment with an understanding of the potential costs involved, especially since winter installations can incur higher charges due to ground conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system to over $120,000 for large capacity systems. Factors influencing the price include tank size, excavation difficulty, and additional equipment needs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom house would need a tank that stores at least 36,000 gallons if refilling every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation generally takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting processes, and weather. Factors like rocky terrain or difficult access can affect the timeline and increase installation duration.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property's water availability. Cisterns are often a better alternative in areas with deep water tables or unreliable well yields, providing a consistent water supply for both residential and agricultural needs.
