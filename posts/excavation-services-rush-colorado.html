Local Challenges in El Paso County

The Rush area boasts beauty and serenity, but it also presents unique challenges for excavation projects. At elevations between 7,000 and 10,000 feet, the weather can be unpredictable, with extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall that can delay construction timelines. Access to many properties may be hindered by steep driveways and limited winter access, complicating dirt work and earthmoving tasks.

Moreover, the local soil conditions are often rocky, which can lead to increased costs during foundation excavation. The frost line in this region typically ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating deeper digging for foundations and utility trenches. Most properties require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil types, and potential homeowners must also plan for water needs, as many areas necessitate cisterns or water hauling due to deep water tables. Understanding these local challenges is key to successful excavation.

Cost Factors for Excavation Services in Rush

Understanding the costs associated with excavation services in Rush is crucial for budgeting. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical prices for various excavation tasks:

  • Site Preparation/Clearing: Basic lot clearing starts from $8,000 and can go up to $20,000 depending on the extent of the work involved. Heavy brush or tree removal can range from $5,000 to $15,000 per acre.
  • Driveway Construction: For a gravel driveway, you can expect costs between $15 and $35 per linear foot. A 200-foot driveway could cost between $5,000 and $10,000, while a 500-foot driveway could range from $12,000 to $25,000, especially if it‚Äôs steep or requires switchbacks.
  • Foundation Excavation: For a standard basement dig (about 1,500 sq. ft.), costs typically range from $15,000 to $30,000. If rock excavation is required, this could add 50-200% to your total costs, and hauling excess material can range from $50 to $100 per load.
  • Utility Trenching: A standard trench may cost between $8 and $15 per linear foot, but rocky conditions can increase that to $20-$50 per linear foot. Remember that trench depth requirements vary based on the frost line, which is typically between 36 and 48 inches in Rush.

For properties requiring cisterns, the size is typically calculated based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day). Installed costs for cistern systems can vary widely, encompassing tank, excavation, pumps, trenching, and electrical work. As always, costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, so we recommend planning for the high end of these ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs can vary significantly depending on the conditions of the site. For basic excavation, you might expect costs starting from $8 to $15 per cubic yard, but this can increase if you encounter rocky conditions or other challenges.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation can vary based on the scope of the project and site conditions. Typically, for basic clearing and grading, you might expect the process to take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Mountain excavation often requires specialized equipment such as excavators, bobcats for smaller areas, and dump trucks. Equipment choice will depend on the specific tasks, such as trenching or rock removal.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are typically required for excavation projects in Rush. It is essential to secure these through the El Paso County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations.
