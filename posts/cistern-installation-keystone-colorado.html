Local Challenges in Summit County

Summit County‚Äôs diverse terrain, characterized by rocky granite and clay valleys, poses several challenges for cistern installation. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Keystone

Understanding the cost of cistern installation in Keystone is crucial for budgeting your project effectively. The total installed cost typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on the size of the water storage tank and site conditions. For instance, a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank can cost between $13,000 and $15,000 while a 10,000-gallon tank may range from $22,000 to $28,000. Moreover, the total installed costs will include excavation, pump systems, trenching, and electrical connections.

As a general rule, we size your water cistern based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count‚Äîassuming 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a three-bedroom home (6 people) would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, translating to a need for a tank that can accommodate your refill preferences. If you plan for a monthly refill, you would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons, costing up to $55,000 depending on additional factors.

It's also worth mentioning that rocky or difficult terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and winter installations may incur higher expenses due to challenging ground conditions. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000+ depending on the size and specific site conditions. For instance, a 5,000-gallon cistern can cost between $25,000 and $35,000 when factoring in excavation and installation.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need is based on your household's daily water usage. Generally, you calculate 100 gallons per person per day, with the size increasing depending on how often you want to refill the tank.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary, but it generally takes around 2 to 4 weeks from site assessment to completion. This includes securing permits, excavation, and installation of the tank and associated systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water table depth and access conditions. Cisterns provide a reliable alternative in areas where wells are impractical, ensuring you have a consistent water supply even in remote locations.
