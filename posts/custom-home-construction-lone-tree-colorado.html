Why Lone Tree Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Lone Tree, located in the stunning Douglas County of Colorado, is an ideal setting for your dream mountain home. With an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, the unique environment presents both breathtaking views and specific construction challenges. Building a custom home here requires expertise in navigating complex building regulations, particularly because Douglas County has stringent permit requirements that must be adhered to. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting is prepared to provide the professional service necessary for successful new construction, ensuring that every step is managed efficiently and effectively for your custom home.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

Building in Lone Tree comes with unique challenges that require careful planning and execution. The local terrain features rocky granite and clay-rich valleys, making foundation engineering a critical aspect of your project. The frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, necessitating specialized foundation designs. Moreover, the water table can be deep, and many properties require cisterns for water supply. Effective site planning is essential to address issues like steep driveways and limited winter access. Additionally, fire mitigation strategies must be implemented due to the forested areas surrounding many lots. Understanding these challenges will enable you to make informed decisions during your home building journey.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we employ a step-by-step approach to custom home construction that ensures your project runs smoothly, from concept to completion. Here‚Äôs an overview of our comprehensive process:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before you decide to buy land, we recommend obtaining a Build Feasibility Report. This report will assess site conditions, setbacks, and overall buildability.
  2. Well Permit: Obtain your well permit early, as most Colorado counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit. If you opt for a cistern instead, you can often begin construction without delay.
  3. Site Plan: We create a detailed site plan, identifying the house footprint, driveway, and utility routes.
  4. Architectural Plans: Choose from our network of architects or select designs from ArchitecturalDesigns.com.
  5. Engineered Foundation Plans: Custom designs will be provided based on site conditions, including soil type and slope.
  6. Land Survey: A professional survey will confirm property boundaries and identify any easements.
  7. Septic System Plans: If no sewer connection is available, we provide engineered septic plans per Colorado Regulation 43.
  8. Compile & Submit for Building Permit: We handle all documentation and submit it to the Douglas County Building Department.
  9. Pre-Construction Planning: This involves finalizing plans, ordering materials, and scheduling subcontractors.
  10. Construction: We will guide you through every phase from site preparation to final inspections.
  11. Certificate of Occupancy: After passing the final inspection, you will receive your certificate and can move into your new home!

This entire journey typically spans 12-24 months, depending on various factors including weather conditions and project complexity.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Lone Tree

When considering custom home construction in Lone Tree, it's essential to have a clear understanding of the costs involved. The average cost per square foot for mountain home construction ranges from:

  • Basic build: $350-$450/sq ft
  • Standard quality: $450-$550/sq ft
  • Custom/higher-end: $550-$700/sq ft
  • Luxury/high-end: $700-$1,000+/sq ft

This means a typical 2,500 sq ft home could cost between $875,000 and $1,375,000. Furthermore, additional expenses for land, cisterns, and other utilities can significantly impact your budget. For instance, cistern installation can range from $25,000 to $75,000, depending on size, which is typically calculated as bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gal/day. Given the rocky terrain and potential access issues, costs may be higher than anticipated. We always recommend planning for the higher end of the pricing spectrum, especially in challenging locations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies widely based on design, materials, and location. In Lone Tree, expect to pay between $350 and $1,000+ per square foot, depending on the level of finishes and complexity of the build.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house in Colorado typically ranges from 12 to 24 months. This includes pre-construction planning and the actual construction phase, which can be affected by weather and site conditions.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a home builder near me, consider their experience with mountain construction challenges, reputation, and ability to manage permits and inspections efficiently. A proven track record in high-elevation builds is crucial.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a Build Feasibility Report before purchasing land is highly recommended. This study assesses site conditions, zoning, and overall buildability, helping you avoid costly mistakes.
