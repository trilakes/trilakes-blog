Local Challenges in Eagle County County

McCoy's elevation and geographic features present several challenges for cistern installation. Additionally, the water table in this region can be notably deep, compelling many homeowners to rely on water hauling to fill their potable water storage tanks. With the frost line reaching depths of 36-48 inches or more, proper installation techniques become imperative to ensure that your buried water tank can withstand Colorado's harsh winters. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in McCoy

The cost of cistern installation in McCoy can vary significantly based on several factors. On average, you can expect to pay around $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. These estimates include not just the tank but also excavation, pump systems, and necessary trenching to your home.

Cistern sizing is primarily determined by your daily water usage, calculated using the formula of bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would need about 600 gallons per day. If you plan to refill every month, a minimum of 18,000 gallons would be advisable. Remember, costs can increase if you encounter rocky terrain, require special equipment for difficult access, or need to winterize the installation.

Additional costs to consider include excavation ($3,000-$8,000+ depending on soil conditions), pump and pressure tank systems ($2,500-$5,000), and any electrical hookup needs ($1,000-$2,500). Understanding these factors will help you budget effectively for your water storage tank installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for a cistern typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 depending on the size and specific site conditions. For a 5,000-gallon system, expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your daily water usage. A general guideline is to use the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For a 3-bedroom home, this would require a tank that holds at least 18,000 gallons if refilling once a month.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes a few days to complete, depending on the complexity of the site and weather conditions. Our team ensures that every aspect, from excavation to final inspections, is handled efficiently to minimize disruption to your daily routine.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water source and specific needs. Cisterns are ideal for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well yields, while wells provide a continuous source of water if conditions are favorable. For many in McCoy, a cistern offers a more viable solution, especially in winter months.
