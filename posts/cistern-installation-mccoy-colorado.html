Why McCoy Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For residents of McCoy, Colorado, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, cistern installation is not just a choice but a necessity. With rocky terrain and a deep water table, many properties lack direct access to a reliable water supply. Cisterns provide a practical solution for potable water storage, especially for homes that do not have wells or are seeking alternatives to drilling. The harsh weather conditions, particularly during winter, make professional installation essential for ensuring your underground water storage tank is properly designed and secured against freezing temperatures. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our expertise ensures that your water needs are met with the utmost care and precision.

Local Challenges in Eagle County

Living in McCoy offers stunning views and a serene mountain lifestyle, but it also comes with unique challenges regarding water management. The area's rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate the excavation process for an underground cistern, often resulting in higher costs. Additionally, the elevation means that the frost line is typically 36-48 inches deep, requiring specialized winter preparation for cistern installation. Furthermore, many properties in the region have steep driveways and limited access during winter, which can affect both the installation timeline and costs. Understanding these factors is crucial for homeowners seeking to implement reliable water storage solutions.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to provide a seamless experience for homeowners in McCoy. It begins with an initial consultation where we assess your property, discuss your water needs, and determine the best water storage tank installation options for you. Next, we handle all necessary permits through the Eagle County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations. Our team will then prepare the site, which includes excavation and leveling, typically a labor-intensive step given the local terrain.

Once the site is ready, we will install your chosen potable water storage tank, usually a Norwesco tank, which is recognized for its durability and reliability. Depending on the size of the tank and the complexity of the installation, the process can take anywhere from one to three days, including the setup of a pump system and necessary plumbing connections. Finally, we ensure that the system is fully operational and provide guidance on maintenance and winterizing your water storage solutions to protect against freezing temperatures.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in McCoy

The cost of cistern installation in McCoy can vary significantly based on various factors including site conditions and tank size. Generally, the cost of a 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank starts at around $25,000 and can go up to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. To determine the appropriate size for your needs, we recommend calculating your daily water usage based on your home's bedroom count‚Äîtypically using 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a three-bedroom home with six occupants would need a tank capable of storing at least 600 gallons a day.

Additionally, many mountain properties in McCoy may require more substantial systems due to challenging winter access. For instance, if you opt for a refill every two to three months, you might need a tank size between 36,000 to 54,000 gallons, pushing costs into the $75,000 to $120,000 range for larger dual tank setups. Other costs to consider include excavation, which can add $3,000-$8,000 depending on rockiness and access, and additional expenses for pumps, electrical work, and water filtration systems. It's essential to plan for these expenses comprehensively to avoid surprises during installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on factors like tank size, site conditions, and installation complexity. For a typical 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank, you can expect to pay between $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon tank may cost $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is typically estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would ideally need a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons if you want to refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern can take anywhere from one to three days, depending on the complexity of the site and the size of the water storage tank. Factors such as excavation challenges or the need for additional equipment can affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on the specifics of your property. Cisterns are ideal for homes without access to reliable groundwater, especially in areas like McCoy where the water table is often deep. Wells can be an alternative, but they may not always be feasible due to geological conditions; for a detailed comparison, check out our Cistern vs Well Comparison.
