Why Elizabeth Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Elizabeth, Douglas County, the need for a reliable source of water cannot be overstated. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet and challenging terrain, many properties lack direct access to a well, making cistern installation an essential solution. Unlike standard water systems, a professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a consistent supply of potable water to meet your household needs, especially during the harsh Colorado winters. A skilled cistern installer not only understands the unique local conditions but also navigates the rigorous building permits required by the Douglas County Building Department, ensuring a seamless installation process.

Local Challenges in Douglas County County

The challenges of installing a water cistern in Douglas County are abundant due to the area's unique geographical characteristics. The rocky granite and clay soil often complicate excavation, making it essential to work with experienced professionals who understand the local geology. Additionally, many properties experience deep water tables, necessitating the use of water hauling or the installation of a reliable buried water tank. The severe winter conditions, with frost lines reaching up to 48 inches, require homeowners to consider winter preparation tips and the engineering of frost-proof cisterns to prevent freezing. Furthermore, access can be tricky during the snowy months, with steep driveways and limited routes presenting logistical challenges that can delay access to your water supply.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and site conditions. For instance, a basic 5,000 gallon system may cost around $25,000, while a larger 10,000 gallon setup may range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage and your refill frequency. As a rule of thumb, calculate your needs using 100 gallons per person per day; for example, a 3-bedroom home with four occupants may need a tank capable of holding at least 36,000 gallons if refilled every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the complexity of the site and weather conditions. Factors like excavation challenges and permitting can also affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your specific location and needs. Cisterns are often better for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, while wells may be more suitable if the groundwater is readily accessible and reliable.
