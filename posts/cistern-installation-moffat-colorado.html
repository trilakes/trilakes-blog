Living in the stunning mountain community of Moffat, Saguache County, offers a unique lifestyle, but it also presents specific challenges when it comes to water supply solutions. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties lack a reliable well, making cistern installation an essential part of ensuring a consistent water supply. Local Challenges in Saguache County

Moffat's unique geography poses several challenges that homeowners should be aware of when considering a water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area can complicate excavation, often requiring engineered systems to ensure proper drainage and installation. Additionally, many properties face a deep water table, making wells impractical or unreliable, necessitating the use of buried water tanks for effective water storage. The frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches‚Äîdeeper at higher elevations‚Äîrequiring careful planning to prevent freezing and ensure freeze protection for your cistern. Given the steep driveways and limited winter access, planning for easy maintenance and refills is crucial for rural water supply systems.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on a streamlined process for cistern installation. We begin with an initial consultation where we assess your property's unique characteristics and your specific water needs. This typically involves calculating your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For instance, a 3-bedroom home will require approximately 600 gallons per day.

Next, we determine the ideal tank size and configuration based on how frequently you want to refill your system. Our team then handles the necessary permits through the Saguache County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations. Once permits are secured, we prepare the site, taking into account the rocky terrain and any potential access issues. Excavation is conducted with care, followed by the installation of the water cistern, pump systems, and any necessary electrical connections.

Installation timelines vary, but most projects can be completed within a few weeks, weather permitting. We also provide winterization tips to ensure your underground cistern remains functional during the colder months, safeguarding your investment and ensuring reliable access to water year-round.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Moffat

The cost of cistern installation in Moffat can vary significantly based on several factors. On average, the total installed cost for a 5,000-gallon basic system ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically costs between $40,000 and $55,000. To calculate the appropriate tank size, we recommend using a formula based on your home's bedrooms, where you estimate water usage at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home housing six individuals would require about 600 gallons daily, leading to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Several factors can increase costs, including the difficult rocky terrain in Moffat, which may add 20-40% to excavation costs. Additionally, remote access issues and winter installations can further elevate pricing. It's essential to remember that while the upfront investment may seem high, larger tanks can significantly reduce the need for frequent refills, ultimately saving you time and hassle. Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need depends on your daily water usage, which is generally calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom house with six residents would require a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation timelines can vary, but most installations can be completed within a few weeks, depending on weather conditions and site access. Our team will provide a more accurate timeline during the initial consultation.
