Local Challenges in Summit County

The terrain in Dillon is not just stunning; it's also challenging. The soil primarily consists of rocky granite and clay in the valleys, creating difficulties for traditional septic system installations. With a deep water table, many properties require cisterns for water hauling, complicating the onsite wastewater management process further. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, which must be considered during installation to avoid freeze-related issues. Weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, can also disrupt access to your property, especially in winter. Understanding these local challenges is crucial for a successful septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a systematic approach to septic installation. Our process begins with a thorough soil testing phase, rather than traditional perc tests, to determine the best system for your property. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and collect soil samples for evaluation by a licensed engineer. This report will inform the septic design and determine whether you require an advanced treatment unit (ATU) or a pressure-dosed system.

Once we have the necessary reports and your septic permit from the Summit County Building Department, we can move forward with the installation. The excavation phase typically takes a few days, depending on the site's accessibility and soil conditions. We then install the septic tank and connect the drain field, ensuring optimal placement for effective drainage. After installation, we conduct a septic inspection to confirm everything complies with local regulations before finalizing the project.

Throughout this process, communication is key. We keep you informed at every step, ensuring that your new septic system is not only functional but also built to last.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Dillon

When considering septic installation in Dillon, it's essential to understand the cost factors involved. The total costs for an engineered system generally range from $35,000 to $50,000, while more complex systems, such as sand filter systems, can cost between $45,000 and $65,000. Advanced treatment units (ATUs) may set you back $55,000 to $80,000 or more, especially if rocky terrain adds an additional cost of $10,000 to $25,000.

For properties needing cisterns, the size is typically calculated based on the bedroom count‚Äîspecifically, 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This means a three-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of handling 600 gallons daily. The costs for soil testing and engineering range from $2,000 to $4,000, while permits can vary from $500 to $2,000, depending on local requirements. Excavation can add another $8,000 to $20,000, making it clear that the price can increase quickly based on site conditions.

Understanding these factors will help you budget appropriately and make informed decisions regarding your septic system.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system required, soil conditions, and accessibility. For engineered systems, which are most common in challenging mountain terrain, expect to pay towards the higher end of this range.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process generally takes about one to two weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting. Initial soil testing may take additional time, so it‚Äôs best to start this process well in advance of your planned installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Dillon, due to the challenging soil conditions, will require an engineered septic system. These systems are designed to accommodate the unique characteristics of mountain terrain and ensure effective waste treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests to assess drainage capabilities. This involves digging test holes to gather soil samples, which are then analyzed to determine the appropriate septic system design.
