Local Challenges in Summit County

Heeney's rugged topography poses several challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil found in this region can complicate excavation, often requiring specialized equipment and techniques. Additionally, the area's high elevation creates a deep water table, necessitating an underground cistern for many properties. Homeowners must also consider the frost line, which can reach depths of 36-48 inches, making it essential to properly winterize your potable water storage tank. With extreme temperature fluctuations and heavy snowfall, winter access can be limited, making planning and larger systems more advantageous to avoid frequent refills. Understanding these factors is essential when choosing a cistern company that can effectively address the unique needs of your mountain property.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Heeney

The cost of cistern installation in Heeney can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and additional features. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank typically starts around $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. It's important to note that installation costs depend heavily on the landscape; rocky soil and difficult access may increase excavation costs by 20-40%.

As a rule of thumb, to determine the necessary tank size, multiply the number of bedrooms in your home by 2, and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills. Homeowners often need larger systems to minimize maintenance and refills, especially in winter. The total cost encompasses not just the tank but also excavation, pump systems, trenching to the house, electrical work, and filtration systems, which can collectively add thousands to your investment. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation conditions. For a basic system, expect to pay between $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank, and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Additional costs for excavation and installation can increase the overall price significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage. A general rule is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day and multiply that by the number of bedrooms (assuming 2 occupants per bedroom). For instance, a 3-bedroom home would typically require an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary but generally takes about 1 to 2 weeks from the time permits are secured. This includes site preparation, excavation, tank installation, and system testing. Factors such as site conditions and weather can impact this timeframe.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property's specific circumstances. A cistern is often a better choice for properties with deep water tables or where well drilling is impractical. Cisterns also provide a reliable backup water supply, particularly critical in rural or off-grid settings.
