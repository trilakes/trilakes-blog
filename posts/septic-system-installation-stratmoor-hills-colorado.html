Why Stratmoor Hills Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Stratmoor Hills, nestled in the stunning foothills of El Paso County, is a unique mountain community where the landscape and elevation present distinct challenges for septic installation. At an elevation of over 8,000 feet, homeowners often encounter issues that can complicate the installation and maintenance of a septic system. Without professional guidance, these challenges can lead to costly mistakes, environmental hazards, and regulatory non-compliance.

Engaging a licensed and insured septic installer near me, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, is crucial for ensuring that your system is designed and installed correctly. Our experienced team understands the local soil conditions, necessary permits, and specific requirements for onsite wastewater systems (OWTS) in this region. With our expertise, we guarantee that your septic tank is installed effectively, providing peace of mind and protection for your property.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

The rugged terrain of Stratmoor Hills poses several challenges for effective septic installation. The area is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which can complicate the installation of traditional systems. Many properties require engineered septic systems, as indicated by the Colorado's septic regulations under Regulation 43. The deep water table and frost line, which can reach depths of 36-48 inches, further necessitate advanced planning and system design.

Additionally, winter weather presents unique obstacles. With heavy snowfall and extreme temperature swings, homeowners must consider winterizing their septic systems to avoid freeze damage. Access issues due to steep driveways and limited winter access can also delay installation timelines. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting understands these local challenges and can provide solutions tailored to the specific needs of your property.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure your system is tailored to your land's needs. The first step is to conduct soil testing, where we bring a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples. These samples are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to generate a soils report that determines the appropriate type of system for your property.

Once we have the soils report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the El Paso County Building Department. This permit is essential for compliance with local regulations. After securing the permit, we proceed to design the system based on the report findings and your home's requirements.

Typically, the installation process spans several days to a week, depending on soil conditions, access challenges, and the complexity of the system required. Our team carefully excavates the site, installs the septic tank and associated components, and then ensures the drain field or leach field is properly established. We also provide guidance on maintenance and care to extend the life of your system and address winter preparation tips to protect it from freezing conditions.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Stratmoor Hills

The cost of septic installation in Stratmoor Hills varies significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, site accessibility, and the type of system needed. On average, a basic engineered system will start around $35,000, while more complex systems like sand filter systems can reach up to $65,000. If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is required, expect costs to range from $55,000 to over $80,000, especially if you encounter rocky terrain that complicates installation.

For homes needing water cisterns, the size is calculated based on the bedroom count, assuming two people per bedroom at approximately 100 gallons per day. This adds another layer of cost, with installation ranging from $2,000 to $4,000 for soils testing and engineering, plus $500 to $2,000 for permits. Excavation can run $8,000 to $20,000, depending on site conditions. With all these factors, we recommend budgeting for the high end of the ranges to account for unexpected challenges.

Understanding these dynamics is crucial. Engaging a professional septic contractor ensures that you are prepared for the costs involved, safeguarding your budget and your property's value.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 for a basic engineered system to upwards of $80,000 for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions and necessary permits.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process usually takes anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on factors like soil conditions, access to the site, and the complexity of the system being installed.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains, including Stratmoor Hills, require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions, as mandated by Regulation 43. A professional assessment will determine the specific system needed for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the appropriate septic system design for your property.
