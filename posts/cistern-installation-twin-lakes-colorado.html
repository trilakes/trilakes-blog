Why Twin Lakes Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Twin Lakes, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, ensuring a reliable water supply is essential for both residents and property owners. Many homes in this area lack direct access to well water, making cistern installation a vital solution for potable water storage. Professional cistern installers, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, bring invaluable expertise to the table. We understand the unique challenges posed by the rugged terrain, seasonal weather fluctuations, and the need for a frost-proof cistern system. This ensures that your underground water storage tank is not only functional but also strategically designed to withstand harsh winters and maintain accessibility during snowy months.

Local Challenges in Lake County

Living in Lake County presents unique challenges for cistern installation. The region's rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation, often requiring engineered solutions to navigate the difficult terrain. At elevations of 8,000 feet and above, the water table is frequently deep, prompting many property owners to rely on water hauling or the installation of an underground cistern. With a frost line of 36-48 inches, it's critical to prepare for winter by ensuring that your buried water tank is properly insulated to prevent freezing. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can pose logistical hurdles during installation, making it essential to work with a licensed and experienced cistern company that understands these local conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Twin Lakes

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for planning your budget. The overall price can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions and the size of the tank. For instance, a typical 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank can start at around $13,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000. These numbers represent the tank only; the total installed cost generally ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic system and can go up to $55,000 or more for larger installations.

When sizing your potable water storage tank, consider your daily water usage, which is estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home accommodating six people would require at least 600 gallons daily. If you prefer to refill monthly, a minimum of 18,000 gallons is recommended, whereas a system designed to get you through winter may need 72,000 gallons or more. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and installation complexities. For a basic system, expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank, while larger systems can reach $55,000 or more.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage. A good formula is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of people in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six residents would require at least 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes between 1 to 2 weeks, depending on the complexity of your site and prevailing weather conditions. Proper planning and permitting through the Lake County Building Department can also impact the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's water availability. Cisterns serve as an excellent alternative when wells are impractical due to depth or reliability issues. They can also provide a more consistent water supply, especially in rural areas where well water may be limited.
