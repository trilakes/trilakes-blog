Why Twin Lakes Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the breathtaking mountain community of Twin Lakes is a dream for many. However, the challenges of securing a reliable water supply can turn that dream into a logistical nightmare. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet and varying soil conditions, the need for professional cistern installation cannot be overstated. Unlike lower elevations, Twin Lakes properties often lack access to sustainable well water, making an underground water storage tank essential. By enlisting a licensed and insured cistern installer near me, homeowners can ensure that their systems are designed and installed correctly, providing peace of mind and a steady supply of potable water.

Local Challenges in Lake County County

Twin Lakes presents unique challenges for water access that require careful consideration. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for a buried water tank. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep, often necessitating water hauling for many properties. The frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, adding another layer of complexity to water storage tank installation. The extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall can also affect access to properties, particularly in winter. Furthermore, the Lake County County Building Department requires permits for all cistern installations, ensuring that safety and environmental standards are met. Understanding these local challenges is vital for anyone considering a water cistern for their mountain property.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a comprehensive approach to cistern installation. Our process begins with a thorough site assessment to evaluate soil conditions, topography, and access challenges. Once we understand the specific needs of your property, we calculate the appropriate tank size based on your daily water usage and refill preferences. This often involves considering the number of bedrooms in your home and estimating water consumption at 100 gallons per person per day.

Once the design is finalized, we move into the excavation phase, which may take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions. We typically use Norwesco tanks, renowned for their durability and efficiency, which we install underground for optimal thermal regulation. After the tank is placed, we install the necessary plumbing, including pumps and filtration systems, ensuring everything meets Lake County regulations. Finally, we conduct a thorough inspection to confirm that your new potable water storage tank operates flawlessly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to over $120,000, depending on the size and specifications of the system. For instance, a 10,000-gallon tank alone can cost between $22,000 and $28,000, with additional excavation and plumbing costs adding to the overall price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need is calculated based on your daily water usage, estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an assumed occupancy of 6 people would require at least an 18,000-gallon tank if you want to refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary but generally takes between a few days to a couple of weeks. Factors affecting this timeline include site conditions, tank size, and excavation requirements.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions. Cisterns offer a reliable water supply without the complications of drilling a well, which may be impractical or too deep in areas like Twin Lakes. Additionally, cisterns can serve multiple purposes, including agricultural and fire suppression needs.
