Local Challenges in Fremont County County

Installing a buried water tank in Brookside brings about several challenges that can hinder proper water access. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil types can complicate excavation, making it essential to choose an experienced cistern company that understands these conditions. Many properties in this mountainous region also face a deep water table, which often necessitates the use of cisterns instead of conventional well systems. For those considering a well alternative, installing an underground cistern can provide a reliable and efficient source of water.

The frost line in Brookside ranges from 36 to 48 inches, which means that proper installation of your cistern must account for freeze protection. Without proper precautions, you risk the cistern freezing and becoming unusable during the harsh winter months. Furthermore, many properties have steep driveways and limited winter access, making it crucial to plan your installation around the building season, typically from May to October. Understanding these factors is vital to ensure that your water storage tank installation is successful and meets your needs.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Brookside

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is essential for homeowners in Brookside. The total installed cost typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on the size and complexity of the system. For example, a standard 10,000 gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000.

When sizing your potable water storage tank, we recommend using a formula based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count. For instance, if you have a 3-bedroom home, assuming 2 people per bedroom, you would need around 600 gallons per day (100 gallons per person). If you prefer a refill every month, your minimum tank size should be approximately 18,000 gallons. This strategic planning helps avoid frequent refills, especially during winter months when access can be challenging.

Several factors can increase costs, including:

  • Rocky soil: Difficult excavation can add 20-40% to your costs.
  • Access issues: Steep driveways or limited winter access increase labor and equipment costs.
  • Additional systems: Installing a comprehensive pump system and filtration can add $5,000 or more to the total.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on the size and complexity of the system. For example, a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, which includes excavation, pump systems, and other necessary components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person per day. For a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants, a minimum of 18,000 gallons is recommended for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions, but typically it takes about 1-2 weeks from permitting to completion. Factors like weather, excavation difficulty, and tank size can impact the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns are often better suited for areas with a deep water table or rocky soil, providing a reliable backup water supply without the complications of well drilling.
