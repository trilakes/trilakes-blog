Furthermore, the deep water table means many homeowners need to rely on cisterns for water, complicating the installation further. With frost lines reaching depths of 36-48 inches, winter preparation is crucial. The building season is limited to May through October, and access can be tricky due to steep driveways and heavy snow loads. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process begins with a thorough consultation and site assessment. We perform comprehensive soil testing rather than traditional perc tests, which involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and collecting soil samples at varying depths. These samples are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the appropriate septic design based on the soil characteristics. This important step ensures that the system we install will function efficiently and effectively.

Once we receive the soil report, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Gunnison County Building Department. The actual installation process typically takes between 1 to 3 weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system required. We handle all aspects of the installation, including excavation, tank placement, and connection to the drain field. Our focus is on ensuring a seamless installation that meets all local regulations and stands the test of time.

As part of our commitment to quality, we also provide guidance on winterizing your septic system to prevent freezing and other winter-related issues, ensuring your system remains functional and efficient year-round.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Ohio City

When considering septic installation in Ohio City, it's important to understand the various factors that influence the overall cost. The total installed cost for a basic engineered system typically starts around $35,000 and can go as high as $50,000, depending on the specifics of your property. For a sand filter system, the costs can range from $45,000 to $65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) could set you back between $55,000 and $80,000. These figures are influenced by factors such as soil conditions, the accessibility of the site, and whether additional excavation is needed due to rocky terrain.

For homeowners needing water storage, remember that the size of your cistern is often calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home‚Äîspecifically, two people per bedroom with an estimated usage of 100 gallons per day. This calculation is vital for ensuring your system meets your household‚Äôs demands.

Remember also that costs will vary based on site conditions; rocky or difficult terrain can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your total installation expense. Additionally, remember to factor in the costs for soil testing, permitting, and other components, which can add significantly to the overall project budget.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on site conditions and the type of system required. For a basic engineered system, costs typically start around $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. More advanced systems like sand filters or ATUs may range from $45,000 to over $80,000 depending on specific site challenges.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a septic system generally ranges from 1 to 3 weeks. This depends on factors such as site conditions and the complexity of the system being installed. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting strives to ensure a smooth and efficient installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Ohio City and the surrounding mountainous regions require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations outlined in Regulation 43. These systems are designed to effectively manage waste where conventional systems would fail.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil testing methods. We conduct soil tests to assess soil conditions by digging test holes and analyzing samples, which helps determine the most appropriate septic system design for your property.
