Local Challenges in El Paso County

Ramah's unique topography and climatic conditions present a variety of challenges for water storage solutions. The soil in El Paso County ranges from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, which complicates excavation and can drive up installation costs due to increased labor and equipment. Additionally, at elevations like those found in Ramah, the frost line reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning for winterizing cistern installation. Access to many properties is limited by steep driveways and can be treacherous in winter months, making it crucial to consider the ease of water delivery and maintenance. With extreme temperature fluctuations and heavy snow in the winter, having a reliable buried water tank is not just a convenience but a necessity for maintaining a consistent and safe water supply.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Ramah

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for homeowners in Ramah. The total installed costs can vary significantly based on site conditions and tank size. For instance, a typical 5,000-gallon cistern can cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. These prices include excavation, installation, pumps, and necessary electrical work.

To determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank, use this formula: number of bedrooms x 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home may require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, or even larger systems to accommodate less frequent refills, especially during winter.

Bear in mind that costs can increase due to challenging terrain, as rocky soil can add 20-40% to excavation costs. Additionally, if winter conditions necessitate special equipment or handling, expect higher costs for installation. Early planning and consultation with our team can help you achieve the best water storage solutions for your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and site conditions. For a standard 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank typically costs $40,000 to $55,000, including installation and necessary equipment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on the number of bedrooms in your home. A general rule of thumb is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, while larger systems may be necessary for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes about 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site conditions, tank size, and any necessary permitting. Our team works efficiently to minimize disruption while ensuring that your system is installed correctly and meets all local standards.

Q: Cistern vs. well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often comes down to site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are a reliable alternative when wells are impractical due to deep water tables or rocky terrain. They offer flexibility for water storage and can serve as a backup water supply for low-yield wells.
