Why Goldfield Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Goldfield, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, water access can be a significant challenge. With the rocky granite soil and often deep water tables, many properties require reliable cistern installation to ensure a consistent potable water supply. This is especially true in a mountainous region where well drilling may be impractical or impossible due to geological conditions. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we specialize in underground water storage tank installations tailored to meet the unique needs of our mountain community.

By choosing professional services for your water storage tank installation, you gain access to expert knowledge about local regulations and soil conditions. Our experienced team not only handles the technical aspects of installing a water cistern but also ensures compliance with Teller County permit requirements. When you invest in a quality cistern system, you secure peace of mind knowing your water supply is safe, reliable, and ready for winter.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Living in Goldfield comes with its own set of challenges, particularly regarding water supply and cistern installation. The rugged mountain terrain presents difficulties such as rocky granite and clay soils, complicating excavation and installation. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, which means that any buried water tank must be installed deep enough to avoid freezing during the harsh Colorado winters.

Moreover, many properties in this area experience limited access, especially in winter months when heavy snow can block steep driveways. This can affect not only the installation timeline but also the ease of water hauling for those relying on deliveries. The extreme temperature swings and potential for afternoon thunderstorms add another layer of complexity to the planning and implementation of an effective off-grid water system.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process to ensure successful cistern installation. Initially, we conduct a detailed site assessment to evaluate soil conditions, property access, and your specific water needs. Understanding your daily water usage is crucial; for instance, a typical 3-bedroom home may require around 600 gallons per day. Cistern sizing is then determined based on your daily usage and desired refill frequency, ensuring your system is as efficient as possible.

Once we finalize the design, we will apply for the necessary permits through the Teller County Building Department. This step is essential to comply with local regulations regarding water storage solutions. Our team then begins excavation, which can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on soil conditions and site accessibility. We typically use Norwesco tanks for their durability and reliability, ensuring you receive a high-quality underground water storage tank.

After the tank is installed, we set up the pump system and pressure tank, followed by trenching to connect to your home. Finally, we conduct thorough testing to ensure your water cistern is fully operational. Our goal is to leave you with a reliable, efficient system that meets your water needs year-round, especially as you prepare for winter.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Goldfield

When considering cistern installation in Goldfield, it's crucial to understand the various factors that influence costs. The price for a basic 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. For properties with higher water needs, dual tank systems can range from $50,000 to $75,000 or more, depending on specific requirements.

Sizing your water cistern is essential and is generally based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home (6 occupants) would need approximately 600 gallons per day. If you prefer fewer refills during winter, you might opt for a system that holds 36,000 to 54,000 gallons to last 2-3 months. Keep in mind that excavation costs can vary significantly, often between $3,000 to $8,000 or more depending on rock and access challenges.

Additional costs may include plumbing for the pump system ($2,500-$5,000), trenching to the house ($25-$50 per foot), and electrical hook-up ($1,000-$2,500). Make sure to factor these elements into your budget as they contribute significantly to the overall investment in your water storage tank installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and installation requirements. For larger systems, especially those that can serve multiple purposes such as agricultural water storage, costs can rise significantly, often reaching up to $120,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill it. A general rule is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, then size the tank based on the number of bedrooms in your home and your desired refill frequency.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions, access, and weather. The process includes site assessment, excavation, tank installation, and final hookup, all of which are tailored to your specific needs.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and well largely depends on your property‚Äôs geological conditions. Cisterns are often a better option in areas with deep water tables or challenging soil, providing a reliable backup water supply without the complications of well drilling.
