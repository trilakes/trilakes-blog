Local Challenges in Eagle County

Eagle County's challenging landscape presents several factors that influence the success of a septic system. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area often require engineered septic systems rather than traditional options. Additionally, with a water table that tends to be deep, many properties rely on cisterns for their water supply, further complicating the septic installation process.

At elevations between 7,000 and 10,000 feet, the frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning for septic tank placement to avoid freeze damage. Moreover, building season is limited to May through October, and steep driveways can hinder access during winter months, complicating any necessary septic inspection or maintenance. Understanding these local challenges is crucial for homeowners looking to install a reliable septic system.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, step-by-step process for septic installation to ensure your system meets all regulations and functions effectively:

  1. Soil Testing: We conduct thorough soil testing, utilizing a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and send samples to a licensed engineer for analysis. This is crucial to determine the appropriate system type.
  2. Septic Design: Based on the soil report, we design an onsite wastewater treatment system tailored to your property‚Äôs unique needs. This may include pressure-dosed, sand filter, or advanced treatment units (ATUs).
  3. Permitting: We assist in obtaining all necessary septic permits through the Eagle County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Installation: Our experienced team executes the installation, which typically involves excavation, tank placement, and connection to the leach field or drain field.
  5. Inspection: After installation, we schedule an inspection to confirm the system is operational and meets all codes set forth by Regulation 43.
  6. Winterizing: Given the harsh winters in Eagle, we provide winter preparation tips to help you protect your septic system from freezing temperatures.

This process generally takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting timelines. Homeowners can trust that we prioritize quality and compliance throughout every phase of the installation.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Eagle

The cost of septic installation in Eagle can vary significantly based on several factors. The total installed costs for a basic engineered system typically start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. For sand filter systems, the costs range from $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can reach $55,000 to $80,000 or more. Factors that can increase costs include challenging rocky terrain, steep access driveways, and the need for larger excavation due to soil conditions.

For properties requiring a cistern, the size needed is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in the home, with the formula being: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. This can impact both your upfront costs and ongoing water management. It is crucial to factor in these variables when budgeting for your septic installation project.

Additional expenses may include soil testing and engineering fees, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, and permit costs that may vary between $500 and $2,000. Homeowners should always expect to quote the higher end of these ranges, especially considering the unique challenges in Eagle.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system and site conditions. Basic engineered systems start around $35,000, while advanced treatment units can exceed $80,000, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary, but it generally takes several weeks to complete the entire process, from soil testing to final inspection. Factors such as permitting and site conditions can impact the duration.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Eagle require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations. A soils report will determine the appropriate system for your site.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests to assess soil suitability for a septic system. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to guide the design of the appropriate system.
