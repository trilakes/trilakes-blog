Local Challenges in Chaffee County County

Excavating in Chaffee County is not without its challenges. The soil composition can vary significantly, with rocky granite and clay often complicating site development. The high elevation and deep water table require careful planning, especially for those needing cisterns for water storage and engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. Additionally, the frost line ranges from 36 to 48 inches at these elevations, which can impact foundation excavation projects.

Weather conditions in Johnson Village can also be extreme, with heavy snow loads in winter and sudden thunderstorms in summer. These factors can affect construction timelines and require additional consideration during excavation planning. Steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate logistics further, making it essential to have a knowledgeable excavation contractor who understands the local terrain and climate.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs typically range from $50 to $100 per cubic yard, depending on factors such as soil condition and accessibility. In challenging terrains like Johnson Village, these costs can be at the higher end of the spectrum.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration for site preparation usually falls between one to three weeks, depending on project complexity and site conditions. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures a thorough evaluation and efficient execution to minimize delays.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Mountain excavation often requires specialized equipment such as excavators, bobcats, and dump trucks. The choice of machinery depends on the specific tasks at hand, including rock removal, grading, and foundation excavation.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are required for excavation projects in Johnson Village. All permits must be obtained through the Chaffee County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations.
