Local Challenges in Saguache County

The mountainous terrain and distinct climate of Saguache County present unique challenges for water sourcing. With rocky granite and clay soils, achieving proper excavation for a buried water tank can be complex and costly. The water table in many areas is often deep, requiring homeowners to rely on water hauling and cistern systems. Additionally, the frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful consideration during cistern installation to prevent freezing. Access to properties can be limited during winter months, which is critical when planning your water storage tank installation. Furthermore, the extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads can complicate installation processes, making it essential to partner with a skilled cistern installer near me who understands these local nuances.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Center

The overall cost of cistern installation in Center can vary significantly based on numerous factors. Here are the starting price ranges you can expect:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: typically between $13,000 and $15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: typically between $22,000 and $28,000
  • Excavation costs range from $3,000 to $8,000+, depending on site conditions
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500 to $5,000
  • Trenching costs: $25 to $50 per linear foot
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000 to $2,500
  • Total installed costs for a 5,000-gallon system can reach $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000.

Determining the right size for your potable water storage tank hinges on your daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of 6 people will need at least 600 gallons per day. Factors like rocky terrain, access issues, and the need for frost protection can significantly increase your installation costs. Opting for a larger system can lead to long-term savings by reducing the need for frequent water hauling, especially in winter months when deliveries may be limited.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and installation complexity. Typically, a 5,000-gallon system can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system generally costs between $40,000 and $55,000, inclusive of excavation and system setup.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is typically calculated based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day). For instance, a 3-bedroom home may require a minimum of 36,000 gallons if refilling every 2-3 months, or 72,000 gallons for winter storage.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take approximately 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. A thorough site assessment and efficient excavation are crucial for a timely installation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific circumstances. Cisterns are often preferred in areas with a deep water table or challenging drilling conditions, while wells may offer a more consistent water supply if the conditions allow.
