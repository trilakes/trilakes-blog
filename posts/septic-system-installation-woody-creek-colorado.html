Local Challenges in Pitkin County County

Woody Creek's unique geography and climate significantly affect the installation of onsite wastewater treatment systems (OWTS). The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in this area often necessitate engineered systems due to their inability to effectively drain wastewater. Properties in this elevation range also face a frost line of 36-48 inches, demanding careful planning for septic installation during the limited building season, which typically runs from May to October. Additionally, properties with steep driveways can pose accessibility challenges, especially during winter months when heavy snowfall restricts access. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a comprehensive approach to septic system installation, ensuring every detail is meticulously addressed. Our process begins with a thorough soil testing phase, where we use a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer who will provide a detailed soils report, essential for determining the appropriate system type for your property.

Once we receive the soils report, we move on to designing the septic system, which may include options such as pressure-dosed systems or sand filter systems, depending on site conditions. We then assist you in navigating the Pitkin County Permit Guide to obtain the necessary permits from the county building department. This is a critical step, as all permits must go through this office to ensure compliance with Regulation 43.

After securing permits, we schedule the installation, which typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site complexity and weather conditions. We prioritize winter preparation tips for homeowners, such as proper winterizing of your system to prevent freeze damage. Our goal is to ensure not only a compliant installation but also a sustainable and reliable septic system that will serve your mountain home for years to come.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost for septic installation in Colorado generally ranges from $35,000 to $80,000 depending on system complexity and site conditions. Basic engineered systems start at around $35,000, while more advanced systems can exceed $80,000 due to factors like rocky terrain or specific regulatory requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Typically, septic installation can take 1-2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and weather conditions. The process involves multiple stages including soil testing, engineering, permitting, and the actual installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, properties in the Mountain Community of Woody Creek require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are designed to meet the standards set by Regulation 43 and are crucial for effective waste management.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging deep test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities, which is essential for determining suitable septic system designs.
