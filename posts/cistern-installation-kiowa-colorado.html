Why Kiowa Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the picturesque mountain community of Kiowa, Douglas County County, comes with its own unique challenges, especially regarding water access. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet, many properties in this area lack reliable well water sources, making cistern installation not just a convenience but a necessity. Homeowners often turn to cisterns as a practical solution for potable water storage, irrigation, and even fire suppression. Opting for professional service ensures that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, complies with Douglas County building regulations, and is equipped to withstand the harsh winter conditions and rocky terrain.

Local Challenges in Douglas County County

The unique topography and weather patterns of Kiowa present distinct challenges for homeowners. The soil here primarily consists of rocky granite and clay in the valleys, making excavation for a water storage tank installation complex and potentially costly. The water table is often deep, which means many properties must rely on water hauling or installing underground cisterns for their rural water supply. Additionally, the frost line ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning to ensure your cistern is properly winterized. Steep driveways and limited access during winter months can complicate delivery and installation. Fire mitigation is another critical factor, as defensible space regulations are paramount in forested areas to protect against wildfires.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Kiowa

Understanding the costs involved in cistern installation is crucial for planning your project budget. The overall price typically reflects several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and additional system requirements.

When sizing your underground water storage tank, consider the number of bedrooms in your home. A good rule of thumb is to calculate daily water usage as follows: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom house with 6 occupants would need approximately 600 gallons per day. Depending on how frequently you wish to refill, your cistern size will vary:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: minimum 8,400 gallons
  • Refill monthly: minimum 18,000 gallons
  • Refill every 2-3 months: between 36,000-54,000 gallons
  • Winter survival: 72,000+ gallons (multiple tanks)

We typically install Norwesco tanks, which are industry leaders in potable water storage solutions. Pricing starts at:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

Additional costs can include excavation (ranging from $3,000-$8,000+ based on rocky terrain), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching to your home ($25-$50 per linear foot), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500). Consequently, a basic 5,000-gallon system can run between $25,000-$35,000, while larger systems can reach $120,000 or more. Remember, rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, so plan accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $120,000 or more for larger, complex systems, depending on site conditions and additional features required.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on the number of bedrooms in your home and your daily water usage. A general formula is to assume 100 gallons of water per person per day, multiplied by the number of people living in the house, which is typically estimated as 2 per bedroom.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from 1 to 3 weeks, depending on the complexity of your site, including soil conditions and weather factors that may impact excavation and installation timelines.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on your property's specific circumstances. Cisterns are a reliable alternative for properties where drilling a well is impractical or the water table is too deep, offering greater flexibility in water management.
