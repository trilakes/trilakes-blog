A professionally installed underground water storage tank not only provides a consistent supply of potable water but also protects against potential freeze damage during the harsh winters. Trying to navigate these complexities without the help of a qualified cistern installer can lead to costly mistakes and inadequate water solutions. Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

The challenges of installing a water cistern in Silver Plume are multifaceted. Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for water storage tank installation to ensure your system is efficient and durable. The first step involves conducting a thorough site assessment, which allows us to understand the unique characteristics of your property, including soil type, slope, and access challenges.

Next, we help you determine the appropriate size for your underground cistern based on your daily water usage and refill preferences. For example, a typical calculation would involve estimating around 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home.

Once sizing is established, we‚Äôll prepare the site, which may involve excavation and grading. Excavation costs can range from $3,000 to $8,000+ depending on rock and access issues, so it's essential to factor this into your planning. Following excavation, we install the tank, typically a Norwesco tank known for its durability and efficiency. Finally, we complete the system with pumps, electrical connections, and any necessary filtration systems.

The entire process usually takes a few days, but this can vary depending on site conditions and weather. We pride ourselves on being thorough and addressing all aspects of your water storage solutions, ensuring everything is set for optimal performance.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Silver Plume

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Silver Plume is crucial for proper budgeting. For basic systems, the total installed costs range from approximately $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000 gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000 gallon system. These estimates include the tank, excavation, pumps, trenching, and electrical hookup, among other necessary components.

To size your potable water storage tank, consider your household's water usage, calculated as follows: number of bedrooms x 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day. If you prefer to refill every 2 weeks, you would need at least 8,400 gallons of capacity.

Several factors can influence your installation costs beyond the tank itself. Rocky terrain may increase excavation costs by 20-40%, and remote access can add hauling fees. It's important to note that when planning for your water storage tank installation, you should quote the high end of the pricing ranges to avoid surprises.

For ongoing water hauling, expect to pay about $200-$350 per delivery for 3,000 gallons, making larger tanks more economical in the long run.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range from approximately $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and installation requirements. This includes the tank, excavation, pumps, and necessary hookups, but costs can vary based on site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your household water usage. A general rule of thumb is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several days, but the timeline can vary depending on site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the installation. A thorough site assessment is crucial for accurate timing.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns provide a reliable water source when wells are impractical or too deep, while wells can offer continuous access to groundwater. Consider your local water table and site conditions before deciding.
