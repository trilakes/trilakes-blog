Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Building a water supply system in Clear Creek County, particularly in Silver Plume, involves navigating various local challenges. The rocky granite soil, coupled with clay found in the valleys, complicates excavation for buried water tanks. At elevations above 8,000 feet, the water table can often be deep, necessitating the use of cisterns and water hauling for properties that cannot rely on traditional wells.

Moreover, the frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, which adds complexity to water storage tank installation. During the winter months, steep driveways can limit access, making it crucial to consider winterizing your system to ensure it remains functional and protected. Understanding these local challenges is vital for anyone looking to install a water cistern in this beautiful mountain community.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Silver Plume

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Silver Plume is crucial for budgeting your project. The total cost is influenced by factors such as tank size, site conditions, and any additional installations required. For example, if you have a 3-bedroom home, you would need a system capable of handling approximately 600 gallons per day. This translates to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we typically install Norwesco tanks, which are known for their durability and efficiency. A 5,000-gallon tank starts at around $13,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. When factoring in excavation costs, which can vary from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the terrain, and additional expenses like pump systems and electrical hookups, the total installed cost can reach $40,000 to $55,000 for a standard 10,000-gallon system.

It's important to keep in mind that rocky or challenging terrain can increase costs by 20-40%, and winter installations may be more expensive due to ground conditions. Proper planning and a larger tank can reduce the need for frequent water deliveries, ultimately saving you money in the long run.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on several factors, including tank size and site conditions. For instance, a 5,000-gallon system can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically costs between $40,000 and $55,000, including installation and necessary components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage. For example, in a 3-bedroom home with an estimated usage of 600 gallons per day, you would need at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills or a larger tank for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions and project complexity. Generally, installations can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, especially when factoring in excavation and permitting processes.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

Cisterns are often the better option in areas like Silver Plume, where wells may be impractical due to depth or reliability issues.
