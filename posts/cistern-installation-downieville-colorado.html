Why Downieville Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Downieville, Clear Creek County, the need for reliable water storage solutions is critical, especially at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet. With rocky terrain and challenging soil conditions, attempting a DIY cistern installation can lead to costly mistakes. Professional cistern installation is not just about digging a hole and placing a tank; it requires a comprehensive understanding of local regulations, soil composition, and the unique environmental factors at high altitudes. By choosing a licensed and insured contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting, you ensure that your water storage tank installation meets all safety standards and is designed to provide consistent, long-term access to potable water.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Downieville's rugged and beautiful mountain landscape presents several challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can make excavation difficult, often leading to increased costs. Additionally, the water table is typically deep in this area, meaning that many properties do not have access to well water and must rely on water hauling and underground cisterns as their primary water supply. Winter conditions pose another significant challenge; with a frost line of 36-48 inches, it's essential to ensure that your potable water storage tank is properly insulated to prevent freezing. Homeowners should also consider accessibility during the winter months, as steep driveways can limit access for water delivery services.

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for planning your project. The cost of a cistern typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. These prices include the tank, excavation, pumps, trenching, electrical hookups, and any additional equipment. To determine the right size for your water storage solutions, consider your daily water usage. A general guideline is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for a basic cistern installation typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank. These figures can increase significantly based on site conditions, such as rocky terrain and access challenges.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. A general rule of thumb is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a tank size between 36,000-54,000 gallons for a bi-monthly refill schedule.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern typically spans 2-4 weeks, depending on site evaluation and conditions. Factors such as excavation difficulties and permitting through the Clear Creek County Building Department can influence this timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. For properties with a deep water table or difficult access to well drilling, a cistern may be a more viable solution. Cisterns also offer the advantage of flexibility and can serve as a backup water supply for low-yield wells.
