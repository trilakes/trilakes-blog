Local Challenges in El Paso County

Installing a septic system in El Paso County presents several challenges, particularly due to the local soil conditions and the mountainous terrain. Most properties in this area feature rocky granite or clay soils, which can complicate traditional septic installation. The deep water table often requires additional solutions such as cisterns or water hauling, further complicating installation. Additionally, the frost line in Calhan reaches depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating deeper excavations for both the septic tank and drain field.

The weather conditions in this region are also noteworthy. With extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall, careful planning for winter preparation and freeze protection is essential for maintaining the functionality of your septic system. Furthermore, many properties feature steep driveways and limited access, which can hinder installation efforts. Fire mitigation is also critical, as defensible space requirements need to be adhered to in forested areas.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, client-focused process for septic system installation that ensures your system meets both regulatory requirements and your needs:

  1. Initial Consultation: We start with a site visit and consultation to understand your property and needs.
  2. Soil Testing: We perform in-depth soil testing, not traditional perc tests, by digging two test holes up to eight feet deep. This helps us determine the type of system required based on the soil report from a licensed engineer.
  3. System Design: Based on the soils report, we will design an appropriate system‚Äîmost commonly an engineered system due to Calhan's challenging soils.
  4. Permitting: We handle the necessary septic permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  5. Installation: Our team will excavate, install the septic tank, and create the drain field or leach field as per the approved design.
  6. Inspection and Finalization: Once installation is complete, we schedule an inspection to ensure everything meets local standards before finalizing the project.

This thorough process typically spans several weeks, depending on the complexity of the installation and local permitting timelines.
