Local Challenges in Park County

Shawnee's mountainous landscape poses several challenges for septic installation. The soil here varies significantly, from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, making soil testing crucial before any installation begins. A deep water table often means that many properties in the area require cisterns for water supply, complicating the onsite wastewater (OWTS) management further. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches poses a threat in the winter months, requiring specialized installation techniques to prevent freeze damage. Properties often have steep driveways and limited access during winter, making it essential to plan installations during the building season, which typically runs from May to October. Fire mitigation is also critical in forested areas, so adhering to local regulations and creating defensible space around your property is non-negotiable.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a thorough process for septic installation that ensures compliance with all regulatory requirements and addresses the unique challenges of Shawnee. First, we conduct soil testing to determine the appropriate system type for your property. This involves digging two test holes, each eight feet deep, to sample the soil at various depths. These samples are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to produce a soils report, which guides our septic design.

Once the soils report is available, we assist homeowners in obtaining the necessary septic permits from the Park County Building Department, as all permits must go through this office. After securing the permit, we schedule the installation, which typically takes a few days, depending on site conditions and weather. Our experienced team will excavate the site, install the septic tank and necessary components, and construct the drainage system, including any leach fields or drain fields as required. Post-installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before we complete the project.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Shawnee

The cost of septic installation in Shawnee can vary significantly based on multiple factors. Generally, the total installed cost for a basic engineered system starts from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, while a sand filter system can range from $45,000 to $65,000. For those needing an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), the costs can rise to $55,000-$80,000+. Factors that influence these costs include the challenging rocky terrain, access difficulties, and specific site conditions that may require additional excavation or specialized systems. Additionally, if your property requires a cistern, the tank size is determined based on your bedroom count‚Äîspecifically, two individuals per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. The preparation costs also include soil testing and engineering, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, while permits can vary between $500 and $2,000 depending on the county requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions and system type. For most properties in Shawnee, a basic engineered system can start from $35,000 and go up to $50,000, while advanced systems may range from $55,000 to $80,000+.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a new septic system typically ranges from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions, the complexity of the system, and weather factors. Proper planning is crucial to ensure timely installation, especially in the challenging mountainous terrain of Shawnee.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Shawnee require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and regulations set forth by Colorado's Regulation 43. This is especially true for properties with rocky terrain or poor drainage capabilities.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with a thorough soil testing process. We dig test holes to analyze the soil, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the site's characteristics and what type of septic system is most suitable.
