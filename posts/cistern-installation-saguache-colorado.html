With an elevation of over 8,000 feet, many properties do not have access to a consistent well water supply, making cistern installation an essential service. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we specialize in installing underground water storage tanks that provide homeowners with practical and dependable solutions for their potable water storage needs. Local Challenges in Saguache County

Saguache County is known for its rugged terrain, characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which can pose significant challenges during water storage tank installation. The water table in this mountainous region is often deep, making many properties reliant on cisterns and water hauling for their water supply. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, winter preparations are critical to avoid system failures due to freezing temperatures. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate the installation process, underscoring the importance of hiring a cistern installer near me who understands these unique local conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Saguache

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is crucial for planning your project. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we provide transparent pricing; however, it's important to note that costs can vary significantly based on site conditions and required equipment. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Underground Water Storage Tanks: We offer Norwesco tanks, with prices ranging from $13,000-$15,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank and $22,000-$28,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavating for burial can cost between $3,000 to $8,000, depending on the rocky terrain and accessibility.
  • Pump System & Pressure Tank: Expect to invest between $2,500 and $5,000 for a reliable pumping system.
  • Trenching: Connecting the tank to your house typically costs $25 to $50 per linear foot.
  • Additional Components: Costs for risers, access lids, and electrical hookups can range from $500 to $2,500 or more, depending on your specific needs.

For an installed system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Larger dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons) can range from $50,000 to $75,000 or more. Remember, rocky terrain and access issues can add 20-40% to your total excavation costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely, but typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 for installed systems. This includes the tank, excavation, plumbing, and other necessary components, with prices on the higher end for challenging sites.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine your tank size, calculate your daily water usage by assuming 100 gallons per person daily and multiplying by your home‚Äôs bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would need at least 600 gallons per day, leading to a minimum tank size of 36,000 gallons for quarterly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The total timeline for cistern installation typically ranges from 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific situation. Cisterns are often more reliable in areas with a deep water table or rocky terrain, where well drilling may be impractical. Cisterns provide a flexible and consistent water supply, especially for off-grid properties.
