Why Saguache Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the picturesque mountain community of Saguache, Colorado, comes with unique challenges, especially concerning water access and storage. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties are not serviced by traditional water supply systems. This is where cistern installation becomes essential. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a reliable source of potable water, especially in this rural area, where well drilling may not be a feasible option due to deep water tables. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the specific needs of Saguache homeowners, providing expert service to ensure your water supply is secure all year round.

Local Challenges in Saguache County

Saguache County presents several challenges for property owners considering cistern installation. The rocky terrain often consists of granite and clay, making excavation difficult and expensive. With a water table that is frequently deep, many properties must rely on water hauling or potable water storage through cisterns. Additionally, the frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating thoughtful planning for winterization and freeze protection. Properties often have steep driveways that can limit access during winter months, making timely water delivery a challenge. The extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, further complicate the installation and maintenance of water systems, reinforcing the need for professional expertise.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our goal is to make your cistern installation as seamless as possible. Our process starts with a comprehensive consultation, where we assess your property and understand your specific water needs. We calculate your required tank size based on daily water usage‚Äîtypically 75-100 gallons per person per day‚Äîand your desired refill frequency. For instance, a 3-bedroom home housing six people may need a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Next, we handle all necessary permits, which are obtained through the Saguache County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations. Excavation follows, where we carefully dig a site for the buried water tank. Our team is skilled in navigating rocky soil, and we take all precautions to minimize additional costs associated with difficult terrain.

Once the site is prepared, we install your chosen water storage tank, typically a Norwesco model known for its durability and reliability. We also set up the necessary pump systems and pressure tanks, followed by trenching to connect the cistern to your home. Finally, our team will install risers and access lids, ensuring your system is user-friendly. We typically complete installations within a few weeks, depending on site conditions and weather, allowing you to enjoy uninterrupted water access.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Saguache

The cost of cistern installation in Saguache can vary significantly based on several factors. For instance, the size of the underground water storage tank plays a crucial role. Using our formula of bedroom count multiplied by two people per bedroom and 100 gallons per day, a 3-bedroom home would require approximately 600 gallons daily, suggesting a minimum capacity of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, with larger tanks costing more.

Prices for Norwesco tanks alone are as follows: a 5,000-gallon tank typically ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank costs between $22,000 and $28,000. The total cost of an installed system‚Äîincluding excavation, pump systems, trenching, and electrical hookup‚Äîcan range from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system depending on site conditions. Rocky terrain and difficult access can increase excavation costs by 20-40% or more, making it critical to budget accordingly.

Lastly, ongoing costs for water hauling should be considered, with typical delivery rates between $200 and $350 for 3,000 gallons. A larger cistern can lead to fewer deliveries, resulting in long-term savings, especially during winter months when access may be restricted.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado starts from $40,000 for a basic system, which includes a 10,000-gallon tank and necessary installation components. However, prices can vary significantly based on site conditions, tank size, and excavation challenges.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank needed is typically based on daily water usage calculated by bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, ensuring you have enough water supply year-round.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take several weeks, depending on the complexity of the project and site conditions. Once permits are secured, excavation and installation typically proceed quickly, but weather and accessibility can influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns can serve as a reliable alternative where wells are impractical due to deep water tables, while wells provide a continuous water source but may require high upfront costs and maintenance.
