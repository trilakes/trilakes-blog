Why Florissant Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Florissant, where the elevation reaches a stunning 8,200 feet, reliable access to water is essential. Many properties here lack the availability of traditional wells, making cistern installation a practical solution for delivering a dependable water supply. A professional cistern installer ensures that your underground water storage tank is tailored to your specific needs, accounting for the unique challenges of high-altitude living. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the intricacies of local regulations and climate, providing you with peace of mind as you prepare for both summer's irrigation needs and winter's freeze protection.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Florissant's rugged terrain presents several challenges when it comes to cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils common in Teller County complicate excavation, often requiring engineered systems for septic solutions as well. Additionally, the water table is frequently deep, leading many homeowners to rely on water hauling or the installation of a water cistern for their potable water storage needs. With a frost line of 36-48 inches at this elevation, winterizing your water storage tank installation is crucial to avoid freeze damage during the harsh Colorado winters. Furthermore, steep driveways and limited winter access can hamper installation and maintenance, making a professional cistern company invaluable for ensuring your system remains functional year-round.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to cater specifically to the needs of Florissant homeowners. The first step involves an initial consultation, where we assess your property, discuss your water needs, and evaluate site conditions. We also help you navigate the necessary permits, which are required through the Teller County Building Department.

Once we finalize the project plan, we proceed with excavation, which can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on soil conditions and the complexity of the project. Our expert team handles the installation of your underground water storage tank, typically a Norwesco model, known for its durability and reliability. Following tank placement, we install a pump system and pressure tank to ensure adequate water flow, and we will also trench to your home to connect the system.

After installation, we conduct thorough testing to guarantee everything is functioning properly. Our focus is not only on effectively meeting your immediate water supply needs but also on providing long-term water storage solutions that can withstand the region's extreme weather conditions. By considering future requirements like winter access and refill frequency during installation, we help you avoid hassles down the road.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Florissant

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is vital for homeowners in Florissant. The total installed cost for a 5,000-gallon system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000. Sizing your underground water storage tank is essential, often calculated based on your home's bedroom count‚Äîusing the formula of two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require approximately 600 gallons per day, equating to a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or up to 72,000 gallons for winter survival.

Several factors can influence these costs. Challenging rocky terrain may increase excavation costs by 20-40%, while winter installations typically come at a premium due to adverse ground conditions. Additionally, ongoing costs for water hauling, which can range from $200 to $350 per delivery for 3,000 gallons, should be considered, as larger tanks can reduce the frequency and overall cost of deliveries.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and site conditions. A typical 5,000-gallon system ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000. Factors such as excavation challenges and the need for additional components can increase these estimates.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's water usage and refill preferences. A common formula is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day and multiply by the number of bedrooms (2 people per bedroom). For example, a 3-bedroom home typically needs at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary, typically ranging from a few days to a couple of weeks. This timeframe depends on factors such as site conditions, excavation challenges, and permitting processes through Teller County.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. A cistern is an excellent option for properties with deep water tables or challenging drilling conditions, while a well may offer a continuous supply if feasible. Consult with a local expert to determine the best solution for your property.
