Many properties in this mountain community lack direct access to wells due to the deep water table and rocky terrain, making cistern installation a practical necessity. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that your home has a steady supply of potable water, especially crucial during winter months when water hauling becomes limited. Local Challenges in Summit County County

Living in Summit Cove comes with unique challenges that necessitate expert cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for a buried water tank. In addition, the elevation can lead to harsher weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and extreme temperature fluctuations, which can impact your water system. As the frost line in this area reaches depths of 36-48 inches, it is imperative to install a frost-proof cistern to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months. Additionally, many properties feature steep driveways that limit access, particularly during winter, making it crucial to plan the logistics of water storage tank installation carefully.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Summit Cove

The cost of cistern installation in Summit Cove can vary significantly due to several factors, primarily dictated by site conditions and the size of the system. For example, to determine the appropriate size of your potable water storage tank, consider your daily water usage, which is typically around 100 gallons per person. For a 3-bedroom home with an expected occupancy of 6 people, you would need approximately 600 gallons per day.

Here‚Äôs a basic guideline for sizing:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: 8,400 gallons minimum
  • Refill monthly: 18,000 gallons minimum
  • Refill every 2-3 months: 36,000-54,000 gallons
  • Get through winter: 72,000+ gallons (multiple tanks)

When considering installation costs, here‚Äôs a high-end breakdown of what to expect:

  • 5,000-gallon basic system: $25,000-$35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: $40,000-$55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): $50,000-$75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): $75,000-$120,000+

Additional costs may include excavation ($3,000-$8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot). Keep in mind that rocky terrain and challenging access can significantly increase these costs by 20-40%, so it's essential to plan your budget accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and installation specifics. For a 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system may range from $40,000 to $55,000. Additional installation costs, such as excavation and pump systems, can also add to the overall expense.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank will depend on your household's water usage. For a typical 3-bedroom home, you would need a minimum of 36,000 gallons if you plan to refill every 2-3 months. We recommend calculating your daily usage based on 100 gallons per person and factoring in how often you'd like to refill.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process generally takes between 2-4 weeks, depending on site conditions and tank size. The timeline includes site preparation, permitting, excavation, and installation, ensuring that all components meet the required safety and functionality standards.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are an excellent alternative for homeowners in areas where wells are impractical or too deep, while wells can provide a continuous supply of water. However, wells can face issues like low yield, making cisterns a reliable backup water supply option for many mountain properties.
