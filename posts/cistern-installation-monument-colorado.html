Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Monument

When it comes to cistern installation in Monument, several factors influence the overall cost. The size of your underground water storage tank is primarily determined by your daily water usage and how often you anticipate needing to refill the tank. A general rule of thumb is to calculate your daily usage by multiplying the number of bedrooms in your home by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For example:

  • 2-bedroom home = 4 people = 400 gallons/day
  • 3-bedroom home = 6 people = 600 gallons/day
  • 4-bedroom home = 8 people = 800 gallons/day

Using this formula, if you have a 3-bedroom home and need to refill every month, you will require a minimum of 18,000 gallons. Pricing for Norwesco tanks starts at $22,000 to $28,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Considering additional costs such as excavation ($3,000-$8,000), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and other site-specific requirements, the total installed system cost is typically between $40,000 and $55,000.

Keep in mind that the rocky terrain commonly found in El Paso County can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and winter installations generally incur higher expenses due to ground conditions. Moreover, access difficulties may necessitate additional hauling and equipment costs. Investing in a larger system can lead to long-term savings on maintenance and water delivery, as it reduces the frequency of water hauling services.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies widely based on size and site conditions. Typically, a 5,000-gallon basic system ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, including installation factors like excavation and pump systems.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need is largely determined by your household's daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly. For more convenience during the winter, larger tanks may be necessary to reduce refill frequency.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes between two to four weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. The process includes site evaluation, excavation, and the installation of the tank and associated systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns are ideal for areas with deep water tables and limited well access, providing a reliable backup water supply. Wells, while often a less expensive upfront investment, can be unreliable in rural areas where water yield may be low.
