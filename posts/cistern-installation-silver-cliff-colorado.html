Local Challenges in Custer County

The terrain in Silver Cliff presents several challenges for water storage tank installation. The soil mainly consists of rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay found in valleys, making excavation for an underground cistern both labor-intensive and costly. Add to that the water table's depth, which often necessitates cisterns for those without nearby wells. The frost line here reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, meaning winterizing your buried water tank is crucial to prevent freeze damage during the harsh winter months. Furthermore, extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall can hinder access to properties, especially those with steep driveways, making professional expertise vital for successful installations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The total installed costs for a cistern in Colorado typically range from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on tank size and site conditions. Smaller systems may start at around $25,000, while larger setups can exceed $100,000, particularly in challenging terrains.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home will generally require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if you want to refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and required permits. Factors such as weather and access can also influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site-specific conditions. Cisterns are ideal for properties with deep water tables or unreliable wells, while wells can be more sustainable if water access is good. For a thorough comparison, check our Cistern vs Well Comparison.
