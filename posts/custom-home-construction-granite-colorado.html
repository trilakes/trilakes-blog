Local Challenges in Lake County

The terrain in Granite poses several challenges that require careful planning and expert execution. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate foundation engineering, leading to higher costs and longer timelines if not properly managed. At elevations above 8,000 feet, weather can be unpredictable, with heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms impacting construction schedules. Moreover, many properties in this area have steep driveways and limited winter access, making logistics a critical consideration for any home construction project.

Additionally, the region‚Äôs water table is often deep, necessitating cisterns for water storage, while many homes will require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. Understanding these local challenges is essential for any prospective homeowner, and that's why obtaining a build feasibility report is crucial before making any land purchases.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our custom home construction process is designed to ensure clarity and efficiency from start to finish. Here‚Äôs how we guide you through building your dream mountain home:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before you buy land, we conduct a thorough assessment to ensure it is buildable, evaluating soil conditions, elevation, and local building codes.
  2. Well Permit Application: Most counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit. We advise you to file your well permit early, as driller wait times can extend up to a year.
  3. Site Planning: A detailed site plan will specify where your home, driveway, and utilities will be located, accounting for setbacks and drainage.
  4. Architectural Plans: We offer various options for obtaining architectural designs tailored to your vision.
  5. Foundation and Framing Plans: Our licensed engineers prepare stamped plans that consider local conditions, including frost depth and snow load.
  6. Land Survey: A professional survey is required for permitting and includes property boundary verification and easement identification.
  7. Septic System Plans: If your property is not connected to a sewer, we develop engineered septic plans.
  8. Building Permit Submission: We compile all necessary documents and submit them to the Lake County Building Department to obtain your building permit.
  9. Pre-Construction Planning: Prior to breaking ground, we finalize plans, schedule subcontractors, and establish a payment schedule.
  10. Construction: Our team manages the construction phases, ensuring quality and compliance with inspections throughout the process.
  11. Certificate of Occupancy: After passing final inspections, we obtain the certificate that allows you to move into your new home!

Our comprehensive approach ensures that you stay informed and involved, making your custom build a rewarding experience.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Granite

When planning your custom home construction in Granite, it‚Äôs essential to understand the cost factors involved. The typical cost per square foot for a custom build ranges from $550 to $700 for higher-end projects, while luxury homes can exceed $1,000 per square foot. For example, a 2,000 square foot home could range from $1,100,000 to $1,500,000. This pricing reflects the unique challenges of building in a mountain environment, where rocky terrain and the need for engineered solutions can significantly influence costs.

For water needs, if you opt for a cistern, remember the sizing formula: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. This will guide you in determining the appropriate cistern size, which can range from $25,000 to $75,000 depending on installation specifics. The cost of site access, utility extensions, and specialized foundation engineering also contributes to the overall budget, making it crucial to plan for a high-end estimate to avoid surprises later on.
