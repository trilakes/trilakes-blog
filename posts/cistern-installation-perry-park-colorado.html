Why Perry Park Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the picturesque mountain community of Perry Park, Douglas County County, offers breathtaking views and a serene atmosphere. However, at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, water supply can become a significant challenge for homeowners. For properties lacking a reliable well, cistern installation is not just a convenience but often a necessity. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a consistent and safe supply of potable water, especially during harsh winters when water hauling becomes more complicated. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we specialize in creating effective water storage solutions tailored to the unique needs of Perry Park residents.

Local Challenges in Douglas County County

Perry Park presents unique challenges for homeowners looking to install a water cistern. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate excavation, often requiring engineered systems to ensure proper drainage and stability. Additionally, the water table in this area is frequently deep, making water hauling a common alternative for many properties. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, any cistern installation must consider freeze protection measures to prevent damage during the frigid winter months. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited access add logistical challenges, making it crucial to work with a knowledgeable cistern installer near me who understands the terrain and local building requirements.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Perry Park

When considering cistern installation in Perry Park, it's essential to understand the various cost factors involved. The size of the underground water storage tank is generally determined by daily water usage, which can be calculated as follows:

Daily water usage is typically estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home, assuming an occupancy of 6 people, would need approximately 600 gallons per day. Depending on how often you want to refill your cistern, you can estimate the required tank size:

  • For a monthly refill (18,000 gallons minimum) or a larger system for less frequent refills, you could be looking at a 10,000 gallon system costing between $40,000 and $55,000.
  • If you want to get through winter (4-5 months), a system might need to hold over 72,000 gallons, potentially costing up to $120,000 or more for multiple tanks.

Keep in mind that installation costs can vary significantly due to local factors, including rocky terrain, which can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Also, winter installations may incur higher costs due to challenging ground conditions. Additional components such as pumps, electrical work, and water filtration systems can further influence your total investment, typically bringing a 5,000 gallon basic system to a starting range of $25,000 to $35,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation complexity. A basic 5,000 gallon system typically starts around $25,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, depending on the site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The appropriate size for your potable water storage tank depends on your household's water usage. As a general guideline, calculate your daily water needs by multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary but typically takes several weeks. This includes obtaining necessary permits, preparing the site, and performing the actual installation, all of which may be affected by weather conditions.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on several factors including water table depth, property accessibility, and personal preferences. For many properties in Perry Park, where well drilling may be impractical, a cistern offers a reliable alternative water source.
