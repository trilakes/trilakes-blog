Local Challenges in Summit County County

Mountain properties in Blue River face distinct challenges that significantly impact cistern installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can make excavation difficult and costly, adding to installation expenses. Additionally, the water table is often deep, necessitating the use of underground cisterns for effective water storage, particularly in homes without wells. Many properties also require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions.

Weather plays a critical role in your decision-making process. With extreme temperature fluctuations, heavy snowfall, and sudden afternoon thunderstorms, you'll need a robust system capable of handling the elements. The frost line at 36-48 inches requires that any buried water tank is properly protected from freezing, while steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder installation and maintenance. Fire mitigation is also a concern in this forested area, making it essential to have adequate fire suppression water storage. Understanding these local challenges is vital for effective planning and implementation of your water storage solutions.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is thorough and designed to address the unique challenges of Blue River. The first step involves a comprehensive site assessment to determine the best location for your underground cistern. This includes analyzing soil conditions, accessibility, and compliance with Summit County County permit requirements.

Once the assessment is complete, we will assist you in selecting the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank. The size generally depends on your daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill the cistern. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of 6 people will require around 600 gallons per day. If you want to refill every month, you would need at least an 18,000-gallon system.

After sizing and planning, we prepare for excavation, which typically takes place from May to October due to weather constraints. Excavation costs may vary significantly based on the rocky terrain, often ranging from $3,000 to $8,000 or more. Once the site is ready, we install the chosen cistern tank, often a reliable Norwesco tank, and a pump system to ensure efficient water delivery to your home.

The entire installation process usually takes several days, depending on the complexity of the site and any additional features, such as water filtration or treatment systems. We prioritize safety and efficiency, ensuring that your water storage tank installation is completed on time and to the highest standards.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Blue River

The costs associated with cistern installation in Blue River can vary significantly based on site conditions and specific requirements. For a basic system, including a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000. If you opt for a larger 10,000-gallon tank, the total installed cost ranges from $40,000 to $55,000. Multiple tank systems for larger capacities (15,000-20,000 gallons) can range from $50,000 to $75,000 or more, while large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons) can reach $75,000 to $120,000.

Keep in mind that these costs can escalate based on factors like excavation difficulties due to rocky terrain, which can add 20-40% to overall expenses, and the accessibility of your property. If your property requires specialized equipment for installation, this can further increase costs. It's essential to factor in additional expenses for the pump system, trenching to your home, and electrical hookups, which can total between $2,500 and $5,000, depending on your specific needs.

Using our sizing formula, you can calculate your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For instance, a 4-bedroom home with 8 occupants would require approximately 800 gallons per day, leading to a larger cistern to ensure adequate supply. Remember, the convenience of larger systems often leads to fewer refills and long-term savings on water hauling costs, which typically range from $200 to $350 per 3,000 gallons.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range significantly depending on size and site conditions. A basic Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank installation typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and refill frequency. As a general rule, you need approximately 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes several days, depending on site complexity and weather conditions. Once excavation begins, the installation process can be completed within a week, allowing for the necessary setups such as pump systems and electrical hookups.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are a reliable alternative when well drilling is impractical or too deep. They provide a consistent water supply for residential or agricultural needs, making them ideal for properties in challenging terrains such as those in Blue River.
