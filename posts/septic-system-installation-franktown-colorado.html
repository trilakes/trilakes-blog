Local Challenges in Douglas County County

The beautiful yet rugged terrain of Franktown presents unique challenges for septic system installation. Many properties have rocky granite and clay in valleys, which complicates soil testing and septic design. At elevations of 8,000 feet, home builders often encounter a deep water table, requiring supplemental cisterns or water hauling. The frost line here can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, especially in higher elevations, necessitating careful planning during the winter months. Additionally, properties may have steep driveways and limited winter access, impacting the logistics of installing a new septic tank or system. Fire mitigation is also a critical consideration in forested areas, adding another layer of complexity to site preparation and septic design. All these factors make it essential to work with a knowledgeable local septic contractor who understands the specific needs of Franktown residents.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on a comprehensive and efficient septic system installation process tailored to the unique needs of Franktown homeowners. The first step begins with soil testing, where we deploy a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, to collect soil samples for analysis. This critical step determines the type of system required based on the findings of a licensed engineer. Next, we assist homeowners in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Douglas County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43 guidelines.

Once permits are secured, we move into the excavation phase, typically scheduled between May and October to accommodate the building season. During this phase, we carefully install the septic tank and associated components, including any necessary leach fields or drain fields. The installation process culminates with a thorough septic inspection, ensuring every aspect of the system meets local regulations and functions optimally.

The entire installation process usually spans several weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system being installed. Our team is committed to keeping homeowners informed every step of the way, ensuring a seamless experience from start to finish.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Franktown

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Franktown is essential for homeowners planning their budget. The cost for a basic engineered system typically starts at $35,000 and can range up to $50,000. For those considering a sand filter system, expect to invest between $45,000 and $65,000. If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is required, costs can rise to between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

Several factors can influence these costs, including terrain challenges such as rocky soil, which can add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall price. Access to the property also plays a crucial role‚Äîproperties with steep driveways or limited access may incur higher excavation and labor costs. When calculating costs for water supply systems like cisterns, remember the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern capable of accommodating approximately 600 gallons per day.

Be sure to factor in the expenses for soil testing (ranging from $2,000 to $4,000), permits (which can vary from $500 to $2,000), and the tank and components (around $5,000 to $15,000). Understanding these elements will help you prepare for the financial commitments associated with septic installation in the Franktown area.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, septic installation costs vary widely based on site conditions, but homeowners can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000 for engineered systems. Factors like rocky terrain and access issues can significantly increase these costs, so it's crucial to consult with a local septic contractor for an accurate estimate.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The time required for septic installation typically spans several weeks, depending on factors such as permitting, soil testing, and site conditions. Engaging with a professional septic contractor can help streamline the process and keep you informed throughout the installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Franktown area require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations outlined in Regulation 43. A licensed septic contractor can evaluate your land and determine the best system for your specific needs.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging deep test holes to analyze soil conditions, which helps determine the most suitable septic system for your property, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
