Local Challenges in Douglas County County

Excavating in Lone Tree is not without its challenges, primarily due to the rocky granite and decomposed granite soils that can complicate foundation excavation and land clearing. The presence of clay in certain valleys can also contribute to drainage issues, making it vital to have an expert who understands the regional soil composition. Additionally, the deep water table may necessitate the installation of cisterns for adequate water supply, which adds another layer of complexity to the excavation process.

The frost line, reaching depths of 36-48 inches, particularly affects excavation timelines and methods, especially in winter when access to many properties can be limited. Factors such as steep driveways and access roads must be considered, as they can significantly impact the equipment and techniques used. Moreover, the region's weather, characterized by extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads, creates further obstacles, emphasizing the importance of meticulous planning and professional execution.

  • Site Preparation/Clearing: Basic lot clearing ranges from $3,000-$8,000 per acre, while heavy brush and tree removal can cost between $5,000-$15,000 per acre.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs typically range from $50-$100 per cubic yard, depending on the soil type and site conditions. In rocky areas, costs may increase substantially due to the need for specialized equipment and techniques.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation can vary widely based on the size and complexity of the project. Generally, it can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks to complete site preparation and land clearing in Lone Tree.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Mountain excavation often requires specialized equipment such as excavators, bobcats, and backhoes. These machines are essential for navigating the steep and rocky terrain typical of Lone Tree.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, any excavation work in Lone Tree requires permits obtained through the Douglas County Building Department. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting can assist you with the entire permitting process to ensure compliance.
