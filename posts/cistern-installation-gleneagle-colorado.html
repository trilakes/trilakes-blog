Why Gleneagle Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Gleneagle, Colorado, comes with unique challenges, particularly when it comes to securing a reliable water supply. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet, many properties lack access to a consistent well water source, making cistern installation a vital service for homeowners. Without a proper water storage solution, residents may face difficulties during the dry season or harsh winters, when water hauling becomes impractical. A professional cistern installer near you, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, ensures that your underground water storage tank is correctly sized, installed, and winterized to provide an uninterrupted supply of potable water throughout the year.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Gleneagle's beautiful yet rugged terrain presents numerous challenges for homeowners seeking water storage solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation difficult, often increasing costs and installation time. The water table in this area is typically deep, forcing many homeowners to rely on water hauling or the installation of a buried water tank. Additionally, the frost line at elevations above 8,000 feet reaches depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning and execution during the cistern installation process to prevent freezing and ensure reliable operation during winter. With steep driveways and limited access in winter months, it's crucial to factor in these local conditions when planning your water system.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and systematic process for water storage tank installation to ensure the best results for our clients:

  1. Initial Consultation: We meet with you to understand your water needs and evaluate your property.
  2. Site Assessment: Our team assesses the site for soil conditions, access, and the best location for the cistern.
  3. Permitting: We handle all necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Excavation: We excavate the area for your underground cistern, taking into account soil conditions and frost depth.

  7. Winterization: Our team will provide guidance on winterizing your cistern to protect it from freezing temperatures, ensuring reliable water access all year round.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Gleneagle

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is vital for every homeowner. The total installed cost for a water storage tank in Gleneagle can range based on several factors:

  • Tank Size: The size of your cistern is primarily determined by your household's daily water usage, which averages 75-100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require an estimated 600 gallons per day, needing a cistern size of at least 36,000-54,000 gallons for a refill every 2-3 months.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavation costs can vary significantly based on soil conditions; rocky terrain may add 20-40% to the excavation price, which typically ranges from $3,000-$8,000.
  • Installation Components: Additional costs for pumps, trenching, electrical hookup, and filtration can range from $6,000 to $12,000+ depending on your system's complexity.

For a basic 5,000-gallon system, you can expect total installation costs to start around $25,000-$35,000, while larger systems (10,000-gallon) can range from $40,000-$55,000. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $120,000 or more for larger installations, depending on site conditions and specific requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The required size of your underground water storage tank is generally calculated by multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need at least a 36,000-54,000-gallon tank if refilling every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 2-4 weeks, but this can vary based on factors like site conditions and the complexity of your water system.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property‚Äôs water table and access to reliable water. Cisterns often serve as a better option in areas where wells are impractical, too deep, or unreliable.
