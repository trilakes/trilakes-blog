Our Cistern Installation Process

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Gleneagle

When considering cistern installation in Gleneagle, understand that costs can vary significantly based on various factors. As a guideline, the cost of a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically ranges from $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can fall between $22,000-$28,000. However, installation costs can escalate depending on site conditions.

For example, excavation costs can run from $3,000 to $8,000 or more, especially if rocky terrain complicates the process. Additionally, the installation of the pump system and pressure tank will add another $2,500 to $5,000 to your total bill. Other costs to consider include trenching to your home at $25-$50 per linear foot, electrical hookups ranging from $1,000 to $2,500, and optional water treatment systems costing between $2,000 to $5,000.

Ultimately, the total installed system ranges can be significant: a basic 5,000-gallon system may cost between $25,000 to $35,000, while a dual tank system for larger capacity can range from $50,000 to $75,000 or more. For those wanting to ensure ample supply for winter, systems holding over 72,000 gallons can run from $75,000 to $120,000 or higher. Remember, larger tanks reduce the frequency of refills, which can be crucial in remote areas where winter access is difficult.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on factors such as tank size and installation conditions. Typically, a basic 5,000-gallon system will range from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems, such as dual tank setups, can range from $50,000 to $75,000 or more.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. A common formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms by two and then by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a three-bedroom home would need at least a 10,800-gallon tank if refilling every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from one week to several weeks, depending on site conditions. Factors such as excavation complexities and local permit approvals play significant roles in determining the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site-specific conditions. Cisterns can serve as a reliable alternative when wells are impractical due to depth or water quality issues. They also provide a more flexible option for water use, particularly in areas where access for drilling wells is challenging.
