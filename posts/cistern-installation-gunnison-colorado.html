Local Challenges in Gunnison County

The unique geographical and climatic conditions in Gunnison County present several challenges when it comes to water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil types often necessitate specialized excavation techniques, which can increase costs significantly. Additionally, many properties in the area have a deep water table, making cisterns an essential alternative for those without access to a well. The frost line in Gunnison can reach depths of 36-48 inches, which means that installations must be carefully planned to avoid freeze damage. Homeowners also contend with extreme weather conditions such as heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, which can impede access, especially during the winter months. Furthermore, fire mitigation is a critical concern in forested areas, requiring additional planning for fire suppression water storage.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Gunnison

The cost of cistern installation in Gunnison can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and accessibility. For example, when determining the size of your underground water storage tank, we typically calculate your needs based on the number of bedrooms in your home and average water usage. A general guideline is to assume 100 gallons of water per person per day, with 2 people per bedroom. Therefore, a 3-bedroom home would typically require a tank that holds at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.

Here are some typical costs for water storage tank installation:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

Additional costs can include excavation, which may range from $3,000 to $8,000+ depending on the rocky terrain and access challenges. Pump systems and pressure tanks typically add $2,500 to $5,000. Therefore, the total installed cost for a basic 5,000-gallon system in Gunnison can reach $35,000, while larger systems can go as high as $120,000, especially for dual tank setups. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado can range significantly based on tank size and site conditions. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon system can cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can go up to $120,000 depending on the specific requirements of your property.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is generally determined by your household's daily water usage and how often you want to refill the tank. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires at least a 10,000-gallon tank if refilled monthly, while a larger capacity tank may be needed for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation timelines can vary, but most projects take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. Factors such as site conditions, excavation needs, and weather can influence the duration of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your property's unique circumstances. Cisterns are often preferable in areas with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, while wells provide a continuous supply of water if properly situated. For many Gunnison properties, especially those without reliable well access, a cistern is an excellent alternative.
