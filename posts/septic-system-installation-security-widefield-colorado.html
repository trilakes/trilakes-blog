Local Challenges in El Paso County

Security-Widefield is characterized by its rocky granite soils, clay-filled valleys, and deep water tables, making septic system installation particularly complex. Many properties must utilize engineered systems due to the challenging soil conditions, which often necessitate permits from the El Paso County Building Department. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate installations, making it vital to choose a contractor who understands these challenges. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure the highest quality results. First, we conduct a thorough soil testing assessment, utilizing a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. This allows us to gather soil samples that are then sent to a licensed engineer for analysis. Based on the soils report, we determine the most suitable system for your property, which could range from a conventional gravity system to more advanced engineered systems.

Once the design is finalized, we assist you with obtaining the necessary septic permit through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43. Our team then schedules the excavation, where we prepare the site, install the septic tank, and set up the leach field or drain field as required. The entire installation process typically takes a few days, depending on site conditions, with completion usually occurring between May and October, when access is easier. After installation, we provide a comprehensive septic inspection to confirm everything meets local regulations and functions efficiently.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Security-Widefield

The cost of septic installation in Security-Widefield can vary significantly based on various factors, including site conditions and system type. For a basic engineered system, expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, the cost can range from $45,000 to $65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) may start at $55,000 and can soar upwards of $80,000.

It's important to note that rocky terrain and difficult access can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your total costs. Additionally, costs for a soils test and engineering typically range from $2,000 to $4,000, and the excavation can add another $8,000 to $20,000. If your property requires a cistern, remember that the size is determined by the bedroom count‚Äîspecifically, two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This calculation is vital to ensure you have adequate water storage for your household.

Overall, investing in a quality septic system is crucial for protecting your property and the environment. Proper planning and execution can save you money in the long run by preventing failures and prolonging the life of your system.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?
