Local Challenges in Douglas County

Roxborough Park is characterized by its rugged terrain, with rocky granite and clay soils that can complicate water storage tank installation. The water table in this region is often significantly deep, leading many residents to rely on water hauling or install a water cistern as a more practical solution. The frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, requiring careful planning to ensure that your buried water tank is properly insulated against extreme winter conditions, especially with heavy snowfall and temperature fluctuations common in the area.

Access can also be challenging, particularly during winter months when steep driveways hinder construction. This necessitates the need for efficient and reliable cistern installation that accounts for potential access difficulties. Moreover, as a community surrounded by natural beauty, fire mitigation is critical, and having an adequate fire suppression water supply is vital for property safety.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly depending on the size and installation requirements, with a typical installed system ranging from $25,000 to $120,000. A basic 5,000-gallon cistern installation can cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems may exceed $75,000 depending on site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and how often you wish to refill it. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of 6 people would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, while a "get through winter" system would require 72,000 gallons or more.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary, but typically, the process takes about one to two weeks, depending on excavation challenges, permitting requirements, and weather conditions. Our team works efficiently to minimize inconvenience while ensuring compliance with Douglas County regulations.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Whether a cistern or a well is better for your property depends on various factors, including water table depth and reliability. Cisterns are often preferable in areas like Roxborough Park where well drilling may be impractical or too deep, providing a dependable alternative for potable water storage.
