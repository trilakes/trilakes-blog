Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Black Forest

The total cost of water storage tank installation can vary significantly based on several factors. For example, a 5,000-gallon system will typically range between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. The sizing of your cistern is primarily determined by your daily water usage‚Äîgenerally calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would need a system that can store at least 600 gallons per day.

Considering refill frequency is crucial; if you prefer less hassle in winter months, opting for larger tanks can save you from frequent refills. Here are some examples based on a 3-bedroom home:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: Minimum of 8,400 gallons.
  • Refill monthly: Minimum of 18,000 gallons.
  • Refill every 2-3 months: Between 36,000 and 54,000 gallons.
  • Get through winter (4-5 months): 72,000+ gallons.

Beyond the tank costs, be prepared for additional expenses such as excavation and site preparation, which can add 20-40% to your total, particularly in rocky terrain. Other costs include pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot), and optional filtration ($2,000-$5,000). Therefore, it‚Äôs crucial to consider all these factors when planning your cistern installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and specific site conditions. For larger capacity systems, costs can escalate to $120,000 or more, particularly in challenging terrains like Black Forest.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is generally based on your daily water usage, calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would need a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

Cisterns also provide immediate access to stored water, which can be a significant advantage.
