Living in the scenic mountain community of Pitkin, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, comes with unique challenges, especially regarding water supply. Many properties in this area lack direct access to a reliable well, making cistern installation a practical solution for securing a consistent water source. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the intricacies of installing efficient and durable underground water storage tanks, tailored to meet the specific needs of our local homeowners.

Working with a professional cistern installer near you ensures that the installation process adheres to all local regulations and building codes set forth by the Gunnison County County Building Department. Our expertise in navigating the rocky terrain and harsh weather conditions means we can help you establish a reliable and long-lasting water storage solution that supports your household, ranch, or agricultural needs.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County County

Pitkin, located within Gunnison County, presents specific challenges for homeowners considering cistern installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil found here can complicate excavation and installation processes, often resulting in increased costs. Moreover, the water table can be significantly deep, making access to groundwater unreliable and often requiring water hauling for properties without a well.

As winter approaches, homeowners must also consider the frost line, which ranges from 36 to 48 inches at higher elevations. This necessitates the installation of frost-proof cisterns to prevent freezing during the harsh Colorado winters. Additionally, many properties are situated on steep driveways, limiting access for construction vehicles during winter. The extreme weather, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, further emphasizes the need for specialized expertise in water storage tank installation.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Pitkin

The cost of a cistern installation in Pitkin varies significantly based on several factors, including the size of the tank, site conditions, and additional components required for a complete system. For instance, a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank generally costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon model ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. However, we advise our clients to budget for the higher end of these ranges due to potential complications with excavation and installation in rocky terrain.

To accurately size your cistern, consider the number of bedrooms and daily water usage: multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 (assuming 2 people per bedroom) and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day. Depending on your refill preference, you'll want to calculate the total capacity needed. If you prefer refilling every two months, you could require upwards of 36,000 gallons.

Additional costs include excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on access and rocky conditions, and other necessary components like pump systems, which can add another $2,500 to $5,000. For a comprehensive overview of expected costs, refer to our Cistern Cost Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can range up to $120,000 or more for larger, dual-tank systems. Factors such as excavation difficulties, site access, and additional components will influence the final price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank, calculate your daily water usage (100 gallons per person per day) and multiply it by the number of occupants. For a 3-bedroom home, you would need a tank that can store at least 36,000 gallons if you refill every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process for a cistern typically takes between 3 to 7 days, depending on site conditions and whether any unexpected challenges arise, such as difficult excavation or harsh weather conditions.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific location and water needs. For properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources, a cistern offers a dependable alternative for water storage solutions. A detailed review of both options can be found in our Cistern vs Well Comparison.
