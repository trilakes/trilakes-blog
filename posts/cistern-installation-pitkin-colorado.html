Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Pitkin

The cost of cistern installation in Pitkin varies significantly based on several factors. The primary determinant is the size of the underground water storage tank, which is usually calculated based on your household‚Äôs daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, a three-bedroom home typically accommodates up to six people, translating to a need for approximately 600 gallons per day. If you prefer to refill every month, you‚Äôll need at least an 18,000-gallon capacity. Here‚Äôs how the costs typically break down:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Total installed systems: A basic 5,000-gallon system generally starts from $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system ranges from $40,000-$55,000, depending on excavation and additional setup costs.

Additional costs may arise from excavation ($3,000-$8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500). It's essential to consider that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, so a detailed site evaluation will ensure accurate budgeting. The goal is to invest wisely upfront to minimize hassle and avoid frequent water hauling, which can cost between $200-$350 for every 3,000 gallons delivered.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of installing a cistern in Colorado typically starts from $25,000 for a 5,000-gallon system and can range up to $55,000 or more for larger systems. This total includes the tank, excavation, plumbing, and electrical setup, but costs can increase depending on site conditions and specific requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your potable water storage tank is based on your daily water usage and how frequently you wish to refill it. For a standard three-bedroom home, a tank of at least 18,000 gallons is recommended for monthly refills, while larger capacities are preferable for less frequent access, especially during winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, typically taking several weeks. This duration includes site assessment, permitting, excavation, and installation, with weather and site conditions influencing the overall time frame.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on property-specific conditions. For many properties in Pitkin where the water table is deep or unreliable, a cistern offers a more dependable solution. Wells may also incur higher long-term maintenance costs. Ultimately, consulting with a local expert can help determine the best option for your specific needs.
