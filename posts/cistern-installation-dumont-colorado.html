Why Dumont Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Dumont, nestled high in the Rockies at over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners regarding water supply. Many properties in this mountainous area lack direct access to a reliable water source, making cistern installation an essential solution for both residential and agricultural needs. With a professional cistern installer near you, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, you can ensure your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, meeting your specific needs and local regulations. Our expertise guarantees that your potable water storage tank will be both functional and resilient, providing you peace of mind as you enjoy everything this stunning mountain community has to offer.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County County

The terrain in Dumont can be unforgiving, with rocky granite and clay present in many valleys, resulting in a deep water table. This often makes it impractical to rely solely on wells. Many homeowners must consider alternatives such as water hauling or an underground cistern for their water needs. The frost line in the area reaches depths of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, necessitating careful planning for frost-proof cistern installations. Additionally, with steep driveways and limited access during winter, having a reliable water storage tank installation is critical to avoid frequent refills and potential water supply disruptions. Fire mitigation is also paramount in this forested region, making the need for fire suppression water storage a vital consideration.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Dumont

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for planning your project. Based on your daily water usage, we recommend a sizing formula based on the number of bedrooms in your home:

  • Each bedroom typically accommodates 2 people.
  • Assuming 100 gallons of water usage per person per day, a 3-bedroom home (6 people) would require approximately 600 gallons per day.
  • To ensure your needs are met, consider how often you want to refill; for instance, if you prefer a refill every two months, you'll want a tank size around 36,000-54,000 gallons.

The estimated costs for water storage tank installation are as follows:

  • 5,000 gallon Norwesco tank: $13,000 - $15,000
  • 10,000 gallon Norwesco tank: $22,000 - $28,000
  • Excavation costs: $3,000 - $8,000+
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500 - $5,000
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000 - $2,500
  • Water filtration/treatment: $2,000 - $5,000

In total, a basic system can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can exceed $75,000. Always keep in mind that rocky terrain, challenging access, and additional features can increase costs significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary greatly; basic systems start from $25,000 and can go up to $120,000 or more for larger capacities, including excavation and installation costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home, which typically accommodates six people, would require around 36,000-54,000 gallons if refilled every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take several weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting, with the excavation and installation phases being the most time-consuming.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better solution in areas like Dumont, where wells may be impractical due to deep water tables or unreliable yields. Cisterns provide a consistent water supply without the challenges of well drilling.
