Local Challenges in Grand County

Fraser‚Äôs picturesque surroundings come with significant challenges that impact cistern installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can make excavation difficult and costly, often adding 20-40% to overall installation expenses. Additionally, many properties have a deep water table, necessitating the use of underground water storage tanks for reliable access to water. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, proper winterization of your water cistern becomes critical to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months.

Access can also be an issue, especially for properties situated on steep driveways or in remote locations. This can limit winter access for both installation and ongoing water delivery services. Moreover, the region is known for its extreme weather fluctuations, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, which can further complicate installation logistics. Understanding these local challenges will help you make informed decisions about your water storage solutions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Fraser

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for budgeting your project. The price typically reflects site-specific conditions and the chosen system size. For instance, if you have a 3-bedroom home, calculating your water needs involves multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day:

3 bedrooms x 2 people x 100 gallons = 600 gallons per day.

Considering refill frequency is also essential. If you want to refill monthly, you would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons; for quarterly refills, aim for 36,000-54,000 gallons; and to ‚Äúget through winter,‚Äù plan for 72,000+ gallons, potentially requiring multiple tanks.

Here are some estimated costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000 (tank only)
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000 (tank only)
  • Total installed system ranges: 5,000-gallon basic system: $25,000-$35,000; 10,000-gallon system: $40,000-$55,000.

Keep in mind that rocky terrain, deep frost lines, and remote access can significantly increase excavation and overall installation costs. Always consider the value of investing in a larger tank for fewer refills in the long run, especially in a mountain community like Fraser.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 or more for a 10,000-gallon system. These prices include the tank, excavation, and installation but can vary based on site conditions and additional features needed.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person. For a 3-bedroom home accommodating 6 people, you would need at least a 10,000-gallon tank to ensure adequate supply.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern can take from a few days to several weeks, depending on factors such as site accessibility, excavation conditions, and weather. Proper planning and professional execution can streamline the process significantly.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and personal needs. A cistern is typically a better option in areas with deep water tables or where well drilling is impractical, while wells can provide a continuous supply if properly maintained.
