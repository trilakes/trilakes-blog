Local Challenges in Custer County

Constructing a home in Custer County presents distinct local challenges that can impact the planning and execution of your project. The rocky soil, including decomposed granite and clay, often requires specialized foundation engineering, as many properties necessitate engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. In addition, the water table can be deep, leading many homeowners to opt for cisterns to supply water. It's crucial to consider the frost line, which ranges from 36 to 48 inches, particularly at higher elevations. Access to properties can be limited, especially during winter, where steep driveways and heavy snow can hinder construction progress. Armed with knowledge about these challenges, a seasoned general contractor can help you overcome obstacles and ensure a smoother construction process.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive step-by-step process for custom home construction. Our journey begins with a Build Feasibility Report, which assesses the buildability of your plot. This includes site visits to analyze elevation, slope, and soil conditions, alongside a water flow analysis. Next, we assist in obtaining well permits or preparing for cistern installation, which is vital before moving forward with your new construction. Once we have the necessary permits, we develop a detailed site plan that considers the positioning of the house, driveways, and utility routes. Following this, architectural plans are crafted, either through your architect or our recommended partners. Each set of plans undergoes rigorous stamped engineering to ensure they are built to last.

After obtaining the building permit from the Custer County Building Department, we initiate pre-construction planning, finalizing schedules, budgets, and materials. Construction itself is broken down into phases, each requiring inspections to ensure compliance with local codes. Finally, upon passing the last inspection, we issue a Certificate of Occupancy, allowing you to move into your new mountain haven. The entire process typically spans from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the project and local weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Silver Cliff

Understanding the cost of building a custom home in Silver Cliff is essential for budget planning. Construction costs in this mountainous region can range from $350 to $1,000+ per square foot. For a basic build, expect to pay around $350-$450 per square foot, while standard quality homes may range from $450 to $550 per square foot. Custom or high-end homes commonly fall into a $550 to $700 per square foot range, while luxury homes can even exceed $1,000 per square foot. For instance:

  • 1,500 sq ft home: $525,000-$825,000
  • 2,500 sq ft home: $875,000-$1,375,000
  • 3,000 sq ft home: $1,050,000-$1,750,000+

When planning for your new home construction, it‚Äôs important to factor in additional costs such as land acquisition, septic installation ($35,000-$80,000), and cistern setup, which typically ranges from $25,000 to $75,000, depending on size. The formula for cistern sizing is based on the number of bedrooms, calculated as (bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day). With the unique terrain challenges in Silver Cliff, the high-end of these pricing ranges is often more accurate.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $350 to $1,000+ per square foot, depending on the quality and complexity of the build. For instance, a standard quality home can cost between $450 and $550 per square foot, whereas luxury homes can start at $700 per square foot and go upwards of $1,000.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: Building a home can take anywhere from 10 to 18 months, depending on factors such as project complexity and local weather conditions. The pre-construction phase, including obtaining permits and planning, can add an additional 2 to 6 months to the timeline.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, it‚Äôs vital to consider their experience in your desired area, references from past clients, and their understanding of local building codes and requirements. Look for a contractor who is licensed and insured, ensuring they can handle the unique challenges of mountain construction.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a Build Feasibility Report before purchasing land is crucial. This study assesses site conditions, zoning restrictions, and potential challenges, helping you avoid costly mistakes and ensuring the land is suitable for your planned construction.
