Why Silver Cliff Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Building a custom home in Silver Cliff, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, requires a specialized approach due to the unique challenges this mountain community presents. Whether you're looking for a cozy mountain retreat or a luxury home, a custom home builder with local expertise is invaluable. Here at Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the intricacies of home construction in Custer County, including the necessity for precise planning, navigating local regulations, and ensuring that your home is built to withstand the mountain climate. With heavy snowfall, afternoon thunderstorms, and extreme temperature fluctuations, investing in expert guidance is essential for a successful build. Our team is here to turn your dream home into reality, tailored to your specific needs and surroundings.

Local Challenges in Custer County County

Silver Cliff is characterized by rocky granite and decomposed granite soils, which pose significant challenges for foundation engineering. The water table can be deep, often necessitating cisterns for water supply rather than traditional wells. Furthermore, the frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, depending on the elevation, making proper foundation design critical to avoid future structural issues. With steep driveways and limited winter access, site planning becomes crucial to ensure that your home is accessible year-round. Additionally, our general contractor services include fire mitigation strategies to meet defensible space requirements, ensuring safety in this forested environment.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive and structured process for new construction. It begins with a build feasibility report, essential before purchasing land to evaluate site conditions, water availability, and local building codes. Once you have your property, we assist with obtaining necessary permits from the Custer County Building Department, which can take between 2-8 weeks for approval.

Our subsequent steps include creating a detailed site plan, drafting architectural plans, and engineering foundational and framing plans. We ensure these plans are stamped by licensed professionals to comply with local regulations. Following that, we conduct a professional land survey and develop engineered septic plans if needed. The entire pre-construction phase typically takes 2-6 months, after which we move into the physical construction phase that can range from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the design and local weather conditions.

Throughout this process, we prioritize communication and transparency, discussing each phase with you to ensure your vision is realized in every detail.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Silver Cliff

The cost of building a custom home in Silver Cliff varies significantly based on multiple factors. For a custom home builder, the typical cost per square foot ranges from $550 to $700 for a higher-end build. For example, a 2,500 sq ft home could cost between $1,375,000 and $2,375,000, depending on materials and site conditions. Additionally, factors such as rocky terrain, steep access, and unique architectural features can elevate these costs.

When planning for water needs, a cistern is often chosen over a well due to the lengthy drilling wait times. It's recommended to size your cistern based on bedroom count, using the formula: (number of bedrooms x 2 people x 100 gallons/day). This consideration impacts initial costs, which can range from $25,000 to $75,000 for cistern installation. Overall, anticipate that the total costs will also include land purchase, septic installation, and utility extensions, which vary widely based on location and services required.

For a more in-depth understanding of construction costs, refer to our Custom Home Cost Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: Building a custom home in Colorado typically costs between $550 to $700 per square foot for higher-end constructions. For example, a 2,500 sq ft home could range from $1,375,000 to $2,375,000, depending on specific site conditions and designs.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a home varies based on complexity but generally takes 10 to 18 months for construction after the pre-construction phase, which can take an additional 2-6 months. Weather conditions, particularly in mountain areas, can also affect construction schedules.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience with local terrain, knowledge of local building codes, and a solid portfolio of previous projects. It's essential to choose a builder who prioritizes communication and transparency throughout the construction process.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a build feasibility report before purchasing land is crucial. This study assesses site conditions, zoning regulations, and utility availability to ensure that your potential property is suitable for construction.
