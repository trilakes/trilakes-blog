Why Morrison Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Building a custom home in Morrison, Colorado, is an exciting venture, offering the unique opportunity to create a personalized mountain retreat. However, with the stunning mountain views and serene surroundings come specific challenges that require the expertise of a seasoned custom home builder. At elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, properties in Morrison face unique weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and temperature swings. A professional general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your home construction adheres to local building codes and is designed to withstand these elements. With our comprehensive knowledge of the area, we can help you navigate the complexities of building in a mountain community, ensuring your new construction is both beautiful and functional.

Local Challenges in Jefferson County County

Constructing a home in Morrison‚Äôs mountain terrain presents several challenges that require special attention. The soil conditions vary, with rocky granite and clay often complicating foundation engineering. This rocky terrain influences the choice of foundation types, making engineered systems a necessity for septic installations and deep water tables in many areas. Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on our systematic approach to custom home construction. Our process involves several critical steps:

  • Step 1: Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Report - Before you purchase land, we conduct a thorough feasibility study to assess site conditions, setback requirements, and overall buildability. This is crucial to avoid future complications.
  • Step 2: Well Permit - Given that proof of water is often required for building permits, we recommend filing for a well permit early on. Alternatively, many clients opt for a cistern to expedite the building process.
  • Step 3: Site Plan - We develop a detailed site plan that outlines where your home will sit on the property and how utilities will be routed.
  • Step 4: Architectural Plans - You can either provide your own architect, choose from our network, or select plans from ArchitecturalDesigns.com.
  • Step 5: Engineered Foundation & Framing Plans - Our plans are stamped by licensed engineers to ensure they meet local and state codes.
  • Step 6: Land Survey - A professional survey confirms property boundaries and topography.
  • Step 7: Septic System Plans - If no sewer connection is available, we provide engineered septic plans.
  • Step 8: Compile & Submit for Building Permit - We handle all submissions to Jefferson County's Building Department.
  • Step 9: Pre-Construction Planning - Material ordering, scheduling, and budgeting are finalized before breaking ground.
  • Step 10: Construction - We oversee every phase of construction, ensuring quality and adherence to timelines.
  • Step 11: Certificate of Occupancy - Once all inspections are passed, we provide a certificate of occupancy.

This comprehensive approach ensures a smooth and efficient building experience, enhancing your peace of mind as you embark on creating your dream mountain home.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Morrison

Building a custom home in Morrison can vary significantly in cost depending on various factors. As of now, the cost per square foot for new construction generally ranges from $350-$1,000+ based on quality, with most clients opting for the high end of the spectrum:

  • Basic builds: $350-$450/sq ft
  • Standard quality: $450-$550/sq ft
  • Custom/higher-end: $550-$700/sq ft
  • Luxury builds: $700-$1,000+/sq ft

For example, a 2,500 sq ft home could range from $875,000-$1,375,000, and prices can escalate due to site conditions like rocky soil, challenging access, or steep driveways. Additionally, for homes relying on a cistern, the tank should be sized based on the number of bedrooms, calculated as (bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gal/day). Other costs include land, septic installation, and necessary utility extensions, which can add tens of thousands of dollars to your project. Being aware of these factors can help you budget effectively and make informed decisions during the construction process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost of building a custom home in Colorado varies significantly depending on factors such as location and site conditions. Most projects typically range from $350-$1,000+ per square foot, with high-end custom builds easily exceeding $700 per square foot.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house in Colorado can range from 10-18 months for typical projects, with complex or large homes potentially taking up to 24 months due to weather conditions and permitting. Pre-construction steps usually require an additional 2-6 months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience in mountain construction, licensing, and insurance. It's also beneficial to review their portfolio and check references to ensure they can handle the unique challenges of your specific site.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a build feasibility report before purchasing land is crucial. This report assesses site conditions, setback requirements, and overall buildability, helping you avoid costly mistakes and ensuring your investment is sound.
