Local Challenges in Saguache County

Center's mountainous landscape presents a unique set of challenges for home building. The region's rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate foundation engineering, requiring specialized designs to ensure stability. Additionally, properties may have a deep water table, necessitating the use of cisterns for water supply, as many homes cannot rely on traditional well systems, especially in remote locations.

Weather is another critical factor; with heavy snow loads and extreme temperature variations, homes must be designed with fire-resistant construction and proper insulation to withstand the elements. Access can be limited during the winter months due to steep driveways, making the site planning phase crucial. Understanding these local challenges is essential for a successful new construction project, ensuring that your mountain home is not only beautiful but also functional and safe.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a structured process to ensure your custom home is built to perfection. The journey begins with a build feasibility report to assess the land and determine its suitability for construction. After that, we move on to securing the necessary building permits through the Saguache County Building Department.

Our process includes several key steps: starting with the site plan, which outlines the placement of your home, driveway, and utilities, followed by creating architectural plans tailored to your vision. Once the plans are in place, we address the engineered foundation and framing plans, ensuring they meet local codes and withstand the area's environmental conditions.

Once we compile all required documents for your building permit, the construction phase begins, typically from May to October for higher elevations. Our team manages everything from site preparation to final inspections, ensuring a seamless experience. Expect a typical timeline of 10-18 months for construction, depending on the complexity of your home. You can trust us to handle all the details, allowing you to focus on enjoying the journey of building your dream mountain home.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $550 to $700 per square foot for standard builds, with luxury homes costing upwards of $1,000 per square foot. Additional expenses such as site preparation and utilities can further increase this cost.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a custom home in Colorado usually spans 10-18 months, depending on various factors such as design complexity and site conditions. Pre-construction tasks can add an additional 2-6 months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When choosing a custom home builder, consider their experience in mountain construction, licensing, insurance, and past project portfolios. It's also essential to ensure they offer a detailed process and are responsive to your needs throughout the project.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study is crucial before purchasing land. It assesses whether the property is suitable for building, considering factors like soil conditions and access, which can save you from costly mistakes down the road.
