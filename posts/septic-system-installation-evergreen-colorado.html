Local Challenges in Jefferson County County

Evergreen, nestled in Jefferson County, presents several unique challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate traditional septic installation methods and often necessitate engineered septic systems to ensure proper effluent distribution and treatment. With the water table frequently deep, many properties require additional water storage solutions like cisterns, making soil testing and proper design even more critical.

Moreover, the frost line at elevations over 8,000 feet reaches depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating specific measures to protect your septic system from freezing during harsh winters. Additionally, access to many properties can be limited, especially during snowfall, which often restricts construction activities to the typical May-October building season. Understanding these local challenges is essential for effective septic installation and maintenance.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we ensure a thorough and efficient septic system installation process, tailored to the unique needs of your Evergreen property. The first step involves conducting a comprehensive soils test, where we bring a mini excavator to your site to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. We collect soil samples from multiple depths, which are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the appropriate type of septic system for your location.

Once we receive the soils report, we proceed to design your septic system, ensuring it meets Regulation 43 standards. Next, we assist you in acquiring the necessary septic permit through the Jefferson County Building Department. After permitting, we schedule excavation, which can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions and access.

Our installation typically includes the septic tank, leach field, and, if required, advanced treatment units (ATUs) or sand filter systems. Following installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning as intended. We emphasize ongoing maintenance to keep your system operational for decades, with proper winterization techniques to protect it from the cold.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Evergreen

When it comes to septic installation in Evergreen, several factors will influence the overall cost. For instance, the soil type, access to the site, and the specific requirements of your property can significantly affect pricing. As a general rule, homeowners should expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000 for a basic engineered system, with costs rising for more complex installations.

For example, a sand filter system typically costs between $45,000 and $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can range from $55,000 to $80,000+, especially if rocky conditions necessitate additional excavation expenses that can add $10,000-$25,000. Additionally, when determining tank size for cisterns, we recommend calculating based on bedroom count‚Äî2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day, ensuring you have adequate water storage.

It's important to note that the costs of soil testing and engineering range from $2,000 to $4,000, while permits can vary between $500 and $2,000 depending on local requirements. Excavation costs typically range from $8,000 to $20,000, and the septic tank and components can add another $5,000 to $15,000. By understanding these cost factors, you can better prepare for your septic installation project.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system. Factors such as soil type, site access, and any additional features like advanced treatment units can increase the price significantly, sometimes reaching $80,000 or more.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary; however, most projects take about one week from excavation to completion. This can be affected by weather conditions, site access, and the complexity of the system being installed.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases in Colorado‚Äôs mountain regions, engineered septic systems are required due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are specifically designed to handle difficult terrains, ensuring effective wastewater treatment and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil tests, where soil samples are taken at various depths to evaluate the site's suitability for a septic system. This approach helps determine the best type of onsite wastewater treatment system needed for your property.
