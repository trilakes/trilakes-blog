Local Challenges in Douglas County

The picturesque landscape of Parker presents unique challenges for cistern installation. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet, the soil here ranges from rocky granite to decomposed granite and clay in the valleys, creating difficulties during excavation. Furthermore, the water table is often deep, making cisterns and water hauling essential for many properties. Homeowners must also contend with a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring specialized frost-proof cisterns for winter readiness. In addition, steep driveways and limited access during winter can complicate installation, making it crucial to work with a knowledgeable cistern installer near me who understands these local intricacies.

Our Cistern Installation Process

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a thorough discussion about your water needs, property conditions, and any specific requirements you have.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Parker

When considering water storage tank installation, it‚Äôs essential to understand the cost factors involved. The starting price for a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank typically costs between $22,000 and $28,000. Total installation costs, which include excavation, pump systems, and other components, can range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.

The size of your cistern should be based on your daily water usage, calculated as follows: each household member typically uses 100 gallons per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would require approximately 600 gallons per day, prompting a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills. However, many homeowners opt for larger systems for convenience, especially given the challenging winter access in Parker.

Additional costs can arise from rocky soil and difficult terrain, potentially increasing excavation costs by 20-40%. Other factors include trenching, pump systems, and electrical hookups, which can further influence the overall price.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and installation requirements. Typically, a 5,000-gallon system ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, including excavation and pump systems.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank, consider your daily water usage. For a 3-bedroom home with a family of six, you would typically need at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills, or even larger if you want to limit trips during winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can range from a few weeks to a couple of months. This depends on site conditions, permitting, and operational factors such as weather conditions, particularly during the winter months.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors, including water table depth and property conditions. A cistern serves as a viable well alternative for properties with deep water tables or poor well reliability, providing a dependable water source through water hauling when necessary.
