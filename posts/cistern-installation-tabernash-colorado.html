Local Challenges in Grand County County

Tabernash's rugged landscape and extreme weather conditions present unique challenges for water management. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation for buried water tanks, significantly increasing costs and installation time. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating advanced techniques for winter preparation and freeze protection during installations. Limited winter access due to steep driveways and heavy snowfall can further complicate water storage tank installation. Many residents rely on water hauling to supplement their supply, making professional cistern installation essential for reliable access to potable water.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Tabernash

The investment in a cistern installation can vary significantly based on various factors. The starting point for a potable water storage tank typically includes the tank itself, excavation, and installation costs. For example:

  • A Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000.
  • Additional costs for excavation may range from $3,000 to $8,000, depending on the rocky terrain and access issues.
  • For full installation, including pumps and trenching, you can expect total system costs to be between $25,000 and $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

When sizing your cistern, consider your daily water usage, which averages about 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a family living in a 3-bedroom home (assuming 6 people) will require a minimum of 600 gallons per day. If you prefer to refill every 2-3 months, you may need a tank capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons or more. Remember that rocky terrain, winter conditions, and remote access can add 20-40% to excavation costs, so planning ahead is crucial for your budget.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size, excavation needs, and additional systems required. For example, a 5,000-gallon system will start around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may run closer to $40,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need is based on your daily water usage. On average, you should calculate about 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six residents would require at least a 36,000-gallon tank if refilling every few months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and any necessary permits. The timeline includes excavation, tank installation, and system integration.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns can be a more reliable solution for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, where wells may be impractical. Cisterns offer a controlled water supply, while wells can vary in yield.
