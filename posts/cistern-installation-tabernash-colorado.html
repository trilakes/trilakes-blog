Why Tabernash Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the stunning mountain community of Tabernash, Colorado, where homes often sit at elevations of 8,000 feet and above, having a reliable source of water is essential. For many residents, especially those without access to a well, cistern installation becomes a practical solution. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have sufficient potable water storage for daily needs and emergencies. From fire suppression to agricultural water storage, cisterns serve multiple purposes, making them a valuable asset for both permanent and seasonal homes. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we specialize in water storage tank installation, providing tailored solutions suitable for the unique conditions of Grand County.

Local Challenges in Grand County

Tabernash presents unique challenges that require careful consideration during cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for an underground cistern, often leading to increased costs and extended timelines. Additionally, the water table in this mountainous region is frequently deep, making cisterns a necessary alternative to unreliable or impractical wells. Winter access can be particularly challenging, especially with steep driveways and heavy snowfall that can disrupt water delivery and limit access to your property. Furthermore, the frost line at depths of 36-48 inches necessitates proper insulation and installation practices to prevent freezing, especially in a frost-proof cistern. Understanding these local conditions is crucial to successful water storage solutions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Tabernash

The cost of cistern installation in Tabernash can vary significantly based on a variety of factors. For instance, the size of the water storage tank installation is crucial. To determine the appropriate size for a potable water storage tank, consider your daily water usage, which averages around 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants could require a tank capable of storing at least 600 gallons per day.

Refill frequency is another critical factor. Many homeowners prefer larger systems to reduce the frequency of refills, especially given the difficulties presented by winter weather. For instance, if you plan on refilling every 2-3 months, a tank capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons may be necessary. On the other hand, systems designed to last through winter might require over 72,000 gallons.

When it comes to costs, here are some typical estimates:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: starting from $13,000 to $15,000.
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: starting from $22,000 to $28,000.
  • Additional excavation costs may range from $3,000 to $8,000+, depending on site conditions.
  • Complete installed systems can range from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on design and capacity.

It's essential to account for potential increases in costs due to rocky terrain, access challenges, and the overall depth of the frost line in this region.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for installing a cistern in Colorado can range significantly based on capacity and site conditions. Typically, a 5,000-gallon system starts from $25,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. It's crucial to consider additional costs such as excavation, pump systems, and electrical hookups that can raise the overall price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need is determined by your household‚Äôs daily water usage. As a rule of thumb, calculate your daily needs based on bedroom count: 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of residents in your home. This helps ensure that you have enough capacity to meet your needs, especially during winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration of cistern installation can vary, but it generally takes anywhere from a few days to several weeks. Factors influencing the timeline include site preparation, excavation conditions, and the time required to obtain necessary permits from the Grand County building department.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific location and water needs. Cisterns can be a more reliable option in areas where wells are impractical due to deep water tables or poor water quality. They also offer flexibility for seasonal use and backup water supply, making them ideal for many mountain properties.
