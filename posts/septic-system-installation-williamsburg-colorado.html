Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process to ensure your septic installation is executed flawlessly. Initially, we conduct thorough soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes to a depth of eight feet, collecting soil samples at various depths. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer for a soils report, which determines the most suitable type of system for your property.

Once we receive the soils report, we can begin the design phase. We will help you secure the necessary septic permit from the Fremont County Building Department. Our team specializes in engineered systems, which are commonly required for the challenging soils found in the area. Typical installation timelines range from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions.

After obtaining the permit, we proceed with excavation, installation of the septic tank, and the creation of the leach field or drain field. Our advanced systems, like sand filter systems or ATUs, ensure the effective treatment of wastewater. Finally, we conduct a thorough inspection to guarantee everything meets local regulations and functions optimally. By following this comprehensive process, we ensure that your on-site wastewater system is reliable and compliant.

Generally, the total installation costs for an engineered system start at around $35,000 and can reach as high as $80,000+ for advanced systems like ATUs. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 for basic engineered systems to upwards of $80,000 for more advanced systems like ATUs. Factors such as soil conditions and site accessibility can influence these costs significantly.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes between a few days to two weeks. This timeline depends on factors like the complexity of the system and local permitting processes.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Williamsburg require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. An engineered system is designed to effectively treat wastewater while complying with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil composition and drainage capabilities, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design.
