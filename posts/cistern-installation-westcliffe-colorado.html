Why Westcliffe Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Westcliffe, Colorado, where homes often lack access to reliable well water, cistern installation is not just a necessity, but a smart investment. Many properties rely on underground water storage tanks to ensure a consistent supply of potable water, especially during the harsh winter months. With elevations soaring above 8,000 feet, the unique challenges presented by the rugged terrain and variable weather conditions make it crucial to employ a professional cistern installer. Choosing an experienced contractor ensures that your water storage tank installation meets local building codes and is equipped to handle the specifics of high-altitude living.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Westcliffe

The cost of cistern installation in Westcliffe varies based on several factors, including the size of the tank, site conditions, and additional components needed for a complete system. For instance, a typical potable water storage tank is sized based on daily water usage‚Äîapproximately 100 gallons per person, multiplied by the number of occupants. For a family living in a 3-bedroom home (with an assumed occupancy of 6 people), this means a daily usage of about 600 gallons.

To avoid frequent refills, homeowners often opt for larger tanks. If you want to refill every two months, you‚Äôd need a minimum capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons, which can drive costs higher. For instance, a 10,000-gallon Norwesco tank alone ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. When accounting for excavation (typically $3,000-$8,000), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and additional costs for trenching and electrical hookups, a total installed system could range from $40,000 to $55,000. Remember, challenging terrain and remote access can add 20-40% to excavation costs, emphasizing the importance of working with an experienced contractor.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system, factoring in the tank, excavation, and necessary plumbing. Costs may vary based on site conditions, such as rocky terrain or remote access, which can increase expenses significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, estimated at 100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a tank capable of holding at least 36,000-54,000 gallons if refills are needed every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process usually takes 1-2 days, not including the time needed for permit approval. Factors such as site conditions and weather can influence the timeline, especially in the winter months.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on several factors, including water availability and soil conditions. Cisterns are often a better option in areas like Westcliffe, where deep water tables make well drilling impractical or unreliable, while providing a reliable backup water supply.
