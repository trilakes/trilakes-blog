Why Lone Tree Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the breathtaking mountain community of Lone Tree, Colorado, homeowners face unique challenges when it comes to water supply. With an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties lack a reliable well, making cistern installation a crucial solution for obtaining potable water. Cisterns provide an effective way to store water for residential use, irrigation, and fire suppression, especially in areas where the water table is deep and access is limited. By hiring a licensed and insured professional like Tri-Lakes Contracting, you ensure your water storage tank installation is compliant with local regulations, efficient, and tailored to your specific needs.

Local Challenges in Douglas County County

Lone Tree's rugged terrain presents several challenges for water storage solutions. The soil is predominantly rocky granite and clay, making traditional excavation for an underground water storage tank complex and costly. Moreover, the frost line reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, especially at higher elevations, necessitating specialized techniques for winterizing cistern installation to prevent freezing. The extreme weather patterns, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, further complicate access to many properties, particularly during winter months. As a homeowner in this mountain community, understanding these challenges is vital for selecting the best cistern installer near me.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Lone Tree

The cost of installing a cistern in Lone Tree can vary significantly based on several factors. Using our established formulas, we calculate the appropriate tank size by multiplying your bedroom count by 2 people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs for water storage tank installation:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Excavation for burial: $3,000-$8,000+ (depending on rock and access)
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to the house (per linear foot): $25-$50
  • Additional electrical hookup: $1,000-$2,500

When factoring in all elements, a basic installed system for a 5,000-gallon tank typically starts from $25,000 and can reach up to $35,000. If you need a larger capacity, such as a 10,000-gallon system, costs can range from $40,000 to $55,000. Keep in mind that rocky terrain may increase expenses by 20-40%, and winter installations may incur additional charges due to challenging ground conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado generally starts at around $25,000 for a basic installed system, which includes a 5,000-gallon tank and necessary excavation and installation services. Costs can increase significantly based on site conditions, tank size, and additional features, reaching up to $55,000 or more for larger systems.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage, which is typically calculated based on bedroom count. A 3-bedroom home, for example, would need a tank that holds at least 36,000 to 54,000 gallons for every 2-3 month refill, or about 72,000 gallons to last through the winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary, but typically ranges from a few days to a couple of weeks. This timeframe can be influenced by site conditions, permitting, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are often a better choice in areas where wells are impractical or unreliable due to deep water tables or rocky soil, making them an excellent alternative for rural water supply solutions.
