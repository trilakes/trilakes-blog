Why Lone Tree Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In Lone Tree, where homes often sit at an elevation of 8,000+ feet, having a reliable water supply is crucial. Many properties in this scenic mountain community do not have access to wells, making cistern installation a viable solution for potable water storage. Additionally, the unique challenges presented by the rugged Colorado terrain necessitate professional expertise. A licensed and insured contractor, such as Tri-Lakes Contracting, can ensure your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, providing safe and reliable access to water year-round.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

Douglas County is known for its stunning mountain landscapes but comes with its fair share of challenges when it comes to water supply. The rocky granite and clay soils can make digging for a cistern difficult, often requiring specialized excavation techniques. At elevations of 8,000+ feet, properties frequently experience deep water tables, making water hauling and cistern installation necessary alternatives for many homeowners. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, which means that proper winterization is essential to protect your water storage tank from freezing temperatures. With steep driveways and limited winter access, having a professional cistern installer near you is vital to ensure that your system is both effective and compliant with Douglas County building permits.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Lone Tree

The cost of water storage tank installation in Lone Tree can vary significantly based on several factors. For instance, if you opt for a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank, you can expect to pay between $13,000 and $15,000 for the tank alone. With installation, excavation, and additional components like pumps and electrical hookups, the total cost can range from $25,000 to $35,000. If you require a 10,000-gallon system, the tank price will be between $22,000 and $28,000, with total costs reaching $40,000 to $55,000.

It's important to consider that costs can increase due to site conditions such as rocky terrain or difficult access. For instance, properties with steep driveways may require additional equipment for excavation, adding 20-40% to the overall costs. Furthermore, the refill frequency you desire for your cistern will influence the size and ultimately the cost of the system. For example, a family in a 3-bedroom home should plan for a minimum of 36,000 gallons for a system refilled every 2-3 months, which significantly impacts the overall investment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the tank size and installation complexities. For instance, a 5,000-gallon system may start around $25,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can go up to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, typically estimated at 100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an average of 6 occupants would need at least 36,000 gallons to sustain them for 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary, but generally, it takes several days once excavation begins. The entire process, including permits and site assessments, may take a few weeks, especially considering potential weather delays.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and personal needs. Cisterns can be a better option for properties with deep water tables or unreliable wells, while wells may provide a more continuous water source if feasible.
