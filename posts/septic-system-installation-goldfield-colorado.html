Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic system installation process is designed to be thorough and efficient, ensuring that every aspect is covered for your peace of mind. The first step involves a comprehensive soil test using a mini excavator to create two test holes, each eight feet deep, to assess the soil conditions. This detailed analysis, sent to a licensed engineer, will determine the type of septic system you need, whether it be a conventional gravity system or an engineered solution. Following the soil report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Teller County Building Department. Once permits are secured, our team will begin the excavation and installation process, typically scheduled between May and October due to favorable weather conditions. Each installation is tailored based on site conditions, including the need for elevated systems, like sand filters or ATUs, especially in challenging terrains. All installations are conducted with an eye toward future maintenance, ensuring your septic system operates efficiently for decades to come.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Goldfield

When considering septic installation in Goldfield, it's essential to understand the various factors that influence costs. The total price for a typical engineered septic system can range from $35,000 to $50,000, with sand filter systems averaging between $45,000 and $65,000. If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is necessary, costs increase to approximately $55,000 to $80,000 or more, depending on site-specific challenges, such as rocky terrain or difficult access. In addition, the size of your onsite wastewater system, including tanks and leach fields, is often determined by the number of bedrooms in your home. As a rule of thumb, you should calculate your cistern size based on two people per bedroom, multiplied by 100 gallons per day. Thus, for a three-bedroom home, you would need a cistern capable of handling around 600 gallons per day. It‚Äôs crucial to remember that excavation costs, which can range from $8,000 to over $20,000, can significantly affect your overall project budget, particularly on rugged or steep sites.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for engineered systems, with more complex options like sand filters and ATUs ranging from $45,000 to $80,000 or more, depending on site conditions and soil type.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Septic installation can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the complexity of the system, the weather, and the permitting process. Typically, installations are completed during the warmer months from May to October.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Goldfield require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions and deep water tables. These systems are designed to handle the unique environmental factors present in the Colorado mountain terrain.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil testing, where soil samples are taken to determine the appropriate septic system design. This process is critical in identifying how well the soil drains and what type of septic system will be most effective.
