Local Challenges in Eagle County

Wolcott's mountainous terrain presents several challenges for septic system installations. The region‚Äôs rocky granite and clay soils often require engineered systems rather than conventional ones, as they significantly impact how wastewater is managed. Additionally, the water table can be deep, necessitating the use of cisterns for water supply, which complicates the on-site wastewater management further. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our approach to septic installation is thorough and tailored to your specific site conditions. The process begins with a detailed soil assessment, where we conduct soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. Our team brings in a mini excavator to dig two 8-foot deep test holes, sampling soil from various depths to understand its composition. This step is vital for developing an appropriate septic design.

Once we have the soils report‚Äîoften conducted pre-purchase for prospective buyers‚Äîwe move forward with the necessary permits, which must be obtained from the Eagle County Building Department. This ensures compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43, which governs all onsite wastewater systems in the state.

After receiving the green light on the design and permits, we schedule the installation. Depending on the complexity of your system and site access, the installation typically takes 1 to 3 days. During installation, we ensure that all aspects comply with local regulations, including proper placement of the leach field and drain field, to ensure a safe and effective septic system.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Wolcott

When considering the costs associated with your septic installation in Wolcott, several factors come into play, particularly due to the unique challenges presented by the mountain terrain. The total cost can range significantly based on site conditions and specific system requirements. Typically, you can expect a basic engineered system to cost between $35,000 and $50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000, and an advanced treatment unit (ATU) can range from $55,000 to $80,000+.

For properties that require cisterns, remember that the size is calculated based on your bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day). Costs for essential components, such as soil testing and engineering, typically start from $2,000 to $4,000, while permits may vary between $500 and $2,000. Excavation costs can also add up, often falling between $8,000 and $20,000+. If your site has rocky terrain or difficult access, expect costs to increase by an additional $10,000 to $25,000. Each of these factors contributes to the overall investment in your septic system, so it‚Äôs crucial to have a detailed understanding before moving forward.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary widely based on site conditions and system design. Typically, homeowners can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000+, depending on the complexity of the system needed due to challenging mountain soils.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process for a septic system generally takes between 1 to 3 days, depending on the complexity of the system and site access conditions. Obtaining the necessary permits from Eagle County may add additional time to your project timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Wolcott require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soils and terrain. Conventional systems are rarely feasible, and engineered solutions ensure compliance with Colorado's regulations while effectively managing wastewater.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil conditions, which is vital for determining the appropriate type of septic system for your property.
