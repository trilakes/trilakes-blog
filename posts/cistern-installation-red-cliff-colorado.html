Local Challenges in Eagle County County

Installing a water cistern in Red Cliff comes with its own set of challenges. The rocky granite and clay soil can make excavation a tricky task, leading to increased costs and longer installation times. Additionally, the water table in Eagle County is often deep, making cisterns a more practical choice for properties where drilling a well is unfeasible or prohibitively expensive.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is straightforward yet thorough, ensuring every detail is covered for our clients in Red Cliff:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a free consultation to assess your property and discuss your specific water needs.
  2. Site Assessment: Our team will evaluate your land for soil conditions, access, and the best location for your underground water storage tank.
  3. Permitting: All installations in Eagle County require permits, which we handle through the local building department.
  4. Excavation: We prepare the site by excavating the area for the cistern, taking into account rocky terrain, which may increase costs.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Red Cliff

Understanding the cost of cistern installation is crucial for homeowners in Red Cliff. The typical costs range based on several factors, including tank size, excavation needs, and site conditions. For example, if you're considering a 3-bedroom home, your daily water usage calculation would estimate around 600 gallons per day (100 gallons per person, assuming 2 people per bedroom). Here‚Äôs how you might size your cistern:

  • Refill monthly: A system of 18,000 gallons minimum.
  • Refill every 2-3 months: Requires a larger capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons.
  • To "get through winter" (4-5 months): You will need at least 72,000 gallons.

Typically, we install Norwesco tanks, which have the following cost ranges:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

Considering additional costs like excavation (which can range from $3,000-$8,000 depending on rocky conditions), pump systems, and trenching, the total installed cost can range from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on your specific needs. It's essential to note that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies widely but typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000+ for a complete installed system, including the tank, excavation, pumps, and other necessary components. For a smaller 5,000-gallon system, anticipate costs starting around $25,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and preferred refill frequency. For a 3-bedroom home, a minimum of 18,000 gallons is advisable for monthly refills, while larger systems of 72,000 gallons may be needed for winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, excavation needs, and permitting through the Eagle County building department. We strive to complete the installation efficiently while ensuring quality and compliance.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better option for properties with a deep water table or rocky terrain, where well drilling is impractical. Cisterns provide a reliable source of water and can be tailored to meet your specific needs, while wells may have limitations in yield and require ongoing maintenance.
