Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, step-by-step process to ensure your custom home is built to perfection. Here‚Äôs how we work:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before buying land, we conduct a feasibility study to assess if the property is suitable for building.
  2. Well Permit: Securing a well permit early is crucial, especially since many counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit.
  3. Site Plan: Our team creates a comprehensive site plan showing the house footprint, driveway, and utility routes.
  4. Architectural Plans: We can collaborate with your architect or connect you with trusted professionals, including options from ArchitecturalDesigns.com.
  5. Engineered Foundation Plans: We ensure your foundation is engineered based on your site conditions to withstand snow loads and ground movement.
  6. Land Survey: A professional survey is conducted to confirm property boundaries and any necessary easements.
  7. Septic System Plans: If no sewer connection is available, we design an engineered septic system that meets Colorado regulations.
  8. Compile & Submit for Building Permit: We gather all necessary documents and submit them to the El Paso County Building Department.
  9. Pre-Construction Planning: Final reviews and material orders are confirmed before breaking ground.
  10. Construction: From foundation to final finishes, we oversee every aspect of the building process.
  11. Certificate of Occupancy: After passing inspections, we provide you with the keys to your new home!

The entire process typically takes 12 to 24 months, depending on the complexity of the project and the conditions of the mountain environment. This timeline allows us to ensure that each phase meets the highest standards of quality and safety.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Monument

When planning your custom home construction, understanding the costs involved is essential. In Colorado's mountain communities, the cost per square foot can vary significantly:

  • Basic build: $350-$450/sq ft
  • Standard quality: $450-$550/sq ft
  • Custom/higher-end: $550-$700/sq ft
  • Luxury/high-end: $700-$1,000+/sq ft

For example, a 2,000 sq ft home could cost between $700,000 and $1,100,000. Additionally, consider factors that can drive up costs, such as rocky terrain, challenging access, and the need for engineered systems like septic installations, which can range from $35,000 to $80,000. For water supply, if you opt for a cistern, remember to budget for approximately $25,000 to $75,000, depending on the size, which is typically calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

Consult with your custom home builder early in the process to refine your budget based on the unique site conditions and challenges in your area.

Also, don‚Äôt forget to factor in the cost of securing building permits, which can range from $5,000 to $20,000+ depending on home size and complexity.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies widely based on several factors, including size and complexity. On average, expect to pay between $350 and $1,000+ per square foot, with total home costs ranging from approximately $700,000 to over $3 million for luxury builds.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house can range from 12 to 24 months, depending on the complexity of the design and local conditions. Pre-construction planning typically takes 2-6 months, while the actual construction phase usually lasts 10-18 months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience with mountain construction, their reputation in the community, and their ability to navigate local regulations and building permits. It‚Äôs also important to review their previous projects to ensure they align with your vision.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study is highly recommended before purchasing land. This study evaluates site conditions, zoning regulations, and potential construction challenges, helping you avoid costly mistakes and ensuring the land is suitable for your custom home construction.
