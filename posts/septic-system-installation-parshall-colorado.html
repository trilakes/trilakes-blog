Local Challenges in Grand County County

Parshall‚Äôs remarkable elevation and mountainous terrain present unique challenges for homeowners. Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Parshall

The cost of installing a septic system in Parshall can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000+ depending on the complexity of the project. Here's a breakdown of potential costs:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: $2,000-$4,000 to evaluate the site and determine the appropriate system.
  • Permits: Expect to pay between $500 and $2,000 for the necessary permits.
  • Excavation: Depending on the terrain, this can range from $8,000 to over $20,000.
  • Septic Tank and Components: Costs for the tank and accompanying parts can be between $5,000 and $15,000.
  • Advanced Treatment Units (ATU): If needed, these can add $8,000 to $15,000 to the total cost.
  • Electrical and Controls: Expect an additional $2,000-$4,000 for electrical work.

For properties that require cisterns, the size is typically calculated based on the number of bedrooms: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. Keep in mind that challenging site conditions, such as rocky soil or steep access, can significantly increase installation costs, possibly adding anywhere from $10,000 to $25,000 to your project.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can range from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on the specific site conditions and the type of system required. Factors like soil conditions, terrain accessibility, and additional components can significantly impact the overall price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation typically spans several weeks, particularly in mountain regions like Parshall. Factors such as weather conditions, necessary permits, and site-specific challenges can affect the duration of the project.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Colorado, especially in mountainous regions, require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. This ensures proper functioning and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil testing that involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples. This helps determine the type of septic system necessary for proper wastewater management.
