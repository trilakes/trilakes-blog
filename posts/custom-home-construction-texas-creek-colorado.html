Local Challenges in Fremont County

Texas Creek is known for its stunning mountain landscapes, but this beauty comes with its own set of construction challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soils can complicate foundation engineering, requiring specialized methods and materials. Additionally, the frost line can reach up to 48 inches, which must be considered when planning your foundation to prevent future issues. The area often experiences extreme weather conditions, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, which can impact your construction timeline. Access to many properties can be limited, with steep driveways that may pose challenges, especially during winter months. Furthermore, fire mitigation is critical in forested areas like Texas Creek, necessitating defensible space requirements to protect your new mountain home.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our custom home construction process is designed to guide you seamlessly from concept to completion. Here‚Äôs how it works:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before you even purchase land, we recommend a feasibility study to assess site conditions, setback requirements, and overall buildability.
  2. Well Permit: If you're planning a well, file your application early; wait times can be lengthy. If a cistern is preferred, we can help you navigate that process too.
  3. Site Plan Development: We create a detailed site plan that outlines the layout of your home, driveway, and utilities.
  4. Architectural Plans: You have the option to bring your architect or choose from our trusted partners or ArchitecturalDesigns.com.
  5. Engineering Plans: We secure stamped engineering plans tailored to your specific site conditions, ensuring compliance with local codes.
  6. Land Survey: A professional land survey is conducted to confirm property boundaries and easements.
  7. Septic System Plans: If necessary, we‚Äôll design an engineered septic system to meet Colorado regulations.
  8. Building Permit Submission: We compile all necessary documents and submit them to the Fremont County Building Department for your building permit.
  9. Construction: We manage the construction process, ensuring timely progress through each phase, from site preparation to final inspections.
  10. Certificate of Occupancy: Upon passing final inspections, we will secure your Certificate of Occupancy, allowing you to move into your new home!

This process typically takes 12 to 24 months, depending on the complexity of your project and local weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Texas Creek

Understanding the costs associated with building a custom home in Texas Creek is essential. The average cost per square foot for new construction in Colorado‚Äôs mountainous areas can range from $350 to over $1,000, depending on the quality and complexity of your build. For example:

  • Basic builds: $350-$450/sq ft
  • Standard quality: $450-$550/sq ft
  • Custom/higher-end: $550-$700/sq ft
  • Luxury builds: $700-$1,000+/sq ft

For a typical 2,000 sq ft home, costs can range from $700,000 to $1,100,000. Additional expenses often include land, cistern installation ($25,000-$75,000), septic installation ($35,000-$80,000), and driveway access ($10,000-$50,000).

When considering a cistern for your water supply, remember that sizing is typically calculated based on bedroom count‚Äî2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This can significantly influence your overall cost and planning.

Factors such as rocky terrain and limited access can further increase total costs, so it's vital to account for these variables when budgeting your project.
