Why Saguache Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the breathtaking mountain community of Saguache, Colorado, comes with its own unique set of challenges, particularly when it comes to septic installation. At elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, the soil and weather conditions can complicate the installation and functionality of a septic system. Without professional guidance, homeowners may inadvertently choose systems that are ill-suited for their property, leading to costly repairs and environmental concerns. This is where licensed experts like Tri-Lakes Contracting come in, providing essential knowledge and superior service for every aspect of your septic tank installation.

Local Challenges in Saguache County County

The diverse terrain of Saguache County presents specific challenges when installing an onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS). The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil, combined with clay present in the valleys, often necessitate engineered septic systems to ensure proper function. Additionally, the water table is frequently deep, which can require homeowners to consider options such as cisterns for water hauling. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, especially at higher elevations, planning for freeze protection is crucial during the installation process. Furthermore, steep driveways and limited access during winter months can complicate installation efforts, making professional assistance indispensable for navigating these challenges.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and tailored to meet the unique needs of each property:

  1. Site Assessment: We begin with a comprehensive site evaluation, including soil testing to determine the appropriate system for your land.
  2. Soils Report: Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to collect soil samples that inform our design. This report is crucial for understanding the type of system required.
  3. Septic Design: Based on the soils report, we design an engineered system compliant with Regulation 43, ensuring optimal functionality for your specific terrain.
  4. Permits: We assist in acquiring the necessary septic permit from the Saguache County Building Department, which oversees all permits in the area.
  5. Installation: Our experienced team executes the installation, taking into account factors like access, weather conditions, and any required freeze protection measures.
  6. Final Inspection: Once the system is installed, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly and in compliance with local regulations.

The timeline for installation can vary, typically ranging from one to two weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting processes.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Saguache

Understanding the costs involved with septic installation in Saguache is essential for homeowners planning their budgets. Installed costs can range significantly based on site conditions, from a basic engineered system starting at approximately $35,000 to $50,000, to more complex systems like sand filters, which typically cost between $45,000 and $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) can further increase costs, usually totaling $55,000 to $80,000 or more.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for a septic installation typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system, site conditions, and any necessary engineering required. For example, a basic engineered system can start at approximately $35,000, while more advanced systems can exceed $80,000 in challenging terrains.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation in Saguache generally ranges from one to two weeks. This duration can vary based on the complexity of the site, permitting processes, and weather conditions, especially during winter months.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Saguache require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and regulations outlined in Regulation 43. A professional assessment is essential to determine the best system for your specific site.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions and determine the appropriate septic system design, which is crucial for proper waste treatment.
