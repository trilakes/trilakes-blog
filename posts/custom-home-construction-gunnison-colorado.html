Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Gunnison County is known for its breathtaking views and outdoor lifestyle, but building here requires careful consideration of several challenges. The soil composition ranges from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, which can complicate foundation engineering. Moreover, the water table tends to be deeper, necessitating the installation of cisterns or innovative water hauling solutions. These factors, coupled with a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches at higher elevations, highlight the need for professionally designed foundation systems. Additionally, the building season typically runs from May to October, and access to properties can be limited due to steep driveways and heavy snow loads during winter. For home builders looking to navigate these rocky challenges, a comprehensive build feasibility report is crucial for assessing the viability of the land.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on a transparent, step-by-step approach to new construction. The process begins with a pre-purchase property inspection and feasibility study to assess the land before you buy. This includes a site visit and an in-depth analysis of local setbacks, soil conditions, and utility availability. Once you decide to proceed, we help you obtain the necessary building permits through the Gunnison County Building Department, which can take 2-8 weeks for approval.

Following that, we develop a detailed site plan and work with licensed engineers to create stamped architectural and foundation plans. The construction phase involves multiple inspections to ensure compliance with local codes, and we handle everything from excavation to final finishes. Typically, the entire timeline from pre-construction to obtaining a certificate of occupancy can range from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the build and the weather conditions at this elevation.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Gunnison

The cost of building a custom home in Gunnison can vary significantly based on multiple factors. For example, the average cost per square foot for a basic build ranges from $350 to $450, while more luxurious options can climb to between $700 and $1,000+ per square foot. To give you a clearer picture, a typical 2,500 sq ft home may cost between $875,000 and $1,375,000. It‚Äôs essential to remember that the costs can increase based on rocky terrain, challenging access, and the necessity for engineered septic systems, which can range from $35,000 to $80,000.

In addition, if you choose to install a cistern for water, costs can range from $25,000 to $75,000, based on the size needed. A common formula for sizing your cistern is to calculate 100 gallons per day per bedroom (assuming two people per bedroom). Thus, for a three-bedroom home, you would need at least 6,000 gallons to sustain daily needs. With all these factors in mind, a detailed budget should be established early in the process to avoid any surprises.
