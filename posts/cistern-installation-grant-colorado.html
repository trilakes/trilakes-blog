Why Grant Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Grant, Colorado, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, the need for a reliable water source is paramount. With the local water table often deep and challenging to access, many properties rely on cistern installation as an essential solution for their water needs. The unique mountain terrain presents specific challenges that require the expertise of a professional cistern installer. Attempting to install a water storage tank without professional guidance can lead to costly mistakes, especially with the rocky soil and extreme weather conditions typical in this region.

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we specialize in underground water storage tanks tailored to meet the needs of mountain communities like Grant. We understand the intricacies involved in providing a dependable source of potable water, whether for residential use, irrigation, or fire suppression. Choosing a professional service not only ensures compliance with Park County building regulations but also guarantees a system designed for longevity and functionality in the high-altitude environment.

Local Challenges in Park County County

Grant's unique geographical and environmental challenges necessitate careful consideration during cistern installation. The region's rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs. Moreover, properties at this elevation frequently encounter a frost line of 36-48 inches, making proper installation crucial to prevent freezing and ensure the long-term efficacy of your water cistern.

Access to many properties can be limited due to steep driveways and harsh winter weather, which can restrict water hauling options. As a result, many homeowners opt for larger potable water storage tanks to minimize the frequency of refills during snowy months. Additionally, the extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads characteristic of Grant necessitate a well-planned installation process to avoid complications during the winter months. For these reasons, homeowners are encouraged to consult with a local cistern company familiar with these challenges.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to be thorough and efficient while addressing the specific needs of your mountain property. We start with a comprehensive site assessment to evaluate the soil conditions, accessibility, and your water usage requirements.

Next, we determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank based on your household size and refilling preferences. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, but larger systems may be necessary for properties with limited winter access.

After finalizing the design, we obtain the required permits from the Park County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations. The excavation phase follows, where we carefully prepare the site for your buried water tank. Our team uses specialized equipment to navigate Grant's challenging terrain, which may add to excavation costs.

Once the site is prepared, we install the tank, which typically involves a Norwesco brand tank for its durability and reliability in mountain conditions. Finally, we set up the pump system, electrical connections, and any necessary filtration systems to ensure your water supply is clean and safe. This entire process usually takes several weeks, depending on weather conditions and site-specific challenges.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Grant

The costs associated with water storage tank installation in Grant can vary significantly due to multiple factors. Generally, the total installed costs for a 5,000-gallon system range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically costs between $40,000 and $55,000. Sizing is based on your daily water usage, which is calculated by multiplying the number of bedrooms in your home by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require around 600 gallons/day, translating to a minimum of 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly.

Additional considerations that can influence costs include:

  • Excavation: Costs can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on rock presence and access issues.
  • Pump system & pressure tank: Typically between $2,500 and $5,000.
  • Trenching to the house: Costs between $25 and $50 per linear foot.
  • Risers & access lids: Can add an additional $500 to $1,500.
  • Electrical hookup: Costs between $1,000 and $2,500.
  • Water filtration/treatment: Typically $2,000 to $5,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 depending on the size of the tank and specific site conditions. For example, a basic 5,000-gallon system can cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system may range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage. Generally, you can calculate this by multiplying the number of bedrooms in your home by two people per bedroom, and then multiplying that number by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on weather conditions and site-specific challenges, but generally, it takes several weeks from the initial assessment to final installation. This includes obtaining necessary permits and completing excavation work.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns are an excellent option for properties where wells are too deep or unreliable. They provide flexible water storage solutions, especially in areas with difficult access or deep water tables like Grant.
