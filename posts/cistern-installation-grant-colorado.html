Why Grant Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Grant, Colorado, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, reliable water sources become a vital consideration for homeowners. Many properties in this area lack sufficient well access due to deep water tables or unreliable yields. As a result, cistern installation emerges as a practical solution for potable water storage, irrigation, and even fire suppression needs. Choosing a licensed and insured professional like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, providing peace of mind and long-term reliability. Our expertise in local regulations, including necessary permits through the Park County Building Department, guarantees a smooth installation process tailored to the unique challenges of mountain living.

Local Challenges in Park County

Grant's unique terrain presents several challenges when it comes to water storage solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for a cistern, while the frost line stretches down 36-48 inches, making winter preparation a crucial aspect of installation. Many properties feature steep driveways that limit access, especially in winter months when heavy snowfall can restrict movement. Homeowners often find themselves relying on water hauling services due to the impracticalities of well drilling in this mountainous region. Additionally, with extreme temperature fluctuations, it is essential to consider freeze protection for your buried water tank, ensuring that your water remains accessible year-round.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a streamlined process for cistern installation that ensures efficiency and quality. Initially, our team conducts a site assessment to determine the best location for your water cistern, considering factors such as access, soil composition, and your specific water needs. Once we finalize the site, we will obtain the necessary permits from the Park County Building Department. Following approval, we begin excavation, which can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on the site's conditions. We typically install Norwesco tanks, renowned for their durability and reliability, ensuring that your water storage tank installation is completed to the highest standards. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Grant

Understanding the cost of cistern installation in Grant is crucial for planning your budget. The pricing of an underground water storage tank typically starts at $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can reach up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank, depending on installation specifics. The size of your cistern is primarily determined by your daily water usage, which is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require approximately 600 gallons per day. The refill frequency also plays a significant role in sizing; if you prefer fewer refills, a larger system is necessary. Additional costs, such as excavation (which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on rocky terrain), pump systems, and electrical hookups, can further increase the total investment. Therefore, homeowners should expect to budget for a total installed system ranging from $40,000 to over $120,000, particularly if the property has challenging access or rocky conditions that complicate excavation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 for standard installations, depending on the tank size and site conditions. Additional factors such as excavation and installation complexities can increase the total investment to upwards of $120,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is based on your daily water usage, calculated by multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom and 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 2-3 weeks from site assessment to completion, depending on weather conditions and site access. Excavation can take several days, followed by the installation of the tank and necessary systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on the specific conditions of your property. Cisterns are ideal for areas with deep water tables or unreliable well yields, while wells may be suitable for properties with consistent groundwater access.
