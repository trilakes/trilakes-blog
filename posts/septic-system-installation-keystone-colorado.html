Local Challenges in Summit County

Summit County, particularly Keystone, is characterized by its challenging soil conditions and extreme weather variations. Properties here often have rocky granite or clay soils that can complicate the installation of a septic tank. The water table is typically deep, which means many homes require additional solutions like cisterns for water supply and engineered systems for waste management. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning and execution to ensure proper function throughout winter. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous step-by-step process for septic installation. Initially, we conduct a soils test rather than a traditional perc test, which involves digging two test holes up to eight feet deep to analyze the soil. This data is sent to a licensed engineer for a detailed soils report, determining the best type of system for your property. Following this, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Summit County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43 governing onsite wastewater systems.

Installation typically occurs during the building season from May to October, lasting anywhere from several days to a few weeks, depending on site complexity. Our team carefully excavates the area, installs the septic components, and ensures the system is fully operational and meets all regulatory requirements. Throughout the process, we focus on minimizing disruption and maintaining safety, particularly essential in our mountainous terrain. Our commitment to quality service means your septic system will function efficiently for many years to come.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Keystone

The cost of septic installation in Keystone can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil condition, system type, and site accessibility. For a comprehensive installation, expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000. This includes the soils test and engineering report, which typically costs between $2,000 and $4,000, and the necessary permits that range from $500 to $2,000. Excavation, which can be challenging on rocky terrain, may add $8,000 to $20,000 to your total costs.

If you're considering a cistern for water, remember that sizing is based on the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated as 100 gallons per day per two occupants per bedroom. This can lead to additional costs depending on the size required. For properties needing advanced systems like sand filters or ATUs, costs can rise significantly, reflecting the installation of more complex systems needed for challenging mountain soils. Be prepared that difficult sites may incur additional costs of $10,000 to $25,000 based on conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely, typically ranging from $35,000 to $80,000. Factors affecting the price include soil conditions, site accessibility, and the specific type of septic system required.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can range from several days to a few weeks, depending on site complexity and permitting processes. Generally, installations are performed during the building season from May to October.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Keystone require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions per Regulation 43. These systems are designed to function effectively in the unique geological and environmental context of the area.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil tests, which involve analyzing soil samples to determine suitable septic system types. This is essential for ensuring proper wastewater treatment and system longevity.
