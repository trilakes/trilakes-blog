Why Franktown Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Franktown, Douglas County, ensuring a reliable water supply is essential, especially given the challenges posed by our unique mountain environment. With elevations over 8,000 feet, properties often face deep water tables and rocky soils that complicate traditional water sourcing methods. Many residents rely on cistern installation as a dependable solution for storing potable water, especially when wells are impractical or unreliable. Working with a licensed and insured contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting not only guarantees compliance with local regulations but also ensures that your installation meets the specific needs of your property.

Local Challenges in Douglas County County

Franktown's beautiful but rugged terrain presents unique challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation difficult and costly, often increasing excavation expenses by 20-40%. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep in this area, requiring many homeowners to rely on water hauling for their potable water storage. The average frost line here ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating specialized installation techniques to ensure your underground water storage tank is frost-proof. Moreover, winter access can be limited, making it crucial to consider your refill frequency and tank size during decision-making. Fire mitigation is also a significant factor, particularly in forested areas, requiring homeowners to maintain defensible space around their properties.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Franktown

The cost of a cistern installation in Franktown can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, homeowners can expect to pay:

  • 5,000-gallon tank system: $25,000-$35,000, including excavation, pump, and electrical work.
  • 10,000-gallon tank system: $40,000-$55,000 for a more extensive setup.
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): Ranging from $75,000-$120,000 for those needing substantial water storage.

To determine the appropriate size for your water storage tank, consider your daily water usage. For example, if your home has three bedrooms, the calculation would be:

3 bedrooms x 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day = 600 gallons/day. If you prefer to refill every month, you should aim for a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons.

Additional costs may arise from excavation work, which can range from $3,000-$8,000 depending on site conditions, as well as pump systems ($2,500-$5,000) and electrical work ($1,000-$2,500). Keep in mind that rocky terrain and limited access can increase expenses significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the size and specific site conditions, such as rocky terrain and access challenges.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, a three-bedroom home needs a minimum of 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 2-4 weeks, depending on site challenges, weather conditions, and permitting processes with Douglas County.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better option in areas like Franktown where the water table is deep and wells can be unreliable. Cisterns provide a consistent water supply and are ideal for properties without viable well access.
