Local Challenges in Fremont County

Coaldale‚Äôs beautiful but rugged landscape presents a unique set of challenges for custom home builders. Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our custom home construction process is designed to ensure that your vision becomes reality while adhering to local regulations and environmental considerations. Here‚Äôs a detailed overview of our step-by-step process:

Step 1: Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study - Before acquiring property, we conduct a comprehensive feasibility study, assessing site conditions, soil types, and water flow analysis to determine buildability.

Step 2: Well Permit - If your property requires a well, we advise filing for a well permit early, as wait times can range from 6-12 months.

Step 3: Site Plan - We develop a detailed site plan that outlines the house footprint, driveway location, and utility routes, ensuring compliance with setback requirements.

Step 4: Architectural Plans - You can either provide your architect or select from our trusted network or even ArchitecturalDesigns.com, which offers a variety of plans suitable for mountain homes.

Step 5: Engineered Foundation & Framing Plans - Our licensed engineers create stamped foundation and framing plans tailored to your specific site conditions.

Step 6: Land Survey - A professional land survey is conducted to verify property boundaries and easements.

Step 7: Septic System Plans - We handle septic system design, which is essential in this region where public sewer access is often unavailable.

Step 8: Compile & Submit for Building Permit - We compile all necessary documents and submit them to the Fremont County Building Department, ensuring a smooth approval process.

Step 9: Pre-Construction Planning - Prior to breaking ground, we finalize all plans, schedules, and budgets.

Step 10: Construction - Our team oversees all construction phases, from excavation to final inspections.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Coaldale

The cost of building a custom home in Coaldale varies based on numerous factors, including terrain challenges, design complexity, and material choices. Generally, you can expect to pay between $550-$700 per square foot for custom or higher-end builds. For example, a 2,500 sq ft home would typically cost between $1,375,000 and $1,750,000. Keep in mind that additional costs for land, cisterns, wells, and septic systems can add significantly to your overall budget.

For cisterns, the size typically follows the formula of (bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day). This ensures adequate water supply for your household needs. Additionally, factors such as rocky soil and steep access roads may necessitate more expensive foundation types and construction techniques. In the long run, investing in high-quality materials and energy-efficient designs can save you money on maintenance and utilities, making it a wise choice for your mountain home.
