Local Challenges in Fremont County

Coaldale sits at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, where the rocky granite soil presents unique challenges for new construction. The soil composition often includes decomposed granite and clay, making foundation engineering a critical step in the planning process. Furthermore, the area experiences extreme temperature swings, heavy snowfall, and afternoon thunderstorms, which can complicate site planning and construction timelines.

Accessing many properties can also be tricky, especially during winter months when steep driveways become challenging to navigate. Because of the deep water table, most homes will require a cistern or a well, impacting the overall timeline and costs. Understanding these local challenges allows us to create a tailored approach to your custom build, ensuring a smoother process from start to finish.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, step-by-step process to ensure your custom home meets your expectations and adheres to local regulations. The journey begins with a pre-purchase build feasibility report to assess site conditions, including soil quality, elevation analysis, and setback requirements. This critical step can help you avoid potential pitfalls before purchasing land.

Once your land is secured, we recommend applying for a well permit early; this is essential, as many areas have lengthy waiting periods for drilling. After securing your water source, we will create a professional site plan, outlining where your home will sit, how utilities will be routed, and ensuring compliance with local setback and zoning regulations.

The next phases involve developing architectural plans, which can either be created by your own architect or selected from our trusted network, including options from ArchitecturalDesigns.com. Following this, our team will collaborate with licensed engineers to develop stamped foundation and framing plans, ensuring that your home can withstand the challenges of mountain weather and soil conditions.

Once all plans are approved, we compile and submit for the necessary building permits through the Fremont County Building Department. After receiving the permit, we can start construction, which typically spans several months, depending on the complexity of your design and the current weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Coaldale

When considering a custom home build in Coaldale, it's important to understand the associated costs. Generally, the cost per square foot for mountain construction ranges from $350 to $1,000+, depending on the complexity and luxury of the home. For instance, a basic build may average around $450 per square foot, while a luxury home could reach $1,000+ per square foot.

For a 2,000 square foot home, you might expect total costs to fall between $700,000 and $1,100,000, not including the cost of land. Additionally, costs can escalate due to factors such as challenging soil conditions, which may require engineered septic systems, and the need for cisterns, where the total installation could range from $25,000 to $75,000. To size your cistern correctly, remember the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would need a tank capable of holding 6,000 gallons.

Moreover, consider that rocky terrain and limited access can lead to higher excavation and driveway construction costs, anywhere from $10,000 to $50,000. Given these variables, it‚Äôs wise to quote the high end of the price ranges to ensure you cover all potential expenses.
