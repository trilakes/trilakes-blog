In the breathtaking mountain community of Querida, Custer County, the unique landscape and challenging soil conditions make professional septic installation an absolute necessity for homeowners. Local Challenges in Custer County County

Installing a septic system in Querida involves navigating several local challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area often require engineered septic systems, as conventional systems are rarely suitable for mountain terrain. The depth of the water table can be problematic, often necessitating the use of cisterns or water hauling for many homes. Additionally, the frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, posing difficulties for installation during colder months and requiring careful winter preparation. Homeowners must also consider steep driveways and limited winter access, which can complicate installation logistics. With extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads during winter, it‚Äôs essential to ensure that your septic tank is designed for longevity and reliability.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our approach to septic installation is thorough and tailored to the unique needs of Querida properties. The first step is a comprehensive soil testing assessment. Unlike traditional perc tests, we conduct detailed soil evaluations by digging two test holes, each eight feet deep. The samples are sent to a licensed engineer, who provides a soils report that determines the type of system needed.

Once we have the soils report, we move forward with the design and permitting process. Homeowners must obtain a septic permit from the Custer County Building Department, which oversees all installations in the area. After securing the necessary permits, our team begins the excavation. Installation typically takes place from May to October, aligning with the best weather conditions.

We carefully install the chosen system, whether it‚Äôs a pressure-dosed, sand filter, or advanced treatment unit (ATU). After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before finalizing the project. This meticulous process ensures that your onsite wastewater treatment system is reliable, efficient, and built to last.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Querida

The costs associated with septic installation can vary significantly based on site conditions, but it‚Äôs essential to be prepared for the high-end of the pricing spectrum. For most properties in Querida, a basic engineered system will start at around $35,000, with options like sand filter systems ranging from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units costing between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

In addition to the cost of the system itself, homeowners should budget for a soils test and engineering report, which typically runs between $2,000 and $4,000. Permits can also add $500 to $2,000, depending on the requirements set by the Custer County Building Department. Excavation costs can range from $8,000 to over $20,000, especially in rocky terrain where additional site preparation may be needed.

It's also important to consider that the size of the cistern required is based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, if you have three bedrooms, you would need a cistern size of approximately 600 gallons (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). Given the unique challenges of Querida's mountain landscape, investing in an engineered system is not just practical, it's often required by Regulation 43, which governs septic systems in Colorado.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary widely, but for properties in Querida, you should expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000, depending on the type of system required and the specific site conditions.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation typically ranges from one to two weeks, depending on the complexity of the project, site conditions, and permit acquisition from the Custer County Building Department.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Querida, due to challenging soil conditions, will require an engineered septic system. This is crucial to ensure proper functionality and compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves deep soil sampling to assess the suitability of the soil for a septic system, which is crucial for proper design and installation.
