Local Challenges in Saguache County

Living in Saguache County comes with its own set of challenges, particularly when it comes to septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils found in the valleys often require engineered systems rather than conventional options. As most properties in this area necessitate specialized systems due to challenging soil conditions, soil testing is crucial. Furthermore, the deep water table means that many residents rely on cisterns and water hauling, complicating the installation process. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, significantly affecting the design and placement of your septic tank. Understanding these local insights is vital for anyone considering a septic installation in this mountainous terrain.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a systematic approach to septic installation to ensure that your system meets all local and state regulations. Our process begins with a thorough soil testing assessment, where we use a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze soil conditions. This information is sent to a licensed engineer who will create a septic design tailored to your property.

Once the design is approved, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Saguache County Building Department. After securing the permit, we schedule the installation, which typically takes about a week, depending on site conditions such as access and terrain. Our team handles all aspects of the installation, from excavation to system setup and final inspection, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43. This comprehensive approach minimizes disruptions and guarantees a functional and efficient onsite wastewater system for years to come.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on various factors such as soil conditions and system type. Most installations require engineered systems due to the challenging mountain terrains, which can also influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes about one week, contingent upon site conditions and access to the property. The process begins with soil testing and obtaining the necessary permits, which can add time to the overall timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Colorado, especially in mountainous regions like Center, require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are designed to handle the unique requirements of each site and ensure compliance with Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil conditions, which are then assessed by a licensed engineer to determine the appropriate septic system type for your property.
