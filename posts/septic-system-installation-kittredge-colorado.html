Local Challenges in Jefferson County County

The picturesque environment of Kittredge comes with its own set of challenges when it comes to septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in Jefferson County complicate the installation of conventional systems, often necessitating engineered septic solutions. Additionally, the water table is frequently deep, which means many properties require cisterns for water hauling. With a frost line that can reach 36-48 inches, properly winterizing your septic system is critical. Extreme temperature fluctuations and heavy snow loads introduce further complexities, especially in forested areas where fire mitigation is key. Access is another concern, as many properties feature steep driveways that limit access, particularly during winter months.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a systematic approach to septic installation to ensure your system is tailored to your specific needs. Our process begins with comprehensive soil testing to determine the best septic design suitable for your property. This involves bringing a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples at various depths, which are then analyzed by a licensed engineer. Based on the soils report, we proceed to apply for the necessary septic permit through the Jefferson County Building Department.

Once the permit is secured, we move on to the installation phase. Depending on the complexity of the site, this can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. We will excavate the area, install the septic tank and leach field, and connect all necessary components. Our experienced team ensures that every step adheres to the highest standards and regulations, including those outlined in Regulation 43. After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to verify that everything is functioning properly. This attention to detail guarantees that your onsite wastewater system will serve you efficiently for years to come.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Kittredge

When considering septic system installation in Kittredge, it is crucial to understand the various costs involved. The total installed cost for engineered systems typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the installation. For instance, basic engineered systems can start at around $35,000, while more sophisticated sand filter systems may range from $45,000 to $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) can escalate the costs to between $55,000 and $80,000+, particularly if rocky terrain or difficult access is involved.

In addition to the installation costs, homeowners should factor in the price of soil testing and engineering, which typically costs between $2,000 and $4,000. Permits can vary widely, generally ranging from $500 to $2,000. Excavation costs may add another $8,000 to $20,000+ to your project. If you require a cistern, the size will depend on the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated as 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day.

Always remember that while initial costs may seem high, investing in a high-quality engineered septic system can save you from costly repairs and replacements down the road.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on the type of system and site conditions. Engineered systems are the most common due to challenging soils, with costs reflecting the complexity of the installation.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of septic installation can vary based on site conditions, but it generally takes anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. Factors like permitting, weather, and access to the site can influence the timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, properties in Kittredge will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions outlined in Colorado‚Äôs Regulation 43. A soil test will determine the specific type of system needed for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not conduct traditional perc tests. Instead, we perform soil testing by digging test holes to analyze soil composition and determine the appropriate septic system design. This is crucial for ensuring the system's effectiveness in the area's unique conditions.
