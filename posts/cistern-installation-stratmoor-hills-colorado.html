Local Challenges in El Paso County

Stratmoor Hills, part of El Paso County, is characterized by its rocky granite and clay soil, which complicates excavation for cistern installation. The elevation of over 8,000 feet means that the frost line reaches 36-48 inches deep, necessitating substantial consideration during installation to prevent freeze-related issues. With a deeper water table, many homeowners find that an underground cistern is the most viable option for a reliable rural water supply.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Stratmoor Hills

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation in Stratmoor Hills is crucial for homeowners planning for a cistern installation. The price can vary based on several factors, including the chosen tank size and the specifics of your property‚Äôs terrain. Here‚Äôs a quick breakdown:

To estimate your tank size, consider your daily water usage, which averages about 100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an assumed occupancy of six people would need about 600 gallons per day. If you want to refill every two months, a minimum tank size of 36,000 gallons would be required. For winter convenience, many homeowners opt for 72,000+ gallons, which can involve multiple tanks.

For Norwesco tanks, pricing typically starts at:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

Total installed costs can range from:

  • 5,000-gallon basic system: $25,000-$35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: $40,000-$55,000
  • Large-capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): $75,000-$120,000+

Additional costs include excavation ($3,000-$8,000+ depending on rock/access), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot). Remember, rocky terrain can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Always plan for the higher end of these price ranges due to the unpredictable nature of installation in Colorado‚Äôs mountainous regions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation specifics. For a standard 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system typically runs from $40,000 to $55,000. Additional site-specific factors, such as rocky terrain, can increase costs significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, in a 3-bedroom home with six residents, you would require a minimum of 36,000 gallons if refilling every two months, or even larger for convenience during winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several weeks to complete, depending on factors like site conditions and weather. The best time for installation is usually between May and October, allowing for optimal ground conditions and access.

Q: Cistern vs. well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific property conditions. Cisterns are ideal for properties where well drilling is impractical or where the water table is too deep. They serve as a reliable alternative, especially for off-grid or remote locations with limited access to water.
