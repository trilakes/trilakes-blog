Why Stratmoor Hills Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Stratmoor Hills, perched at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners in need of reliable water solutions. With the fluctuating weather conditions and rocky terrain, proper cistern installation is crucial. Many properties in this mountainous region do not have access to a well, making underground water storage tanks an essential option for storing potable water. DIY methods may seem tempting, but professional installation ensures that your water cistern is tailored to meet local codes and withstand the harsh conditions of Colorado mountain living.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

The soil composition in Stratmoor Hills varies significantly, ranging from rocky granite to clay in the valley areas. This variability often makes accessing a reliable water table complicated, leading many homeowners to rely on cistern installation and water hauling for their needs. The deep frost line, reaching 36 to 48 inches, further complicates the installation process, especially during winter months when the ground is frozen. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can create logistical challenges for installations. With heavy snow loads and extreme weather swings, homeowners must ensure their water storage solutions are robust and well-constructed to handle these conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 for larger systems. Factors such as excavation, site conditions, and local regulations can increase these costs significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. As a rule of thumb, assume 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires at least a 10,000-gallon tank for adequate supply.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes between one to three weeks, contingent upon site conditions and permitting processes. Our team ensures that every step is carried out efficiently while adhering to local regulations.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on individual property conditions. Cisterns are a viable option where well water is too deep or unreliable. They also provide a reliable backup water supply and can be tailored for winter usage, making them an excellent choice for many mountain properties.
