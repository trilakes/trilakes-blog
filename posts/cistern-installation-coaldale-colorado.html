Local Challenges in Fremont County

Fremont County‚Äôs unique geography poses several challenges for homeowners considering a cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil found throughout the region can complicate excavation and increase costs, as it often demands specialized equipment and techniques. Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a streamlined process for water storage tank installation that begins with an in-depth consultation. Our team will assess your property, discuss your water needs based on daily usage, and determine the best tank size for your household. Our sizing formula accounts for the number of bedrooms in your home, estimating 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with up to six occupants would require a system capable of storing approximately 18,000 gallons if you prefer a monthly refill schedule.

Once we establish the right system size, we handle the necessary permits through the Fremont County Building Department. From there, our skilled team will excavate the site, install your underground water storage tank, and set up any required pump systems. Depending on site conditions and access challenges, the entire installation process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks.

Throughout the project, we prioritize communication and transparency, ensuring you are informed every step of the way. We also provide guidance on winter preparation and winterizing cistern installation to protect your investment from freezing temperatures.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Coaldale

The cost of cistern installation in Coaldale can vary significantly based on several factors. For instance, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically starts at around $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000-$28,000. However, the total installed cost, which includes excavation, pump systems, trenching, and electrical hookups, brings your final investment to approximately $25,000-$35,000 for a basic system and up to $55,000 for larger setups.

Site conditions can dramatically affect these costs. If your property has rocky terrain or limited access, expect an additional 20-40% increase in excavation expenses. As mentioned, the cistern sizing formula is based on the number of bedrooms, with 2 people per bedroom using approximately 100 gallons each day. For a 3-bedroom home, this translates to needing a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or even larger systems if you want to minimize winter refills.

Always remember that investing in a larger tank can lead to long-term savings on water hauling costs. The upfront investment will often pay off in fewer deliveries and reduced hassle during the winter months.
