Why Crestone Properties Need Professional Excavation Services

Crestone, nestled at over 8,000 feet in elevation, is a stunning mountain community that requires specialized excavation services tailored for its challenging terrain. As an experienced excavation contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands that proper site preparation is crucial for building foundations, driveways, and utilities in this unique environment. Attempting DIY excavation can lead to costly mistakes, particularly with the rocky granite soil and potential frost issues. Hiring a licensed and insured general contractor like Tri-Lakes ensures that your excavation project is handled professionally, mitigating risks associated with construction at high altitudes.

Understanding the costs associated with excavation services in Crestone can help you budget effectively for your project. For basic site preparation and clearing, expect to invest between $8,000 and $20,000, with specific activities like heavy brush or tree removal costing between $5,000 and $15,000 per acre. If your project involves foundation excavation for a typical 1,500 sq ft basement, you should budget around $15,000 to $30,000, with additional costs for rocky conditions potentially increasing expenses by 50-200%.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs can vary widely based on the site conditions and type of excavation required. On average, you can expect to pay between $50 to $150 per cubic yard for standard excavation, but costs can increase significantly in rocky areas, often reaching up to 200% more.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation depends on the project's complexity and size. Typically, basic site prep can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, especially if extensive land clearing or grading is required. Factors such as weather conditions and access challenges also play a significant role.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Mountain excavation requires specialized equipment to handle the rocky terrain and steep slopes. Commonly used machinery includes excavators, bobcats for light work, backhoes for digging, and dump trucks for hauling materials. Each project may require a unique combination of these tools to ensure efficiency and safety.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, all excavation projects in Crestone require permits from the Saguache County Building Department. This ensures that the work complies with local regulations and safety standards. Tri-Lakes Contracting can assist you with the permit application process, making it easier for you to focus on your project.
