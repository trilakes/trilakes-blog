Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure every aspect meets local and environmental standards:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a detailed discussion about your property, including soil conditions, water availability, and specific needs.
  2. Soil Testing: Our team performs soil tests, digging two test holes and taking samples to determine the appropriate system. This is crucial in mountainous areas where soil conditions vary significantly.
  3. Septic Design: Based on the soil report, we design an engineered septic system tailored to your property. This design adheres to Regulation 43 standards.
  4. Permitting: We assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with all local regulations.
  5. Installation: Our experienced team carries out the installation, carefully managing excavation and placement of the septic tank and leach field.
  6. Final Inspection: After installation, we coordinate with local inspectors to ensure everything meets safety and functionality standards.
  7. Maintenance Tips: We provide guidance on maintaining your septic system to prolong its lifespan and functionality.

This thorough approach ensures that your septic installation is completed efficiently and effectively, minimizing potential issues down the line.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Ramah

When considering the costs associated with septic installation in Ramah, several factors influence the final price. Costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, requiring a tailored quote. For a basic engineered system, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can escalate to between $55,000 and $80,000, especially for difficult sites with rocky terrain.

Additionally, soil testing and engineering services typically run between $2,000 and $4,000, with permits costing between $500 and $2,000. Excavation costs can also vary, usually falling between $8,000 and $20,000, depending on the complexity of the terrain and access to the site. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary widely based on site conditions. For basic engineered systems, expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000, while advanced systems like sand filters may range from $45,000 to $65,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation typically ranges from one to three weeks, depending on the complexity of the site and the permitting process with the El Paso County Building Department.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains require engineered septic systems due to challenging soils and regulations set by Regulation 43. A professional assessment will determine the best type for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil types, which are then assessed to determine the appropriate septic system design.
