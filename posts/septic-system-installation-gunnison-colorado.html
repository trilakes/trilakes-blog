Why Gunnison Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Gunnison, nestled at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for septic installation. The rugged mountain terrain and unpredictable weather can dramatically affect the performance of your septic system. Installing a septic system is not merely a DIY task; it requires an understanding of local regulations, soil types, and the specific needs of your property to ensure effective onsite wastewater treatment (OWTS).

Choosing a licensed and insured septic contractor is crucial to navigate the complexities of the installation process. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we have the expertise to design systems tailored for the local conditions, ensuring longevity and compliance with Gunnison County permit requirements.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County

The unique characteristics of Gunnison County's terrain make septic installation a complex endeavor. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area often necessitate engineered systems, which can be more costly but essential for proper function. With the water table frequently being deep, many properties require cisterns for water supply, making it vital to understand the layout of your land.

Additionally, the frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, especially at higher elevations, which can have a direct impact on the installation depth of your septic tank. Extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, further complicate installation schedules, typically limiting the building season from May to October. Access to many properties can also be challenging, especially during winter months, necessitating planning and expertise from a local septic installer near me.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and designed to meet the unique needs of your property. It begins with a comprehensive soil testing procedure, where we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze the soil composition. This step is crucial for determining the most suitable type of system, whether it be a conventional gravity system or an engineered system like a pressure-dosed system or sand filter system.

After obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Gunnison County Building Department, we move forward with the design phase, creating a tailored plan based on the soils report. Once the design is approved, we prepare the site, ensuring that any access issues are addressed. Installation typically takes 1-3 weeks, depending on the complexity of the site and weather conditions. Finally, an inspection ensures that the system meets all local regulations and functions effectively.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Gunnison

The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on numerous factors. For properties in Gunnison, the average total installed cost for a basic engineered system typically starts from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, depending on site conditions. If your property requires a sand filter system, expect costs to range from $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) may reach $55,000 to $80,000 or more.

When planning for a cistern, remember to calculate the size based on your bedroom count. For example, if you have three bedrooms, the size should accommodate six people, resulting in a minimum of 600 gallons per day (3 bedrooms x 2 people x 100 gallons/day). This adds to the overall costs, especially if rocky or difficult terrain is encountered, which can increase excavation costs by $10,000 to $25,000. Additionally, all installations must consider permits, which generally range from $500 to $2,000, and soil testing and engineering, which can add another $2,000 to $4,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000 or more, depending on the type of system required and specific site conditions such as soil type and access challenges.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process for a septic system generally takes between 1 to 3 weeks, depending on factors such as site complexity, weather conditions, and necessary permits.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Gunnison require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations. A soils report conducted by a licensed firm will determine the system type needed for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests, not traditional perc tests, to assess the suitability of your land for a septic system. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities.
