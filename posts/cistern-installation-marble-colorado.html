Why Marble Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

If you own a property in Marble, Colorado, you understand that reliable access to water is crucial, especially at elevations above 8,000 feet. Many homes in this mountainous area lack direct access to wells, making cistern installation the best solution for your water needs. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that your home has a consistent supply of potable water storage, whether for daily living, irrigation, or fire suppression. Opting for professional installation not only guarantees adherence to local building codes but also minimizes the risks associated with improper installations, which can lead to costly repairs down the line.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Marble's unique topography presents a set of challenges for those considering cistern installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soils can complicate excavation work, sometimes increasing costs by 20-40%. Additionally, the water table can be quite deep, which often necessitates the use of an underground cistern or water hauling services for adequate supply. With a frost line of 36-48 inches, ensuring your buried water tank is winterized and protected from freezing is paramount, especially during the harsh Colorado winters. Navigating steep driveways and limited winter access can also complicate ongoing maintenance and emergency services, making it essential to establish a robust off-grid water system tailored to these conditions.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for water storage tank installation to ensure a seamless experience for our clients in Marble:

  • Initial Consultation: We assess your property, discuss your water needs, and recommend the appropriate tank size based on your daily usage and refill preferences.
  • Permitting: All installations must comply with Gunnison County regulations. We handle the permit applications through the local building department to ensure all legal requirements are met.
  • Site Preparation: Our team will prepare the site by clearing any obstructions and marking the location for excavation. This step is crucial in rocky terrain.
  • Excavation: Using specialized equipment, we excavate the area for the cistern, taking into account the soil type and depth of the frost line.
  • Tank Installation: We install high-quality Norwesco tanks, ensuring proper connections and access points. This includes the pump system, pressure tank, and any necessary filtration systems.
  • Backfilling: After the tank is securely in place, we backfill the excavation site, ensuring that the tank is well-protected from external elements.
  • Final Inspection: We conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly and complies with all regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The costs for cistern installation in Colorado can vary, but you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $55,000 for a complete installed system, depending on tank size and site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your potable water storage tank is determined by your home's bedroom count and daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home (6 people) would need a minimum of 36,000-54,000 gallons for quarterly refills or 72,000+ gallons to get through the winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process generally takes 2-3 weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting requirements. Factors like rocky terrain and local regulations can influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on the specific conditions of your property. Cisterns are ideal for areas with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, making them a reliable alternative when wells are impractical or unreliable.
