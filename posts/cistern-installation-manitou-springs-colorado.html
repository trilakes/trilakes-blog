Local Challenges in El Paso County

Manitou Springs is characterized by its stunning granite cliffs, steep driveways, and variable soil conditions, which can complicate cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil in this mountainous region often mean that traditional wells are either impractical or require extensive drilling. As a result, many residents must turn to water hauling or install an underground cistern to secure a reliable water source.

At elevations over 8,000 feet, the water table can be quite deep, making it necessary for homeowners to consider substantial potable water storage systems. Additionally, the frost line in this area ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months. The weather also brings challenges, with heavy snow loads and extreme temperature swings that can affect both access to properties and the integrity of your water systems. Fire mitigation is another critical concern in forested areas, making it essential to have adequate fire suppression water availability as part of your overall water management strategy.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a streamlined cistern installation process designed specifically for the unique characteristics of Manitou Springs:

  • Initial Consultation: We begin with a thorough assessment of your property and discuss your specific water needs, including daily usage and refill preferences.
  • Site Evaluation: Our team evaluates the soil conditions, elevation, and access points to determine the best location for your buried water tank.
  • Permitting: We handle all necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  • Excavation: Our experienced team performs excavation, taking into account any rocky terrain that may complicate the process.
  • Installation: We install the potable water storage tank, typically a Norwesco tank, and implement all necessary plumbing and electrical systems.
  • Testing: Once installed, we conduct thorough testing of the system to ensure optimal performance and reliability.
  • Winterization: We provide recommendations for winterizing your cistern system to protect against freezing temperatures.

The entire installation process can take several weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system. We strive to complete installations before the onset of winter, ensuring you have a reliable water supply during the cold months.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Manitou Springs

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation can help you budget effectively. The size of your underground water storage tank will significantly depend on your daily water usage, which is typically calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example:

  • A 2-bedroom home with 4 occupants needs approximately 400 gallons per day.
  • A 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants requires about 600 gallons per day.
  • A 4-bedroom home with 8 occupants would need around 800 gallons per day.

Based on refill frequency, the size of the tank can vary significantly. If you prefer less frequent refills during winter, you might need a larger capacity:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: Minimum of 8,400 gallons.
  • Refill monthly: Minimum of 18,000 gallons.
  • Refill every 2-3 months: At least 36,000-54,000 gallons.
  • Winter survival system: 72,000+ gallons (multiple tanks).

Typical costs for water storage tank installation include:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Excavation: $3,000-$8,000+
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to house: $25-$50 per linear foot
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000-$2,500

Given the rocky terrain and potential challenges in access, costs can increase 20-40%. Therefore, it's essential to budget for a total installed system, which may range from $25,000 to $120,000+ depending on your specific needs and site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can range from $25,000 to $120,000 or more, depending on the tank size and site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon system typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can exceed $75,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on daily usage and how often you want to refill. A general rule is to calculate 100 gallons per day per person; for example, a 3-bedroom home may need a tank that holds at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern can take several weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting processes. We aim to complete installations swiftly to ensure homeowners have reliable water access, especially before winter.
