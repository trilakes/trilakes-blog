Why Vail Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Vail, Colorado, where properties often sit at elevations of over 8,000 feet, reliable water access is essential. Many homes in this region lack direct access to wells, making cistern installation a practical solution for storing potable water. A professionally installed underground water storage tank can provide homeowners with not only a reliable water source for everyday needs but also for fire suppression and irrigation purposes. The complexities and challenges unique to high-altitude construction, including rocky terrain and extreme weather conditions, necessitate the expertise of professional cistern installers like Tri-Lakes Contracting. Our licensed and insured team ensures that your water cistern is properly sized, expertly installed, and built to withstand the local environment, giving you peace of mind for years to come.

Local Challenges in Eagle County County

Eagle County's mountainous terrain presents unique challenges for cistern installation. The soil composition, often consisting of rocky granite or decomposed granite, can complicate excavation, driving up costs and installation time. Additionally, the water table in many areas is typically deep, necessitating the use of water hauling services for properties lacking a viable well. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, proper winterization is crucial for any underground cistern to prevent freezing during harsh winter months. Furthermore, the steep access roads common in Vail can pose logistical challenges in both summer and winter, making it imperative to hire a qualified cistern company that understands the local landscape and regulatory requirements. All installations must adhere to the guidelines set forth by the Eagle County Building Department, ensuring compliance and safety.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for water storage tank installation that ensures efficiency and quality:

  1. Consultation and Assessment: We start with a thorough evaluation of your property, including a discussion of your water needs and potential challenges based on your location.
  2. Permitting: Before any work begins, we handle all necessary permits through the Eagle County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  3. Excavation: Our team will prepare the site, excavating the necessary area for your buried water tank. This can range from $3,000 to $8,000, depending on site conditions.
  4. Tank Installation: We typically install high-quality Norwesco tanks, which come in various sizes to meet your specific needs. The installation process includes setting the tank and connecting it to your home.
  5. System Integration: We will install the necessary pump systems, pressure tanks, and any additional components, such as electrical hookups and water filtration systems.
  6. Final Inspection: Once the installation is complete, we conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before handing the system over to you.
  7. Maintenance and Support: We offer ongoing support and maintenance services to ensure your water cistern continues to operate efficiently.

The entire process can typically take several weeks, depending on factors such as weather conditions, site accessibility, and tank size.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Vail

When considering cistern installation in Vail, it's crucial to understand the various cost factors involved. The size of your water storage tank is primarily determined by your household's water usage, which is roughly estimated at 100 gallons per person per day, with two people per bedroom assumed. For example:

  • A 2-bedroom home (4 people) = 400 gallons/day.
  • A 3-bedroom home (6 people) = 600 gallons/day.
  • A 4-bedroom home (8 people) = 800 gallons/day.

Based on refill preferences, homeowners often opt for larger systems. For a 3-bedroom example, if you prefer to refill every two months, you would need a minimum of 36,000 gallons. The installation costs for a basic 5,000-gallon system start from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system begins around $40,000 to $55,000. Additional costs may include excavation (up to $8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching to the house, and electrical hookups, leading to total installed costs ranging from $75,000 to $120,000 for larger systems.

Remember, factors like rocky terrain and limited access can significantly increase these costs by 20-40%. Thus, it's vital to quote the high end of these ranges to account for any unexpected expenses that may arise during the installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly depending on size and installation complexity. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system starts at around $40,000 and can go up to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage. A good rule of thumb is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeframe for cistern installation can range from a few weeks to several months, depending on factors such as weather, site accessibility, and the size of the cistern. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting keeps you informed throughout the process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are a reliable alternative for properties where drilling a well is impractical due to deep water tables or rocky terrain, making them an excellent choice for many Vail homeowners.
