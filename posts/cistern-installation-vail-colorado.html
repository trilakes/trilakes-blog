The geographical conditions of Vail present several unique challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, often increasing costs significantly. With a frost line that ranges from 36 to 48 inches, proper burial depth becomes crucial to avoid freeze damage during the harsh Colorado winters. Factors like extreme temperature fluctuations, heavy snow loads, and afternoon thunderstorms further complicate the logistics of water storage tank installation, requiring expertise that only a local cistern company can provide.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to be efficient and thorough. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Vail

The cost of cistern installation in Vail can vary significantly based on several factors. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we typically quote starting prices from the higher end of the range to account for potential complications. For instance, a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank alone can cost between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank may range from $22,000 to $28,000. Additional installation costs can include excavation expenses, which can run from $3,000 to $8,000 or more depending on soil conditions and accessibility. Furthermore, the total installed system cost‚Äîfactoring in all necessary components‚Äîcan range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic setup, escalating to between $40,000 and $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.

To size your cistern correctly, it‚Äôs crucial to calculate your daily water usage. A simple formula is to take the number of bedrooms in your home, multiply by two people per bedroom, then multiply by 100 gallons per day. This helps determine how much water you‚Äôll need and how often you‚Äôll require refills, which significantly affects the overall cost. Properties with rocky terrain or limited access should expect additional costs due to increased excavation time and difficulty, making a larger upfront investment often more economical in the long run.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. Factors like terrain difficulty and excavation requirements can significantly influence these prices.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your household size and water usage. A common formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms in your home by two people per bedroom, and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site complexity, but typically it can take anywhere from several days to weeks. Factors such as excavation challenges, weather conditions, and local permitting processes can influence the duration.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water supply situation. A cistern offers a reliable solution for homes with deep water tables or challenging drilling conditions, while a well can provide a continuous supply if conditions are favorable. For many mountain properties, a cistern is often the more practical choice.
