Local Challenges in Fremont County

Fremont County presents several unique challenges when it comes to cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area can complicate excavation and increase costs significantly, often by 20-40%. Additionally, the frost line sits at 36-48 inches, necessitating deeper burial for your potable water storage tank to prevent freezing during the harsh winters. With steep driveways and limited winter access, many properties face logistical hurdles that can affect the installation timeline and costs. Understanding these local challenges is crucial to designing an effective water storage system that meets your needs year-round, especially for those relying on a backup water supply or agricultural water storage.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Canon City

The cost of water storage tank installation can vary significantly based on several factors. For instance, the size of the cistern is determined primarily by the number of bedrooms in your home and your water usage needs. As a general rule, you can calculate your daily water needs by multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 (occupants) and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilling monthly, or 36,000-54,000 gallons for every 2-3 months.

Here are some typical pricing ranges you can expect for cistern installation:

  • 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank: starting from $13,000-$15,000.
  • 10,000-gallon tank: starting from $22,000-$28,000.
  • Full installation costs, including excavation and plumbing, range from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.

Keep in mind that rocky terrain can significantly increase excavation costs, and remote access may add to your overall expenses. It's always best to budget for the higher end of the projected costs to avoid surprises when installing your buried water tank.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely depending on size and installation conditions. A basic 5,000-gallon system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, including excavation and plumbing.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage and refill preferences. A general rule is to multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern typically spans 2-4 weeks. This includes site assessment, obtaining necessary permits, excavation, and installation, which can be affected by weather conditions and site access.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Whether a cistern or well is better depends on several factors, including your property's water table and soil conditions. For many Canon City properties, cisterns offer a reliable alternative to wells, especially when wells are too deep or impractical due to rocky terrain.
