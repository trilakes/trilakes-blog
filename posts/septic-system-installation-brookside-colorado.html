Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Brookside

The cost of septic installation in Brookside varies significantly based on site conditions and system type. On average, basic engineered systems start at around $35,000 and can go as high as $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, expect to pay between $45,000 and $65,000, while more advanced systems like the ATU can range from $55,000 to $80,000. Understanding the pricing breakdown is essential. Before installation, homeowners can anticipate costs for soil testing and engineering, which typically range from $2,000 to $4,000. Permitting fees in Fremont County can vary from $500 to $2,000, depending on specific requirements. Excavation costs can also fluctuate, generally falling between $8,000 and $20,000, while the tank and components will add another $5,000 to $15,000. For properties needing cisterns, the size is based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day), influencing overall costs. It's crucial to note that challenging terrain and rocky conditions can significantly increase costs by $10,000 to $25,000. Therefore, it's wise to plan for the high end of the ranges to avoid any budget surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions and the type of system required. For most mountain properties, basic engineered systems typically start around $35,000 and can reach $50,000, while advanced systems like ATUs can exceed $80,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary based on several factors, including site preparation and permitting. Generally, once permits are secured, installation can take one to two weeks, depending on site conditions and system complexity.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Brookside area require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions and elevation. Regulation 43 mandates this for proper septic installation to ensure environmental protection and system longevity.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed; instead, soil testing is conducted to assess soil suitability for septic systems. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the best system for your property.
