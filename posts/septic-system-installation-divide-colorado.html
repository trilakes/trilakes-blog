Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and efficient septic installation process tailored to the unique needs of Divide homeowners. The first step is to conduct a comprehensive soil testing and engineering analysis. This involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, and collecting soil samples for a licensed engineer's review. The results will guide the design of your septic system, ensuring it meets local regulations and site conditions.

Once we have the soils report, we can apply for the necessary permits through the Teller County Building Department. This step is crucial, as all septic permits must comply with local regulations. After obtaining the permits, we begin the excavation process, which includes preparing the site for the septic tank and any additional components such as leach fields or drain fields.

The entire installation process can take anywhere from one to three weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting timelines. Our team is committed to keeping you informed throughout the process, ensuring a smooth and efficient installation tailored to your home‚Äôs needs.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Divide

Understanding the cost of septic installation in Divide is essential for budgeting your project effectively. The total cost of installing a typical engineered system ranges from approximately $35,000 to $50,000. Factors influencing the price include the complexity of the site, soil conditions, and the specific system design required. For example, a sand filter system may cost between $45,000 and $65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) can range from $55,000 to $80,000+, depending on specific site nuances.

In addition to installation costs, you should factor in the costs of a soils test and engineering, which typically ranges from $2,000 to $4,000, as well as permit fees that can vary from $500 to $2,000. If your property has rocky terrain or steep access, be prepared for increased excavation costs that can add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to your total. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely, but typical ranges start from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system. More advanced systems, like sand filters or ATUs, can cost from $45,000 to $80,000+, depending on site conditions and requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system generally takes between one to three weeks, depending on the complexity of your site and the permitting process required by the Teller County Building Department.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Divide require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soils and regulations set forth by Colorado's Regulation 43. A soils test will help determine the type of system that is necessary for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design.
