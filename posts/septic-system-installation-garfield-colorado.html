Local Challenges in Chaffee County County

Garfield, located within Chaffee County, is characterized by its stunning but challenging terrain. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate septic installation, necessitating engineered systems rather than conventional ones. Many properties require thorough soil testing to ensure proper drainage, especially given the often deep water tables in this region. Properties here can also present access challenges with steep driveways, limiting winter access while increasing costs for septic installation. Additionally, considering the extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads, proper planning for winterization is essential. This includes ensuring your septic system is equipped for freeze protection.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is designed to efficiently address the unique needs of your property while ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Step 1: Soil Testing - Before any installation, we conduct thorough soil testing using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. The samples are analyzed to determine the most suitable system for your property.

Step 2: Design and Permitting - Based on the soil report, our team will design a system that meets Regulation 43 standards. We will assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permits through the Chaffee County Building Department.

Step 3: Installation - Our skilled team will carefully excavate the site, install the septic tank and associated components, and ensure optimal placement of the drain field. We handle everything from excavation to site restoration.

Step 4: Inspection and Maintenance - After installation, we will help schedule a septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning properly and compliant with local regulations. We also provide guidance on ongoing maintenance to extend the life of your system.

The total timeline for septic system installation typically ranges from a few days to several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather constraints.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Garfield

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation is vital for homeowners in Garfield. Several factors can influence the overall price, especially due to the unique terrain and regulations in Chaffee County.

On average, you can expect to invest in the following:

  • Soils test and engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000, depending on county requirements
  • Excavation costs: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Septic tank and components: $5,000-$15,000
  • ATU unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical and controls: $2,000-$4,000

The total costs for an engineered system typically start at $35,000-$50,000, while sand filter systems can range from $45,000-$65,000, and ATU systems may reach $55,000-$80,000+. Difficult sites with rocky terrain can add an additional $10,000-$25,000 to your overall costs. It's essential to remember that these estimates can vary based on specific site conditions.

For properties requiring a cistern, sizing is determined by counting bedrooms: two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day, ensuring you have adequate water supply for your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for engineered systems and can range up to $80,000 for more complex systems like Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs). Factors such as site conditions, permit fees, and the specific type of system required can significantly impact the final cost.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a septic system can vary from a few days to several weeks based on permits, weather, and site conditions. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting prioritizes efficiency while ensuring compliance with all local regulations.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. A professional soils report will determine the specific requirements for your site, ensuring your system meets local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This process involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps determine the most suitable septic system design for your property.
