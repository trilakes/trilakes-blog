Local Challenges in Eagle County

The picturesque landscape of Gypsum presents several challenges when it comes to installing a septic tank. The rocky granite and clay soils found in the area often require engineered systems to ensure proper functionality. Most properties here experience a deep water table, making traditional systems less viable and often necessitating additional features like cisterns for water storage.

The frost line here ranges from 36 to 48 inches, meaning your septic installation must be prepared for extreme winter conditions. Heavy snowfall and sudden afternoon thunderstorms can also hinder access to many properties, complicating installation schedules. Furthermore, Eagle County‚Äôs strict building regulations require all septic permits to go through the local Building Department, adding another layer of complexity to the installation process.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic system installations to ensure compliance with all local regulations and the specific needs of your property. The first step involves comprehensive soil testing, where we dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, to evaluate the soil composition. The samples collected are sent to a licensed engineer who will create a septic design tailored to your unique site conditions.

Once we secure the required septic permit from Eagle County, our team begins the installation process, typically during the prime building season from May to October. We will excavate the site, install the septic tank and components, and create the necessary leach field or drain field based on the engineered plans. Throughout the installation, we focus on minimizing disruption to your property while ensuring the system is installed correctly and efficiently.

Following installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to confirm everything is functioning as intended. We also provide guidance on winterizing your septic system installation to prevent freezing and ensure long-term performance.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Gypsum

The cost of septic installation in Gypsum can vary significantly based on site conditions and the specific system type required. For most properties in this region, an engineered system is mandatory due to challenging soil conditions and depth to the water table. Expect the total installed cost for a basic engineered system to start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. If you require a sand filter system, costs typically range from $45,000 to $65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) may cost between $55,000 and $80,000.

It‚Äôs important to factor in additional costs such as the soil testing and engineering fees, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, plus permit fees, which vary from $500 to $2,000 based on the county's regulations. The excavation work, which can heavily depend on the presence of rock and the steepness of your property, might cost between $8,000 and $20,000. If you're considering a cistern, remember that the size should be determined based on the number of bedrooms; typically, this is calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

Always plan for the high end of the price ranges when budgeting for your septic installation to avoid surprises down the line.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary widely, but for most engineered systems, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000 depending on site conditions, the need for special systems, and excavation challenges.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system typically takes between one to two weeks, depending on factors such as the complexity of the system, permitting times, and site conditions, including terrain and access.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Gypsum require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and depth to the water table. This ensures proper functionality and compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves analyzing soil samples to determine the appropriate septic system design needed for your property, ensuring a tailored approach to your septic installation.
