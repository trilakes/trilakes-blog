Why Silverthorne Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Silverthorne, located at an impressive elevation of 8,790 feet, is a stunning gateway to the Gore Range, offering breathtaking views and outdoor adventure. However, building in this beautiful environment comes with its own unique challenges, particularly when it comes to installing a septic system. Proper septic installation is crucial for protecting the local ecosystem and ensuring that your property meets all state regulations. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the complexities of onsite wastewater systems in Colorado, especially in high-elevation areas like Summit County. Relying on professionals ensures your septic system is installed correctly, which can save you time, money, and frustration in the long run.

Local Challenges in Summit County

Silverthorne presents specific challenges that require expertise in septic system installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil often complicate traditional septic installation methods, necessitating engineered systems that can handle poor drainage and varying soil types. Furthermore, the area's deep water table means many properties rely on cisterns or water hauling solutions. Another consideration is the frost line, which can reach depths of 36-48 inches, making winterizing your septic system essential to avoid costly freeze damage. Additionally, access to properties can be limited, especially during heavy snowfall, making the expertise of a local septic installer near me invaluable for navigating these unique challenges.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic system installation to ensure compliance with Colorado's stringent regulations outlined in Regulation 43 (5 CCR 1002-43). The first step involves conducting comprehensive soil testing. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to assess the soil's characteristics. This testing is fundamental not only for determining the appropriate septic design but is also a smart step for potential property buyers to undertake before closing on land.

Once we obtain the necessary soils report from a licensed engineer, we can recommend the best system for your property. The installation typically occurs between May and October, considering the building season in higher elevations. After securing a septic permit through the Summit County Building Department, our team will begin excavation and installation. Throughout the process, we keep you informed, ensuring you understand each step, from the installation of the septic tank to the establishment of the drain field or leach field, all tailored to the specific needs of your property.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Silverthorne

Installing a septic system in Silverthorne involves various cost factors that can significantly influence the total price. On average, a basic engineered system will start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, while more complex systems, such as sand filter systems, range from $45,000 to $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) offer heightened treatment capabilities, typically costing between $55,000 and $80,000, especially for properties with challenging terrain.

To determine the necessary tank size for cisterns, use the formula: (bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day). This calculation helps ensure your septic system meets your household's water usage needs. Additional costs may arise due to rocky terrain or difficult access, potentially adding $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall expenses. Always keep in mind that prices can vary based on site conditions, making it crucial to consult with an experienced septic contractor for an accurate estimate tailored to your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: Septic installation costs in Colorado typically start from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000 for more advanced systems, such as ATUs. The final cost depends on factors like soil condition, site accessibility, and necessary permits.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of septic installation can vary but typically ranges from 1 to 3 weeks. Factors influencing the timeline include weather conditions, permitting processes, and the complexity of the installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, properties in Summit County require engineered systems due to the challenging soil conditions and strict regulations outlined in Regulation 43. This ensures proper wastewater treatment and protects the environment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design needed for your property.
