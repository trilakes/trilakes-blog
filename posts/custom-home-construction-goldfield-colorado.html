Local Challenges in Teller County

Goldfield's unique landscape offers breathtaking beauty, but it also presents significant challenges for construction. The soil types, including rocky granite and clay in valleys, can complicate the foundation engineering process. Many properties require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions, which must be designed according to local regulations. Additionally, the area's high elevation means a deeper frost line of 36-48 inches, affecting both foundation depth and overall building strategy. Weather patterns can be unpredictable, with heavy snow loads and extreme temperature swings. Properties often have steep driveways, making access difficult, especially during winter months. All these factors make hiring a knowledgeable general contractor essential for a successful build.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a structured process for new construction that ensures your custom home meets your needs and complies with local regulations. Here's a brief overview:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before buying land, we conduct a thorough assessment to evaluate site conditions, utility access, and local zoning regulations. This step is crucial in avoiding potential issues later on.
  2. Well or Cistern Permit: Water availability is essential, and we assist you in applying for the necessary well or cistern permits early in the process to avoid delays.
  3. Site Plan Development: We create a detailed site plan that outlines the positioning of your home, driveway, and utility routes, ensuring compliance with setback requirements and local building codes.
  4. Architectural Plans: Utilizing our network of architects or ArchitecturalDesigns.com, we develop customized home designs tailored to your vision.
  5. Engineering Plans: We provide stamped foundation and framing plans, ensuring that your home is structurally sound and meets snow load requirements.
  6. Permitting: We handle all necessary applications and paperwork for building permits through the Teller County Building Department.
  7. Construction Phase: Our team oversees the entire building process, from site preparation to final inspections, ensuring that every detail aligns with your expectations.
  8. Certificate of Occupancy: Upon completion, we ensure all inspections are passed, and you receive your Certificate of Occupancy, allowing you to move into your new home.

This detailed, step-by-step approach guarantees a smooth and efficient build experience, tailored specifically for the challenges of Goldfield's mountain environment.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Goldfield

When planning your custom home construction, understanding the costs involved is crucial. In Colorado's mountainous regions, the price per square foot for a basic build typically starts around $350-$450, while standard quality homes range from $450-$550 per square foot. For custom or luxury builds, expect to pay from $550-$700 or even upwards of $700-$1,000+ per square foot, depending on the complexity and finishes of your home.

When determining water needs for your custom home, remember that the size of your cistern should accommodate approximately 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. For instance, if your home has three bedrooms, you would need a cistern capacity of about 6,000 gallons, translating to a cost of $25,000-$75,000 for installation.

Additional factors influencing costs include the rocky terrain, which can significantly increase foundation engineering expenses, and the potential need for specialized septic systems due to soil conditions. Access issues also play a role, often leading to higher costs for driveways and utility extensions. Therefore, it's important to consult with experienced professionals like Tri-Lakes Contracting to develop a realistic budget tailored to your unique site.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado can vary widely based on factors such as location, design, and materials. Typically, you can expect to pay between $350-$450 per square foot for basic builds, while luxury homes can cost over $700-$1,000+ per square foot.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house typically ranges from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the design and the site conditions. Pre-construction tasks can take an additional 2-6 months, particularly if permits are needed.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience in mountain home construction, their understanding of local zoning and building codes, and their ability to provide a comprehensive project timeline and budget. Checking references and past projects is also essential.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a Build Feasibility Report before purchasing land is highly recommended. This assessment helps identify site conditions, utility access, and zoning restrictions, ensuring you avoid costly mistakes down the line.
