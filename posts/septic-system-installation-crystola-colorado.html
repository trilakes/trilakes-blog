Local Challenges in Teller County County

The mountainous terrain of Teller County presents various challenges for any septic system installation. The soil is often a mix of rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay, which complicates the design and installation process. Many properties require engineered systems due to deep water tables and restrictive soil conditions. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, meaning that proper winterization and freeze protection for your septic tank are essential. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited access in winter can hinder installation efforts. As a responsible homeowner, it's vital to understand these local challenges to ensure a successful and compliant installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic installation to ensure compliance with local regulations and the unique needs of your property. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step overview:

  1. Initial Consultation: We meet with you to discuss your specific needs and assess your property.
  2. Soil Testing: Utilizing a mini excavator, we dig two test holes to a depth of eight feet to collect soil samples. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer for a detailed soils report, which informs the type of system required.
  3. System Design: Based on the soils report, we design an appropriate onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS) that meets local regulations.
  4. Permit Application: We handle the necessary permits through the Teller County Building Department, ensuring everything is compliant with Teller County regulations.
  5. Installation: Our team will execute the installation, which typically occurs from May to October, optimizing for weather conditions.
  6. Post-Installation Inspection: After installation, we perform a thorough inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly.

This process usually takes a few weeks from initial consultation to completion, depending on factors like weather and site access. We pride ourselves on clear communication and efficient service, ensuring that your septic system is ready for use as soon as possible.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Crystola

Understanding the cost of your septic installation in Crystola is crucial for budgeting. Generally, you can expect costs to start on the higher end due to the unique challenges presented by the terrain. Here's a basic breakdown of the expected costs:

  • Soils Test + Engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000, depending on the specifics of your site.
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Septic Tank + Components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical/Controls: $2,000-$4,000

Overall, a basic engineered system typically ranges from $35,000-$50,000, while a sand filter system can cost between $45,000-$65,000. If you require an Advanced Treatment Unit, you may be looking at $55,000-$80,000+. Additionally, if your property has rocky or difficult terrain, you should anticipate an increase of $10,000-$25,000. For those considering a cistern, remember that the size should be calculated based on your bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day).

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost for septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000 for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions and specific requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process usually takes a few weeks, beginning from the initial consultation to completion. Factors like weather conditions and access to the site can influence this timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Colorado, especially those in challenging mountain terrains, require engineered systems as per Regulation 43 due to the specific soil and water table conditions.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which informs the design of your septic system.
