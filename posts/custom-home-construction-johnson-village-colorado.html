Local Challenges in Chaffee County

The terrain in Chaffee County, particularly Johnson Village, is characterized by rocky granite and decomposed granite with clay in the valleys, complicating foundation engineering. This region has a deep water table, often requiring homeowners to incorporate cisterns or water hauling into their designs. Additionally, with a frost line extending 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, meticulous planning is essential for foundation stability. The building season typically runs from May to October, but weather fluctuations, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can delay construction timelines. Access to many properties is challenging, especially in winter when steep driveways can limit accessibility. Fire mitigation is also a critical consideration in forested areas, necessitating defensible space and other protective measures. Understanding these local challenges is essential for any successful custom home builder in this area.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our approach to custom home construction is both comprehensive and client-focused. We start with a pre-purchase build feasibility report, which evaluates your potential property to ensure it meets your building needs. This step includes a site visit, setback analysis, and a water flow analysis, allowing us to identify any challenges upfront.

Once your property is deemed feasible, we assist with securing the necessary building permits through the Chaffee County Building Department. Next, we develop a detailed site plan that outlines the house's footprint, driveway location, and utility routes. This is followed by creating architectural plans tailored to your vision, which can be sourced from our network of trusted architects or from ArchitecturalDesigns.com.

After the architectural details are finalized, our team produces engineered foundation and framing plans, ensuring they meet the specific site conditions. We then conduct a land survey to verify property boundaries and identify any easements. With septic systems often required in this area, we also prepare stamped plans for engineered septic systems. Finally, we compile all necessary documents for the building permit application, which is submitted to the county for approval. The overall construction timeline typically spans 10-18 months, depending on complexity and site conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Johnson Village

When considering a custom build in Johnson Village, it's essential to understand the associated costs. The price per square foot for mountain home construction generally ranges from $350 to over $1,000, with the average for a standard quality build falling between $450-$550/sq ft. For example, a 2,500 sq ft custom home could cost approximately $875,000 to $1,375,000, depending on your design choices and site conditions.

One significant factor impacting costs is the need for a cistern for water. The standard sizing formula is based on bedroom count: 2 people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern that holds around 6,000 gallons, which could cost between $25,000 and $75,000. Other considerations include the complexity of the terrain, access to the site, and the specific requirements of engineered systems such as septic installations, which can add $35,000 to $80,000 to your budget. Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: Building a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $450 to $1,000+ per square foot, depending on the quality and complexity of the build. For instance, a standard quality 2,500 sq ft home may cost between $875,000 and $1,375,000.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The construction timeline for a custom home in Johnson Village usually spans 10 to 18 months. However, this can vary based on factors such as design complexity, site challenges, and weather conditions.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, prioritize their experience with mountain construction, local building codes, and permits. It's also essential to check their track record for project completion timelines and client satisfaction.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a pre-purchase build feasibility study is crucial before buying land. This report can help identify potential challenges such as soil conditions, access issues, and water availability, preventing costly mistakes.
