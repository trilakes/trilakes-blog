Local Challenges in Saguache County

Moffat's unique landscape presents several challenges that make septic system installation more complicated than in other regions. The rocky granite and clay soils found throughout Saguache County often necessitate engineered septic systems due to poor drainage. The water table can be quite deep, and many properties require additional solutions such as cisterns or water hauling, further complicating the installation process.

Additionally, the frost line in this mountain region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning to ensure that your system is not only functional but also resilient against freezing temperatures. Seasonal access can also be a challenge, as many properties have steep driveways and limited access during winter months. This makes the expertise of a local septic contractor invaluable when planning for your septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, step-by-step process for septic system installation to ensure that every aspect is thoroughly addressed:

  • Initial Consultation: We begin with an on-site assessment to discuss your needs, property layout, and any unique challenges.
  • Soil Testing: Unlike traditional perc tests, we conduct soil testing by digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples for analysis. This provides us with a comprehensive soils report that informs the necessary septic design.
  • Septic Permit: All permits for septic installation must be processed through the Saguache County Building Department, ensuring your system meets local regulations.
  • System Design: Based on the soil report, we will design an engineered septic system tailored for your property, considering options like sand filter systems or Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs).
  • Installation: Once approved, our team will expertly install the system, ensuring that all components, including the septic tank and drain field, are positioned correctly for optimal performance.
  • Final Inspection: After installation, we coordinate with the local authorities for a final inspection to confirm compliance with all regulations.

This comprehensive approach typically ensures that your installation is completed efficiently and to the highest standards, allowing you to enjoy your mountain home without worry.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Moffat

The cost of septic installation in Moffat can vary significantly based on several factors. As a baseline, expect to pay between $35,000-$50,000 for a basic engineered system. This includes essential components such as the septic tank, excavation, and installation of the drain field. The complexity of the site can increase costs, especially if rocky terrain is present, which can add an additional $10,000-$25,000.

For those needing a sand filter system, the total installed cost typically ranges from $45,000-$65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit can run between $55,000-$80,000+. It's also important to factor in the costs of soil testing and engineering, which generally range from $2,000-$4,000, and permits that can vary from $500 to $2,000 depending on the county.

For homes that require cisterns, the size is based on the number of bedrooms, calculated at two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This is a crucial consideration for properties with limited water access.

In summary, while the initial costs for a septic system may seem high, investing in a properly engineered system will ensure long-term reliability, reduced maintenance issues, and compliance with local regulations as outlined in the Saguache County Permit Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies based on site conditions, but typically ranges from $35,000 to over $80,000 depending on the complexity of the engineered system required. Factors such as rocky terrain, access limitations, and specific system needs can significantly affect the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but most installations in Moffat typically take between 1-2 weeks once permits are secured. However, this can change based on soil testing results and the availability of materials.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains, including Moffat, require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions as mandated by Colorado's Regulation 43. This ensures that the system is tailored to the specific requirements of your site, minimizing failure risks.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil testing. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples for its absorption rates, which informs the design of the necessary septic system.
