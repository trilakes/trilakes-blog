Why Midland Properties Need Professional Excavation Services

In the pristine mountain community of Midland, where elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, excavation services are essential for ensuring the success of any construction project. The unique terrain and environmental challenges require expertise that only a licensed and insured excavation contractor can provide. From the initial site preparation to the final grading, professional services guarantee that your project complies with local regulations, particularly those set by the Teller County Building Department. Without professional help, you risk costly delays and potential hazards in a region subject to extreme weather and rocky soil conditions.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Teller County presents unique challenges that can complicate excavation projects. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soils can make foundation excavation and land clearing particularly daunting. As property elevations rise, accessing the site becomes more complicated, especially in winter when steep driveways are covered in snow, limiting equipment access. Additionally, most properties in the area face a deep water table, often necessitating cisterns for water supply, which adds another layer of complexity to your excavation needs. Furthermore, with a frost line that can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, excavation must account for freeze protection, making professional earthmoving services a critical component of any construction project.

Our Excavation Services Process

  • Initial Consultation: We begin with an on-site evaluation to understand your needs and the unique characteristics of your property.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs typically vary based on site conditions, ranging from $15 to $50 per cubic yard. The final price may increase significantly if the terrain is rocky or difficult to access.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation can range from several days to a few weeks, depending on the size of the project and local conditions. Complexities such as rocky soil and required permits will influence the timeline.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Typical equipment includes excavators, bobcats, and backhoes for various tasks like grading and trenching. Specialized machinery may be necessary for rocky terrain and hillside excavation.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are required for excavation projects in Midland. All permits must be obtained through the Teller County Building Department, and we can assist you with this process to ensure compliance with local regulations.
