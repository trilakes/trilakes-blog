Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Empire, located in Clear Creek County, presents unique challenges for water supply solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils complicate installation, often necessitating engineered septic systems due to their challenging nature. With the water table frequently situated deep below the surface, many residents must rely on water hauling or install an underground water storage tank for their needs. Additionally, the frost line at elevations of 8,000+ feet can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it critical to properly winterize your cistern installation. Access to many properties can be steep and limited during winter months, which adds to the complexity of maintaining a reliable water supply. Furthermore, the region is prone to extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, making it essential to have a robust water storage solution in place, particularly for fire suppression and backup supply.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Empire

The cost of cistern installation in Empire can vary significantly based on several factors. The first consideration is the size of the underground water storage tank you need, which depends on your daily water usage. For example, a typical calculation is based on bedroom count: assume two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. Thus, a 3-bedroom home would require around 600 gallons daily.

Next, consider how often you want to refill your tank. If you prefer refills every month, you will need a minimum of 18,000 gallons, while a system designed to get you through the winter could require 72,000 gallons or more.

Here are some typical costs you might expect:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Excavation costs: $3,000-$8,000+, influenced by rocky terrain
  • Pump system & pressure tank installation: $2,500-$5,000
  • Total installed costs for a 5,000-gallon system can range from $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can be $40,000-$55,000.

It‚Äôs crucial to note that rocky or challenging terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and winter installations generally incur higher costs due to ground conditions. For ongoing water hauling, expect to pay around $200-$350 for every 3,000 gallons delivered. A larger tank reduces the frequency of deliveries, offering long-term savings and convenience.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 for a complete installed system, depending on tank size and site conditions. For a basic 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay around $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost $40,000-$55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill it. A common formula is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of occupants based on bedrooms.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary but typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, permits, and weather. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we strive for a smooth process to minimize disruption.

Q: Cistern vs. well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. Cisterns provide a reliable alternative in areas with deep water tables or unreliable well sources, while wells may be suitable if your property has accessible groundwater.
