Why Fairplay Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the picturesque town of Fairplay, located at an elevation of 9,953 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners, especially when it comes to septic installation. The high altitude and distinct soil conditions require a tailored approach to all septic systems. With the right professional guidance, you can ensure a reliable and efficient septic system that meets local regulations and will serve your property for years to come. Attempting to install a septic tank or an onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS) without expert help can lead to costly mistakes and potential health hazards.

Local Challenges in Park County

The terrain in Park County poses multiple challenges for septic installation. The soil can vary from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, making it essential to conduct thorough soil testing before installation. Due to the area's high elevation and deep water table, many properties require engineered systems to function effectively. Standard conventional systems are rarely feasible in this region. Furthermore, the frost line ranges from 36-48 inches, complicating installation during the winter months. Heavy snow loads and limited winter access can also impede progress, making it crucial to plan your septic system installation during the optimal building season, typically from May to October.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take pride in our comprehensive approach to septic installation. Our process begins with an initial consultation where we assess your property and discuss your needs. Next, we perform soil testing, which involves bringing in a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis, leading to a detailed septic design that conforms to Colorado's Regulation 43.

Once we have the necessary approvals and your septic permit from the Park County Building Department, we proceed with excavation and preparation of the site. Depending on the system type required, such as a pressure-dosed system or a sand filter system, we ensure all components, from the septic tank to the leach field, are properly installed. We also integrate winter preparation tips during installation to ensure your system is protected against freeze conditions. Once the installation is complete, we conduct thorough septic inspections to verify everything is functioning correctly.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Fairplay

Understanding the cost of septic installation in Fairplay is essential for homeowners. Costs can vary significantly based on the type of system required, soil conditions, and site accessibility. Generally, you can expect to pay:

  • Soils test + engineering: Starting from $2,000 to $4,000
  • Permits: Ranging from $500 to $2,000
  • Excavation: Typically $8,000 to $20,000+
  • Tank + components: Between $5,000 and $15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000 to $15,000
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000 to $4,000

In total, you should budget approximately $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system. If your property has difficult terrain or rocky soil, you may need to add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to your budget. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the system type and site conditions. For engineered systems, which are the most common in mountain areas, expect higher costs due to challenging soil and terrain.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but generally takes between 1 to 3 weeks, depending on permitting, weather conditions, and site access. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures timely and efficient service while meeting all regulatory requirements.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Fairplay require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil and elevation conditions. These systems are designed to function effectively in areas where conventional systems would fail, making them a necessary choice for many homeowners.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not perform traditional perc tests. Instead, we conduct soil tests using a mini excavator to assess soil conditions. This information is crucial for determining the appropriate type of septic system for your property.
