Local Challenges in Chaffee County

Chaffee County‚Äôs unique terrain poses specific challenges for septic installation. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is designed to be thorough and efficient. Once the soils report is received, we will assist you in completing the required septic permit applications through the Chaffee County Building Department. After permits are secured, we schedule the excavation and installation, which typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Salida

The cost of septic installation in Salida can vary significantly based on several factors. For most properties requiring an engineered system in Colorado, installation costs typically range from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic system. If your property requires a sand filter system, expect costs to be around $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can range from $55,000 to $80,000+. Challenging sites, especially those with rocky terrain or steep driveways, may add $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall price.

It's important to note that additional costs include soil testing and engineering fees, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, as well as permits that may vary from $500 to $2,000 depending on local regulations. Excavation costs typically start from $8,000 and can go much higher based on site conditions. For properties needing cisterns, the size is calculated based on bedroom count, with a formula of two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

Given the complexities involved in septic installation, always consider quoting on the higher end of price ranges to ensure you‚Äôre prepared for any unexpected expenses related to site conditions or additional requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+ depending on the type of system required, site conditions, and additional factors like excavation and permits. For engineered systems, expect to pay on the higher end due to the challenging terrain and specific regulations.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes 1-2 weeks, depending on various factors such as weather, site access, and the complexity of the system being installed. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting works efficiently to minimize any disruptions to your property.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, properties in Colorado, especially in mountain regions like Salida, require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems provide effective wastewater treatment and are designed to last longer with proper maintenance.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil types and conditions, which then informs the necessary design for your septic system. Understanding your soil is crucial for successful installation and long-term performance.
