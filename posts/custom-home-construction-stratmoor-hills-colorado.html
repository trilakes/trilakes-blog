Local Challenges in El Paso County

The Stratmoor Hills area poses distinct challenges for home construction, primarily due to its rocky granite soil and varying elevation. Properties often require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions, and a deep water table may necessitate the installation of cisterns for water storage. Additionally, the frost line at this elevation can reach depths of 36-48 inches, impacting foundation engineering and design. The steep driveways common in this mountainous region can limit winter access, making proper planning essential. Moreover, the extreme weather conditions, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, require fire-resistant construction techniques and careful site planning to create defensible space around your home. Understanding these factors is crucial for any homeowner looking to build a custom mountain home in this beautiful area.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive and structured process for custom home construction. It begins with a pre-purchase build feasibility report to assess the site's viability. This includes a site visit, setback analysis, soil conditions evaluation, and a water flow analysis, costing between $1,500-$3,500. Next, we help you navigate the well permitting process, which must be completed before applying for a building permit. Once you have secured your water source, we create a detailed site plan that outlines everything from the house footprint to utility routes, costing around $1,500-$4,000. Following this, architectural plans are developed, which can be sourced from your architect, our network, or through our affiliate at ArchitecturalDesigns.com. The cost of these plans typically ranges from $2,000-$15,000+. Next, we focus on engineered foundation and framing plans, which are stamped by licensed engineers, costing approximately $5,000-$15,000. A land survey follows, ensuring accurate boundaries and topographical data, with costs of $1,500-$5,000. Once these plans are in place, we compile and submit all necessary documents to the El Paso County Building Department, with permit fees ranging from $5,000-$20,000. This process takes 2-8 weeks. After securing your building permit, we move into the pre-construction phase, finalizing details and preparing for construction, which can take between 10-18 months depending on the complexity of your home.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Stratmoor Hills

When considering the cost of building a custom home in Stratmoor Hills, it‚Äôs important to account for various factors that influence the overall price. Basic construction starts around $350-$450 per square foot, while standard quality builds are typically priced between $450-$550 per square foot. For custom or higher-end projects, costs can escalate to $550-$700 per square foot, and luxury builds can reach $700-$1,000+ per square foot. For example, if you are planning a 2,500 square foot home, the typical cost would range from $875,000 to $1,375,000. In addition, homeowners should budget for additional costs such as land, which varies widely, as well as water solutions like cisterns ($25,000-$75,000) and wells ($30,000-$80,000). For water storage, the sizing of your cistern is crucial and is usually calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. You would determine the tank size needed by multiplying the bedroom count by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. Moreover, factors such as rocky terrain, difficult access, and the need for engineered septic systems can significantly impact construction costs, adding to the complexity and expense of your project.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies significantly with basic builds starting at around $350-$450 per square foot. For standard builds, expect to pay between $450-$550 per square foot, while luxury homes can run from $700 to over $1,000 per square foot, depending on the level of customization and finishes.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house in Colorado typically spans 10-18 months for construction alone, with an additional 2-6 months needed for pre-construction planning and permitting. Complex homes may take longer, especially given the mountainous terrain and weather conditions.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, it‚Äôs essential to look for experience and expertise in mountain construction, strong local knowledge of regulations, and a proven track record of quality work. Reading reviews and asking for references can also help you gauge their reliability and craftsmanship.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a build feasibility report before purchasing land is crucial. This study helps identify potential challenges, such as soil conditions, setback requirements, and water availability, allowing you to make an informed decision about your investment.
