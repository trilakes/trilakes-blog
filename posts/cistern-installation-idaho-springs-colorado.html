Why Idaho Springs Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Idaho Springs, nestled in the Rocky Mountains at an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners when it comes to water supply. Many properties here are situated in remote areas where access to a reliable water source can be difficult. If you're considering cistern installation for your home, it's crucial to engage professional services like Tri-Lakes Contracting. Our expertise ensures that your cistern installation adheres to local regulations and is optimized for the specific conditions of Clear Creek County. We use high-quality materials and advanced installation techniques to create an efficient underground water storage tank that meets your water needs year-round, even during harsh winters. With our guidance, you can rest assured that your potable water storage tank will be built to last, providing peace of mind for you and your family.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County County

Building in Idaho Springs comes with its own set of challenges that can affect water storage solutions. The terrain is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, making excavation a complex process. The high elevation means that the water table is often deep, and many properties require water hauling solutions or cistern installation to ensure a consistent supply of water. In addition, the frost line in the area ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating specialized installation techniques to protect your buried water tank from freeze damage during the winter months. Homeowners must also consider the extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads that can impact accessibility and water delivery, especially during winter. All these factors underscore the importance of choosing a reputable local cistern company that understands the unique landscape and regulations of Clear Creek County, including necessary permits through the County Building Department.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to be thorough and efficient, ensuring that every project meets your specific needs. Initially, we conduct a detailed site assessment to determine soil conditions, access routes, and your water usage requirements. This assessment helps us select the appropriate water storage tank installation and size the system based on your daily water needs, which we calculate from your home's bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires around 600 gallons per day, guiding us to determine the necessary tank size.

Once the planning phase is complete, we will secure all required permits from the Clear Creek County Building Department. Then, we begin excavation, which may take 1-2 days, depending on site conditions. After preparing the site, we install the chosen underground cistern, commonly a Norwesco tank, which we then connect to your home's plumbing and pump system. The entire installation typically takes about a week, including backfilling and site restoration. Our team is committed to ensuring that your system is not only installed correctly but also ready to withstand the unique challenges presented by Idaho Springs winters.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Idaho Springs

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is crucial for budgeting your project. The total installed costs for a cistern can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and additional features. For a residential system, we generally install Norwesco tanks, which range from:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: typically $25,000-$35,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: typically $40,000-$55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): typically $50,000-$75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): typically $75,000-$120,000+

These prices include not only the tank but also excavation, a pump system, trenching, and electrical work. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range significantly based on size and site conditions. For a standard 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons if you plan to refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?
