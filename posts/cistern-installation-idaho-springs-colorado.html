Why Idaho Springs Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the breathtaking mountain community of Idaho Springs, Colorado, the need for reliable water solutions is paramount. At an elevation of over 8,000 feet, many properties are far removed from conventional water sources, necessitating cistern installation as a practical solution. With a rocky terrain, unpredictable weather, and a deep water table, navigating the complexities of installing an underground water storage tank can be daunting for homeowners. That's where professional cistern installers, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, come into play. Our expert team not only provides top-notch installation services, but we also ensure your system is tailored to meet your unique needs and facilitate compliance with Clear Creek County regulations.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Idaho Springs presents unique challenges that can complicate water storage tank installation. The soil here varies widely, consisting of rocky granite and decomposed granite, making excavation a significant aspect of the process. Additionally, the high elevation often results in a water table that is much deeper than average, leading to a reliance on water hauling for many properties. Homeowners must also contend with a frost line that reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, especially at higher elevations, which necessitates careful planning for freeze protection. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder installation timelines and accessibility. Understanding these factors is crucial for homeowners as they consider their cistern installation options.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Idaho Springs

The cost of cistern installation in Idaho Springs can vary significantly based on several factors, including tank size, excavation challenges, and site accessibility. As a rule of thumb, the size of your water storage tank depends on your daily water usage. For example, a typical calculation assumes 100 gallons per person per day. If your home has three bedrooms, accommodating up to six people, your daily usage would be approximately 600 gallons.

For monthly refills, you would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons, translating to a 10,000-gallon tank, which typically starts from $40,000 and can go as high as $55,000 when factoring in installation and additional costs. If you prefer fewer refills during the winter, a larger system of 72,000 gallons or more may be necessary, with total installed costs ranging from $75,000 to $120,000.

Additional costs can include excavation, which may range from $3,000 to $8,000+ depending on rock conditions, and trenching to your home, typically costing $25-$50 per linear foot. Installation of pumps, pressure tanks, filters, and electrical hookups can add another $5,000 to $10,000 to your total costs. Keep in mind that rocky terrain may increase costs by 20-40%, so having a professional handle the logistics ensures that you stay within budget while adhering to all local regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of installing a cistern in Colorado can range significantly. For example, a 10,000-gallon system can start from $40,000 and go up to $55,000, depending on site conditions and specific installation requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank depends on your household water usage and refill frequency. For instance, a three-bedroom home may require at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills, while a winter supply could necessitate a tank of 72,000 gallons or more.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but typically it takes several weeks to complete the project. This includes time for permitting, excavation, installation, and final inspections.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources. They provide a consistent backup water supply and can be tailored to meet specific household needs, whereas wells can be expensive to drill and maintain.
