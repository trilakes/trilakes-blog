Local Challenges in Eagle County

Edwards, located in Eagle County, presents several challenges for septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in this region often necessitate engineered systems as per Colorado's Regulation 43. The high water table in some areas can also complicate installation, requiring additional measures such as cisterns for water storage. Furthermore, the frost line here reaches depths of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, making proper winterization and freeze protection crucial. The steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate installation logistics, emphasizing the need for experienced septic contractors familiar with these local conditions.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Edwards

The cost of installing a septic system in Edwards can vary significantly based on several factors. On average, you can expect to pay:

  • Soils test and engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank and components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Units (if required): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical controls: $2,000-$4,000

Considering all these factors, the total costs for a basic engineered system range from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems can cost between $45,000 and $65,000. For ATU or advanced systems, expect to budget $55,000 to $80,000+, particularly if your site presents challenges like rocky terrain or limited access.

When considering a cistern for water needs, the size is determined by the bedroom count; you'll need approximately 100 gallons per day per two occupants per bedroom. Keep in mind that challenging site conditions can add $10,000-$25,000 to your total costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on site conditions and system type. Generally, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000+, depending on whether you need a basic engineered system, sand filter, or advanced treatment unit.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it typically takes several weeks to complete, given factors such as weather conditions, site accessibility, and the complexity of the installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Edwards require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations. This ensures effective waste treatment and compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil quality and determine the appropriate septic system design.
