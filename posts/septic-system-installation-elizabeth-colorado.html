Why Elizabeth Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the beautiful mountain community of Elizabeth, Colorado, at an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, comes with its unique set of challenges, especially for septic system installations. Many homes in this picturesque area rely on onsite wastewater treatment systems (OWTS) due to the rocky terrain and varying soil types. Properly installing a septic system is crucial to ensuring efficient waste management, protecting the environment, and maintaining property value. In this region, where soil conditions can be unpredictable, hiring a licensed and insured septic contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your system meets Colorado's stringent Regulation 43 standards while providing the right solution for your property.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

Douglas County is known for its varying elevations and challenging terrain, particularly in Elizabeth, where the soil often consists of rocky granite and clay in the valleys. These soil conditions can complicate septic installation processes, making it necessary to conduct thorough soil testing to determine the appropriate system for your property. The high water table in many areas means that some properties may require cisterns for water supply, adding another layer of complexity. Furthermore, with a frost line extending 36-48 inches deep, particularly at higher elevations, winter preparation becomes critical as homeowners must consider winterizing septic system installation to prevent freeze-related issues. Access to properties can also be limited during winter months due to snow accumulation, making professional installation services an invaluable asset.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive step-by-step process to ensure your septic system is installed correctly and efficiently. Our first step is to perform soil testing, which involves bringing a mini excavator to your site to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. We collect soil samples from various depths, which are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis. This report will guide us in designing the most suitable engineered septic system for your property.

Once the soils report is received, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Douglas County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations. The installation process typically spans several weeks, depending on the complexity of your site. Factors such as rocky terrain and access challenges can extend the timeline, but we work diligently to minimize disruption while maintaining quality.

After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure your system is functioning correctly before completion. We also provide guidelines on maintenance to ensure the longevity of your system, which can last 30-50 years with proper care. Our experts are always available for any follow-up questions or concerns you may have.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Elizabeth

The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions and system requirements. For properties in Elizabeth, the total installed costs for a basic engineered system typically start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, while a sand filter system can range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can start from $55,000 and reach up to $80,000 or more.

Factors that can increase the overall cost include the presence of rocky soil, which may add an additional $10,000 to $25,000, and challenging access routes, especially if steep driveways are involved. For properties requiring cisterns, the size is calculated based on the number of bedrooms, with the formula being bedroom count multiplied by two people per bedroom, then multiplied by 100 gallons per day.

Additionally, costs for permits can range from $500 to $2,000 depending on the specific requirements set forth by Douglas County. Excavation for the system can cost anywhere from $8,000 to $20,000+, emphasizing the importance of factoring in all these elements when budgeting for your septic system installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 and can go up to $80,000, depending on the complexity of the system required. Factors such as soil conditions, design type, and local permit fees all influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system generally takes a few weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system. Factors such as the need for soil testing, permit acquisition, and access to the site can extend the timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains, including those in Elizabeth, require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A professional assessment will determine the best system for your specific site.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps in determining the appropriate type of septic system needed for your property.
