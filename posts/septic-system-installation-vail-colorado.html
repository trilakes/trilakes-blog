Why Vail Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In Vail, Colorado, where stunning mountain landscapes meet complex terrain, proper septic installation is crucial for homeowners. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, the unique geological and environmental factors create challenges that require professional expertise. A reliable septic contractor understands the intricacies of installing an onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS) that complies with local regulations, ensuring not only functionality but also environmental safety. Trusting a licensed and insured general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting means your septic system will be designed and installed correctly, minimizing future issues and costly repairs.

Local Challenges in Eagle County County

The mountainous terrain of Eagle County, home to Vail, poses various challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils found here often require engineered systems, as standard systems rarely meet the rigorous demands of the landscape. Furthermore, at elevations of 8,000 feet and above, the frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, complicating installation processes. Homeowners must also consider the deep water table, which may necessitate the use of cisterns for water storage. Access to many properties can be limited, particularly during winter months when steep driveways can become hazardous. Understanding these local challenges is essential for a successful installation that lasts for decades.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and systematic approach to ensure your septic installation meets all requirements and functions effectively. Our process begins with a comprehensive soil testing procedure to determine the best type of system for your property. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, deep enough to reach the necessary soil layers. These samples are sent to a licensed engineer who produces a soils report, guiding our design and installation.

Once we have the soils report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Eagle County Building Department. Following permit approval, we schedule excavation and installation typically during the building season from May to October. Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Vail

When it comes to septic installation in Vail, costs can vary significantly based on site conditions and system requirements. For instance, a basic engineered system may cost between $35,000 and $50,000, while a sand filter system can range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an ATU, expect to invest between $55,000 and $80,000, particularly if the site poses challenges such as rocky terrain or limited access, which can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your total costs.

Calculating the size of your cistern is also important; it typically involves multiplying your bedroom count by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a home with three bedrooms would need a cistern capable of holding 600 gallons. We recommend planning for these expenses early, as obtaining a soils test and engineering report will cost between $2,000 and $4,000, while permits may range from $500 to $2,000, depending on specific requirements outlined in the Eagle County Permit Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can range significantly based on site conditions and the type of system required. For engineered systems, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000 for basic models, while more complex systems like sand filters or ATUs can cost between $45,000 and $80,000 or more.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of septic installation can vary widely, typically taking anywhere from a few days to several weeks. Factors influencing the timeline include the complexity of the system, the need for soil testing, and obtaining the necessary permits through the Eagle County Building Department.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Vail area require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations outlined in Colorado's OWTS Regulation 43. An engineered system ensures adequate treatment and longevity of your septic tank.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. These tests involve digging test holes to assess soil composition and drainage capabilities, which helps determine the appropriate type of system for your property.
