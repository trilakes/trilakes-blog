Local Challenges in El Paso County

In Yoder, El Paso County, homeowners encounter several challenges when considering cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, significantly affecting the cost and timeline of your water storage tank installation. Furthermore, the water table is often deep, making a cistern a practical solution for many properties that would otherwise rely on costly water hauling. With extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall during winter, it's essential to ensure that your underground cistern is appropriately winterized to avoid freezing. The frost line at 36-48 inches necessitates careful planning to protect your investment. Additionally, many properties in the area have steep driveways, which can limit access for installation and maintenance, making professional help crucial for a successful outcome.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we believe in a thorough and efficient cistern installation process. First, we conduct an on-site assessment to determine your daily water needs based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For instance, a 3-bedroom house typically requires around 600 gallons per day. With this information, we can recommend the appropriate tank size, which often ranges from 5,000 to 10,000 gallons or more, depending on your refill preferences. Our team will then apply for necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Next, we prepare the site by excavating the area for the buried water tank. This process can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on soil conditions and access. Once the excavation is complete, we install high-quality tanks such as Norwesco or Snyder, which are renowned for their durability and reliability. After the tank is securely placed, we connect it to your home‚Äôs plumbing system, which includes installing a pump and pressure tank. Finally, we conduct a quality check to ensure everything operates efficiently. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Yoder

The cost of installing a water storage tank in Yoder can vary significantly based on several critical factors. The starting price for a 5,000-gallon tank is typically around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. Sizing your potable water storage tank is based on daily water usage, which is about 100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an assumed occupancy of 6 people would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, leading to a recommended tank size of at least 18,000 gallons if monthly refills are preferred.

Additional costs can arise from excavation, which may range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rocky terrain of Yoder. Other considerations include pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot), and electrical work ($1,000-$2,500). If you need water treatment or filtration, this could add another $2,000-$5,000. Therefore, it‚Äôs essential to plan for a comprehensive budget that accommodates these variables to avoid unexpected expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system, including necessary excavation and plumbing work. Additional factors such as rocky terrain and accessibility can further increase these costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your household size and daily water usage. A general rule is to estimate about 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 people would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refills are monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes about 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and excavation challenges. Factors such as rocky terrain or limited access can extend this timeframe.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs unique conditions. For many properties with a deep water table or challenging soil, a cistern installation is often a more feasible solution than drilling a well, which can be unreliable or prohibitively expensive.
