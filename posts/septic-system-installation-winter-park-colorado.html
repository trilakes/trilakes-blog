Local Challenges in Grand County

Installing a septic system in Grand County presents numerous challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils often necessitate engineered septic systems rather than conventional ones. Additionally, the deep water table in many areas means that properties may require cisterns for water storage, complicating the installation process. The frost line here can extend to 36-48 inches, demanding careful planning during the winter months when temperatures can swing drastically. With limited winter access on steep driveways, the best time for installation typically falls between May and October. Furthermore, fire mitigation is crucial in forested areas, which can impact your septic installation plans. Understanding these local challenges is vital for a successful outcome.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we have a comprehensive, step-by-step process for septic installation tailored to the unique conditions of Winter Park:

  1. Soil Testing: Every installation begins with soil testing, which involves digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze the soil composition. This report, prepared by a licensed engineer, will determine the most suitable type of septic system.
  2. Design and Planning: Based on the soil report, we design an onsite wastewater treatment system that meets local regulations and the specific needs of your property.
  3. Permitting: All permits for your septic system will be acquired through the Grand County Building Department, ensuring compliance with all local regulations.
  4. Installation: Our team will then proceed with the installation, taking care to minimize disruption while adhering to safety and quality standards.
  5. Inspections: After installation, a septic inspection will ensure that everything is functioning as it should, giving you peace of mind.

We aim to complete most installations within a few weeks, depending on weather and terrain conditions. Ensuring a smooth process is our top priority, particularly when considering winter preparations and freeze protection.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Winter Park

The cost of installing a septic system in Winter Park can vary widely based on several factors, including soil conditions and site accessibility. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank and Components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical and Controls: $2,000-$4,000

As a guide, the total cost for a basic engineered system typically starts from $35,000-$50,000, while more complex sand filter systems can range from $45,000-$65,000. For an ATU system, costs can exceed $55,000, particularly for rocky sites that add significant excavation expenses. Additionally, if you need a cistern, consider that the size is based on a formula of bedroom count multiplied by two people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on site conditions and system types. Generally, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000+, depending on factors like soil complexity and terrain challenges, including the need for advanced treatment systems.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation typically ranges from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on the complexity of the system and weather conditions. Factors such as site accessibility and required inspections can also influence the duration of the installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In many cases, especially in mountainous areas like Winter Park, an engineered septic system is necessary due to challenging soil conditions outlined in Regulation 43. Most properties require these systems to ensure effective waste treatment and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed; instead, we conduct soil tests. This process involves digging deep test holes to assess the soil's drainage capabilities, informing the design of your septic system.
