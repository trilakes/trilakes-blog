Local Challenges in Eagle County

The scenic beauty of Bond, located in the heart of Eagle County, comes with its own set of challenges when it comes to water supply solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for buried water tanks, and the deep water table often necessitates a cistern installation or water hauling as a well alternative. Furthermore, the frost line at elevations over 8,000 feet can reach depths of 36-48 inches, demanding thorough planning and execution to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months. Homeowners must also consider access issues, especially during the winter when steep driveways may become impassable. Fire mitigation is another critical aspect, especially in forested areas, where maintaining a defensible space is essential for safety.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Bond

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is crucial for homeowners in Bond. The typical total installed cost for a 5,000-gallon cistern ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system will cost between $40,000 and $55,000. Sizing your potable water storage tank is based on daily water usage, estimated as 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of occupants (2 people per bedroom). For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require around 600 gallons per day, suggesting a system size of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or 72,000 gallons to sustain through winter without frequent deliveries.

Several factors can increase these costs, including:

  • Excavation Challenges: Rocky or difficult terrain can increase costs by 20-40%.
  • Accessibility: Properties with steep driveways may require specialized equipment and add to the overall cost.
  • Winter Installations: Ground conditions can make winter installations more expensive.

When planning your budget for a cistern installation, consider that additional costs such as excavation (ranging from $3,000 to $8,000), pump systems ($2,500 to $5,000), and electrical hookups ($1,000 to $2,500) will also contribute to the overall expense.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system, depending on site conditions and installation specifics. Additional costs for excavation, pump systems, and electrical work can also add to the total price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on the number of bedrooms in your home and the daily water usage. As a rule of thumb, calculate 100 gallons per person per day, and consider how often you want to refill the tank for convenience.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation in Bond typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, permits, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors, including depth to the water table, site conditions, and personal needs. For many properties in Bond, where wells may be impractical or unreliable, a cistern installation offers a more dependable solution for water supply.
