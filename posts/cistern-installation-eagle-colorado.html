Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Eagle

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Eagle County is essential for planning your project effectively. The total installed cost for a water storage tank installation can range from $25,000 to $120,000+, depending on various factors. For instance, a typical 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000.

The sizing of your cistern is crucial and is generally determined by the number of bedrooms in your home. As a rule of thumb, you can estimate your daily water usage at 100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home (assuming 2 people per bedroom) would require about 600 gallons per day. Depending on how often you want to refill your tank, you could need anywhere from 18,000 gallons (monthly refill) to over 72,000 gallons (to last through winter). This need for larger systems contributes to the overall cost.

Additional costs can include excavation (averaging $3,000-$8,000), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500). Importantly, rocky terrain and difficult access can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Hence, it‚Äôs wise to prepare for the higher end of the cost spectrum to avoid any surprises during the installation process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and installation complexity. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may range from $40,000 to $55,000, factoring in excavation and additional components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank will depend on daily water usage, which is typically calculated at 100 gallons per person. For a 3-bedroom home, you might need a tank that holds 18,000 gallons or more if you prefer fewer refills, particularly during the winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and the complexity of the installation. Factors such as rocky terrain and winter access can affect the timeline, so it‚Äôs best to plan for potential delays.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water table and access. Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources, providing a consistent water supply even in harsh winter conditions.
