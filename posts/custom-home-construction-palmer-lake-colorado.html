Local Challenges in El Paso County

Palmer Lake's mountainous terrain comes with its own set of challenges that can complicate home construction. The soil here predominantly consists of rocky granite and decomposed granite, making foundation engineering crucial. Many properties may require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and deep water tables, necessitating careful site planning and evaluations. Additionally, the frost line, which can reach depths of 36-48 inches, means that builders must account for significant frost heave during construction. Fire mitigation is essential in our forested areas, and understanding local building permit requirements is vital for compliance. With steep driveways and limited winter access, having a reliable home builder near me who understands these challenges can save you time and money in the long run.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous step-by-step process to ensure your custom home is built to exceed your expectations:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before purchasing land, we conduct a thorough feasibility study to assess site conditions, setback analysis, and soil evaluations to ensure your chosen property is buildable.
  2. Well or Cistern Permit: If your property lacks a water source, we guide you through the process of obtaining a well permit, or if you opt for a cistern, we help you with the necessary documentation to expedite your construction timeline.
  3. Site Plan Development: Our team creates a detailed site plan that includes the layout of your home, driveway, septic systems, and utility routes, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Architectural Plans: Collaborating with skilled architects or utilizing resources from ArchitecturalDesigns.com, we design your dream home to suit your style and vision.
  5. Engineering and Surveying: We coordinate with licensed engineers for stamped foundation and structural plans, and conduct a land survey to confirm property boundaries.
  6. Septic System Plans: For homes not connected to public sewer systems, we develop custom septic plans that meet Colorado regulations.
  7. Building Permit Submission: We compile and submit all necessary documents to the El Paso County building department to secure your building permit.
  8. Pre-Construction Planning: Before construction begins, we finalize plans, order materials, and establish a detailed timeline.
  9. Construction: Our team carefully manages the construction phases while coordinating inspections at key milestones.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $350 to $1,000+ per square foot, depending on quality and site conditions. For a standard quality build, budget around $450-$550 per square foot, leading to total costs between $700,000 to over $1.5 million for mid-sized homes.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a custom home often spans 10-18 months for construction alone, with pre-construction planning adding an additional 2-6 months. Complex builds may take even longer, especially in mountain regions where weather can impact schedules.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience with mountain construction, knowledge of local regulations, and ability to provide a detailed construction timeline and budget. Look for testimonials and past project examples to assess their work quality.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, a feasibility study is crucial before purchasing land in Palmer Lake. It assesses site conditions like soil type, elevation, and water flow, helping you avoid potential pitfalls and ensuring that your chosen property is suitable for building.
