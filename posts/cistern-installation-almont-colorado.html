Why Almont Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Almont, Colorado, nestled at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges when it comes to water sourcing. Many properties in this stunning mountain community lack direct access to wells, making cistern installation a crucial solution for both residential and agricultural water needs. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the intricacies of installing underground water storage tanks in high-altitude environments. Our expertise ensures that your water cistern is installed correctly, effectively, and in compliance with local regulations set forth by the Gunnison County Building Department. Relying on professional service not only guarantees a reliable water supply but also provides peace of mind that your system is built to withstand the elements and terrain specific to our beautiful region.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County County

Almont is characterized by its rocky granite and clay soils, which pose significant challenges for cistern installation. The water table in this area is often deep, leading many homeowners to rely on water hauling to meet their needs. Furthermore, the local climate, with extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall, necessitates careful planning for winterization. The frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it essential to install a frost-proof cistern to prevent freezing and ensure that your potable water storage remains accessible year-round. Access to properties can also be limited due to steep driveways and challenging winter conditions, which complicates both installation and ongoing maintenance of the system.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Almont

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Almont is crucial for planning your water solution. Prices for an underground water storage tank vary based on size and site conditions. For example, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank starts at approximately $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank runs between $22,000-$28,000. Total installed costs, including excavation, pump systems, and trenching, typically range from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the complexity of the installation.

When sizing your potable water storage tank, consider your household's needs: a 3-bedroom home, accommodating six people, would require around 600 gallons per day. If you prefer to refill every two weeks, you would need a minimum of 8,400 gallons, while a monthly refill would necessitate a tank of at least 18,000 gallons. Factors that can increase costs include rocky terrain, remote access, and other site-specific challenges. Always be prepared for the higher end of the pricing spectrum to ensure you are not caught off guard.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on factors such as tank size and installation complexity. For example, a basic 5,000-gallon system typically starts around $25,000 and can exceed $35,000 when including excavation and plumbing costs, particularly in challenging terrains like Almont.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The appropriate size for your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and refill frequency. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would generally require around 18,000 gallons if refilling monthly or up to 72,000 gallons for a larger system to last through winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration of cistern installation can vary based on site conditions and permitting. Typically, the complete process‚Äîfrom site assessment to installation‚Äîtakes several weeks, particularly in areas with challenging access or rocky soil.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific property conditions. If your water table is too deep or well drilling is impractical, a cistern may be the more reliable option for a rural water supply, especially for properties in high-altitude areas like Almont.
