Why Pine Junction Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Pine Junction, Colorado, comes with its fair share of unique challenges, particularly when it comes to water supply. With properties often lacking direct access to wells and facing deep water tables, cistern installation becomes an essential solution for homeowners. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the intricacies of installing underground water storage tanks at elevations over 8,000 feet. Our expertise ensures that your installation adheres to local regulations and is designed to withstand the extreme weather conditions characteristic of our area, including heavy snow loads and temperature fluctuations.

Local Challenges in Park County County

Pine Junction is nestled in the heart of Park County County, characterized by its rocky granite and decomposed granite soils. These challenging conditions can complicate water storage tank installations, often requiring engineered septic systems due to the soil types. The region's elevation leads to a water table that can be quite deep, making water hauling a common necessity for many properties. Additionally, with a frost line extending 36-48 inches deeper at higher elevations, ensuring your potable water storage tank is winterized and protected against freezing is crucial. Property access can also be limited, especially during the winter months, impacting delivery and installation timelines.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive cistern installation process designed to ensure your project is seamless and efficient:

  1. Consultation and Site Assessment: We begin by assessing your property to determine the best location for the water cistern while considering factors like access, soil type, and local regulations.
  2. Permitting: All necessary permits are acquired through the Park County County Building Department. This step ensures compliance with local codes and standards.
  3. Design and Planning: Based on your water needs, we calculate the appropriate cistern size. For example, a typical 3-bedroom home may require a system that holds approximately 18,000 gallons to minimize refills.
  4. Excavation and Installation: Our team will excavate the site, considering any rocky conditions that may increase costs. Once the site is prepared, we install the buried water tank, including any necessary pumps and filtration systems.
  5. Final Inspection: After installation, we conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything is functioning properly and meets local regulations.
  6. Winter Preparation: To protect your cistern from winter freeze, we provide winterization tips tailored to your system.

Typically, the entire process can take several weeks, depending on the complexity of the installation and site conditions.

To size your underground cistern, you can use the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require at least 600 gallons per day, leading to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons if you prefer monthly refills. Factors like difficult access and winter installation can increase costs significantly. Therefore, a full installation cost for a basic system usually ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, with larger capacity systems costing upwards of $75,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of installing a cistern can range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system. Depending on site conditions, larger systems can go up to $120,000 or more.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, typically calculated as bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom house needs at least 18,000 gallons if you prefer monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The cistern installation process can take several weeks, depending on site assessments, permitting, and installation complexities. Factors like winter conditions may also extend the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on your specific property conditions. In many cases, especially in areas with deep water tables, a cistern is a more reliable and cost-effective solution.
