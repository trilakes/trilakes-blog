Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation that begins with a detailed site evaluation. Here‚Äôs how we do it:

  • Initial Consultation: We discuss your property specifics and assess your needs, including the size of your home and the number of bedrooms, which helps determine your required water usage.
  • Soil Testing: We conduct soil tests, not traditional perc tests, by digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze soil conditions. Samples are sent to a licensed engineer for evaluation.
  • System Design: Based on the soils report, we design the most suitable septic system, often an engineered system tailored to your property‚Äôs unique needs.
  • Permitting: We assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Summit County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  • Installation: Our skilled team installs the system, including the septic tank, leach field, and any necessary components, while taking care to protect your property and minimize disruption.
  • Inspection: After installation, we perform a thorough septic inspection to confirm everything adheres to local standards.

This entire process typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting timelines. We recommend initiating the process early in the building season, typically from May to October, to ensure a successful installation.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Summit Cove

The cost of septic installation in Summit Cove can vary widely based on several factors, including soil conditions, system design, and site access. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of what to expect:

  • Soils Testing and Engineering: Expect to invest between $2,000 and $4,000 for soil tests and engineering reports, which are essential for designing your system.
  • Permits: The cost for permits can range from $500 to $2,000 depending on the specifics of your project, and these must be obtained through the Summit County Building Department.
  • Excavation: Excavation costs will typically run from $8,000 to $20,000+, influenced by the rocky terrain.
  • System Components: The septic tank and system components can add another $5,000 to $15,000 to your total, while advanced systems like the ATU (Advanced Treatment Unit) can significantly increase the price.
  • Total Estimates: For a basic engineered system, expect costs to start from $35,000 to $50,000. A sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000, while an ATU system can start from $55,000 and go up to $80,000 or more based on site complexities.

Keep in mind that challenging site conditions, such as rocky terrain and steep access, can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your overall costs. If your home requires a cistern, calculate the size based on your bedroom count, using the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 for a basic engineered system to upwards of $80,000 for advanced treatment options, depending on site conditions and system requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but typically it takes several weeks from initial consultation through permitting and installation, especially in areas with challenging terrain like Summit Cove.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Summit Cove area require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions outlined in Colorado's Regulation 43. These systems ensure proper waste management and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil conditions, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design needed for your property.
