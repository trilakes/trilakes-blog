Why Colorado Springs Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living at an elevation of 6,035 feet in Colorado Springs presents unique challenges, particularly when it comes to water supply management. For many properties in the area, conventional wells may be too deep or impractical, making cistern installation a crucial solution. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting specializes in this area, providing homeowners with reliable underground water storage tanks that cater to both potable water storage and agricultural needs. Our expertise ensures that your installation is not only compliant with local regulations but also tailored to your specific usage requirements, ensuring you have a sufficient backup water supply for those harsh winters.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Colorado Springs is characterized by its rocky granite and clay soils, which can complicate water storage tank installation. Properties often experience a deep water table, leading to increased reliance on water hauling and cistern installation. Furthermore, the frost line, which ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitates careful planning to protect your buried water tank from freezing temperatures. The extreme weather, including heavy snow and afternoon thunderstorms, combined with steep driveways and limited winter access, can also pose challenges during installation. Homeowners should be aware that the El Paso County Building Department requires permits for all cistern installations, ensuring your project adheres to local codes and regulations.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and systematic approach to cistern installation. Our process typically begins with an initial consultation to assess your property and water needs. This includes evaluating your daily water usage, which is based on your home's bedroom count. Once we determine the right size for your water cistern, we move on to the planning phase, where we secure the necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department.

Installation begins with excavation, which can vary based on the rocky terrain commonly found in Colorado Springs. After digging the hole, we install the underground water storage tank, commonly a Norwesco brand, ensuring it meets local safety and quality standards. We then connect the tank to your home‚Äôs plumbing system, incorporating a pump and pressure tank to ensure consistent water flow.

The entire process can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and any unforeseen challenges. After installation, we provide guidance on maintaining your system, particularly on winterizing your cistern installation to prevent freezing.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Colorado Springs

When considering cistern installation in Colorado Springs, it‚Äôs essential to understand the various cost factors involved. A basic 5,000-gallon system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. Sizing is critical and is based on daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home, assuming 2 people per bedroom, would need to account for 600 gallons of water daily. If you desire a refill every month, you would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons of storage.

Additional costs can arise from excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more, depending on the rocky soil and accessibility of your property. If you need a pump system and pressure tank, budget another $2,500 to $5,000, plus additional costs for trenching, electrical work, and filtration systems. It's crucial to quote on the high end to accommodate any unexpected site conditions that could increase your overall costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 or more for larger capacities, depending on various factors such as site conditions and additional components needed for installation.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your household's daily water usage. A good rule of thumb is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, then multiply by the number of occupants to determine your minimum storage needs.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but typically it takes a few days to a couple of weeks. This includes the time needed for permitting, excavation, installation, and connection to your plumbing system.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. In many areas of Colorado Springs, wells may be too deep or unreliable, making cistern installation an ideal alternative for water storage needs. Cisterns can also serve multiple purposes, such as irrigation and fire suppression.
