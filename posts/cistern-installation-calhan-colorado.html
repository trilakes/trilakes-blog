Local Challenges in El Paso County

Calhan's geographic and climatic conditions present specific challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, often leading to higher costs and longer project timelines. Additionally, the elevation often results in a water table that is quite deep, necessitating the use of water hauling for many residents. The frost line ranges from 36 to 48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, meaning that any buried water tank must be properly insulated to prevent freezing in harsh winters. Moreover, steep driveways and limited access during winter months can impede both installation and maintenance of your water storage tank. As you prepare for the cold season, it's essential to consider these factors to ensure your system remains functional.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Calhan

When considering cistern installation in Calhan, understanding the costs involved is critical. The total installed price for a basic 5,000-gallon system typically starts from $25,000 and can go up to $35,000. This includes the tank, excavation, pump systems, and necessary electrical hookups. For larger tanks or dual systems, such as a 10,000-gallon tank, you can expect to spend between $40,000 to $55,000.

The size of your underground water storage tank will depend on your daily usage, which can be calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a three-bedroom home typically needs around 600 gallons per day, which means a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly. Keep in mind that rocky terrain and difficult access can add 20-40% to your excavation costs, which is crucial to factor into your budget.

For properties requiring extensive systems, such as those needing backup water supply or fire suppression capabilities, costs can escalate significantly, reaching up to $120,000 for larger capacity systems (30,000+ gallons). Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and site conditions. Typically, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, and costs can escalate to $75,000 or more for larger capacities, factoring in excavation and installation challenges.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is calculated by the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a three-bedroom home generally requires a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, but larger systems may be necessary for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the project. Factors such as soil conditions and accessibility can significantly affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific needs and site conditions. Cisterns are often preferable in areas with deep water tables or unreliable well yields, providing a consistent and accessible source of water without the complications of well drilling.
