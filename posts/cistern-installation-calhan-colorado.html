Local Challenges in El Paso County

Calhan's rocky granite soil and high elevation present significant challenges for cistern installation. The water table can be deep, making it impractical for many homeowners to rely solely on wells, thus necessitating the use of an underground cistern. Additionally, the frost line in this area reaches depths of 36-48 inches, which complicates the installation process significantly. With extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, it is critical to plan for winter preparation and ensure that your cistern is winterized properly to prevent freeze damage.

Access can also be problematic, especially for properties with steep driveways or limited winter access. This can increase costs and complicate the logistics of both the installation and any necessary water hauling services. Understanding these local challenges is vital for ensuring a successful and hassle-free installation process.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Calhan

The cost for cistern installation can vary significantly based on several factors. Generally, a 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank installation starts at around $25,000 and can go up to $35,000 when considering excavation, pump systems, and other necessary components. For a larger 10,000-gallon tank, costs can range from $40,000 to $55,000. A common sizing formula is based on your daily water usage‚Äîtypically calculated as 100 gallons per person per day, with 2 people per bedroom. Additional costs may include excavation fees that can range from $3,000 to $8,000, depending on soil conditions and accessibility. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado varies, but for a basic 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000. For a larger, 10,000-gallon tank, costs typically range from $40,000 to $55,000, depending on site-specific conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be determined based on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. A general guideline is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, with 2 people per bedroom. A 3-bedroom home would need at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration of cistern installation can vary based on site conditions, but typically, the complete process takes between 1 to 3 weeks, including permitting, excavation, and installation. Weather conditions, especially in winter, can affect timelines.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are beneficial for properties without reliable well access or where the water table is too deep. They also provide greater storage flexibility and can be less expensive over time when considering ongoing water hauling costs.
