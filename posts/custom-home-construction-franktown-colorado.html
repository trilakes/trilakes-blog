Local Challenges in Douglas County

Franktown's rocky granite and clay soil can pose significant challenges for home construction. The region often requires specialized foundation engineering due to the deep frost line of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations. Many properties necessitate engineered septic systems because of challenging soil conditions, which can also complicate site planning. Additionally, the extreme weather, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can affect your construction timeline and methods. Accessing these properties can be tricky, especially during winter months when steep driveways may be difficult to navigate. Understanding these local challenges is critical, and that's where a reliable general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting comes into play.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we use a comprehensive, step-by-step approach to custom home building. Here's a brief overview of our process:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before you buy land, we conduct a thorough feasibility report to assess site conditions, setback requirements, and utility access.
  2. Well Permit: Securing your well permit early is crucial, as it can take months. If you choose a cistern, we ensure you have water access right away.
  3. Site Plan: We create a detailed site plan that outlines your home's footprint and utility routes.
  4. Architectural Plans: Collaborating with licensed architects, we finalize your home's design tailored to your needs and preferences.
  5. Engineered Foundation and Framing Plans: We provide stamped plans that account for local soil conditions and frost depth.
  6. Land Survey: A professional survey verifies property boundaries and easements.
  7. Septic System Plans: If a sewer connection is unavailable, we develop engineered septic plans.
  8. Compile and Submit for Building Permit: We handle the compilation and submission of all necessary documents to Douglas County.
  9. Pre-Construction Planning: Final reviews, material orders, and subcontractor scheduling ensure everything is in place before construction begins.
  10. Construction: Our team manages all phases of construction, from site preparation to the final walkthrough.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Franktown

The cost to build a custom home in Franktown can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, the complexity of the design, and local building regulations. Typically, you can expect costs to land in the following ranges:

  • Basic Build: $350-$450/sq ft
  • Standard Quality: $450-$550/sq ft
  • Custom/Higher-End: $550-$700/sq ft
  • Luxury/High-End: $700-$1,000+/sq ft

For example, a 2,500 sq ft custom home could range from $875,000 to $1,375,000. It's crucial to factor in additional costs, such as land acquisition, well or cistern installation ($25,000-$75,000), septic systems ($35,000-$80,000), and any necessary utility extensions. The size of your cistern should be calculated based on bedroom count, using the formula: 2 people x number of bedrooms x 100 gallons/day.

Furthermore, the costs can increase due to the rocky terrain, access challenges, and the need for special site engineering. Therefore, partnering with a custom home builder like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures you receive accurate estimates from the start, avoiding unexpected expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost of building a custom home in Colorado can range from $350 to over $1,000 per square foot, depending on the quality and complexity of the build. For a typical 2,500 sq ft home, expect to pay between $875,000 to $1,375,000.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a house in the Colorado mountains typically ranges from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the home and weather conditions. Factors such as permitting and pre-construction planning can add an additional 2-6 months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience with mountain construction, licensing and insurance verification, and the ability to provide comprehensive project management services. Look for a builder with a solid reputation in your local community.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study before purchasing land is crucial. It helps assess whether the property is buildable by evaluating soil conditions, access, and local building codes, potentially saving you from making a costly investment in unbuildable land.
