Why Palmer Lake Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the charming community of Palmer Lake, nestled at an elevation of 7,237 feet, comes with its unique set of challenges, particularly when it comes to water supply. Many homes in this historic mountain town lack direct access to reliable well water due to the deep water table and rocky terrain. This is where cistern installation becomes crucial. A properly installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a consistent and safe water supply for drinking, irrigation, and fire suppression. Choosing a licensed and insured cistern installer near you is essential for ensuring compliance with local regulations and for the longevity of your water system.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

The geographical and climatic factors in Palmer Lake significantly influence water management solutions. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil, common in the area, can complicate installation processes and increase excavation costs. Furthermore, winters here can be harsh, with a frost line reaching depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating a frost-proof cistern to prevent freezing. Access to many properties is limited, especially in winter, making water hauling a common practice. These challenges underscore the need for a robust water storage solutions plan that can meet both daily needs and seasonal demands.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take pride in our comprehensive cistern installation process tailored for the unique environment of Palmer Lake. Our first step is a thorough site assessment, considering factors like soil composition, elevation, and property access. This assessment helps us determine the best location for your potable water storage tank.

Next, we will work with you to calculate the required tank size based on your daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of six people typically requires around 600 gallons per day. Based on how often you wish to refill, which could be monthly or every few months, we can help you select the right size. Our team will then handle all necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

The installation process involves excavation, which can take between 1 to 3 days depending on site conditions. Following excavation, we carefully install the buried water tank, ensuring it is connected to your home‚Äôs plumbing and any necessary filtration systems. We also provide guidance on winterizing your cistern installation to protect against freezing temperatures, a crucial step for winter preparation in our mountainous region.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Palmer Lake

The total cost of your water storage tank installation will depend on several factors, primarily the size of the cistern, excavation complexities, and additional system requirements. For instance, the industry-leading Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank costs between $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. However, it's important to note that installation costs can add significantly to these base prices.

Using the sizing formula based on bedroom count, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, translating to a total installed system cost ranging from $40,000 to $55,000. Additional costs can arise from excavation, which may range from $3,000 to $8,000, depending on the rocky terrain and access issues. Furthermore, the installation of a pump system and pressure tank can add another $2,500 to $5,000 to your total. It is always wise to consider that challenging site conditions can increase costs by 20-40%.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely based on size and site conditions. For a 5,000-gallon system, you can expect total installed costs starting from $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and desired refill frequency. For example, a 3-bedroom home often requires around 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, while a winter storage solution may necessitate a tank system of 72,000 gallons or more.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process typically takes 1 to 3 days, depending on site conditions and excavation requirements. However, additional time may be needed for permit approval through the El Paso County Building Department and for any necessary system integrations.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. Cisterns serve as an excellent alternative for properties with deep water tables and limited access to reliable well water, providing a consistent water supply without the challenges of drilling.
