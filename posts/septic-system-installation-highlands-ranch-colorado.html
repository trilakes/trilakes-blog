Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Highlands Ranch

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Highlands Ranch is essential for budgeting your project. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of what to expect:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: Expect to pay between $2,000 and $4,000 for thorough soil testing and engineering reports.
  • Permits: Permit fees vary, typically ranging from $500 to $2,000 depending on the complexity of your installation.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavation alone can cost anywhere from $8,000 to $20,000+, especially in rocky terrain.
  • Tank and Components: The cost for the septic tank and its components can range from $5,000 to $15,000.
  • Advanced Treatment Units: If an ATU system is required, expect an additional cost of $8,000 to $15,000.
  • Electrical and Controls: Budget $2,000 to $4,000 for electrical setups and controls.

In total, the average cost for a basic engineered system typically starts at $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. For more complex systems like sand filters or ATUs, costs can range from $45,000 to $80,000 or more, especially if your site presents additional challenges such as steep access or rocky soil. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000 or more for advanced treatment options, depending on site conditions, soil type, and specific installation requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes several weeks from consultation to final inspection, influenced by permitting processes and site-specific challenges.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Highlands Ranch will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and depth of the water table, as mandated by Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes and collecting soil samples to determine the suitable type of septic system for your property.
