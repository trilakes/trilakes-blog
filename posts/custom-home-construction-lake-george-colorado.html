Local Challenges in Park County

Park County presents various challenges that can complicate the home building process. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil found in the area can affect foundation design, often requiring specialized engineered systems. Additionally, the deep water table in many properties means homeowners frequently need to consider cisterns or water hauling solutions. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, particular attention must be paid to foundation depths. The building season is typically limited to May through October, and steep driveways can complicate access during winter months. It's crucial to incorporate fire mitigation strategies in your planning due to the dense forested environment, ensuring a defensible space around your home.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive, step-by-step process for home construction tailored to the unique conditions of Lake George. It starts with a Build Feasibility Report, ensuring the land is viable for your dream home. Once secured, we proceed with obtaining a wells permit or planning for a cistern, as water access is essential before a building permit can be issued. Following this, we create a detailed site plan that includes everything from house orientation to utility routes, which helps streamline the construction process.

The next steps involve working with architects for architectural plans, followed by rigorous engineering for foundations and framing. We then conduct a land survey to confirm property boundaries and any easements. After compiling all necessary documents, we submit for a building permit through the Park County Building Department, which can take 2-8 weeks for approval. Construction begins with site preparation and progresses through a series of inspections to ensure quality and safety, ultimately leading to the issuance of a Certificate of Occupancy.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Lake George

When considering a custom home in Lake George, it's essential to understand the cost factors involved. Typically, the cost per square foot can range significantly based on various conditions. Basic builds start around $350-$450 per square foot, while standard quality homes are generally between $450-$550 per square foot. For higher-end custom builds, expect to pay between $550-$700 per square foot, and luxury homes can range from $700 to over $1,000 per square foot.

When calculating costs, it's important to factor in the specifics of your site. For example, soil conditions may necessitate more expensive foundation types, and steep terrain could increase access costs. If you're planning on using a cistern, remember that the size is usually calculated based on the number of bedrooms‚Äî2 people per bedroom times 100 gallons per day. This means for a three-bedroom home, you would need a cistern capable of holding approximately 6,000 gallons. Additional expenses like well drilling, septic installation, and driveway construction can also contribute significantly to your overall budget. Therefore, it's wise to plan for a budget on the higher end of the scale to accommodate the unique challenges of building in this beautiful mountain community.
