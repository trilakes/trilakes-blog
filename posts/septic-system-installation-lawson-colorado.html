Why Lawson Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the scenic mountain community of Lawson, Clear Creek County, where elevations soar above 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners, particularly when it comes to septic installation. The rocky terrain and fluctuating weather conditions require expertise in septic system design and installation that only a licensed professional can provide. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand that a properly functioning septic tank is crucial for your property's health and the surrounding environment. Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Lawson's unique geological features, including rocky granite and clay-filled valleys, make septic installation a complicated process. Most properties require engineered systems due to the challenging soil conditions, which necessitate thorough soil testing and a comprehensive understanding of the local water table, often found at significant depths. The frost line can reach 36 to 48 inches at higher elevations, meaning that winter preparation is essential to protect your septic system from freezing temperatures. With limited access during winter months and steep driveways, planning for installation requires careful consideration. Heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms can further complicate the building process, making it vital to partner with an experienced septic contractor who knows how to navigate these challenges.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a systematic approach to ensure your septic installation is seamless and efficient. Our process begins with a thorough site evaluation and soil testing. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, to gather soil samples that are then analyzed by a licensed engineer. This soils report helps us determine the most suitable system for your property, whether it be a pressure-dosed system, a sand filter system, or an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU).

Once the system type is confirmed, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Clear Creek County Building Department. Our team will then schedule the excavation, which typically takes place between May and October due to weather constraints. Installation usually takes a few days, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system. After the installation, we conduct a septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly and meets all local regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Lawson

The cost of septic installation in Lawson can vary based on several factors, including soil conditions, system type, and site accessibility. For a basic engineered system, you can expect costs to start from $35,000 and go up to $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, the total installed cost will be around $45,000 to $65,000. Should an ATU system be necessary, expect to invest between $55,000 and $80,000. Additional costs may arise from challenging terrain, such as rocky or steep sites, potentially adding $10,000 to $25,000 to the total.

For homeowners needing a cistern, the size is based on the formula of bedroom count multiplied by 2 people per bedroom and then multiplied by 100 gallons per day. For instance, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern capable of handling 600 gallons per day. Remember that these are starting estimates, and actual costs may vary. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for a basic engineered system and can go as high as $80,000 for advanced systems, depending on site conditions and the type of system required.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Installation of a septic system in Lawson generally takes a few days, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions, with most installations occurring between May and October due to weather constraints.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Lawson require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and steep terrain. An engineered system is crucial for compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43 and ensures optimal functionality.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with soil tests, which involve digging test holes to assess soil composition. This information is vital for determining the right septic system for your property.
