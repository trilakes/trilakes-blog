Local Challenges in El Paso County

Falcon's mountainous terrain presents several challenges for septic system installations. The area is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which often necessitate engineered systems to ensure proper function. Traditional gravity systems are rare due to the soil's drainage capabilities, which can lead to increased costs for property owners. Additionally, the water table is often deep, meaning many homeowners require cisterns for water hauling.

The weather is another factor to consider; with extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall, winterizing your septic installation is essential. The frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches or more, increasing the need for thoughtful planning during installation. Furthermore, many properties have steep driveways and limited access during the winter months, adding complexity to the installation process. Fire mitigation is also critical in forested areas, impacting the layout of systems.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and tailored to the specific needs of your property. We begin with a comprehensive site evaluation, including soil testing, to assess ground conditions. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each reaching 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples that will determine the best septic design for your property.

Once we have the soils report from a licensed engineer, we will apply for the necessary septic permit through the El Paso County Building Department. This step is crucial, as it ensures compliance with Regulation 43 governing Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS) in Colorado. After securing the permit, we move into the excavation phase, which can vary in complexity depending on site conditions.

The installation of the septic tank and associated components typically occurs within a few weeks after permit approval. Our team is committed to minimizing disruption to your property while ensuring that the installation is durable and reliable. We will also guide you through the winter preparation tips, ensuring your system is ready to withstand the cold months ahead, safeguarding your investment for years to come.

The total installed cost for most engineered systems typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on site-specific conditions. A soils test and engineering typically cost between $2,000 and $4,000. Additionally, permits can range from $500 to $2,000, and excavation can add another $8,000 to $20,000+

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on specific site conditions. Factors such as soil type, terrain challenges, and the need for engineered systems can significantly influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process for a septic system usually takes a few weeks after obtaining the necessary permits. This includes site evaluations, soil testing, and installation, which can be affected by weather conditions and site access.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Falcon require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions as outlined in Regulation 43. Conventional systems are rarely feasible, making engineered solutions essential for proper function and compliance.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not utilized; instead, we conduct soil testing, which involves digging test holes to evaluate soil conditions. This information is crucial for determining the appropriate septic system design for your property.
