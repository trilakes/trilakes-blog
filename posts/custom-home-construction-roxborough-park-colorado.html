Local Challenges in Douglas County

Building in Roxborough Park means facing unique local challenges that can significantly impact your custom home project. One primary concern is the soil composition, which varies from rocky granite to decomposed granite and clay in the valleys. This variability affects foundation engineering and landscaping. Additionally, the water table can be quite deep, often necessitating the installation of cisterns or water hauling systems. With a frost line that reaches depths of 36-48 inches, especially at higher elevations, your home‚Äôs foundation must be designed to withstand intense winter conditions. Furthermore, Douglas County's steep terrain often results in limited access during the winter months, emphasizing the importance of thorough site planning. As a reliable residential contractor, we prioritize fire mitigation strategies due to the area's forested landscapes, ensuring your custom home is built with safety in mind.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for custom home construction that ensures your project runs smoothly from start to finish. The first crucial step is conducting a build feasibility report, which assesses the property for elevation, slope, soil conditions, and utility availability. This study helps determine whether the land is suitable for your envisioned home. Once this foundational decision is made, we assist in obtaining necessary permits from the Douglas County Building Department, ensuring all local requirements are met.

Next, we develop a comprehensive site plan that outlines the positioning of your home, driveway, and utility routes. Afterward, we collaborate with architects to create customized architectural plans tailored to your preferences. Our licensed engineers then prepare the necessary foundation and framing plans, ensuring they meet Colorado's stringent regulations.

After securing all permits, we begin the construction phase, which typically takes 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the design and weather conditions. Throughout the process, we maintain open communication with homeowners to facilitate a seamless experience. Each phase of construction is followed by inspections to guarantee safety and compliance, culminating in the issuance of a certificate of occupancy upon project completion.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Roxborough Park

Understanding the costs involved in building a custom home is crucial for any homeowner. In Roxborough Park, the cost per square foot for new construction typically ranges from $550 to $700 for custom or higher-end builds. The overall cost for a 2,500 sq ft home may reach between $875,000 and $1,375,000. Keep in mind that various factors can influence these figures, such as terrain challenges, access issues, and the need for engineered systems due to rocky soil.

For those considering water systems, the sizing of cisterns is vital and should account for the number of bedrooms, calculated at 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. This means for a three-bedroom home, a cistern would need to hold at least 6,000 gallons to accommodate daily use. Additional costs for essential installations, such as septic systems, driveways, and utility extensions, can further impact your budget, emphasizing the importance of thorough planning and budgeting.

Consider planning for potential cost overruns due to the unique challenges of mountain construction. It's always best to quote on the high end of price ranges to avoid surprises later on.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $550 to $700 per square foot for higher-end constructions in mountain communities like Roxborough Park. A standard 2,500 sq ft custom home may range from $875,000 to $1,375,000, depending on various factors such as site conditions and design complexity.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a custom home usually spans 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the design and weather conditions. During the pre-construction phase, which includes obtaining permits and finalizing plans, expect an additional 2 to 6 months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, prioritize their experience with mountain construction, local knowledge of site conditions, and a strong portfolio of completed projects. Working with a licensed and insured general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures adherence to local regulations and a focus on quality craftsmanship.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study before purchasing land is crucial. This report assesses site conditions, setbacks, and utility availability, helping you avoid costly mistakes by ensuring the land is suitable for your custom home project.
