Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we adhere to a comprehensive process for septic installation that ensures your system is efficient and compliant with local regulations. Here‚Äôs what you can expect:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with an assessment of your property to understand its unique characteristics.
  2. Soils Testing: Utilizing a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to collect soil samples. This crucial step helps determine the type of septic system you need.
  3. Design Phase: Based on the soils report, we design your system, whether it‚Äôs a pressure-dosed system, sand filter system, or an advanced treatment unit (ATU).
  4. Permitting: We guide you through the permitting process, working closely with the Pitkin County Building Department to ensure all necessary paperwork is completed.
  5. Installation: Our team handles all aspects of the installation, including excavation, setting up the septic tank, and establishing the drain field.
  6. Final Inspection: After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning as it should.

This process typically takes several weeks, depending on conditions such as soil type and weather. Preparing your property for winter with proper winterization techniques is also part of our service, ensuring your system is protected from freezing.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Snowmass Village

The cost of septic installation in Snowmass Village can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, for an engineered system, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000. For a sand filter system, the cost ranges from $45,000 to $65,000, and for an ATU system, it can reach $55,000 to $80,000, especially if your site has challenging rocky terrain.

For those requiring a cistern, sizing is based on the formula of bedroom count multiplied by 2 people per bedroom and then multiplied by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of handling 600 gallons daily.

Additional costs include soils testing and engineering, which can run $2,000 to $4,000, and permits that range from $500 to $2,000, depending on the county regulations. Excavation can add anywhere from $8,000 to $20,000, while the septic tank and associated components typically cost between $5,000 and $15,000. Factors like difficult access, rocky terrain, or the need for winterization techniques can raise these estimates significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on site conditions. For engineered systems, expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000, while sand filter systems are typically between $45,000 and $65,000, and ATU systems can range from $55,000 to $80,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process can take several weeks, depending on various factors such as soil conditions and weather. Proper planning can ensure that your system is installed efficiently.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Snowmass Village require engineered septic systems due to the challenging rocky and clay-rich soils. These systems are designed to accommodate the unique conditions of the area, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze the soil composition, which is essential for designing an appropriate onsite wastewater treatment system.
