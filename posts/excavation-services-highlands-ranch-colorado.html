Local Challenges in Douglas County County

Excavation in Highlands Ranch comes with its own set of unique challenges due to the region's elevation of over 8,000 feet, rocky granite soil, and the often deep water table. Many properties may require complex solutions like cisterns for water storage or engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. Additionally, the frost line here ranges from 36 to 48 inches deep, making foundation excavation and utility trenching particularly demanding.

The steep terrain can also pose access issues, especially during the winter months when heavy snow can limit accessibility to construction sites. Cost Factors for Excavation Services in Highlands Ranch

Understanding the costs associated with excavation services is essential for planning your project effectively. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical excavation pricing in the Highlands Ranch area:

  • Site Preparation/Clearing: For basic lot clearing, you can expect costs starting from $8,000, with heavy brush removal ranging from $5,000 to $15,000 per acre.
  • Driveway Construction: Constructing a gravel driveway typically costs between $15 and $35 per linear foot. For a 200 ft driveway, expect to budget around $10,000.
  • Foundation Excavation: A typical basement dig (around 1,500 sq ft) can run between $15,000 and $30,000, while rock excavation can add 50-200% to costs.
  • Utility Trenching: Standard trenching costs range from $8 to $15 per linear foot, but rocky conditions may increase this to $20-$50.

When planning for a cistern, remember that the size is based on your bedroom count; each bedroom typically requires about 200 gallons of water per day (2 people per bedroom x 100 gal/day). Total installed costs will include the tank, excavation, pumps, trenching, and electrical work.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs can vary significantly, but typically, you may expect rates starting around $50 to $100 per cubic yard, depending on site conditions and whether rock removal is necessary.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation varies based on project complexity, but most basic site preparation tasks can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, particularly when considering local conditions and permitting processes.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Essential equipment for mountain excavation includes excavators, bobcats, and backhoes. Depending on the project, specialized machinery for rock removal and trenching may also be required to navigate the challenging terrain.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, all excavation projects in Douglas County require permits, which must be obtained from the local building department. Tri-Lakes Contracting can assist you through this process to ensure compliance.
