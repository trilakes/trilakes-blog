Why Johnson Village Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Johnson Village, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges when it comes to securing a reliable water supply. Many properties lack a viable well due to the deep water table and rocky terrain, making cistern installation a practical solution. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that homeowners have access to potable water storage while navigating the complexities of local building codes and environmental factors. With the right expertise, you can avoid costly mistakes and ensure your water system is efficient and compliant with Chaffee County regulations.

Local Challenges in Chaffee County

The terrain of Chaffee County is characterized by rocky granite and decomposed granite, with soil conditions that can make excavation a challenge. With an elevation of 8,000+ feet, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning for water storage tank installation to prevent freeze damage during winter months. Access to many properties can be limited due to steep driveways, especially during heavy snowfall. Additionally, the extreme weather, including afternoon thunderstorms and heavy snow loads, can complicate construction schedules. These factors underscore the necessity of hiring a knowledgeable cistern installer near you who understands the nuances of the local landscape and climate.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous step-by-step process for reliable cistern installation. The initial consultation involves assessing your property to determine the best location for your water storage tank, taking into account soil conditions and winter access. We will help calculate your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home, utilizing the formula of 100 gallons per person per day to size your cistern appropriately.

Once we finalize the design and obtain the necessary permits through the Chaffee County Building Department, we begin the excavation process. This can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions. After the excavation, we will install the buried water tank, typically a Norwesco tank, along with the pump system and necessary plumbing. We pay special attention to winterizing your system to ensure it remains operational during the colder months. Our goal is to provide a seamless experience, ensuring your water storage solutions are installed efficiently and effectively.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Johnson Village

The cost of cistern installation in Johnson Village can vary significantly based on several factors. For a typical installation, you can expect to pay between $40,000 and $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system, which includes the tank itself, excavation, pump installation, and other necessary components. Cistern sizing is essential; for example, a three-bedroom home would require approximately 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, or even larger for less frequent refills.

Cost factors that can increase your total expenditure include rocky terrain, which can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and challenging access that may require additional equipment. Other considerations include electrical hookups, water filtration systems, and trenching costs, which can add thousands to your project. Understanding these costs upfront can help you budget effectively for your water storage tank installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. This price includes the tank, excavation, and installation of pumps and plumbing, but can increase based on site-specific challenges.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, which is calculated by multiplying the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom, then by 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would need at least an 18,000-gallon tank if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern can take anywhere from several days to a week, depending on the complexity of the project and site conditions. Factors such as excavation difficulty and weather can also affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. In areas with a deep water table or unreliable wells, a cistern serves as a practical alternative, providing a reliable backup water supply without the challenges associated with well drilling.
