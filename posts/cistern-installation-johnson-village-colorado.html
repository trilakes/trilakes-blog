Many homes in this area do not have access to a reliable well, making cistern installation an essential service for homeowners. Properly installed underground water storage tanks ensure that you have a sufficient and dependable water supply, especially crucial during the harsh Colorado winters. With extreme temperature fluctuations and heavy snowfall, it‚Äôs vital to have a water storage solution that can withstand these elements. Local Challenges in Chaffee County County

Properties in Johnson Village face unique environmental challenges that can complicate water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can make excavation difficult and expensive, potentially increasing costs by 20-40% depending on the site conditions. Additionally, many properties have a deep water table, which means homeowners often need to rely on water hauling or opt for a cistern as an alternative to drilling a well. The frost line in this area is deeper than in lower elevations, ranging from 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning for frost protection and winterization of your underground cistern. Furthermore, steep driveways and limited access during winter months can complicate installation, requiring expertise and specialized equipment.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Johnson Village

When considering a water storage tank installation in Johnson Village, understanding the costs involved is essential. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of key factors that influence pricing:

  • Tank Size: The size of your cistern should be based on your household‚Äôs water usage. A general guideline is to calculate your daily usage (75-100 gallons per person per day) multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home (assuming 2 people per bedroom). For instance, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would need approximately 600 gallons per day. If you prefer refilling every 2-3 months, you‚Äôll need a tank holding between 36,000-54,000 gallons to ensure enough supply.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavation for burial typically ranges from $3,000-$8,000+ depending on rock conditions and access. Rocky and steep terrains can significantly increase these costs.
  • System Components: Additional costs for pumps, filtration, and trenching can add $25-$50 per linear foot, plus costs for electrical hookups and access lids.
  • Total Installed Costs: For a 5,000-gallon cistern system, you can expect to pay between $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may range from $40,000-$55,000. For larger capacity systems (15,000-20,000 gallons), costs can climb to between $50,000-$75,000 or more. Investing more upfront often leads to fewer hassles and a more reliable water supply.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies significantly based on size and installation factors. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, prices range from $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000-$55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your cistern should be based on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill. A 3-bedroom home, for example, would require at least 36,000-54,000 gallons if refilling every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes a few weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. The process includes consultation, permitting, excavation, and installation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, as they provide a reliable supply of water without the complications associated with well drilling.
