Local Challenges in Summit County

Dillon and the greater Summit County area present unique challenges for cistern installation. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Dillon

The cost of cistern installation in Dillon can vary based on several factors, including tank size, excavation difficulty, and any additional features you might require. For a basic system, the pricing typically breaks down as follows:

  • 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank: $13,000-$15,000 (tank only)
  • 10,000-gallon Norwesco tank: $22,000-$28,000 (tank only)
  • Excavation costs: $3,000-$8,000+, heavily influenced by rocky terrain and site accessibility.
  • Pump system and pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to house: $25-$50 per linear foot
  • Final installation costs: Total systems can range from $25,000-$120,000 depending on the size and complexity.

To determine your specific needs, use the formula of daily water usage based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). For instance, a 3-bedroom home should plan for approximately 600 gallons/day, leading to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills. Keep in mind that rocky and difficult terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and we always quote the high end of price ranges to avoid surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the size and complexity of the installation. For example, a 5,000-gallon system averages around $25,000, while larger systems can go up to $120,000 when considering excavation, pump systems, and other necessary components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your daily water usage, which is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. As a rule of thumb, use 100 gallons per day per person; for a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants, a minimum of 18,000 gallons is advisable for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and permit approvals. The installation process includes site assessments, excavation, and setting up the tank and associated systems, which can be affected by weather and accessibility.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water table and soil conditions. In many Summit County areas, wells may be too deep or unreliable, making cistern installation the more feasible option for a consistent water supply, especially in off-grid locations.
