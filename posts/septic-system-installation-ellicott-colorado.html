The cost of installing a septic system in Ellicott typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on various factors. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of what contributes to the overall cost:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: This initial phase can cost between $2,000 and $4,000.

  • Excavation: The cost for excavation can vary widely, from $8,000 to $20,000+, especially in rocky terrain.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on various factors such as soil conditions, system type, and site accessibility. Most installations in mountainous areas, like Ellicott, require engineered systems, which tend to be on the higher end of the price spectrum.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The time required for septic installation can vary but typically spans several weeks. Factors influencing this timeline include soil testing, permitting from the El Paso County Building Department, and site conditions. Proper planning and professional assistance can help expedite the process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Ellicott area require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions and elevation. An engineered system is designed to meet specific site requirements and is crucial for ensuring proper function and compliance with Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: Unlike traditional perc tests, Colorado requires soil testing to assess the soil's suitability for septic systems. During this process, we dig test holes and send samples to a licensed engineer, who provides a soil report that informs the design of the septic system.
