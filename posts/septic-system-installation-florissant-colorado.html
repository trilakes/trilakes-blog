Local Challenges in Teller County

Installing a septic system in Florissant involves navigating several local challenges. The terrain is predominantly composed of rocky granite and clay, which complicates the installation of traditional systems. Properties often require engineered systems due to the depth of the water table and the need for proper drainage, especially in areas with steep driveways and limited winter access. Additionally, the frost line here can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning to avoid freeze damage during the harsh winter months. With extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall, ensuring your septic tank is properly winterized is essential to prevent costly repairs later on.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Florissant

The cost of septic installation in Florissant can vary significantly based on several factors. On average, a basic engineered system can start from $35,000 to $50,000, while more advanced systems like a sand filter can range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can escalate to $55,000-$80,000. These prices include excavation, tank installation, and all necessary components.

When determining your specific costs, consider the size of the system needed, which is typically calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). For properties situated in challenging terrain or those with limited access, you may see an increase of $10,000 to $25,000 in total installation costs. Additionally, the necessary permits from the Teller County Building Department can range from $500 to $2,000. Always consult with experienced septic contractors to get a detailed quote based on your unique situation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000 or more for advanced systems like ATUs. The exact price depends on soil conditions, system type, and site access.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: On average, septic installation can take anywhere from 2 to 5 days, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the system being installed.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and deeper water tables, as outlined in Regulation 43. A soils report will help determine the appropriate system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with soil tests. These tests involve digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the appropriate septic system design for your property.
