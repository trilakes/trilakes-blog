Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Pine's unique geography and climate necessitate a specialized approach to septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area often require engineered septic systems, as conventional setups are rarely feasible. The water table is typically deep, and many properties require cisterns for water supply. Additionally, the frost line in Pine can reach depths of 36-48 inches, demanding careful planning to avoid winter freeze issues. With steep driveways and limited access during winter months, timely installation becomes crucial. Our experts understand these challenges and can navigate them effectively, ensuring your septic installation meets all necessary standards and performs optimally.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic installation that ensures compliance and durability. Our first step involves conducting thorough soil testing to assess the site conditions. We bring a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, where we collect samples for a licensed engineer to analyze. This comprehensive soils report informs the septic design that best suits your property.

Once we obtain the necessary septic permit from the Jefferson County Building Department, our team will outline the installation timeline, typically spanning several days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions. Excavation follows, where we ensure that the system's placement adheres to the required setback distances as dictated by Regulation 43. Our team is trained to work efficiently, minimizing disturbances to your property while ensuring your onsite wastewater system is installed correctly and ready for use.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Pine

The cost of septic installation in Pine can vary widely based on site conditions and system requirements. Generally, the total installed costs for an engineered system start from $35,000 and can go as high as $80,000+, depending on specific needs. For example, basic engineered systems typically range from $35,000 to $50,000, while more complex setups like sand filter systems can range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property necessitates an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), you might be looking at $55,000 to $80,000+.

For properties requiring cisterns, remember that the size is calculated based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day). Additional costs can arise from rocky terrain, limited access, or steep driveways, which may add $10,000 to $25,000 to the project. We recommend budgeting for a soils test and engineering costs, typically ranging from $2,000 to $4,000, plus permits from Jefferson County, which can vary from $500 to $2,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can range up to $80,000+ for more complex setups like Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs). Factors such as site conditions, soil type, and local permitting requirements can significantly influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The length of time for septic installation can vary based on site conditions, but it generally takes anywhere from several days to a couple of weeks. Factors such as weather, access to the site, and the complexity of the system being installed can impact the timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In Jefferson County, due to the challenging mountain soils, most properties require engineered septic systems as per Regulation 43. A thorough soils test will determine the type of system needed for your specific property, ensuring compliance and effective waste treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities, which informs the appropriate septic system design for your property.
