Local Challenges in Saguache County

The terrain of Crestone presents unique challenges for septic system installations. The area's rocky granite and clay soils, coupled with the high elevation, complicate the installation process. Many properties sit on steep driveways that limit access, especially during Colorado‚Äôs harsh winters, where heavy snow and extreme temperature swings can severely impact installation timelines. Additionally, the frost line often lies between 36-48 inches deep, necessitating deeper excavations for septic tanks. This is crucial when considering how to winterize your septic installation to prevent freeze damage. Given these factors, homeowners must conduct thorough soil testing and obtain the appropriate septic permit from the Saguache County Building Department before beginning any work.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is comprehensive and tailored to meet the specific needs of your property. Here‚Äôs how it works:

  1. Initial Consultation: We discuss your requirements, site conditions, and schedule a soil test.
  2. Soil Testing: Our team conducts soil tests by digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to assess soil composition and determine the appropriate system. This step is crucial as it informs the design of your engineered septic system.
  3. System Design: Based on the soil report, we create a customized septic system design that complies with Regulation 43.
  4. Permitting: We assist you in obtaining the necessary permits from the Saguache County Building Department, ensuring all local regulations are met.
  5. Installation: Our experienced crew handles excavation, installation of the septic tank, and connection to the leach field or drain field, as per the engineered design.
  6. Final Inspection: After installation, we coordinate with local authorities for the required septic inspection to ensure all standards are adhered to.
  7. Maintenance Guidance: We provide you with tips on maintaining your system to prolong its life and efficiency.

Typically, the installation process can span several weeks, depending on weather conditions and site accessibility.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Crestone

The cost of septic installation in Crestone varies significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions and site accessibility. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of the typical costs you can expect:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: $2,000 - $4,000
  • Permits: $500 - $2,000 (depending on Saguache County regulations)
  • Excavation: $8,000 - $20,000+ based on site challenges such as rocky terrain and steep access.
  • Septic Tank and Components: $5,000 - $15,000

Considering these factors, a basic engineered system typically starts at around $35,000 and can range up to $80,000+ for advanced systems in challenging terrains. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000+ for advanced systems, especially in challenging terrains like Crestone.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Installation timelines vary but typically span several weeks, depending on factors like the complexity of the site, weather conditions, and permitting processes.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Crestone will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging mountain soils, particularly under Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil quality, which helps determine the best type of septic system for your property.
