Local Challenges in Fremont County

Excavating in Florence comes with its own set of challenges. The local soil composition includes rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay in the valleys, all of which can complicate site preparation. Properties often feature steep driveways with limited access during winter months, making excavation work particularly tricky. Moreover, with a frost line that varies between 36 to 48 inches at this elevation, it's crucial to ensure all excavation is done to the correct depth to prevent future structural issues.

The weather in Fremont County can also pose challenges, from heavy snowfall to sudden thunderstorms, which can interrupt construction timelines. Understanding these local challenges is vital for successful excavation and construction projects. By choosing a seasoned excavation contractor, you can mitigate these risks and ensure your project is completed correctly, on time, and within budget.

Our Excavation Services Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive step-by-step process to ensure your excavation needs are met with precision and care:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your project requirements, budget, and timeline.
  2. Site Assessment: Our team conducts an on-site evaluation to assess soil conditions, drainage, and access challenges that might affect the excavation.
  3. Planning and Permits: We handle all necessary permits through the Fremont County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Site Preparation: This involves land clearing, grading, and any required trenching. We utilize heavy machinery such as backhoes and bobcats to efficiently prepare the site.
  5. Excavating: We will conduct the necessary excavation work based on your project's specifications, whether it be for a foundation excavation or utility trenching.
  6. Final Grading: After excavation, we ensure that the site is properly graded to facilitate drainage and prepare for the next phases of construction.

This entire process typically takes several weeks, depending on project size and complexity. Choosing our expert team means you can expect timely and quality work from start to finish.

Cost Factors for Excavation Services in Florence

Understanding the costs associated with excavation services is essential for budgeting your project. Below are some typical price ranges:

  • Site Preparation/Clearing: Basic lot clearing typically starts from $8,000 per acre, while heavy brush and tree removal can range from $5,000 to $15,000 per acre.
  • Driveway Construction: For a gravel driveway, expect costs between $15 to $35 per linear foot. A standard 200-foot driveway may cost around $10,000.
  • Foundation Excavation: For a typical basement dig (about 1,500 sq ft), costs can run from $15,000 to $30,000. Rock excavation significantly increases costs by 50-200%.
  • Utility Trenching: Standard trenching ranges from $8 to $15 per linear foot, while rocky conditions may spike costs to between $20 to $50 per linear foot.

Additionally, when planning for water cisterns, consider that the size should be based on the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated as bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. Total installed costs for cisterns include the tank, excavation, pumps, and trenching, and can vary widely based on conditions.

As you can see, excavation costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, access, and the specific work required. We advise planning for the higher end of the ranges to avoid any budget surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs typically start around $50 to $100 per cubic yard, depending on the specific site conditions and material being excavated. Rocky or difficult terrain can significantly increase costs.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration of site preparation can vary but generally takes several weeks for typical projects. Factors such as site conditions, weather, and the specific requirements of the project can influence the timeline.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: For mountain excavation, equipment such as excavators, bobcats, and dump trucks are essential. The choice of equipment often depends on the terrain and specific requirements of the project.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are required for excavation projects in Florence and must be obtained through the Fremont County Building Department. This ensures that your project complies with local regulations and safety standards.
