Why Malta Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Malta, at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges regarding water supply. With deep water tables often making wells impractical, many residents rely on cistern installation as a reliable solution for their water needs. This is where professional help becomes invaluable. A skilled cistern installer near me understands the local terrain, soil composition, and weather patterns, ensuring that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly to provide safe, potable water. Professional installation not only guarantees compliance with Lake County County permit requirements but also ensures that your system is designed for the specific needs of your household, whether for daily use or emergency supply.

Local Challenges in Lake County County

The unique geography of Lake County County, particularly in Malta, presents several challenges for homeowners considering a water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs. Additionally, the frost line averages between 36 to 48 inches, necessitating special considerations for depth to prevent freezing, especially during the harsh winter months. Access is another concern, with many properties featuring steep driveways that can limit vehicle access during heavy snowfalls, complicating both installation and ongoing water delivery. Moreover, the extreme temperature fluctuations and potential for afternoon thunderstorms can impact the reliability of surface water sources, making a professionally installed water cistern essential for maintaining a dependable water supply throughout the year.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed process to ensure your cistern installation meets your needs and complies with local regulations. First, we conduct a thorough site assessment to evaluate soil conditions and determine the best location for your underground water storage tank. This assessment is crucial in understanding the challenges presented by rocky terrain and access limitations.

Once the site is approved, we will apply for the necessary permits through the Lake County County Building Department. After securing permits, we begin excavation, which can take 1-2 weeks depending on site conditions. Our skilled team then installs the cistern, typically a Norwesco tank, which can range from 5,000 to 10,000 gallons in capacity, or larger if needed. The installation includes necessary plumbing for your household system, ensuring proper water flow and pressure.

Throughout the process, we prioritize safety and efficiency, aiming to complete installations before winter to avoid complications with frost and snow. Finally, we provide guidance on winterizing your cistern installation to protect against freezing temperatures, ensuring you have a reliable potable water storage tank year-round.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Malta

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Malta is crucial for planning your project. The total installed cost for a 5,000 gallon basic system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000 gallon system will generally fall between $40,000 and $55,000. The size of your tank is primarily determined by daily water usage, calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of 6 people would require approximately 600 gallons per day, translating to a minimum tank size of around 18,000 gallons if you prefer monthly refills.

Costs can vary significantly based on several factors, including the difficulty of excavation due to rocky terrain, which can increase costs by 20-40%. Additionally, factors such as access limitations and the necessity of specialized equipment for remote sites can further elevate expenses. Other installation costs include the pump system, trenching for plumbing, and potential electrical hookups, all of which contribute to the final price.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and installation complexity. For a basic 5,000 gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a larger 10,000 gallon system typically costs between $40,000 and $55,000, with additional site-specific factors influencing the total price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size you need depends on your household's daily water usage. A general rule is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home (assuming 6 people) would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, while larger tanks may be necessary for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes 1-2 weeks from the start of excavation to the final setup, depending on site conditions and weather. It's advisable to schedule installations in the spring or summer to avoid winter complications.
