Local Challenges in Lake County

Malta's high elevation and rugged terrain create specific challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil often found in Lake County can complicate excavation and increase costs. Moreover, the local water table is frequently deep, making many properties reliant on water hauling or potable water storage solutions like underground cisterns. These factors make it crucial to work with a knowledgeable cistern company that understands the unique geological conditions.

Additionally, winter weather in this mountain community can be extreme, with heavy snow loads and temperature fluctuations. The frost line typically reaches depths of 36-48 inches or more, necessitating proper winterizing cistern installation and freeze protection measures. Steep driveways can also limit access for delivery trucks, making larger storage tanks a smart investment to avoid frequent refills during the winter months. Effective planning and geographical awareness are essential for a successful cistern installation.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for water storage tank installation to ensure your system operates efficiently and reliably:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a comprehensive assessment of your property, discussing your water needs and reviewing local regulations, including permits from the Lake County Building Department.

  3. Design and Sizing: Based on your daily water usage and desired refill frequency, we will recommend the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require approximately 36,000-54,000 gallons for a 2-3 month refill schedule.
  4. Excavation and Installation: We handle all excavation and installation tasks, ensuring the tank is buried correctly and safely. Our team is experienced in navigating rocky terrain, minimizing disruption to your property.
  5. System Testing: After installation, we conduct thorough testing to confirm that the system works efficiently and meets safety standards.
  6. Winterization: To prepare for winter, we implement necessary frost-proof measures and advise on ongoing maintenance to ensure your cistern remains operational throughout the colder months.

This entire process typically takes several weeks, depending on the complexity of the installation and local weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Malta

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for budget planning. The total installed cost for a standard system can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and the specific components required. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon system typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. Installation of larger systems, such as dual tank configurations, could exceed $75,000.

When determining the right size for your potable water storage tank, consider your daily water usage. A general estimate is 100 gallons per person per day, with occupancy typically being two people per bedroom. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would need around 600 gallons daily. If you plan to refill every month, you would require a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons.

Other factors affecting costs include:

  • Excavation Costs: Depending on soil conditions and access, excavation can add between $3,000 and $8,000 or more.
  • Pump and Pressure System: A pump system with a pressure tank typically costs between $2,500 and $5,000.
  • Additional Features: Installation of risers and access lids can range from $500 to $1,500, while electrical hookups may add another $1,000 to $2,500.
  • Water Filtration: Depending on your water quality needs, filtration systems can run from $2,000 to $5,000.
