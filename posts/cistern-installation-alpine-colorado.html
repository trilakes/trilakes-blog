Properties at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet often face issues with accessing a reliable water source. For homeowners without a well, cistern installation becomes essential. These underground water storage tanks not only provide a dependable supply of potable water but also offer crucial support for irrigation and fire suppression needs.

Local Challenges in Chaffee County County

The Alpine region presents a variety of challenges for cistern installation due to its high elevation and rugged terrain. The soil here often consists of rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay in valleys, making excavation a labor-intensive process that can significantly impact project costs. Furthermore, the local water table tends to be deep, which often necessitates the use of water hauling to fill your cistern, especially for those who don't have reliable wells.

Winter preparation is another critical aspect of cistern installation in this mountainous area. The frost line ranges from 36-48 inches, depending on elevation, meaning your buried underground cistern must be installed deep enough to avoid freeze damage. The extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall exacerbate the need for robust systems designed for frost protection. Lastly, many properties have steep driveways, limiting access during winter months and complicating the installation process.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Alpine

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for homeowners in Alpine. The overall price can vary significantly based on specific site conditions, such as rocky soil and accessibility. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of the factors that influence costs:

  • Tank Size: The size of your potable water storage tank is primarily determined by your daily water usage, calculated as follows: 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms and assumed occupants. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require at least 600 gallons/day.
  • Installation Costs: Installation costs start from approximately $40,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Additional expenses include excavation (starting at $8,000), pump system and pressure tank (around $5,000), electrical hookups ($2,500), and water filtration systems (up to $5,000).
  • Terrain & Access: Expect excavation costs to increase by 20-40% if your property is rocky or difficult to access. Winter installations can also drive up costs due to labor and equipment needed for harsh conditions.

As a rule of thumb, larger cisterns can save you money in the long run by reducing the frequency of refills, especially in winter when water delivery may be limited. For more detailed pricing, consider reading our Cistern Cost Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system when fully installed. This includes the tank, excavation, pump systems, and other necessary components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank, calculate your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require at least a 10,000-gallon tank if you want to refill every few months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary but typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, permits, and weather. Winter installations can take longer due to frost and access issues.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific needs and site conditions. Cisterns are ideal for properties with deep water tables or unreliable wells, while wells can provide a continuous source of water if conditions allow.
