Local Challenges in Chaffee County

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process to ensure your water storage tank installation is seamless and efficient. Our first step is to conduct an on-site consultation where we assess your specific needs based on your home size, water usage, and desired refill frequency. This initial assessment helps us determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage.

Next, we obtain the necessary permits through the Chaffee County Building Department, as all cistern installations must comply with local regulations. Once permits are secured, we can begin the excavation phase, which typically lasts 1-2 days, depending on site conditions. During this phase, we carefully excavate the area for the underground cistern, accounting for rocky terrain that may increase costs by 20-40%.

Following excavation, we install the tank, which can take another day or two. After this, we will set up the pump system and electrical connections, ensuring everything works smoothly. Finally, we conduct a thorough inspection to confirm your system meets safety standards before we conclude the project. On average, the entire installation process takes about a week, but this can vary based on site-specific factors.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Alpine

The cost of cistern installation in Alpine can vary significantly depending on several factors. For example, the size of the cistern is determined by the number of bedrooms in your home, with the calculation being 100 gallons per person per day, assuming two people per bedroom. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 18,000 gallons for a monthly refill, which translates to a high-end cost of around $40,000-$55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado generally starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $120,000 for larger installations. Costs vary greatly based on site-specific conditions, including excavation challenges and tank size.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: Your tank size is typically calculated based on your home's bedroom count and expected water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home will require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for a monthly refill, which can be achieved with a combination of tanks if necessary.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation usually takes about a week, depending on site conditions and permitting processes. Factors like rocky terrain can extend the timeline, particularly during excavation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on availability and reliability. In many rural areas, wells can be too deep or unreliable, making cisterns a better option for consistent water supply and lower maintenance.
