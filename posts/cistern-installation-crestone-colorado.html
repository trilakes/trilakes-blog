Local Challenges in Saguache County

The terrain in Crestone can be unforgiving, characterized by rocky granite and clay-rich valleys that complicate any construction project. The water table in this mountainous area can often be very deep, making traditional well drilling impractical or even impossible for some properties. As a result, many homeowners must rely on water hauling to meet their needs, which can be costly and inconvenient. Furthermore, the frost line here is between 36 to 48 inches, necessitating deeper installations of buried water tanks to prevent freezing during harsh winters. The building season typically spans from May to October, allowing limited time for installation. Homeowners must also consider access issues, as steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder both construction and ongoing water delivery services.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Crestone

The cost of cistern installation in Crestone can vary significantly based on several factors. One primary factor is the size of the underground water storage tank needed. A general rule of thumb is to calculate daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example:

  • A 2-bedroom home typically requires around 400 gallons/day.
  • A 3-bedroom home would need approximately 600 gallons/day.
  • A 4-bedroom home might require about 800 gallons/day.

To determine the total capacity of your system, consider how often you‚Äôll want to refill the tank. If you want to refill every 2-3 months, you might need a tank that holds 36,000-54,000 gallons, while a system designed to get you through winter could require 72,000 gallons or more.

Here‚Äôs an overview of typical costs for water storage tank installation:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Excavation costs: $3,000-$8,000+ depending on rocky terrain
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to house (per linear foot): $25-$50
  • Electrical hookups: $1,000-$2,500

Considering these factors, a total installed system for a 10,000-gallon capacity can range from $40,000 to $55,000. Remember, rocky and difficult terrain can increase costs by 20-40%. Larger tanks often lead to fewer refills and can save you money on ongoing water hauling costs, which typically runs $200-$350 per 3,000 gallons.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. This price can increase significantly based on site conditions, excavation challenges, and the need for additional systems like pumps and electrical setups.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, a 3-bedroom home may require a tank capable of holding 36,000-54,000 gallons to avoid frequent refills during winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation usually ranges from 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site conditions and the time required for obtaining necessary permits from the local building department.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. In areas like Crestone, where the water table may be too deep for reliable well drilling, a cistern is often a more practical and effective solution for a consistent water supply.
