Building and maintaining a water system in Saguache County poses several challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils complicate excavation, often requiring specialized equipment and techniques. Additionally, the water table is typically deep, making water hauling a common necessity for many residents. Properties in this region may also face winter access issues, as steep driveways and heavy snowfall can limit deliveries. Furthermore, with a frost line that can reach 48 inches at higher elevations, it‚Äôs essential to consider winterizing cistern installation to protect your investment from freezing temperatures. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Crestone

When considering cistern installation in Crestone, it's essential to understand the costs involved. The price of an underground water storage tank can vary significantly based on factors such as tank size, excavation challenges, and additional system components. For a standard installation, you can expect costs to start around:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000 (tank only)
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000 (tank only)

The total installed system cost will typically range from $25,000 to over $120,000, depending on the system's capacity and site conditions. For instance, if you have a three-bedroom home, you would need at least 36,000 gallons (for quarterly refills). This means you might be looking at a dual-tank system, elevating costs into the $50,000-$75,000+ range.

Additional costs can arise from the rocky terrain, which often adds 20-40% to excavation expenses, and the potential need for specialized excavation equipment. Moreover, winter installations can be more expensive due to challenging ground conditions, and remote access can significantly impact the overall cost. Always remember, the more significant your upfront investment in a larger tank, the fewer refills you will need, translating to long-term savings.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies widely depending on the size and installation needs. For a typical installation, expect to pay starting costs around $25,000 for a 5,000-gallon system, with larger systems reaching up to $120,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your home‚Äôs bedroom count and daily water usage. A simple formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms by two people and then by 100 gallons per day. For a three-bedroom home, this would typically require at least 36,000 gallons for quarterly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and required permits. A thorough site evaluation and necessary excavation work can extend the timeline, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your property's specific conditions. Wells can be expensive and impractical if the water table is too deep, while cisterns provide a reliable alternative for potable water storage, especially in rural areas where water delivery is a consideration.
