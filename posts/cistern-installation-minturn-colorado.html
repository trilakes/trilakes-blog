The total cost will depend on various factors, including excavation needs, tank size, and additional systems required for operation.

When sizing your buried water tank, consider your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms. For instance, a three-bedroom home will require around 600 gallons per day. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: Cistern installation costs in Colorado typically start from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, ranging up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank. This includes excavation, tank installation, and necessary pump systems, but costs can vary based on site conditions and additional features.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a three-bedroom home will likely require a tank capable of holding 18,000 gallons minimum if you refill monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process for a cistern typically spans several weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, excavation needs, and weather. We prioritize thorough planning to ensure a smooth installation without unexpected delays.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific site conditions. For properties where the water table is too deep or wells are unreliable, a cistern installation becomes a practical alternative. Cisterns provide flexibility and can serve as backup systems for low-yield wells.
