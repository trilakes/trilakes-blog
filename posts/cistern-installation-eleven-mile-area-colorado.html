Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado varies widely, but you can expect to pay between $25,000 to $55,000 for a complete system, depending on the size and complexity of the installation. Factors such as excavation challenges and the need for additional systems can push costs higher.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be calculated based on your daily water usage, which is typically around 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants might need a tank that holds anywhere from 36,000 to 54,000 gallons, depending on how often you want to refill.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take between two to four weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and equipment availability. Factors such as rocky terrain and access difficulties can prolong the process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. Cisterns are often a better option for areas with deep water tables or where well drilling is impractical. They offer flexibility and can serve as a reliable backup water supply, while wells can be more costly to maintain, especially in challenging geological conditions.
