Local Challenges in Park County

The Eleven Mile Area is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, making septic installation a complex endeavor. Most properties here require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions that often necessitate deep excavation. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, proper planning for winter weather is essential; freezing temperatures can damage poorly installed systems. Additionally, many sites have steep driveways and limited winter access, which complicates the installation process. The local water table is often quite deep, necessitating the use of cisterns for water supply, further complicating the septic system setup. Understanding these local challenges is key for any homeowner considering a new system.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure that all systems meet local regulations and function optimally. First, we conduct soil testing to assess the soil's composition. This involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, allowing us to gather soil samples at various depths for a licensed engineer's report. Based on the findings, we determine the best type of system for your property, whether it‚Äôs a conventional gravity system or an engineered solution.

Once the soil analysis is complete, we will guide you through obtaining the necessary septic permits from the Park County Building Department. With permits in hand, our team will schedule excavation, typically during the May to October building season, depending on weather conditions. After excavation, we will install the septic tank and any necessary components, such as a leach field or engineered systems like a sand filter or ATU. We ensure proper backfilling and site restoration before conducting a final inspection to confirm everything is in compliance with Regulation 43. This meticulous approach minimizes the likelihood of system failure and maximizes longevity.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Eleven Mile Area

When considering septic installation in the Eleven Mile Area, it‚Äôs crucial to understand the associated costs. Installing an engineered septic system typically starts from $35,000 and can go up to $80,000 or more, depending on site conditions and system requirements. For example, sand filter systems usually range from $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATU) can cost between $55,000 and $80,000. The price can vary significantly based on factors such as rocky terrain, difficult access, and the specific type of system needed.

If you require a cistern, the size is determined by the number of bedrooms in your home. A good rule of thumb is to calculate 2 people per bedroom, multiplied by 100 gallons per day, to ensure your system can handle your household needs. Additional costs include soil testing and engineering, which can add $2,000 to $4,000, and permits that usually range from $500 to $2,000 depending on the county. Excavation costs can fall between $8,000 and $20,000 based on site complexity, while the tank and components can range from $5,000 to $15,000.

Expert Tip: Always budget for the high end of the price range, as rocky or difficult terrain can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your installation costs. Planning for these factors ensures you‚Äôre prepared for any surprises during the installation process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000 or more, depending on the type of system and site conditions. Engineered systems are the most common due to challenging mountain soils, with prices varying based on excavation complexity and soil conditions.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes several weeks from the initial soil testing to final inspection and approval. The actual installation phase can take 1 to 2 weeks, depending on weather conditions and site access.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Eleven Mile Area require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and the regulations outlined in Regulation 43. A soils report will determine the best system for your specific property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil testing methods that analyze soil composition for septic system design. This involves digging test holes to assess the soil's ability to absorb water, which is crucial for determining the appropriate system type.
