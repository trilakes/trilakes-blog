Why Bond Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in Bond, Eagle County County, at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for septic installation. The renowned mountain terrain requires expertise that ensures your onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS) functions effectively. Without professional guidance, you risk costly mistakes that can lead to system failures, environmental hazards, and legal troubles with local regulations. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the specific needs of our mountain community, ensuring that your septic installation not only meets but exceeds local standards.

Local Challenges in Eagle County County

The landscape of Bond is as breathtaking as it is challenging for septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay-rich soil can complicate the installation process, often necessitating engineered systems to accommodate the unique conditions. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep, meaning properties frequently require cisterns for water supply. Given the frost line at 36-48 inches, proper winterization becomes essential to prevent freezing issues. Moreover, steep driveways and limited access during winter months can further complicate installation. Understanding these challenges is critical to ensuring a successful septic system that will endure the harsh Colorado winters.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and systematic process to ensure your septic system is installed with precision and care.

  • Initial Consultation: We begin by discussing your specific needs and assessing your property. This includes a preliminary review of the terrain and soil.
  • Soil Testing: We perform comprehensive soil testing, not traditional perc tests, using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. Soil samples are sent to a licensed engineer for evaluation.
  • Design and Permitting: Based on the soils report, we design an engineered septic system tailored to your property. We assist with obtaining necessary permits through the Eagle County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.
  • Installation: Our trained team will perform the installation, including excavation, tank placement, and setting up the leach field or drain field as per the approved design.
  • Final Inspection: After installation, we ensure the system is inspected to meet all local regulations, providing peace of mind that your system is safe and effective.

This entire process typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Our team is committed to transparency, ensuring you're informed every step of the way.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Bond

When considering septic installation in Bond, it's essential to budget for various factors that can influence costs. The total installed costs for an engineered septic system generally range from $35,000 to $50,000. This includes crucial components like excavation, the septic tank, and necessary permits, which can vary from $500 to $2,000 depending on local regulations.

If your property requires a sand filter system, expect costs to increase to between $45,000 and $65,000. For advanced treatment units (ATUs), designed to provide a higher level of wastewater treatment, costs can escalate to $55,000 to $80,000+. Factors such as rocky terrain or difficult access can add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to your total costs.

It's also important to consider cistern sizing, which is based on the number of bedrooms. The formula is straightforward: multiply the bedroom count by two people per bedroom, then by 100 gallons per day. This ensures adequate water supply for your household.

For an accurate estimate tailored to your specific needs and conditions, consult with a licensed septic contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly, typically ranging from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems. More advanced systems, like sand filters or ATUs, can increase costs to between $45,000 and $80,000+, depending on site conditions.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation usually spans several weeks. This includes soil testing, design, permitting, and the actual installation process. Factors such as weather and site conditions can influence the schedule.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Bond require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations outlined in Regulation 43. These systems are designed to properly treat wastewater and protect the environment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing, not traditional perc tests. This involves excavating test holes to analyze soil composition and determine the appropriate septic system design, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
