Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Empire's rugged mountainous terrain, characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, makes septic installation a complex process. The water table is often deep, necessitating engineered systems rather than standard systems. Additionally, the frost line in this area can reach depths of 36-48 inches, which complicates installation during winter months. Heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms add to the challenges, creating limited access during certain seasons. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic system installation to ensure optimal performance and compliance with local regulations. Here‚Äôs how it works:

  1. Initial Consultation: We start with a detailed consultation to understand your needs and property specifics.
  2. Soil Testing: Our team conducts soil testing, not traditional perc tests. This includes digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze soil composition. A licensed engineer reviews the samples and prepares a soils report, determining the appropriate type of system needed.
  3. Design and Permitting: Based on the soils report, we design an engineered septic system tailored to your property. We then submit the necessary plans to the Clear Creek County Building Department for permit approval.
  4. Installation: Once we receive the permit, we mobilize our team to begin installation. This typically occurs during the building season from May to October, given the elevation and weather conditions.
  5. Final Inspection: After installation is complete, we schedule a final inspection to ensure everything meets local and state regulations.

This thorough process ensures that your septic system is installed correctly and efficiently, providing long-term reliability.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Empire

The cost of septic installation in Empire typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the specific needs of your property. Factors influencing this price include:

  • Soils Testing and Engineering Costs: This initial step can cost between $2,000 and $4,000.
  • Permits: Obtaining the necessary permits from the Clear Creek County Building Department can range from $500 to $2,000.
  • Excavation and Installation: Excavation costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, typically ranging from $8,000 to $20,000 or more. Rocky terrain can increase costs by $10,000 to $25,000.
  • Type of Septic System: Conventional systems are rare in this region; most properties require engineered systems, with costs as follows:
    • Pressure-Dosed System: $30,000-$50,000
    • Sand Filter System: $45,000-$65,000
    • Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU): $55,000-$80,000+

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000. This wide range is due to factors like soil type, site access, and the specific type of system required for your property.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but generally takes 1 to 2 weeks to complete once permits are approved. This includes soil testing, design, installation, and final inspection.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in high-elevation areas like Empire require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions. Traditional septic systems are often not viable, and engineered systems provide better performance and longevity.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which informs the design of the appropriate septic system for your property.
