Local Challenges in El Paso County County

The challenges of installing a water cistern in El Paso County are multifaceted. The local soil consists primarily of rocky granite and clay, which complicates excavation and may lead to increased costs. At elevations above 8,000 feet, the frost line sinks to a depth of 36-48 inches, necessitating deeper installations for adequate freeze protection in winter. Additionally, the weather can be extreme, with heavy snow and afternoon thunderstorms, which can hinder access to remote properties during critical installation periods. Many homeowners must also consider fire mitigation strategies due to the forested environment, making the choice of a reliable cistern company crucial for both safety and functionality.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado varies significantly based on size and site conditions. For a 5,000-gallon system, expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can cost upwards of $120,000, depending on excavation and installation specifics.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your household's water usage. A general guideline is to calculate 100 gallons per day per person, multiplied by the number of bedrooms. For example, a 3-bedroom home may need at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern typically ranges from a few weeks to a couple of months, depending on weather conditions, site accessibility, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors. Cisterns serve as a reliable well alternative when water tables are deep or wells are impractical. Cisterns provide flexibility for water storage and can be designed to meet specific needs, making them a favorable option in many mountain properties.
