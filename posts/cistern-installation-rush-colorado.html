Why Rush Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the beautiful mountain community of Rush, Colorado, the elevation of over 8,000 feet presents unique challenges when it comes to water supply. Many properties here lack a reliable well, making cistern installation a vital solution for homeowners. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have access to potable water year-round, whether for residential use, irrigation, or livestock. With the extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall that characterize our region, having a robust water storage system can safeguard your home and family. Choosing an experienced cistern installer near me like Tri-Lakes Contracting provides peace of mind that your system will be implemented correctly and efficiently, adhering to all local regulations.

Local Challenges in El Paso County County

Residents in Rush face a variety of challenges related to water supply and storage due to the area's rugged terrain and climate. The geological composition often includes rocky granite and decomposed granite, which can complicate excavation for your water cistern. Additionally, the water table can be quite deep, making a cistern installation not just a preference but a necessity for many properties. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, proper winterization is crucial to avoid freezing pipes and tanks. The challenging access to many homes, especially during winter months, further underscores the need for a reliable water storage solution. Fire mitigation is another important consideration in forested areas, where having a fire suppression water system could be a lifesaver.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and site conditions. For a basic 5,000 gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000. Larger systems, such as a 10,000 gallon tank, typically range from $40,000 to $55,000, depending on excavation and additional equipment needed.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need is primarily based on your household's daily water usage and how often you plan to refill. A general rule is to estimate 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with 6 residents would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilling monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation typically ranges from a few weeks to several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. We work efficiently to complete installations, especially before the winter season, to ensure you have a reliable water source.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's water availability. In areas like Rush, where the water table can be deep and wells may be impractical, a cistern installation offers a reliable alternative for both potable and agricultural water storage.
