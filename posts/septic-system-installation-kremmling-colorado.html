Local Challenges in Grand County

The unique geology and climate of Kremmling present distinct challenges for septic system installations. Rocky granite and clay soils are common, often necessitating engineered septic systems, which are required by Regulation 43. Additionally, the high water table in many areas can complicate drainage, requiring homeowners to consider alternatives like cisterns for water supply. The frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, making proper winterization of your septic tank crucial to avoid damage during the harsh winters. Moreover, steep driveways and limited access in many mountain properties can further complicate installation logistics. Understanding these local challenges is essential for any homeowner looking to invest in a reliable septic system.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a thorough, step-by-step process for installing your septic system. First, we conduct a comprehensive soils test to assess the condition of your land. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to determine soil composition, which informs the type of system needed. This report is crucial and is often performed before purchasing land to ensure the property is suitable for building.

Once the soils report is obtained, we work closely with you to design a system that complies with local regulations and meets your needs. We then handle the necessary permits through the Grand County Building Department, which can range from $500 to $2,000. After securing the permits, the actual installation begins. For most mountain properties, this typically happens between May and October, when conditions are more favorable.

Installation involves excavating the site, installing the septic tank, and creating the drain field or leach field. The entire process can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions and complexity. Finally, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before handing over the system to you.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Kremmling

The cost of septic installation in Kremmling can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, system type, and site accessibility. A typical engineered system will start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000, depending on the specific requirements of your property. For homes needing advanced systems like a sand filter system or an ATU system, costs can range from $45,000 to $75,000 or more.

In addition to system costs, factors such as rocky terrain, limited access, and the size of the soil treatment area can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your overall installation cost. For water needs, homeowners should consider cistern sizing based on bedroom count‚Äîtypically calculated at 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. Therefore, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern with a capacity of around 6,000 gallons. This water management is essential, especially in areas where water table depth may complicate your on-site wastewater management.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, septic installation costs typically range from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system. More complex systems, such as sand filters or ATU systems, can increase costs to between $45,000 and $75,000, depending on the specific site conditions.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system in Kremmling can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. This timeline depends on various factors, including site conditions, permitting processes, and weather.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Kremmling require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and elevation. A soils report will determine the specific system needed based on your land‚Äôs characteristics.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which informs the type of septic system required for your property.
