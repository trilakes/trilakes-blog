Local Challenges in Teller County

Excavating in Florissant is a task that comes with its own set of challenges, primarily due to the area's rocky granite and clay soil conditions. The water table can be quite deep, often necessitating the installation of cisterns for water hauling. Most properties in the region require engineered septic systems because of the challenging soil conditions. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, especially at higher elevations, creating complications for foundation excavation. Homeowners should also be mindful of the steep driveways and limited winter access that can hinder excavation activities. Weather conditions are another aspect to consider, as extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads can impact timelines and project planning.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs typically range from $50 to $200 per cubic yard, depending on site conditions, access, and the type of soil. More challenging rocky conditions can significantly increase prices.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The duration for site preparation can vary widely based on the project's scope, but most basic preparations are completed within a few days. Complex sites may take longer due to the need for additional earthmoving or rock removal.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Key equipment for mountain excavation includes excavators, bobcats, and dump trucks. Depending on the specific tasks, backhoes and specialized tools for rock removal might also be necessary.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are required for excavation projects in Teller County. It is essential to check with the Teller County Building Department for specific requirements related to your project.
