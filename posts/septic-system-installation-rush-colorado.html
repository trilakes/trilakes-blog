Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and designed to ensure that your system operates effectively for years to come. The first step is performing a soils test, where we use a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, collecting soil samples for a comprehensive report from a licensed engineer. This report guides us in determining the best type of system for your property. Following the approval of the soils report, we assist in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Once permitted, we begin excavation, which may take time depending on site conditions, such as rocky terrain or challenging access. Our team then installs the chosen septic tank and drainage fields, including leach and drain fields if needed. Finally, we conduct a septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly. The entire process typically spans several weeks, depending on various factors, including weather and site conditions.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Rush

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Rush is vital for homeowners. A basic engineered system can range from $35,000 to $50,000, while more complex systems like sand filter systems can cost between $45,000 and $65,000. For advanced treatment units (ATUs), the total can start at $55,000 and go up to $80,000+. These costs include soil testing, excavation, and the installation of the tank and components. For properties requiring cisterns, the size is typically based on the number of bedrooms, calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

It's important to note that factors such as rocky terrain, difficult access, and unique soil conditions can significantly increase costs. For example, installations in hard-to-access areas may add $10,000 to $25,000 to your total bill. Always consult with a qualified septic contractor to get a detailed estimate tailored to your property‚Äôs specific needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go as high as $80,000+ for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions and system complexity.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes several weeks from the initial soils test to the completion of installation, influenced by site conditions and weather.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In Rush, most properties require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and elevation, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43 and protecting groundwater.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to evaluate soil composition, which informs the design of your septic system.
