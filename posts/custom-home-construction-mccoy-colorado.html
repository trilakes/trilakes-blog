Local Challenges in Eagle County

McCoy presents several unique challenges when it comes to custom home building. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil conditions can complicate foundation engineering, often requiring advanced solutions to ensure stability. The water table frequently sits deep below the surface, which means many properties will necessitate cisterns or water hauling systems. Additionally, the frost line at this elevation can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning and engineering to prevent freeze damage. Steep driveways can limit access during winter months, making proper planning vital for construction. Moreover, the region's weather patterns include extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads, which require careful consideration during the design and construction phases. Fire mitigation is also paramount in this forested area, and adherence to defensible space requirements is essential for safety.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

Once permits are obtained, we begin construction, which typically spans 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of the project and weather conditions. Throughout the process, we maintain a transparent communication line, providing you with updates on the construction timeline and any challenges that may arise.
