Local Challenges in Lake County County

The beauty of Lake County comes with its own set of challenges for septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate the installation process, often necessitating engineered systems to comply with Colorado's Regulation 43. At high elevations, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, making winter preparation essential for your septic installation. Additionally, the steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder excavation efforts, adding to the complexity. As a result, properties in Malta often require extensive soil testing to determine the best approach for a viable drain field or leach field. These factors highlight the importance of hiring a skilled septic contractor who understands the local terrain and regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Malta

The cost of septic installation in Malta can vary significantly based on various factors. On average, homeowners can expect the following costs:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000, depending on the specific requirements set forth by the Lake County Building Department.
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000, which can increase substantially if the site has rocky terrain.
  • Tank and Components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if necessary): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical and Controls: $2,000-$4,000

In total, basic engineered systems range from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems can go from $45,000 to $65,000. For more advanced systems, such as ATUs, costs typically start at $55,000 and can exceed $80,000. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can range from $35,000 to over $80,000, depending on the type of system needed, soil conditions, and installation complexities. Most installations typically fall between $35,000 and $65,000 for engineered systems.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of a septic installation can vary, but the process usually takes a few weeks from start to finish, factoring in soil testing, permitting, and actual installation time, which can be longer in challenging terrains.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Malta, due to the rocky and challenging soil conditions, will require an engineered septic system as per Colorado‚Äôs Regulation 43. An assessment by a qualified engineer will determine the best system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?
