Local Challenges in Teller County

Cripple Creek's high elevation and rocky terrain present distinct challenges for water storage tank installation. The soil here varies from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, complicating excavation and installation processes. Additionally, the local water table is often quite deep, necessitating the use of cisterns or water hauling for most properties. The frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, which means any cistern installation must account for freeze protection, especially during harsh winters. With steep driveways and limited access during snowy months, contractors must be well-versed in working under these conditions. Fire mitigation is also critical in forested areas, making it important to consider fire suppression water storage needs when planning your cistern system.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Cripple Creek

When considering a water storage tank installation, understanding costs is vital. The size of your underground cistern will primarily depend on your household's daily water usage and how often you wish to refill it. The formula is fairly straightforward: assuming 2 people per bedroom consuming 100 gallons per day, a 3-bedroom home may require around 600 gallons of water daily. Based on refill frequency preferences:

  • Refilling every 2 weeks: minimum 8,400 gallons
  • Refilling monthly: minimum 18,000 gallons
  • Refilling every 2-3 months: 36,000-54,000 gallons
  • Winter survival: 72,000+ gallons (multiple tanks)

We typically install Norwesco tanks, which provide reliable options for potable water storage. For example, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank costs approximately $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000-$28,000. However, keep in mind that additional expenses may include excavation (estimated at $3,000-$8,000 based on rock availability), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and trenching to the house ($25-$50 per linear foot). Therefore, a basic installed system for a 5,000-gallon tank may range between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems could exceed $75,000. Factors like rocky terrain and limited access can significantly increase costs, sometimes adding 20-40% to excavation expenses.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $120,000 for larger systems exceeding 30,000 gallons. Factors such as soil conditions, accessibility, and the specific tank model will affect the final pricing.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your household's daily water usage, which is generally calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would need at least an 18,000-gallon tank if refilling monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The cistern installation process typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. Our team ensures that every step is completed efficiently while adhering to local regulations.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. In Cripple Creek, where the water table can be deep and unreliable, a cistern often serves as a better alternative, ensuring a consistent water supply without the complications of well drilling.
