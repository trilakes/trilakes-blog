Why Wetmore Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the scenic mountain community of Wetmore, Colorado, comes with its own set of unique challenges, especially when it comes to septic installation. At elevations above 8,000 feet, properties often face rocky soils, deep water tables, and extreme weather conditions that can complicate the installation of a septic system. Often, homeowners find themselves in need of an engineered system to comply with local regulations and ensure efficient performance. Using a professional septic installer near me like Tri-Lakes Contracting guarantees that you receive a system designed specifically for your property‚Äôs unique conditions, ultimately leading to a more reliable and long-lasting solution.

Local Challenges in Custer County

The local terrain in Wetmore includes rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay found in the valleys, making soils testing a critical first step in septic installation. Due to the high elevation, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning to prevent freeze-related issues in winter. Additionally, many properties have steep driveways and limited winter access, complicating construction logistics. With afternoon thunderstorms and heavy snow loads common, you need a robust septic system that can withstand these challenges. Understanding these factors is essential for homeowners who want to ensure their onsite wastewater systems operate efficiently year-round.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a thorough approach to septic installation to ensure your system meets both your needs and local regulations. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step overview of our process:

  1. Initial Consultation: We assess your property, discuss your needs, and explain the types of systems available.
  2. Soils Testing: We perform a detailed soils test using a mini excavator, digging two test holes to a depth of 8 feet. This allows us to analyze soil composition and structure.
  3. Septic Design: Based on the soils report, we design a customized septic system tailored to your property. Most properties will require an engineered system due to the challenging local soil conditions.
  4. Permitting: We help you obtain necessary permits from the Custer County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43 for Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS).
  5. Installation: Our experienced team will handle all aspects of installation, including excavation, tank placement, and connection to the drain field. We prioritize proper winterization practices to avoid freeze issues as we work.
  6. Final Inspection: After installation, we ensure all components are functioning correctly and schedule a septic inspection to verify compliance with local regulations.

This process typically spans several weeks, depending on weather conditions and permitting timelines. By following these steps, we ensure that your septic system not only meets regulatory standards but is also built to last in our unique mountain environment.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Wetmore

The cost of septic installation in Wetmore can vary widely based on several factors, including soil conditions, terrain accessibility, and the type of system required. For instance, the total costs for a basic engineered system typically start from $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. If you require a sand filter system, expect to invest between $45,000 and $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs), which provide enhanced treatment capabilities, can cost between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

When determining the size of a cistern, you can use the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. This is particularly important for properties without direct water access. Keep in mind that rocky terrain and difficult access can add between $10,000 and $25,000 to your installation costs. Additionally, the costs for obtaining permits can range from $500 to $2,000, depending on requirements set by the Custer County Building Department.

Understanding these cost factors will help you budget effectively and make informed decisions about your septic system installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on site conditions and system type. For basic engineered systems, prices typically range from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems range from $45,000 to $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) can cost between $55,000 and $80,000 or more, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically takes several weeks. This includes time for soils testing, obtaining permits, and the actual installation process, which may be affected by weather conditions and site accessibility.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Wetmore require an engineered septic system due to local soil conditions and regulations. An engineered system is designed to function effectively in challenging environments, ensuring compliance with Colorado‚Äôs OWTS regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not used. Instead, we perform soils testing that involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which determines the best septic system design for your property. This step is crucial in understanding the specific needs of your site.
