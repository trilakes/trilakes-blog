Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Pitkin's rugged terrain presents several local challenges when it comes to septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils often require engineered septic systems, as conventional options are infrequently feasible. Additionally, the deep water table can complicate drainage, leading many homeowners to rely on cisterns for water supply. Furthermore, the frost line in this region extends 36-48 inches deep, making winter preparation and system winterization pivotal to prevent freeze damage. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation that ensures you receive the best system for your property. Our first step involves conducting a thorough soil testing analysis. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, collecting samples for a licensed engineer to assess the appropriate system type based on soil conditions. Following this, we guide you through obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Gunnison County Building Department, as required by Regulation 43 for all Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS).

Once the permit is secured, we can begin the excavation process, which typically takes place from May to October due to weather limitations. Our team will install the septic tank and associated components, including the drain field or leach field, while ensuring compliance with all setback requirements. Finally, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to confirm that the system is operating correctly before we complete the project. This step-by-step approach ensures that you receive a reliable, long-lasting system designed for the unique challenges of Pitkin.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Pitkin

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Pitkin is essential for homeowners. Typically, the installation of an engineered septic system can range from $35,000 to $50,000. If a sand filter system is required, costs may increase to around $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can run from $55,000 to $80,000+. It‚Äôs crucial to keep in mind that these ranges can vary significantly based on site conditions, including rocky terrain and access challenges.

For properties needing a water cistern, the size is determined by the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. For example, a three-bedroom home would typically require a cistern capacity of 600 gallons per day. Other cost factors include the soils test and engineering, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, along with excavation costs from $8,000 to $20,000+. As a homeowner, being aware of these details enables you to plan and budget effectively for your septic system needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system, $45,000 to $65,000 for a sand filter system, and $55,000 to $80,000+ for advanced treatment units. These prices can fluctuate based on site conditions and specific system requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system generally takes several days to a few weeks, depending on factors such as permitting delays, weather conditions, and site accessibility. Installation typically occurs during the warmer months, from May to October, in the Pitkin area.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Pitkin will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and high elevation. These systems are designed to effectively manage onsite wastewater and comply with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by comprehensive soils tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil conditions, which helps determine the best septic system for your property.
