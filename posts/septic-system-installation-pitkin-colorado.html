Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Gunnison County is renowned for its breathtaking mountain scenery, but this beauty is matched by its challenges when it comes to septic system installation. The region's soil composition typically includes rocky granite and clay, which complicates the standard drainage and absorption processes. With a water table that is often deep, many properties require supplementary systems like cisterns for water supply, adding another layer of complexity. The frost line here can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, meaning proper winterization and freeze protection are essential considerations for any septic installation. Additionally, steep driveways and limited access during winter months further complicate installation logistics. Homeowners must also be aware of fire mitigation practices in forested areas, focusing on creating defensible space to protect their properties.

Our Septic System Installation Process

  1. Site Assessment: We begin with a detailed site assessment, including soil testing to determine the appropriate system type. This step may involve digging two 8-foot deep test holes to collect soil samples.
  2. Design and Engineering: Based on the soil report, our engineers design an engineered septic system that meets your property‚Äôs needs and complies with Gunnison County permit regulations.
  3. Permitting: We handle the necessary permits, which typically range from $500 to $2,000, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Installation: Our team expertly installs the system, including the tank, leach field, and necessary components, taking care to align with the frost line requirements for winter protection.
  5. Final Inspection: After installation, we conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything meets regulatory requirements before handing over the completed project to you.

This entire process can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Our priority is to ensure that your septic system is installed correctly, efficiently, and in compliance with all regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Pitkin

The cost of septic installation in Pitkin can vary significantly based on several factors. Here's a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Soils Testing and Engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank and Components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs): $8,000-$15,000 (if required)
  • Electrical and Controls: $2,000-$4,000

Considering these factors, the total installed cost for a basic engineered system typically starts around $35,000 and can go up to $80,000+, particularly for challenging sites with rocky terrain or difficult access. For properties requiring cisterns, remember that the size is determined based on bedroom count, calculated at two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This can significantly influence your total costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on site conditions, soil type, and system complexity. It's important to quote the high end of this range due to the unique challenges of mountain terrain.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The time required for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes a few days to several weeks. This depends on the complexity of the site, the permitting process, and weather conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, especially in mountainous areas like Pitkin, an engineered septic system is required due to challenging soil conditions. These systems provide better performance, longevity, and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. These involve digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which help determine the appropriate septic system design.
