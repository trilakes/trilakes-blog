Why Dumont Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the picturesque mountain community of Dumont, Colorado, brings unique joys and challenges, especially when it comes to managing your onsite wastewater treatment system. At an elevation of over 8,000 feet, properties in this serene area often require specialized knowledge for septic installation. A professional septic installer ensures that your system meets local regulations, safeguarding both your property and the surrounding environment. Given the rocky terrain and varying soil types, a licensed and insured general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting can provide the expertise needed to design and install an effective onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS) that stands the test of time.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County County

Building in Dumont presents several challenges that can complicate septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in Clear Creek County often necessitate engineered systems, which are designed to function optimally in difficult terrain. The frost line can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it essential to account for freeze protection during the winter months. Additionally, the local water table is frequently deep, prompting many property owners to rely on cisterns or water hauling. Coupled with steep driveways and limited winter access, these factors make it crucial to work with a knowledgeable septic contractor who understands the unique requirements of the area.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on our transparent and systematic approach to septic installation. Here‚Äôs how our process works:

  1. Site Assessment: We begin with a thorough evaluation of your property to understand its specific needs. This includes soil testing to determine the appropriate system type.
  2. Soil Testing: Utilizing a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze the soil composition. The samples are then sent to a licensed engineer for a detailed soils report.
  3. Septic Design: Based on the soils report, we create a customized septic design that complies with Colorado's Regulation 43.
  4. Permitting: We handle all permitting through the Clear Creek County Building Department, ensuring all necessary documentation is submitted.
  5. Installation: Our team installs the system, which typically takes 1-3 weeks depending on site conditions and weather.
  6. Inspection and Maintenance: After installation, we ensure your system passes all inspections and discuss routine maintenance to extend its lifespan.

By following this comprehensive process, we ensure that your septic system is reliable and meets all local regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Dumont

The cost of septic installation in Dumont can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, system type, and site accessibility. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Soils test and engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits (Clear Creek County): $500-$2,000
  • Excavation for the system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Septic tank and components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if required): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical and controls: $2,000-$4,000

When considering a cistern, remember that the size is based on the bedroom count: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gal/day. Given the rocky terrain and challenging access, property owners should expect total installed costs to start at around $35,000-$50,000 for a basic engineered system and can go up to $55,000-$80,000+ for an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) system. Difficult sites may incur additional costs of $10,000-$25,000, so it‚Äôs wise to plan accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on site conditions, system type, and additional factors such as rocky terrain and access issues.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Installation typically takes 1-3 weeks, depending on the complexity of the project, weather conditions, and permitting processes through the Clear Creek County Building Department.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Dumont require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A professional assessment will determine the most suitable system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which is crucial for determining the appropriate septic system design.
