Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is designed to be thorough and efficient. It begins with a comprehensive soil testing procedure, where we utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each reaching depths of eight feet. This allows us to gather soil samples at various depths, which are then sent to a licensed engineer for analysis. The resulting soils report determines the type of septic system you need, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Once the soil analysis is complete, we guide you through obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Clear Creek County Building Department. This step is crucial, as permits are required for all septic installations and can take time to secure.

After securing the permit, we move on to installation. Depending on the complexity of your site, installation can typically take anywhere from one to two weeks. We‚Äôll excavate for the septic tank, install the necessary components, and create a suitable drain field or leach field as dictated by the engineered system design. Throughout the process, we ensure that your property is protected, and all work is conducted to the highest standards, providing you with a reliable septic system that lasts.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Dumont

The cost of a septic installation in Dumont can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, homeowners should anticipate costs starting from $35,000 for basic engineered systems, while sand filter systems can range from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units (ATUs) can go upwards of $55,000 to $80,000. These prices reflect the complexities of installing systems in mountain terrain, where rocky conditions can add $10,000 to $25,000 to the total cost.

For homeowners interested in cisterns, sizing is based on bedroom count: calculate using two people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. This helps ensure you have adequate water supply alongside your septic system.

Remember that costs can also fluctuate based on the need for additional excavation, local permitting fees (which can range from $500 to $2,000), and specific requirements set by the soils report. Always consider the complexities of your site when budgeting for a new septic system.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: Septic installation costs in Colorado typically start from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can exceed $80,000 for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions and requirements. Factors such as rocky terrain, permitting, and system design can significantly influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes one to two weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and the complexity of the system required. It's essential to allow for proper planning and execution to ensure compliance and functionality.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Dumont require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and regulations set forth in Regulation 43. An engineered system is designed to function effectively in the local environment, ensuring long-term reliability and compliance.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities, which informs the design and type of septic system best suited for the property.
