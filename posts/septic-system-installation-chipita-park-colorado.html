Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure that every system is tailored to the unique needs of your property. First, we conduct a thorough soil testing assessment, which involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. This allows us to gather soil samples, which are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the most suitable type of septic system for your land.

Once the soils report is complete, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Teller County Building Department. This is crucial, as permits must be secured before any installation can begin. Our team will then design an engineered system tailored to your specific site conditions, whether that be a pressure-dosed system, a sand filter system, or an advanced treatment unit (ATU).

The installation process typically takes 2 to 4 weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and the weather conditions. We prioritize using high-quality materials and practices to ensure that your septic tank and associated systems are built to last. Our expert team will oversee every stage of the installation, ensuring compliance with Colorado's stringent Regulation 43 standards.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system needed, site conditions, and specific requirements like engineered systems for challenging terrains. It's crucial to consider factors such as rocky soil or limited access that can increase these costs significantly.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system can take anywhere from 2 to 4 weeks, depending on site complexity and weather conditions. Proper planning during the building season from May to October can help ensure a smoother and quicker installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Chipita Park require an engineered system due to the rocky and clay-rich soils that often fail to meet the criteria for conventional systems. This is necessary to comply with Colorado's Regulation 43 and to ensure effective wastewater treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil tests conducted with a mini excavator, where we dig test holes to assess soil composition. This information is crucial for determining the appropriate septic system type needed for your property.
