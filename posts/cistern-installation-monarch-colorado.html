Why Monarch Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

As a property owner in Monarch, a breathtaking mountain community nestled in Chaffee County, you likely appreciate the serene environment and stunning views. However, living at over 8,000 feet elevation presents unique challenges, particularly regarding water supply. With many properties lacking reliable wells due to deep water tables and rocky terrain, cistern installation becomes a critical solution for ensuring a consistent potable water supply. Professional cistern installation not only guarantees compliance with local regulations but also optimizes your water storage for the specific demands of your mountain property. Trusting a qualified cistern installer near me can safeguard your investment and provide peace of mind, especially as winter approaches.

Local Challenges in Chaffee County County

Living in Monarch means encountering a variety of environmental factors that can complicate water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil prevalent in this area can make excavation challenging and costly. Furthermore, the high elevation contributes to a deeper frost line of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning for winter preparation and freeze protection for your underground water storage tank. The extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads can also affect both the installation and long-term functionality of your water cistern. With many properties having steep driveways and limited winter access, having a reliable water hauling solution becomes critical for ensuring your ranch or estate water system remains operational throughout the winter months.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Monarch

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is essential for planning your project effectively. The initial investment for a cistern system can vary significantly based on several factors, including tank size, excavation challenges, and additional equipment required. At Tri-Lakes, we typically install Norwesco tanks, with costs ranging from $13,000-$15,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank and $22,000-$28,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank. However, the total installed system cost can range from $25,000-$35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system to $40,000-$55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system when you account for excavation, pump systems, and other necessary components.

When determining the best size for your potable water storage tank, consider your household's daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically accommodates about 6 people, resulting in an estimated daily water usage of 600 gallons. This means you would need a tank that can store at least 18,000 gallons if you refill monthly, or 72,000+ gallons if you want to get through the winter without frequent refills. Keep in mind that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, especially for properties with challenging access.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges between $25,000 and $55,000, depending on the size of the tank and site-specific factors like excavation challenges and necessary equipment installation. For larger systems or those requiring unique setups, costs can exceed $75,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage. As a guideline, calculate 100 gallons per person per day, considering the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home may require a tank that holds 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The typical timeline for cistern installation can range from 1-2 weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, excavation challenges, and weather. More complex installations may require additional time for site preparation and permitting.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific site conditions in Monarch. Many properties face deep water tables, making wells impractical. Cisterns provide a reliable alternative for potable water storage, especially in areas with challenging soil or access.
