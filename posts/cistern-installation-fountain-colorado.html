Why Fountain Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the scenic surroundings of Fountain, Colorado, with an elevation of 5,546 feet, reliable water access is paramount. Many homes in this region do not have direct access to well water, making cistern installation a vital solution for ensuring a dependable water supply. Whether you are looking to store potable water for your family or need an efficient water storage tank installation for agricultural purposes, professional installation is critical. A qualified cistern installer can help you navigate the local building codes and secure the necessary permits through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring your system is compliant and safe.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Fountain's unique terrain presents several challenges for homeowners considering underground water storage tanks. The soil composition includes rocky granite and clay, complicating excavation efforts. Many properties experience a deep water table, meaning that wells may be impractical or unreliable, which is why cisterns are often essential. Additionally, the frost line in this area ranges from 36 to 48 inches, requiring careful planning to prevent freezing, especially during winter months. With steep driveways that limit winter access and potential extreme weather conditions, proper winterization of your water cistern is crucial. Understanding these local conditions will help inform your decision-making and planning process for your buried water tank.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a structured approach to ensure seamless cistern installation for our clients:

  1. Consultation and Site Evaluation: We begin with a consultation to understand your water needs, followed by an on-site evaluation to assess soil conditions, access routes, and the best location for your cistern.
  2. Permitting: We will assist you in navigating the permitting process through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with all local regulations.
  3. Excavation: Using specialized equipment, we excavate the site for your water storage tank installation. This step may take longer in rocky areas, and costs can vary significantly based on site conditions.
  4. Tank Installation: We typically install industry-leading Norwesco tanks, known for their durability and effectiveness. The tank is placed in the excavation and secured properly.
  5. Pumping Systems and Connections: We integrate a pumping system and pressure tank, ensuring your potable water storage is functional and efficient.
  6. Testing and Final Inspection: After installation, we conduct thorough testing of the system to ensure everything is operating correctly and efficiently before finalizing the project.

This entire process generally takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. We prioritize your needs and work diligently to provide timely results.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Fountain

Understanding the cost factors associated with cistern installation is essential for homeowners in Fountain. The total installed cost typically ranges from:

  • 5,000-gallon system: $25,000 to $35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: $40,000 to $55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): $50,000 to $75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): $75,000 to $120,000+

To determine the appropriate tank size, consider your daily water usage, calculated as follows: multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 (for occupants) and then by 100 gallons. For example, a 3-bedroom home (6 occupants) would require about 600 gallons per day. Homeowners often prefer larger systems to reduce refill frequency, especially in winter months when access is limited. If you're planning to refill monthly, a minimum of 18,000 gallons would be advisable, while a "get through winter" system could require 72,000 gallons or more.

Additional costs include excavation (which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more depending on rock and access challenges), pump systems, and electrical hookups. Remember, rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs. Thus, it's essential to plan and budget accordingly to avoid surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the size and specific site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon system can cost between $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can reach up to $120,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need is determined by your daily water usage, which is calculated as the number of bedrooms multiplied by 2 (for occupants) and then by 100 gallons. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would need at least a 10,000-gallon tank for adequate supply.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take several weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, permitting, and weather. Our process includes thorough evaluations, excavation, and installation to ensure quality and compliance.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site-specific factors such as water table depth and soil conditions. Cisterns are a reliable alternative when wells are impractical due to depth or yield issues, making them ideal for many properties in Fountain.
