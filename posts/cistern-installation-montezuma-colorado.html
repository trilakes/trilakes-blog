Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Montezuma

Understanding the costs involved in water storage tank installation is vital for planning your project. The price for a cistern can vary significantly based on multiple factors, including the size of the tank and site conditions. For example, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank is between $22,000 and $28,000. However, it's essential to budget for the total installed cost, which includes excavation, pumps, electrical work, and other necessary components.

For sizing, consider that a household of four in a 2-bedroom home will need approximately 400 gallons of water daily. If you want to refill every two months, you‚Äôll require a minimum of 24,000 gallons. This would mean a larger, more expensive system. Additional costs arise from site conditions‚Äîrocky or difficult terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and limited access may increase hauling expenses significantly.

As such, total installed costs for a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000. For larger capacity systems or dual tank setups, the costs can escalate to $75,000 and beyond. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern in Colorado varies widely based on size and installation requirements. For a basic 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000. Larger systems, like a 10,000-gallon tank, may range from $40,000 to $55,000, depending on site conditions and additional installation factors.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage, which averages about 100 gallons per person. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would typically need a tank capable of holding at least 36,000 to 54,000 gallons if refilling every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary, but typically it takes several days to complete. This includes site evaluation, excavation, tank installation, and system integration. Weather conditions and site challenges may extend the timeline, especially during winter months.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water table and access. Cisterns are a great alternative in areas where well drilling is impractical or unreliable, especially in rural settings like Montezuma. They offer the flexibility of a backup water supply and are critical for properties with low-yield wells.
