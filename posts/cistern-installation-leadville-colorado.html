Why Leadville Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the beautiful mountain community of Leadville, Colorado, often means dealing with unique challenges when it comes to water supply. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties in Lake County rely on cistern installation as a practical solution for their water needs. A professional cistern installer ensures that your underground water storage tank is not only installed correctly but also optimized to meet your specific requirements, whether that‚Äôs for residential potable water storage, agricultural irrigation, or emergency fire suppression. A properly installed cistern not only provides reliable access to water, but it also safeguards against the unpredictable mountain weather that can disrupt traditional water sources.

Local Challenges in Lake County County

The unique geography and climate of Leadville present specific challenges when it comes to water storage tank installation. The rocky granite soils make excavation complicated and costly, often adding 20-40% to excavation expenses. Additionally, the water table can be significantly deep, necessitating the need for water hauling or the installation of a cistern for reliable access. High elevation also means that frost lines reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning for freeze protection in your buried water tank. Furthermore, access can be limited, especially during winter months when steep driveways become treacherous. As a result, many homeowners find themselves in need of larger cistern systems to avoid frequent refills during the harsh winter.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for cistern installation that prioritizes client satisfaction and quality. First, we conduct an on-site consultation to assess your property, understanding the specific needs based on your daily water usage and refill preferences. Our team will help you determine the ideal size of your potable water storage tank using a simple formula: for example, a 3-bedroom home generally requires around 600 gallons per day, translating to at least 18,000 gallons for a monthly refill schedule.

Once the size is determined, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary permits through the Lake County County Building Department. This step is crucial, as proper permits ensure that your installation meets local regulations and standards. Excavation will be carried out next, which can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions such as rocky terrain and access challenges. After the excavation, we will install your chosen tank, typically a Norwesco model, and set up the necessary pump and filtration systems. Lastly, we will conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly, providing you with peace of mind as you enjoy your new water storage solutions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Leadville

The cost of cistern installation can vary significantly based on several factors, including tank size, excavation complexity, and additional system components. For example, a typical 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. Given the intricacies of mountain property water needs, homeowners often opt for larger systems. If we consider a 3-bedroom home with a recommended size of 18,000 gallons for a monthly refill, the total installation costs‚Äîincluding excavation, pumps, and electrical systems‚Äîcould range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Additional costs often arise from excavation challenges, which can add $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rocky soil conditions. Electrical hookups, trenching to the house, and water filtration systems can also contribute an extra $5,000 to $10,000 to your total expenses. Keep in mind that larger systems reduce the frequency of water deliveries, which can save you money in the long run, especially in remote areas where winter deliveries may be limited.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely based on factors like tank size and site conditions. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can cost significantly more, with 10,000-gallon installations often reaching $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill it. As a general rule, expect about 100 gallons per person per day, so for a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of six, a minimum of 18,000 gallons is recommended for a monthly refill.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions but generally takes 1-3 weeks from the initial consultation to complete installation. Excavation may take several days, especially in rocky or difficult terrain, followed by the installation of the tank and associated systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions and your specific water needs. Cisterns can be a better option in areas with deep water tables where drilling a well is impractical or unreliable, while wells may provide a more sustainable water source for properties with accessible groundwater.
