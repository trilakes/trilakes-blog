Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Kremmling

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for homeowners in Kremmling. The pricing can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions and tank size. A basic installation for a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000 once excavation, pumps, and other necessary components are included.

When sizing your underground water storage tank, consider your daily water usage, which is typically calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, if you have a 3-bedroom home, assuming an occupancy of 6 people, your daily water usage would be approximately 600 gallons. This means you may want a tank that can hold enough water for monthly refills or even larger for convenience during winter months. Keep in mind that rocky terrain and difficult access can add 20-40% to excavation costs.

Overall, investing in a larger system can save you time and money in the long run by reducing the frequency of refills. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000 depending on the size of the tank and site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon tank may cost around $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $40,000 to $55,000 when including all necessary components like excavation and pump systems.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. Generally, you can calculate your needs by taking the number of bedrooms in your home, multiplying by two people per bedroom, and then multiplying that figure by 100 gallons per person per day.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but it generally takes about one to two weeks from site assessment to completion. Factors such as site conditions, weather, and accessibility can impact the installation timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. If your location has a deep water table or rocky terrain that makes well drilling impractical, a cistern can be a more reliable water storage solution. For properties with limited access or harsh winters, a properly sized cistern can provide a consistent backup water supply.
