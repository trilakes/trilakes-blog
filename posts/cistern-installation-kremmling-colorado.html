Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Kremmling

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is vital for any homeowner considering this option. For a standard 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank, you can expect to pay between $13,000 and $15,000 for the tank alone. For larger systems, such as a 10,000-gallon tank, the total cost can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of cistern installation typically starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Additional costs for excavation, plumbing, and permits can increase the total investment significantly, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is based on daily water usage. A good rule of thumb is to assume 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 4-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 36,000 to 54,000 gallons for a refill every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation time for a cistern can vary based on site conditions, but typically it takes between a few days to a couple of weeks. This timeframe includes permitting, excavation, and the installation of the water storage tank.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are an excellent alternative for properties where well drilling is impractical or where the water table is deep. They provide reliable access to water when well yields are low or inconsistent.
