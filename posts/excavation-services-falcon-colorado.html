Local Challenges in El Paso County

Excavation in Falcon is not without its challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil types can complicate your grading and trenching efforts. Additionally, the water table is often quite deep, necessitating the installation of cisterns for water storage, which can add complexity to site preparation. These factors make it crucial to work with a knowledgeable excavation contractor who can navigate the intricacies of the terrain.

Moreover, the frost line in our area can reach depths of 36-48 inches, particularly at higher elevations, impacting foundation excavation and utility trenching. Local weather patterns, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can also disrupt excavation schedules. Understanding these local challenges is essential for effective site development and prevents costly delays.

Cost Factors for Excavation Services in Falcon

Understanding the costs associated with excavation services is crucial for effective budgeting. In Falcon, basic site preparation and clearing typically starts from $8,000 and can range up to $20,000, depending on the complexity of the land and the amount of heavy brush/tree removal required. For more intensive projects, such as a full site development, costs can exceed $100,000.

Specific pricing includes:

  • Basic lot clearing: $3,000-$8,000 per acre
  • Foundation excavation for a 1,500 sq ft basement: $15,000-$30,000
  • Driveway construction: $5,000-$10,000 for 200 ft, and steep/switchback driveways can exceed $25,000.

Frequently Asked Questions About Excavation Services

Q: How much does excavation cost per cubic yard?

A: Excavation costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, but typically range from $20 to $50 per cubic yard for standard excavating. In rocky conditions, costs can rise to $50-$100 per cubic yard due to the additional labor and equipment required.

Q: How long does site preparation take?

A: The timeline for site preparation varies greatly depending on the site's complexity and size. Generally, basic site prep can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, especially when factoring in weather conditions and permitting processes.

Q: What equipment is needed for mountain excavation?

A: Mountain excavation often requires specialized equipment such as excavators, bobcats, and dump trucks. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we utilize heavy machinery designed for rocky terrain, ensuring efficient and effective excavation services, including foundation excavation and utility trenching.

Q: Do I need permits for excavation?

A: Yes, permits are typically required for excavation projects in Falcon. All permits must go through the El Paso County Building Department, and it's essential to ensure that your excavation contractor is familiar with local regulations and permits to avoid any delays.
