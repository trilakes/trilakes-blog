Local Challenges in Lake County County

Granite's distinctive landscape presents several challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite soils can complicate excavation, often requiring more time and effort, which can increase costs. With a water table that is frequently deep, many properties are left reliant on water hauling as a primary method of water supply. The harsh mountain weather, characterized by extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall, necessitates careful planning for winterizing cistern installations. Additionally, the average frost line of 36-48 inches means that your buried water tank must be installed sufficiently deep to prevent freezing, particularly crucial during the winter months. Limited access to some properties can further complicate installation, especially in the winter when steep driveways may be impassable.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Granite

The cost of cistern installation in Granite can vary widely based on several factors. Using a basic formula to determine your tank size, we recommend calculating your daily water usage based on your home's number of bedrooms:

  • Assume 2 people per bedroom.
  • Each person typically uses 100 gallons of water daily.

For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require about 600 gallons daily. Depending on how often you want to refill, here‚Äôs a quick sizing guide:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: 8,400 gallons minimum
  • Refill monthly: 18,000 gallons minimum
  • For winter support: 72,000+ gallons

Typical pricing for Norwesco tanks ranges from:

  • 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

Additional costs include excavation ($3,000-$8,000), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot), and other essential components, bringing total installed costs to:

  • 5,000 gallon system: $25,000-$35,000
  • 10,000 gallon system: $40,000-$55,000

Keep in mind that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and winter installations may also incur additional expenses. The larger the tank you choose, the fewer deliveries you'll need, ultimately saving you money in the long run.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the tank size and additional installation factors. For instance, a 5,000 gallon system may cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000 gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which can be calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need at least a 10,000-gallon tank for monthly refills or a larger system if you want to minimize refills during winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on site conditions, excavation needs, and local weather. Typically, the process involves several steps, including permitting, excavation, tank installation, and testing.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions. Cisterns are often more feasible in mountainous terrains like Granite, where the water table may be deep or unreliable, while wells can be impractical in these conditions. Cisterns also provide flexibility for backup water supplies and can store larger quantities of water.
