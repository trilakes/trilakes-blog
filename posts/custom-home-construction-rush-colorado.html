Local Challenges in El Paso County County

When considering a custom build in Rush, homeowners face several unique challenges. The terrain often consists of rocky granite and decomposed granite, making foundation engineering a critical part of your home design. The soil conditions vary, with clay prevalent in the valleys, affecting septic installation and requiring engineered systems to ensure proper waste management. Additionally, the water table can be notably deep, necessitating the use of cisterns or water hauling methods for many properties. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, building requires careful planning and execution to avoid winter-related issues. Moreover, access to many properties can be limited, particularly during winter months when heavy snow can obstruct steep driveways. Understanding these challenges is essential for a successful custom home construction project.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous, step-by-step approach to custom home construction to ensure your project runs smoothly. 1. **Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study**: Before buying land, we recommend obtaining a Build Feasibility Report to assess site conditions, setbacks, and utility availability. This report typically costs between $1,500 and $3,500. 2. **Well Permit**: Most Colorado counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit. Obtaining a well permit early is crucial, as drilling can take 6-12 months. 3. **Site Plan**: A professional site plan outlines where everything will be located on your property, costing around $1,500 to $4,000. 4. **Architectural Plans**: Choose from your architect or our recommended professionals, including a variety of designs from ArchitecturalDesigns.com. Expect costs to range from $2,000 to $15,000+. 5. **Engineered Foundation & Framing Plans**: These plans are tailored for your site‚Äôs unique conditions and must be stamped by a licensed engineer, with costs between $5,000 and $15,000. 6. **Land Survey**: A professional survey ensures accurate property boundaries and identification of easements, costing approximately $1,500 to $5,000. 7. **Septic System Plans**: If there‚Äôs no sewer connection, engineered septic plans are necessary, costing between $3,000 and $6,000. 8. **Compile & Submit for Building Permit**: We gather all necessary documents and submit them to the El Paso County Building Department, with permit fees ranging from $5,000 to $20,000+. 9. **Pre-Construction Planning**: Finalize your plans and prepare for construction. 10. **Construction**: This phase includes multiple inspections and construction steps, from excavation to final finishes. 11. **Certificate of Occupancy**: Once all inspections are passed, you receive your CO, and we conduct a final walkthrough before you move in! With careful planning, the typical timeline from pre-construction to completion can range from 10 to 18 months, although this can be extended due to mountain weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Rush

Building a custom home in Rush requires a keen understanding of the associated costs. The cost per square foot for mountain construction is generally as follows: - Basic build: $350-$450/sq ft - Standard quality: $450-$550/sq ft - Custom/higher-end: $550-$700/sq ft - Luxury/high-end: $700-$1,000+/sq ft For instance, a 2,500 sq ft home could range from $875,000 to $1,375,000, depending on various factors like design complexity and material choices. Additionally, homeowners must consider other costs such as land acquisition, septic installation (which can range from $35,000 to $80,000), and cisterns. The size of your cistern should be calculated based on your expected occupancy: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. This could lead to cistern costs of $25,000 to $75,000. Factors such as rocky terrain and difficult access can significantly impact construction costs, making it essential to budget accordingly. Always quote the high end of price ranges to avoid surprises during construction.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $350 to over $1,000 per square foot, depending on the quality and complexity of the construction. For instance, a standard build might cost between $450 and $550 per square foot, while luxury homes could exceed $700 per square foot.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a custom home can vary significantly based on location and complexity, but typical projects take between 10 to 18 months from groundbreaking to completion. Weather conditions, particularly in mountainous areas like Rush, can extend this timeline further.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience in mountain home construction, licensing, and insurance. It's also essential to review their portfolio and client testimonials to ensure they can deliver the quality and style you desire.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a pre-purchase build feasibility study is crucial as it assesses the land's viability for construction and identifies potential obstacles. This can save you from purchasing a property that may be challenging or impossible to build on.
