Why Querida Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Querida, Custer County, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges when it comes to water accessibility. Many properties in this mountainous region lack reliable well water sources, making cistern installation a necessary solution for homeowners. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures you have a consistent supply of potable water, especially during the harsh winter months. With Tri-Lakes Contracting, you benefit from expert knowledge of the local terrain, climate, and permitting processes, ensuring your installation meets all safety and regulatory standards.

Local Challenges in Custer County County

Custer County is known for its stunning mountain landscapes, but the challenging soil conditions, including rocky granite and clay, can complicate water storage tank installation. The depth of the water table often necessitates reliance on cisterns, especially in areas where well drilling may not be practical due to depth or reliability issues. Winter conditions pose additional challenges, as the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning to avoid freezing pipes and ensure systems remain operational. The steep driveways and limited access during winter can also hinder installation and water delivery, making it crucial for homeowners to consider these factors when planning their water storage solutions.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process to ensure your cistern installation is efficient and effective:

  1. Initial Consultation: We assess your property's unique needs, including daily water usage and refill preferences, to determine the appropriate tank size.
  2. Site Evaluation: Our team evaluates the terrain and soil conditions, taking into account factors like rocky soil and access for excavation equipment.
  3. Permitting: We handle all necessary permits through the Custer County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  4. Excavation: Using specialized equipment, we excavate the site for your underground cistern, considering any potential challenges posed by the rocky terrain.
  5. Installation: We install your chosen tank brand, typically Norwesco, along with the required pumps and pressure tanks.
  6. Final Connections: The system is connected to your home, including trenching to the house and any necessary water treatment systems.
  7. Winterization: Before the first snowfall, we ensure your cistern is properly winterized to prevent freezing, providing peace of mind during the colder months.

This process typically spans several weeks, depending on weather conditions and site challenges, with a focus on delivering a reliable and durable water cistern system.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Querida

The total cost of water storage tank installation in Querida varies based on several critical factors, including the size of the tank, site conditions, and additional equipment needed. As a rule of thumb, you can calculate your tank size using the formula: number of bedrooms x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of six people would require a system capable of storing at least 600 gallons daily.

Typically, you can expect the following costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Excavation costs: $3,000-$8,000+, depending on rocky terrain and access
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Total installed systems: Ranges from $25,000 to over $120,000 based on capacity and site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of a cistern installation can range from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on factors such as tank size, excavation requirements, and additional components like pumps and electrical work. It's important to consider your specific site conditions to get an accurate estimate.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need is determined by your daily water usage. A common formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms in your home by 2 people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would typically require at least a 10,000-gallon tank for adequate storage.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process for a cistern typically takes several weeks, depending on factors like site access, weather conditions, and permitting. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that every step is carefully managed to deliver a quality installation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific situation. Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources. They also offer flexibility for backup water supply and can serve multiple uses, such as irrigation and fire suppression.
