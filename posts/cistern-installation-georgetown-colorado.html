Why Georgetown Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Georgetown, Clear Creek County, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, access to potable water can be a significant challenge. Many properties lack reliable well sources due to deep water tables and rocky terrain. This is where professional cistern installation becomes essential. Cisterns and underground water storage tanks provide a dependable solution for residential water needs, especially in a mountain community where harsh winters and limited access can complicate water delivery. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we ensure that your water storage tank installation is done correctly, complying with all local regulations and tailored to your specific needs.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County County

Georgetown's unique geographical and climatic conditions present several challenges for water storage solutions. The soil composition, characterized by rocky granite and clay in the valleys, can complicate excavation and installation processes. Moreover, properties often experience steep driveways and limited winter access, making timely water hauling to refill your potable water storage tank a logistical hurdle. The frost line in this region reaches depths of 36-48 inches, requiring frost-proof cistern installations to avoid freezing during the harsh winters. Understanding these challenges is crucial when planning for an effective underground cistern solution.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Georgetown

When considering cistern installation in Georgetown, it's important to understand the various cost factors involved. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we typically install Norwesco tanks, which are industry leaders in underground water storage. The costs for these tanks are as follows:

  • Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000 (tank only)
  • Norwesco 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000 (tank only)

However, additional costs must be factored in, such as:

  • Excavation for burial: $3,000-$8,000+, depending on rocky conditions and access
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to the house: $25-$50 per linear foot
  • Risers & access lids: $500-$1,500
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000-$2,500
  • Water filtration/treatment: $2,000-$5,000

Thus, the total installed system cost for a 5,000-gallon basic system ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. It's crucial to remember that costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, particularly in rocky or remote areas, which can add 20-40% to excavation costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely, but for a typical installation using a Norwesco tank, you can expect to pay around $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and complexity of the installation. Additional costs for excavation, pumps, and electrical work can raise the total significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your household's daily water usage. A general guideline is to allocate 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a tank that holds between 18,000 and 54,000 gallons, depending on how often you wish to refill.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but generally, it takes several weeks from consultation to completion. Factors such as site accessibility, weather conditions, and the need for permits can all influence this timeframe.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific situation. Cisterns are often better for properties with deep water tables, rocky terrain, or where well drilling is impractical. They provide a reliable, flexible water source, especially in remote or off-grid settings.
