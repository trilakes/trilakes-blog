Why Georgetown Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the stunning mountain community of Georgetown, Colorado, the elevation of over 8,000 feet presents unique challenges regarding water supply. Many properties in this area do not have access to reliable wells, making cistern installation a vital solution for residents. A professional cistern installer near you can ensure that the installation meets local regulations and is tailored to your specific usage needs. With the right expertise, you can safely store potable water for daily use, landscape irrigation, and even fire suppression, essential in our forested surroundings.

Furthermore, the harsh winter conditions and extreme temperature swings require careful planning for winterizing cistern installations. DIY installations can lead to costly mistakes, especially when frost-proofing is not adequately addressed. This is why investing in professional services is crucial for ensuring a resilient and efficient water storage tank installation that will last for years to come.

Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Georgetown's location in Clear Creek County brings a host of challenges that can complicate cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation particularly arduous, often increasing excavation costs by 20-40%. Additionally, the water table in the area is frequently deep, creating a high demand for underground water storage tanks. Many properties rely on water hauling services, especially during the winter months when access becomes limited.

Moreover, the frost line at elevations of 8,000+ feet varies between 36-48 inches, necessitating proper depth and insulation for buried water tanks to prevent freezing. The steep driveways and limited winter access add another layer of complexity to installations. Fire mitigation is also a critical aspect to consider, as having adequate water storage for fire suppression is essential in forested areas where wildfires can pose a significant threat.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous and straightforward process for cistern installation. Initially, we assess your property to determine the best location for the potable water storage tank, considering factors like soil composition, elevation, and access. After that, we help you calculate the appropriate sizing based on your daily water usage and refill preferences.

The steps of our installation process include:

  • Site Evaluation: We evaluate your property for optimal placement and soil conditions.
  • Permitting: We handle permitting through the Clear Creek County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.
  • Excavation: Our team excavates the site for the tank, taking care to manage rocky terrain effectively.
  • Tank Installation: We install the chosen tank, typically a Norwesco model, ensuring proper connections for both water supply and drainage.
  • System Setup: We set up the necessary pump system and pressure tank, ensuring a fully functional water system.
  • Testing: After installation, we meticulously test the system to ensure everything operates correctly.

The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Proper planning and execution are paramount, especially in the winter months when freeze protection becomes critical.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Georgetown

When considering cistern installation in Georgetown, understanding the cost factors is essential. Prices for underground water storage tanks vary based on the tank size, with Norwesco tanks being our preferred choice. The following provides a comprehensive overview of potential costs:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: Starting from $13,000-$15,000 (tank only).
  • 10,000-gallon tank: Starting from $22,000-$28,000 (tank only).
  • Total installed system costs: For a 5,000-gallon system, expect $25,000-$35,000; for a 10,000-gallon system, $40,000-$55,000.

To determine the size of your potable water storage tank, use the formula: (number of bedrooms x 2 occupants x 100 gallons/day). For instance, a 3-bedroom home will require around 600 gallons per day, leading to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons if refilling monthly. Other factors affecting costs include excavation expenses, which can range from $3,000-$8,000+, and pump systems priced around $2,500-$5,000, making a larger investment beneficial for fewer refills and long-term savings.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and installation specifics. A 5,000-gallon tank can start around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may reach $40,000 or more when considering excavation and other additional costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be calculated based on your daily water usage. Generally, you can estimate 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home, assuming 2 occupants per bedroom. For example, a 3-bedroom home would typically require at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation usually takes about 4 to 6 weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and permitting processes. Factors such as rocky terrain and winter accessibility can affect the timeline, making early planning essential.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?
