Why Rosita Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Rosita, Colorado, ensuring a reliable water supply can be challenging due to the high elevation and rocky terrain. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties lack direct access to a well or have water tables that are prohibitively deep. This makes professional cistern installation an essential service for homeowners looking to secure a dependable source of water. Not only do these systems provide essential potable water storage, but they also contribute to agricultural needs and fire suppression efforts, making them a versatile solution for mountain living.

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the unique demands of the Rosita area. Our licensed and insured team specializes in water storage tank installation, ensuring that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly for optimal performance and longevity. With our expertise, you can enjoy peace of mind knowing your water supply is in capable hands.

Local Challenges in Custer County

The challenges of installing a water cistern in Rosita, Custer County, stem from its unique geological and climatic conditions. The rocky granite soil, coupled with clay in the valleys, can complicate the excavation process, often leading to increased costs. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches deep requires careful planning for winter preparation, especially when the ground freezes solid.

Access to many properties can also be limited, particularly during winter months when steep driveways and heavy snow loads make delivery and installation difficult. Homeowners often need to consider not just the installation of their cistern but also the logistics of water hauling during harsh weather conditions. With significant temperature swings and afternoon thunderstorms, planning ahead for a reliable water supply is critical, making the expertise of a local cistern installer near me invaluable.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for cistern installation to ensure each project meets the specific needs of our clients and complies with Custer County permit requirements. Our process begins with a thorough consultation where we assess your property, discuss your water needs, and determine the best sizing for your potable water storage tank.

Once we finalize the design, we secure the necessary permits through the County Building Department. After obtaining approval, we schedule the excavation, which is crucial due to the complicated soil conditions. Excavation can take several days, depending on the site's challenges, such as rocky terrain or limited access. The installation of the tank itself usually occurs within a day, followed by the setup of the pump system and any necessary electrical connections.

Throughout the process, we prioritize safety and precision, ensuring that every installation meets our high standards. Our goal is to complete the work efficiently while minimizing disruption to your property. After installation, we conduct a thorough inspection and provide guidance on winterizing your system to protect against potential freeze damage.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Rosita

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is vital for planning your project. The total cost for a cistern system can vary significantly based on factors such as site conditions, the size of the tank, and additional components needed for installation. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we typically install Norwesco tanks, which are industry leaders in underground potable water storage.

For example, a 5,000-gallon tank generally starts at $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000-$28,000. However, these costs can increase with excavation, which may range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more, depending on the complexity of the site. Additionally, the cost for a pump system and pressure tank is typically between $2,500 and $5,000, and if trenching is required to connect the cistern to your home, expect to pay around $25-$50 per linear foot.

Using the formula of daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person per day, you can estimate the size of your cistern. For example, a 3-bedroom home, assuming an occupancy of six people, will need a system capable of storing approximately 600 gallons per day. If you prefer to refill every month, you would need at least an 18,000-gallon cistern, which could bring total installation costs into the range of $40,000-$55,000 or higher, depending on site conditions. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation needs. For a basic system, the price for a 5,000-gallon tank typically starts around $25,000 and can go up to $35,000 when including installation and related equipment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your cistern depends on your household's water usage and refill preferences. For a 3-bedroom home, you should plan for at least an 18,000-gallon tank if refilling monthly to ensure adequate water supply.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but typically includes a few days for excavation and one day for tank installation. Overall, you can expect the entire process to take about a week, depending on site conditions and permit approval.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property's water availability. Cisterns are a reliable option when well drilling is impractical due to deep water tables or rocky terrain. They also offer the flexibility of storing larger volumes of water for various uses.
