Local Challenges in Douglas County

Roxborough Park's mountainous terrain and high elevation create a unique set of challenges for septic system installations. The rocky and decomposed granite soils often necessitate engineered systems due to inadequate drainage capacity. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep, requiring many properties to rely on cisterns for water supply. The frost line in this area can reach depths of 36-48 inches, meaning installations must be designed with freeze protection in mind. Moreover, access to many properties is limited, particularly in winter months when steep driveways become treacherous. Understanding these local factors is essential for any homeowner considering a new septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is meticulous and tailored to the specific needs of your property. The first step involves conducting a thorough soil testing assessment. We bring a mini excavator to your site to dig two test holes, each reaching depths of eight feet, to obtain soil samples that are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis. This report dictates the appropriate type of system to install, whether it‚Äôs a conventional gravity system, a pressure-dosed system, or an ATU system.

Once the soils report is received, we assist you in securing the necessary septic permit from the Douglas County Building Department, which oversees all installations in the area. After obtaining the permit, our team will schedule excavation, where we carefully consider factors such as the rocky terrain and winter access challenges. The actual installation can typically take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions and system type. Finally, we ensure that all installations comply with Regulation 43 standards, setting you up for long-term success with your septic system.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Roxborough Park

The cost of septic installation in Roxborough Park can vary significantly based on several factors. For engineered systems, which are most common in Colorado, you can expect to pay anywhere from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic setup. If your property requires a sand filter system due to poor soil conditions, costs can escalate to between $45,000 and $65,000. For advanced treatment units (ATUs), which are often necessary to meet stricter regulations, expect to spend from $55,000 to $80,000 or more.

When determining the size of a cistern for your home, the formula involves your number of bedrooms: multiply the bedroom count by two people and then by 100 gallons per day. This calculation helps ensure you have adequate water supply, especially in areas where the water table is deep. Remember, rocky terrain and difficult access can increase installation costs by an additional $10,000 to $25,000. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on site conditions. Typically, you can expect to pay between $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems, while advanced systems can cost upwards of $55,000 to $80,000 or more.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for septic systems usually ranges from a few days to a week. This duration depends on the complexity of the installation and site conditions, including soil type and accessibility.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Roxborough Park will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and high elevation. A soils report will determine the necessary type of system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed; instead, we conduct soil testing to assess drainage capabilities. This involves digging test holes to gather soil samples for analysis, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design.
