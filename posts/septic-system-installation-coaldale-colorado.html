Why Coaldale Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Coaldale, Colorado, situated at over 8,000 feet, proper septic installation is not merely a convenience‚Äîit's a necessity for maintaining both health and environmental integrity. With unique challenges such as rocky soil, varying water tables, and the potential for extreme weather conditions, homeowners should entrust their septic system installation to a professional. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our expertise ensures that your septic system is installed correctly, adhering to Colorado's stringent regulations, particularly Regulation 43 governing Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS). This means not only compliance but also the longevity and reliability of your septic system.

Local Challenges in Fremont County

Coaldale's mountainous terrain presents several challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate the installation process, making it vital to conduct thorough soil testing before proceeding. The elevation often leads to a deeper frost line, ranging from 36 to 48 inches, which can impact the type of system required. Additionally, many properties may necessitate engineered septic systems due to poor drainage. Access can also be difficult, especially in winter when steep driveways can become treacherous. Considering these factors, consulting a qualified septic contractor can save homeowners time and money in the long run, ensuring that their onsite wastewater treatment is efficient and sustainable.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we have developed a systematic approach to septic installation tailored to the unique conditions of Fremont County. Our process begins with a detailed soil testing assessment. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and gather soil samples for analysis by a licensed engineer. This report informs us about the most suitable septic system for your property.

Once the soil report is complete and we have obtained the necessary septic permit from the Fremont County Building Department, we schedule the installation. Typically, installations occur from May to October to avoid winter weather complications. The installation includes excavation, tank placement, and the creation of a leach field or drain field as determined by the soil conditions. The entire process usually takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and weather factors. After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly, providing peace of mind for our clients.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Coaldale

The cost of septic system installation in Coaldale varies significantly based on site conditions and the type of system required. For engineered systems, which are the most common in this area, homeowners can expect costs starting from $35,000 for basic systems and can go as high as $80,000 for advanced systems. Key factors influencing these costs include:

  • Soil Conditions: Rocky or difficult terrain can add $10,000-$25,000 to overall costs.
  • Permitting Fees: These can range from $500 to $2,000, depending on the specific requirements set by Fremont County.
  • Excavation and Installation: Excavation costs can range from $8,000 to over $20,000, based on the complexity of the site.
  • Septic Tank and Components: Expect a range of $5,000 to $15,000 for the tank and necessary equipment.

For homeowners considering cisterns, the size is typically calculated based on the bedroom count‚Äî2 people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. This calculation ensures that your water needs are adequately met, especially in areas with deep water tables.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can range from $35,000 for basic engineered systems to upwards of $80,000 for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions and soil types.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Typically, septic system installation in Fremont County can take 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the installation, particularly during the May to October building season.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Coaldale require engineered septic systems due to challenging soils and high elevation, particularly to comply with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging two test holes and analyzing the soil to determine the appropriate septic system for your property.
