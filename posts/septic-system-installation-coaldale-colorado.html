Why Coaldale Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In Coaldale, nestled at an elevation of over 8,000 feet in the stunning Colorado mountains, proper septic installation is crucial due to the unique challenges posed by the terrain and climate. Homeowners often underestimate the importance of hiring a licensed and insured septic installer for their septic system needs. With rocky granite soils and varying water tables, a professional installation ensures that your septic tank functions optimally and lasts for decades. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the specific regulations and requirements, including the Fremont County permit process, which is essential for a successful onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS).

Local Challenges in Fremont County County

Coaldale's unique soil composition, including rocky granite and clay in the valleys, presents significant challenges for septic installation. With a deep water table often requiring homeowners to install cisterns or rely on water hauling, you need a tailored approach to your septic system design. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, complicating installation further. Moreover, steep access roads can limit equipment and material transport, especially as winter approaches. Understanding these local challenges is critical for ensuring that your septic system remains reliable, particularly during Colorado's extreme weather, from heavy snowfall to afternoon thunderstorms.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is meticulous and straightforward, ensuring that your system meets all local regulations and functions efficiently. First, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests to determine the soil's suitability for a septic tank system. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and send samples to a licensed engineer for analysis. This report guides us in designing the most appropriate engineered septic system for your property.

Once the design is approved, we navigate the permitting process through the Fremont County Building Department and prepare for installation. This typically occurs from May to October, given our challenging mountain terrain. After securing the necessary septic permit, our team will excavate the site, install the septic tank, and set up the leach field or drain field, depending on the system type required. Our skilled installers take care to minimize disruption while ensuring all components are correctly placed for optimal performance.

The entire process, from soil testing to completion, typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Our commitment is to provide you with a reliable, long-lasting septic system that complies with all local regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Coaldale

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Coaldale is essential for homeowners. A comprehensive soils test and engineering report will typically cost between $2,000 and $4,000, and permits can range from $500 to $2,000, depending on Fremont County requirements.

Excavation for the septic system can add another $8,000 to $20,000, while the septic tank and associated components usually range from $5,000 to $15,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), which is common in challenging soils, expect additional costs of $8,000 to $15,000. When considering the total installation costs, basic engineered systems start from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems can reach $45,000 to $65,000. For more advanced systems like ATUs, prices can range from $55,000 to $80,000 or more, especially if rocky terrain complicates the installation.

For homeowners in Coaldale, investing in engineered systems can save money in the long run through reduced maintenance and improved longevity.

For those considering cisterns, remember that the size should be based on your bedroom count, calculated at two people per bedroom, using 100 gallons per day. This approach ensures your water needs are met effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on site conditions and the type of system installed. For engineered systems, costs typically start from $35,000 to $50,000, while advanced systems such as sand filters and ATUs may run between $45,000 and $80,000 or higher.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can range from a few weeks to several months, depending on the complexity of the site conditions, permitting process, and weather. Typically, installations are completed within the building season, which runs from May to October in higher elevations like Coaldale.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Coaldale require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and deep water tables. An engineered system is essential for ensuring proper wastewater treatment and compliance with local regulations, as outlined in Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced by soil tests, which assess soil suitability for a septic system. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and depth, which informs the design of the appropriate system for your property.
