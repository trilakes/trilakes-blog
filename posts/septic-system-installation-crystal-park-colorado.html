Local Challenges in El Paso County

Crystal Park's mountainous terrain introduces several challenges when it comes to installing a septic system. The rocky granite and clay soils present obstacles that often require engineered systems, as traditional methods may not suffice. The water table is often located deep below the surface, meaning many properties rely on cisterns and water hauling for their water supply. This makes accurate soil testing critical; we perform detailed soils tests rather than traditional perc tests to determine the most suitable system for your property.

Additionally, the frost line in our area reaches depths of 36-48 inches, which adds complexity to the installation process. Winterizing septic system installations is vital to prevent freeze-related issues, especially since access can be limited during heavy snowfalls. This combination of elevation, variable weather conditions, and challenging access roads makes choosing a qualified septic contractor essential for ensuring a reliable and effective onsite wastewater treatment solution.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take pride in our thorough and systematic approach to septic system installation. Our process begins with an initial consultation where we assess your property‚Äôs specific needs and discuss potential system options. Afterward, we conduct detailed soil testing using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each reaching 8 feet deep. This testing is not just a formality; it is crucial for informing the septic design that best suits your land.

Once we have the soils report from a licensed engineer, we assist you with obtaining the necessary septic permit through the El Paso County Building Department. This step is vital, as it ensures that your installation meets all local regulations. Following permit approval, we schedule the excavation and installation of your septic tank and associated components.

The entire installation process typically spans 1 to 3 weeks, depending on the complexity of the site and weather conditions. Our team carefully manages every aspect, from excavation to the final inspection, ensuring your system is set up for long-term success. We also provide guidance on winter preparation tips to help protect your system during the harsh Colorado winters.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Crystal Park

The cost of septic installation in Crystal Park can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, access, and the type of system required. For instance, engineered systems are often necessary in our mountainous region due to the challenging soils. The total installed costs typically start at $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can range up to $80,000+ for more advanced treatment units like ATUs.

When considering a cistern for your water supply, the required size is based on bedroom count‚Äîtypically, you should plan for 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This means a 3-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of holding 600 gallons of water. Additional costs can arise from rocky terrain and difficult access, which might add anywhere from $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall project.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on site conditions and the type of system required. Engineered systems are often necessary due to challenging terrain, which can increase costs significantly.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes about 1 to 3 weeks. This timeline depends on factors such as weather conditions, permit approvals, and the complexity of the installation site.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In Crystal Park, most properties require engineered septic systems due to rocky soil and deep water tables. An engineered system ensures compliance with Colorado regulations and provides reliable wastewater treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps determine the appropriate septic design for your property.
