Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Wetmore

The cost of cistern installation in Wetmore varies based on several factors, including site conditions and the size of the tank. On average, a basic underground water storage tank installation runs from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. To determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage, consider your daily water usage, which is typically around 100 gallons per person per day.

For example, a 3-bedroom home accommodating six people would require approximately 600 gallons daily. If you wish to refill every two months, you will need a tank with a capacity of at least 36,000 to 54,000 gallons. Other costs to keep in mind include excavation fees, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more depending on rocky terrain and access issues, and additional systems such as pumps and electrical setups, which can add $2,500 to $5,000.

Keep in mind that complex site conditions can increase costs substantially. It is advisable to plan for the higher end of pricing ranges to avoid surprises. Our experts are here to guide you through the cost analysis and provide a detailed breakdown of your water storage tank installation investment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Additional costs for excavation, pumps, and electrical systems can further increase the total investment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: Your tank size depends on daily water usage and refill frequency. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants may need a tank that holds 36,000 to 54,000 gallons if refilling every two months. For a winter supply, consider a tank larger than 72,000 gallons.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process generally takes several weeks, depending on permitting, excavation, and weather conditions. Expect around 2-4 weeks from the initial consultation to the installation completion, considering local challenges.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. In areas like Wetmore where the water table is deep and well drilling can be impractical, a cistern provides a reliable alternative for water supply and is often more cost-effective in the long run.
