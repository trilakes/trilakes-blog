Local Challenges in Chaffee County

Maysville is known for its stunning scenery and rugged terrain, but these attributes come with unique challenges for cistern installation. The area's rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs. Additionally, the water table is frequently deep, necessitating water hauling for many properties. With extreme weather patterns, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, having a reliable potable water storage tank is crucial. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, requiring special attention during installation to prevent freezing in winter. Homeowners must also consider access issues, as many properties have steep driveways that can be difficult to navigate during the winter months. These factors make engaging a local expert essential for a successful installation.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Maysville

When planning for cistern installation, understanding the cost is crucial for Maysville homeowners. The price of an underground water storage tank primarily depends on two factors: daily water usage and the desired refill frequency. For instance, a typical calculation involves:

  • Assuming 100 gallons per person per day.
  • Occupancy estimated at 2 people per bedroom.

For a 3-bedroom home housing 6 individuals, the daily water usage would be approximately 600 gallons. Homeowners often prefer larger systems to reduce the frequency of refills, particularly for winter months when access may be limited. The sizing would look as follows:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: 8,400 gallons minimum.
  • Refill monthly: 18,000 gallons minimum.
  • Winter survival (4-5 months): 72,000+ gallons.

When it comes to costs, installing a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank typically starts at $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000-$28,000. Additional expenses such as excavation (ranging from $3,000-$8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500) can quickly add up. Therefore, the total installed system for a basic 5,000-gallon setup can range from $25,000-$35,000, while a larger dual-tank system may cost between $50,000-$75,000. Always factor in the rocky terrain, which can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. For a detailed breakdown of costs, refer to our Cistern Cost Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on several factors, including size and site conditions. A typical 5,000-gallon system may start at around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000, depending on excavation and additional requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your daily water usage, which is calculated based on bedroom count and occupancy. For example, a 3-bedroom home might require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or 72,000 gallons for winter survival.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather factors. Excavation and installation may take longer in challenging terrains, especially during winter months.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are a great alternative when wells are impractical due to deep water tables or rocky soils. They provide reliable storage, especially in rural or off-grid areas.
