Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Copper Mountain

The cost of cistern installation in Copper Mountain can vary significantly based on several factors, including the size and type of the tank, excavation difficulties, and site access. For instance, a typical underground water storage tank sizing formula is based on bedroom count, where you assume 2 people per bedroom using approximately 100 gallons of water per day.

For example:

  • A 2-bedroom home (4 people) would need about 400 gallons per day.
  • A 3-bedroom home (6 people) would require around 600 gallons per day.
  • A 4-bedroom home (8 people) should plan for roughly 800 gallons per day.

Considering refill frequency is also crucial. Homeowners often prefer larger systems to minimize the hassle of refilling, particularly during the winter months when access can be limited. Here's a breakdown of potential costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: starting from $13,000-$15,000.
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: starting from $22,000-$28,000.
  • Excavation can range from $3,000-$8,000+ depending on rocky conditions.
  • Pump system and pressure tank installation typically costs between $2,500-$5,000.
  • Final installed systems can range from $25,000 to about $55,000 based on size and installation complexity.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range from $25,000 to over $55,000, depending on factors like tank size, excavation needs, and access challenges. It's essential to budget for additional costs such as pump systems and electrical work, which can significantly impact the final price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on daily water usage and how often you wish to refill. A simple formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 (people per bedroom) and then by 100 gallons per day to calculate your needs.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and excavation challenges. Our team works efficiently to minimize disruption while ensuring all aspects of the installation meet local building codes.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors such as site conditions, water table depth, and your specific water needs. Cisterns offer a reliable alternative for properties where well drilling is impractical or where water supply is inconsistent.
