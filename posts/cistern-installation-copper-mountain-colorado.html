Local Challenges in Summit County

Building in Summit County, particularly in Copper Mountain, presents numerous challenges that require careful planning and expertise. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs and extended timelines. Additionally, the area's high elevation contributes to a water table that is frequently deep, necessitating the installation of a water cistern for many properties. Homeowners must also contend with extreme weather, ranging from heavy snow loads to afternoon thunderstorms, which can hinder access to properties, especially during the winter months. Furthermore, the frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it critical to implement winterizing cistern installation techniques to protect your system against freezing. Obtaining the necessary permits through the Summit County Building Department is also a crucial step in the process.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Copper Mountain

When budgeting for a cistern installation, it‚Äôs essential to consider several factors that influence the total investment. The cost of a potable water storage tank depends primarily on its size, which is calculated based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home would typically require a minimum of 18,000 gallons to allow for a monthly refill, based on the formula of two people per bedroom using about 100 gallons per day.

The following are typical cost ranges for a complete installed system:

  • 5,000-gallon system: $25,000 - $35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: $40,000 - $55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000 - 20,000 gallons): $50,000 - $75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): $75,000 - $120,000+

Additional costs may include excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on site access and rock conditions, and other necessary installations like pump systems and electrical hookups. Be aware that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, making it crucial to work with a knowledgeable cistern company.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado varies significantly based on size and site conditions. For a complete installed system, prices typically range from $25,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank to $120,000 for larger capacities, including excavation and additional equipment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be determined by your daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill it. A common formula is based on bedroom count; for example, a 3-bedroom home will typically require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installations usually take about 2-4 weeks, depending on site conditions, permits, and the complexity of the installation. Factors such as rocky terrain and weather can also influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. If your water table is deep or unreliable, a cistern may be a more dependable solution for rural water supply. Cisterns can provide a consistent water source while avoiding the complications and costs associated with drilling a well.
