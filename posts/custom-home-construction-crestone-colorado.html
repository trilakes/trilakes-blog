Why Crestone Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Crestone, nestled in the stunning mountain ranges of Saguache County at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for custom home construction. The breathtaking views and serene environment are appealing, but the rugged terrain and extreme weather conditions necessitate the expertise of a custom home builder who understands the intricacies of mountain home construction. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on being a licensed and insured general contractor, specializing in new construction tailored to the specific needs of our clients. With our extensive knowledge of local regulations, site conditions, and building best practices, we ensure your custom build is not only beautiful but also structurally sound and compliant with all requirements.

Local Challenges in Saguache County

Building in Crestone comes with its share of challenges, especially given the region's rocky granite soil, clay in valleys, and a water table that is often quite deep. Many properties require engineered septic systems due to the demanding soil conditions, and with a frost line reaching depths of 36-48 inches, proper foundation engineering is essential. Additionally, the steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate both construction and living conditions. Extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads demand careful planning for energy-efficient homes and fire-resistant construction in forested areas. As your trusted residential contractor, we are fully equipped to navigate these obstacles while ensuring your home is built to withstand the elements.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed, step-by-step process for custom home construction that ensures every aspect is meticulously planned and executed. It all begins with a build feasibility report, which we recommend conducting before buying land to assess site conditions, setbacks, and utility availability. Once you've secured your plot, we guide you through obtaining a well or cistern permit, as water access is crucial for permitting in Colorado.

Next, we develop a comprehensive site plan that outlines the positioning of your home, access routes, and utility placements. After that, we work on architectural plans and engage structural engineers for foundation and framing plans, ensuring all documents are stamped for compliance. Professional land surveys are also conducted to verify property boundaries and easements.

Once the plans are ready, we compile everything for a building permit submission to the Saguache County Building Department, which typically takes 2-8 weeks for approval. After receiving your permit, we prepare for construction, which includes finalizing materials and scheduling subcontractors. The construction phase unfolds in stages, from site preparation and excavation to final inspections and occupancy certificate issuance. This thorough timeline typically spans 10-18 months, depending on the complexity of your project.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Crestone

Understanding the costs involved in building a custom home in Crestone is crucial for planning your budget. The pricing for custom home construction in Colorado's mountain communities generally falls within a range of $550-$700 per square foot for standard quality builds, and can escalate to $700-$1,000+ per square foot for luxury homes. For example, building a 2,500 square foot home could cost between $1,375,000 and $2,050,000, depending on the finishes and design complexity.

Additional costs to consider include the installation of a cistern, which can range from $25,000 to $75,000, and septic systems, estimated between $35,000 and $80,000. The size of your cistern is typically calculated using the formula of two people per bedroom multiplied by 100 gallons per day. Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $550 to $1,000+ per square foot, depending on the quality of materials and design complexity. For a 2,000 square foot home, this could result in a total cost of $1,100,000 to $2,000,000.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The construction timeline for a custom home can vary, but it usually takes between 10 to 18 months from breaking ground to obtaining a certificate of occupancy. Complex projects may extend this timeline further.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their licensing, insurance, experience with local regulations, and previous projects. A reputable builder will provide a comprehensive process, including site assessments and an understanding of the unique challenges of mountain construction.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a build feasibility report is crucial before purchasing land. This study assesses the property's buildability, including soil conditions, water flow, and utility availability, helping you avoid potential pitfalls in the construction process.
