Why Almont Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Almont, nestled in the breathtaking Gunnison County, is a mountain community where the beauty of the Rockies meets the necessity for essential services like septic installation. With its elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, properties in this area face unique challenges that make professional septic system installation critical. Attempting to manage a septic system without expert guidance can lead to costly mistakes and environmental hazards. As a licensed and insured septic installer near me, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the specific regulations and requirements imposed by Gunnison County's Building Department, ensuring your system is compliant with Regulation 43.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County

The terrain of Almont presents distinct challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil, coupled with clay deposits in the valleys, often necessitate engineered systems rather than conventional options. With the water table frequently deep, many properties require cisterns for water supply, further complicating the installation process. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches means that any septic tank must be installed below this depth to prevent freeze damage. The extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms, can also hinder access to properties, especially those with steep driveways. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our process for septic installation is thorough and tailored to meet the unique needs of each property in Almont. First, we conduct a detailed soil testing analysis, which involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. These soil samples are sent to a licensed engineer for a comprehensive report that informs the design of your onsite wastewater treatment system (OWTS). Once we receive the report, we proceed with obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Gunnison County Building Department, as all installations must comply with local regulations.

The actual installation typically occurs during the building season from May to October, ensuring ideal conditions for excavation and placement. Our team will excavate the site, install the septic tank, and connect the leach field or drain field as determined by the engineered plan. We prioritize efficiency and safety, ensuring your system is ready for use while adhering to all local guidelines. Finally, we conduct a comprehensive septic inspection to confirm everything is functioning properly before your system goes live.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Almont

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation can help you budget effectively. In Almont, the total installed costs for a basic engineered system start around $35,000 and can range up to $50,000. For homes requiring a sand filter system, expect costs to fall between $45,000 and $65,000. If your property needs an advanced treatment unit (ATU), costs can be between $55,000 and $80,000+, depending on site conditions.

When considering installation, remember that the size of your cistern is based on bedroom count, calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a four-bedroom home would require a cistern sized for 800 gallons daily.

Several factors can increase installation costs, including rocky terrain, challenging access, and the need for specific engineered systems due to poor soil conditions. Additional expenses may also arise from excavation, which can range from $8,000 to $20,000+, and permit fees that vary from $500 to $2,000. Always consult with us for an accurate assessment of your unique site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of septic installation varies significantly based on site conditions. For most properties in Almont, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000 depending on the type of system needed and the complexity of the installation.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically ranges from a few days to a couple of weeks. This includes the time needed for soil testing, receiving permits from the Gunnison County Building Department, and the actual installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Almont require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A professional soils test will determine the type of system necessary for your property to ensure compliance with Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests rather than traditional perc tests to assess drainage capabilities. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples, which is crucial for determining the appropriate septic system design.
