Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our approach to septic installation is thorough and efficient, designed to meet the specific needs of Granite residents. Our process begins with an initial consultation to assess your property and discuss your needs. Next, we conduct soil testing rather than traditional perc tests to gather essential data about the earth beneath your home. This involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes that are eight feet deep, allowing us to evaluate the soil layers accurately.

Once we have the soil report from a licensed engineer, we can recommend the appropriate type of system‚Äîmost often an engineered system due to the region's challenges. After obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Lake County Building Department, we‚Äôll schedule installation, typically between May and October when conditions are more favorable. The installation timeframe can vary based on the complexity of the site, with most installations completing within one to two weeks. Throughout the process, we ensure that every step is compliant with Regulation 43 and that you are fully informed of the progress.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Granite

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Granite is crucial for homeowners. The total installed costs typically range from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems, with sand filter systems falling between $45,000 and $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs), which provide a higher level of treatment, can start from $55,000 and go upwards to $80,000. Factors that may increase costs include rocky terrain, steep access roads, and the need for additional excavation.

For properties requiring a cistern, the size is calculated based on the number of bedrooms‚Äîtypically, two people per bedroom require 100 gallons per day. You can expect costs for soil testing and engineering to be between $2,000 and $4,000, while permits can vary from $500 to $2,000 based on county regulations. Remember, working on challenging sites may also add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to your overall budget. It‚Äôs best to quote on the higher end to avoid surprises and ensure your installation meets all necessary requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions. For basic engineered systems, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000. More complex systems, such as sand filters or ATUs, may range from $45,000 to $80,000 or more, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a septic system typically ranges from one to two weeks. Factors such as site complexity, weather conditions, and the type of system being installed can influence the duration.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most mountain communities like Granite, engineered septic systems are often required due to challenging soil conditions. The soils report will guide the determination of the appropriate system needed for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil characteristics, which helps determine the best type of septic system for your property.
