Local Challenges in Jefferson County

The challenges of installing a septic system in Morrison, located in Jefferson County, are numerous. The region's soil often consists of rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay, making traditional septic installations problematic. Additionally, the water table is frequently deep, necessitating the use of cisterns or water hauling in many cases. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, ensuring that your system is winterized is vital to prevent freeze damage. The steep driveways and limited winter access further complicate the installation process. It's essential to have a septic contractor who understands these challenges and can recommend the most suitable engineered septic systems to meet your wastewater treatment needs.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for installing your septic system, ensuring that every detail is attended to. First, we conduct soil testing to analyze the specific conditions of your land. This involves bringing in a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, from which we gather soil samples for a licensed engineer's report. Based on the results, we can design a customized septic system that will function optimally in your unique environment.

Next, we handle the necessary permits through the Jefferson County Building Department, as mandated by Colorado‚Äôs OWTS regulations. This ensures that we comply with all local regulations and standards. Once we have the green light, our skilled team will begin excavation for the system, which typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on factors like site access and weather conditions. Finally, we install the septic tank and components according to the approved plan, followed by inspections to ensure everything meets regulatory requirements.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Morrison

The cost of septic installation in Morrison can vary significantly based on site conditions and system type. On average, you can expect to pay around $55,000 for an engineered septic system, which is the most common choice in our area due to challenging soil conditions. The complete breakdown of costs includes:

  • Soil testing and engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits (varies by county): $500-$2,000
  • Excavation for the system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank and components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical and controls: $2,000-$4,000

For cistern installations, remember that sizing is based on bedroom count‚Äîspecifically, two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. Therefore, if you have a three-bedroom home, you would need a cistern capable of holding 600 gallons. Please note that rocky terrain and difficult access can add $10,000-$25,000 to your overall costs.

Additionally, winter preparation is key; we recommend winterizing your system to avoid freeze damage, which can incur further costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for septic installation typically starts from around $55,000 for an engineered system. Costs can vary significantly based on soil conditions, the need for cisterns, and other site-specific factors.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on factors like weather, site access, and required permits. Efficient planning and preparation can help minimize delays.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Morrison will require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and high elevation. Regulation 43 mandates these systems to ensure proper wastewater treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to collect soil samples, which are then analyzed to determine the best septic system design for your property.
