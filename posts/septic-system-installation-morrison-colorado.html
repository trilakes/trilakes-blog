Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Morrison's unique terrain poses several challenges when planning for septic system installation. The rocky and often clay-rich soil can significantly impact water drainage and the effectiveness of a septic tank. Additionally, properties at higher elevations often have a deep water table, necessitating engineered systems rather than conventional ones. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it essential to winterize your septic system installation properly. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited access, particularly in winter, can complicate the installation process. Understanding these local challenges is critical for effective septic system installation and maintenance.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic system installation process is thorough and tailored to meet the specific needs of each property in Morrison. Initially, we conduct a detailed soils test at your site, using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. This helps us gather soil samples at various depths, which are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the best septic design for your property. Once we have the soils report, we proceed to apply for the necessary septic permit through the Jefferson County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

After securing the permit, our team will schedule the excavation and installation, which typically occurs during the building season from May to October. We take into account factors such as access, weather conditions, and the need for fire mitigation planning. Once the septic tank and any necessary components such as a leach field or drain field are installed, we conduct a final inspection to ensure everything meets state standards.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Morrison

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Morrison is crucial for homeowners. The total installed cost for a basic engineered septic system typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000. For a sand filter system, expect to budget between $45,000 and $65,000, while an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) can cost from $55,000 to $80,000+. The installation of cisterns is also a consideration, where the size is determined by the bedroom count; specifically, you multiply the number of bedrooms by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day.

Factors that can increase the cost include challenging terrain, rocky soils, and access issues; for difficult sites, you may need to add between $10,000 and $25,000 to your budget. A soils test and engineering report typically costs around $2,000 to $4,000, while permits in Jefferson County can range from $500 to $2,000. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost for septic installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on site conditions. Generally, a basic engineered system will range from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems can cost between $45,000 and $65,000, and Advanced Treatment Units can exceed $55,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically takes about 1 to 2 weeks once all permits are secured. Factors such as weather conditions, site accessibility, and the complexity of the system can affect this timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Many properties in Morrison, due to challenging soil conditions and elevation, require engineered septic systems. These systems are designed to handle the specific challenges of the area and comply with Colorado's Regulation 43.
