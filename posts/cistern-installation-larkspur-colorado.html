Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Larkspur

The cost of cistern installation in Larkspur varies significantly based on several factors. The typical installed cost for a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank starts at $25,000 and can reach up to $35,000. For a 10,000-gallon system, expect to pay between $40,000 and $55,000. Sizing is determined by your household's water needs, calculated as follows: daily water usage (75-100 gallons per person) multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home (2 people per bedroom). For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, leading to a total tank size of at least 18,000 gallons if filled monthly.

Additionally, excavation costs can range from $3,000 to $8,000, depending on rocky terrain and access challenges. Other factors such as pump systems, trenching, and electrical setups can add anywhere from $2,500 to $5,000 more to your total cost. The installation process typically requires a larger initial investment for those looking to avoid frequent refills. A larger capacity system also means fewer water deliveries, which is particularly advantageous during winter months when access can be limited. Always remember that rocky terrain can increase excavation costs by 20-40%, and planning ahead can save you considerable hassle and expenses in the long run.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado generally ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and complexity of the installation. For example, a 5,000-gallon tank typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is calculated at 75-100 gallons per person. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons if refilling monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 1 to 3 weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions and the complexity of excavation. Obtaining necessary permits may also impact the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. Cisterns are ideal for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources, while wells can be a more sustainable solution where feasible. Each option has its advantages and disadvantages based on the specific needs of your property.
