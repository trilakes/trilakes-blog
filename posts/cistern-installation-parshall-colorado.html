Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Parshall

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation in Parshall is crucial for budgeting. Installation costs can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and additional features. For example, the size of your underground water storage tank is primarily determined by daily water usage, which is calculated based on the number of bedrooms in your home. The formula is as follows:

  • 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day per person.

For a 3-bedroom home, this would typically mean a requirement of 600 gallons per day. Depending on how often you prefer to refill your cistern, you might need:

  • Monthly refills: 18,000 gallons minimum
  • Quarterly refills: 36,000-54,000 gallons
  • Winter supply: 72,000+ gallons

Costs for Norwesco tanks start at approximately $22,000 for a 10,000-gallon unit, excluding excavation, pumps, and other necessary installations. Excavation costs can range from $3,000 to over $8,000, especially in rocky areas, while additional systems like pumps and electrical connections could add between $2,500 and $5,000 to your total.

In total, a complete installed system for a 10,000-gallon tank could range from $40,000 to $55,000, depending on site conditions and specific requirements. Remember, investing in a larger system can reduce hassle in the long run, as it minimizes the need for frequent refills, especially during the winter months when water delivery might be limited.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of a cistern can range significantly based on size and site conditions. Typically, a basic installed system for a 10,000-gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000, while larger systems may exceed $75,000 depending on excavation and additional features.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be calculated based on daily water usage, which averages 100 gallons per person. For instance, a 3-bedroom home (6 people) would need a tank that can hold at least 36,000 gallons for quarterly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. The process includes consultation, permitting, excavation, and installation, which are all carefully managed to minimize disruption.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on specific property conditions. Cisterns offer a reliable water source when wells are impractical due to deep water tables or rocky soil. They can also be advantageous for properties requiring additional storage for irrigation or fire suppression.
