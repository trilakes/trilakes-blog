Why Parshall Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the picturesque mountain community of Parshall, Colorado, reliable water sources are essential for homeowners, especially at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet. Many properties face challenges accessing potable water due to deep water tables and restrictive well regulations. This is where professional cistern installation comes into play. A properly installed buried water tank can provide a dependable source of water, whether for residential use, agricultural needs, or fire suppression.

Choosing a licensed and insured cistern company like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your water storage tank installation is done correctly, adhering to local building codes and regulations set forth by the Grand County County Building Department. Our expertise in the unique challenges of the Colorado mountain terrain means that you'll have peace of mind knowing your system is installed with quality and care.

Local Challenges in Grand County County

Installing a water cistern in Grand County presents unique challenges that require expertise and careful planning. The rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay soil found in the region can complicate excavation efforts. As a result, digging can become significantly more expensive, adding 20-40% to overall costs due to difficult terrain.

Add to this the elevation of 8,000+ feet, where the frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, and you‚Äôll understand why winterizing your cistern installation is crucial. During the building season, which typically runs from May to October at these higher elevations, extreme temperature swings, heavy snow loads, and afternoon thunderstorms can arise, making timely and efficient installation critical. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can pose logistical challenges that require a skilled team to navigate.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a structured approach to cistern installation to ensure everything goes smoothly from start to finish. Our process begins with a thorough site assessment, where we evaluate soil conditions, access points, and specific water needs. This initial consultation helps us determine the appropriate tank size based on your daily water usage and refill preferences.

Once we've settled on a size, we will handle the necessary permits through the Grand County County Building Department before scheduling excavation. The excavation typically takes between 1-3 days, depending on the site conditions. We then install the underground water storage tank, which often involves burying a Norwesco tank, a trusted brand known for durability.

After the tank is securely in place, our team will set up the pump system and pressure tank, followed by trenching to connect the system to your home. The entire installation process usually takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on complexity, access, and weather conditions. Finally, we will conduct thorough testing to ensure that your new water storage solutions are fully operational and ready for use.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Parshall

The cost of cistern installation in Parshall can vary significantly based on multiple factors, including the size of the tank and the complexity of the installation. To help you estimate the necessary capacity, consider the daily water usage, calculated as 75-100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home, accommodating six people, would typically require a tank that holds at least 600 gallons per day.

When determining how often you plan to refill the tank, consider this: if you want to refill every two weeks, you‚Äôll need a minimum of 8,400 gallons; for monthly refills, 18,000 gallons is necessary; and for a quarterly refill system, a tank of 36,000-54,000 gallons would be ideal. For those wanting to "get through winter," larger systems of 72,000+ gallons may be necessary.

For a typical installation, you can expect the following costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • Excavation: $3,000-$8,000+
  • Pump system & pressure tank: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching to house: $25-$50 per linear foot
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000-$2,500
  • Water filtration/treatment: $2,000-$5,000

With all these factors combined, the total installed system costs for a standard setup can range from $25,000-$35,000 for a 5,000 gallon tank to upwards of $75,000-$120,000 for larger capacity systems. It‚Äôs important to remember that rocky terrain and challenging access can drive up costs, so it's always best to quote the high end of these estimates.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on the size and specific installation requirements. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can range from $75,000 to $120,000 when considering excavation and other necessary components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank, consider your household's daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants may require a tank with a minimum capacity of 18,000 gallons if you plan for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation typically ranges from 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the project. Excavation can take between 1-3 days, followed by the installation of the tank and final connections.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions and water availability. In areas with deep water tables or challenging soil, a cistern installation is often a more reliable option, providing a consistent water supply without the complications of well drilling.
