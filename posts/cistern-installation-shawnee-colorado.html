Why Shawnee Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Shawnee, Colorado, at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for water supply management. Many properties are off-grid or have limited access to reliable groundwater sources, making cistern installation an essential solution for homeowners. Installing a water cistern ensures you have a consistent and safe water supply for daily use, whether it's for drinking, landscaping, or irrigation. With the potential for extreme temperature swings and heavy snowfall during winter months, working with a professional cistern installer near you can help you navigate local regulations and ensure that your underground water storage tank is properly winterized and protected against freezing. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting specializes in this field, providing expertise that translates to practical, efficient, and compliant installations tailored to the unique needs of your mountain property.

Local Challenges in Park County County

Shawnee's high elevation and rugged terrain pose several challenges when it comes to water storage solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation and installation of your underground water storage tank, potentially increasing costs significantly, especially in areas with difficult access. Additionally, the local water table tends to be quite deep, requiring many homeowners to rely on water hauling or cistern systems as a viable alternative to well drilling. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning to prevent freeze damage to your system. Moreover, as the building season typically runs from May to October at these elevations, it‚Äôs crucial to start planning your cistern installation early to ensure that it can be completed before winter weather sets in.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Shawnee

The costs associated with a cistern installation can vary significantly based on several factors. The size of the cistern is primarily determined by your daily water usage and how often you wish to refill. For instance, a family living in a 3-bedroom home, which typically accommodates six people, may require a minimum of 600 gallons per day. If you wish to refill every month, you would need a system capable of holding at least 18,000 gallons to allow for convenience and reduced frequency of water delivery.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of the typical installed costs for cistern systems:

  • 5,000-gallon system: Starting from $25,000 to $35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: Starting from $40,000 to $55,000
  • Dual tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): Starting from $50,000 to $75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): Starting from $75,000 to $120,000+

Additional costs, such as excavation, pump systems, and electrical hookups, can add anywhere from $10,000 to $30,000 to your total installation price. Rocky terrain and difficult access can increase excavation costs by 20-40%, and properties with steep driveways may incur additional hauling costs. Thus, it's crucial to consider these factors when budgeting for your water storage tank installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely, but homeowners can expect to pay starting from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, with larger systems costing significantly more based on size and site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size you need depends on your household's daily water usage, which is generally estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants might require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes a few weeks from the initial consultation to completion, depending on site conditions, permitting, and weather. Early planning is key to finishing before winter.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and well depends on site conditions. Cisterns serve as a reliable alternative for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil. They provide greater control over water supply and can be more cost-effective in certain locations.
