Local Challenges in Fremont County

Florence's unique geology and climate pose significant challenges when it comes to water storage solutions. The area is characterized by rocky granite and decomposed granite, with clay present in the valleys. This means the water table is often deep, leading many homeowners to rely on water hauling and cistern installation as viable alternatives. With a frost line ranging from 36 to 48 inches, especially at higher elevations, it is crucial to consider winter preparation tips for your water cistern. Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Florence

The cost of cistern installation in Florence can vary widely based on several factors. On average, a basic water storage tank installation can range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank. These costs typically include the tank itself, excavation, pump systems, and any necessary electrical work. To determine the appropriate tank size, we use a formula based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count: multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 (the assumed occupancy) and then by 100 gallons per day.

For example, a 3-bedroom home with an occupancy of six people would require approximately 600 gallons per day. If you want to refill every month, you would need a minimum of 18,000 gallons; for quarterly refills, aim for 36,000 to 54,000 gallons, and for those wanting to ‚Äúget through winter,‚Äù consider a capacity of 72,000 gallons or more. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a typical cistern installation in Colorado ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank. Factors such as rocky terrain and site access can increase these costs significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: Your tank size should be based on your daily water usage and how often you plan to refill it. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would typically need a tank that holds at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, or up to 72,000 gallons to get through the winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process can take several weeks, depending on site conditions and the time of year. We aim to complete installations before winter to avoid complications related to freeze damage.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors, including water availability and soil conditions. In many cases, especially in regions with deep water tables like Florence, a cistern serves as a reliable alternative to well drilling.
