Why Jefferson Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the beautiful mountain community of Jefferson, Colorado, homeowners face unique challenges when it comes to securing a reliable water supply. With elevations exceeding 8,000 feet and a rocky, rugged terrain, many properties lack access to traditional well water. For these homeowners, cistern installation becomes a crucial solution. A professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a consistent and dependable source of potable water, even in the harshest winter months. Moreover, expert cistern installers, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, understand the specific needs of mountain properties, providing you with peace of mind and compliance with local regulations from the Park County Building Department.

Local Challenges in Park County

Jefferson, located in Park County, is characterized by its challenging mountain terrain and extreme weather conditions. The soil composition varies, with rocky granite and decomposed granite prevalent, making excavation for a buried water tank a demanding task. Additionally, the water table in this region is often deep, necessitating the use of a water cistern or water hauling for many residents. With a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, it is essential to consider winterization strategies when planning your cistern installation. The difficulty of accessing many properties during the winter months also means that homeowners should think ahead to ensure a reliable water supply throughout the colder seasons.

The cost of water storage tank installation in Jefferson can vary significantly based on several factors, including the size of the tank, site conditions, and the complexity of the installation. For instance, a typical Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank can range from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank will typically be priced between $22,000 and $28,000. Keep in mind that these costs are just for the tank itself; additional expenses will include excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rocky terrain, as well as pump systems, electrical work, and trenching.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on tank size and installation specifics. For example, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. Including installation costs, the total project can start from around $25,000 for smaller systems and can go up to $120,000 for larger, more complex setups.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on daily water usage, which is estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home, accommodating 6 people, will require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, leading to a total capacity of at least 36,000 gallons if refilling every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks. This timeframe can vary based on site conditions, weather, and required permits. Our team will ensure timely completion while adhering to local regulations and ensuring quality workmanship.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific site conditions and water needs. Cisterns are often more reliable for remote properties with deep water tables or poor well yields, while wells can be a good option if you have adequate water access. For many mountain properties in Jefferson, a cistern installation serves as a practical and efficient solution.
