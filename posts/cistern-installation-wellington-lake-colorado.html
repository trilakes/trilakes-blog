Why Wellington Lake Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning Wellington Lake area of Park County, Colorado, offers breathtaking views and a rustic lifestyle, but it also brings unique water supply challenges. At an elevation of over 8,000 feet, many properties in this mountainous region lack direct access to reliable water sources like wells. This is where cistern installation becomes essential. A professionally installed underground water storage tank not only ensures you have a consistent supply of potable water but also enhances the value of your property. When winter hits, having a well-planned water cistern can be the difference between a cozy, functioning home and a frustrating struggle to maintain water access.

Local Challenges in Park County

The rugged terrain of Wellington Lake presents several challenges for homeowners considering cistern installation. The soil composition varies from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, making excavation a complex endeavor. At this elevation, the water table is often deep, meaning that many properties rely on water hauling or require a buried water tank for adequate supply. Additionally, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning for winterizing cistern installation to prevent freezing. Weather conditions can also exacerbate these challenges; extreme temperature swings and heavy snow can limit access to properties, especially during winter months.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to ensure efficiency and quality. We start with an initial consultation to assess your water needs, taking into account your home's bedroom count and daily water usage. For example, a three-bedroom home typically requires around 600 gallons per day. This helps us determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank.

Once we finalize the design, we navigate the necessary permits through the Park County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations. Next, our team will excavate the site for the underground water storage tank, which can take additional time due to rocky terrain. Installation generally occurs within the warmer months, from May to October, to take advantage of favorable weather conditions.

After installation, we set up the pump system and necessary connections to your home, ensuring that everything is operational. This entire process typically takes several weeks, factoring in excavation, installation, and inspection timelines. We prioritize winter preparation, incorporating frost protection measures to ensure your system remains functional throughout the colder months.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Wellington Lake

The cost of water storage tank installation in Wellington Lake can vary significantly based on several factors. For a typical 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank, the price ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon tank can cost between $22,000 and $28,000. Additionally, total installed costs will generally range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic system, including excavation, pump systems, and trenching.
