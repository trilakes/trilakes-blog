About Cathedral Pines

Cathedral Pines is a picturesque neighborhood nestled in the serene surroundings of Black Forest, Colorado. Known for its stunning natural landscapes, this area is characterized by towering pine trees, rolling hills, and expansive views of the Rocky Mountains. The appeal of Cathedral Pines lies in its tranquil environment, offering residents a sense of privacy and seclusion while still being conveniently located near urban amenities in Colorado Springs. The neighborhood boasts a mix of custom-built homes that often feature luxurious designs and expansive lots, typically ranging from 2 to 5 acres. This allows homeowners both the space to build their dream homes and the freedom to enjoy outdoor activities in their own backyards. The community is also known for its excellent schools, making it a desirable location for families looking for a peaceful yet engaging lifestyle.

Water & Utilities

In Cathedral Pines, residents primarily rely on wells for their water supply, as municipal water services are not available in the area. Many properties come equipped with individual wells, which can be an attractive feature for homebuyers looking for self-sufficiency. When considering a property, it's crucial to research the well's depth, yield, and quality. The Colorado Well website (ColoradoWell.com) provides valuable resources to help prospective homeowners understand local water well regulations and the general availability of groundwater. Additionally, it's advisable to check the water quality through testing, especially if you plan to build on a vacant lot. Cisterns may also be utilized for water storage, particularly in homes where the well water may not meet all household needs. Overall, while the absence of municipal water can seem daunting, many residents in Cathedral Pines appreciate the independence and potential cost savings associated with well water.

Septic Considerations

Cathedral Pines is not serviced by a municipal sewer system, so residents typically rely on septic systems for wastewater management. Most homes in this area use conventional septic systems, although alternative systems may be necessary depending on the specific soil conditions and lot size. The rocky terrain and varying soil types in Black Forest can influence the type of septic system that can be installed, which makes it essential for potential buyers to conduct soil tests before purchasing land. Working with a knowledgeable local contractor can help ensure that the right septic solution is chosen, complying with El Paso County regulations. Homeowners should also be aware of ongoing maintenance requirements for septic systems to ensure their longevity and proper functioning.

Building & Permits

Building in Cathedral Pines falls under the jurisdiction of El Paso County, which manages all necessary permits for construction projects. It's important to familiarize yourself with local building codes and zoning regulations prior to starting any construction. Additionally, Cathedral Pines has a Homeowners Association (HOA) that enforces specific guidelines concerning the aesthetics and construction of homes to maintain the neighborhood's character.
