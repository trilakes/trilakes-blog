About Cameron

The Cameron neighborhood, nestled in the scenic foothills near Cripple Creek, Colorado, is a hidden gem characterized by its breathtaking views and serene landscapes. This area is appealing for those seeking a peaceful retreat from city life while still being close to the amenities of a historic mining town. Cameron offers a unique blend of rustic charm and natural beauty, making it a favorite among outdoor enthusiasts, retirees, and families looking for a tranquil place to call home. The property types in this area vary widely, from cozy cabins and single-family homes to expansive plots of land for those looking to build their dream home. Many properties boast wooded lots, mountain views, and easy access to outdoor recreational activities such as hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching.

Water & Utilities

Cameron primarily relies on well water for its residents, as municipal water services are limited in this rural area. Many homes use private wells or cisterns for their water supply, which can be a great way to ensure a sustainable water source if properly managed. If you're considering building or buying a property in Cameron, it's crucial to understand the water infrastructure in the area. Resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable information on well drilling, maintenance, and local regulations. Be prepared to conduct a thorough assessment of the water quality and quantity available on the property, as this can significantly impact your living experience. Additionally, some homes may have access to electrical utilities provided by local companies, but it's advisable to verify the availability of internet and other essential services in the area, as options may vary.

Septic Considerations

In the Cameron neighborhood, the majority of properties utilize septic systems since there is no centralized sewer system. Common types of septic systems include conventional systems and alternative systems, particularly in areas with challenging terrain or soil conditions. Before purchasing or building, it's essential to understand the specific requirements and regulations for septic systems in this region. The hilly and rocky terrain can pose challenges for installation and maintenance, so consulting with a local expert or septic contractor is highly recommended. Additionally, ensure that the property has sufficient space for proper septic system installation, as this is often a requirement for new builds in the area.

Building & Permits

The Cameron neighborhood falls under the jurisdiction of Teller County for building permits and regulations. Before beginning any construction, it's important to familiarize yourself with local building codes, zoning laws, and permit requirements. Some areas may have additional restrictions or guidelines, especially if they are part of a Homeowners Association (HOA). If you're considering building in Cameron, be sure to check for any HOA rules that may affect your plans, including architectural guidelines, land use, and maintenance standards.
