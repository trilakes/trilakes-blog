Do I Need a Well or Cistern? Colorado Water Options

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

If you're building in Colorado and need a reliable water source, you may require either a well or a cistern. The choice depends on factors like your location, water availability, and intended use. In areas without municipal water supply, a well is often necessary, while a cistern is advisable for those needing to store water for various uses.

Detailed Explanation

In Colorado, understanding your water options is crucial when constructing a home or facility. The decision between a well and a cistern involves several considerations:

  • Location: If your property is in a rural area without municipal water access, a well is often the primary choice. However, in regions with limited groundwater or strict water rights, a cistern may be more appropriate.
  • Water Availability: A well taps into underground aquifers, but not all locations have sufficient water supply. A well requires a permit and may involve testing for water quality.
  • Usage Requirements: Cisterns are beneficial for storing water from other sources, such as rainwater or trucked-in water. If you need water for irrigation, fire protection, or other non-potable uses, a cistern can be a great solution.
  • Cost and Maintenance: Wells generally require more upfront investment and ongoing maintenance, including regular testing and potential repairs. In contrast, cisterns can be less expensive to set up but require regular refilling and monitoring.

Signs you might need a well or cistern include:

  • Living in a remote area without access to a municipal water supply.
  • Experiencing water shortages during dry seasons.
  • Having specific water usage needs that exceed what a typical supply can offer.
  • Desiring to support landscaping or agricultural projects that require a reliable water source.

What This Means for Your Project

Determining whether to install a well or a cistern can significantly impact your construction timeline, budget, and long-term water management strategy. Before proceeding, consider consulting with a local water authority to understand the regulations and requirements for your area. You will also need to assess the geological conditions of your site to evaluate the feasibility of drilling a well.

Next steps involve securing necessary permits, hiring a licensed contractor for installation, and planning for the ongoing maintenance of your water system. Proper planning ensures you have a reliable water source that meets your needs while adhering to Colorado's water regulations.
```
