About High Country Ranches

Nestled in the picturesque foothills of the Rocky Mountains, High Country Ranches is a serene neighborhood located near Divide, Colorado. Known for its breathtaking views, abundant wildlife, and a strong sense of community, this area appeals to those seeking a tranquil retreat away from the hustle and bustle of city life. The region is characterized by its spacious lots, which typically range from two to five acres, providing homeowners with ample room to build their dream homes, gardens, or recreational spaces. With its combination of natural beauty and accessibility to outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and skiing, High Country Ranches has become a sought-after destination for both year-round residents and vacation home seekers.

Water & Utilities

In High Country Ranches, the water supply is primarily sourced from private wells, as there is no municipal water service available in the area. Homeowners typically rely on individual wells or cisterns for their water needs. When considering building or purchasing property here, it's vital to understand the water infrastructure and regulations governing well installations. Prospective buyers can research the specifics of well water availability and quality through resources like ColoradoWell.com, which provides valuable information on well drilling, permits, and testing. It's essential to ensure that any existing well complies with state regulations and has been adequately tested for water quality. Additionally, some properties may have cisterns for supplemental water storage, especially during dry seasons.

Septic Considerations

High Country Ranches does not have a centralized sewer system, meaning homeowners will need to install septic systems for waste management. The type of septic system commonly used in this area includes conventional systems, which consist of a septic tank and drain field. Due to the varying terrain, including sloping landscapes and rocky ground, it is crucial to have a professional evaluation of the land before installation. Soil tests will determine the best septic design suitable for the property. Buyers should be aware that the proper installation and maintenance of a septic system are essential for long-term functionality and compliance with local health regulations.

Building & Permits

The building permits for High Country Ranches are managed by Teller County. Before embarking on construction, it is important to familiarize yourself with the county's zoning laws and building codes. While there are no mandatory HOA restrictions within High Country Ranches, some properties may be subject to voluntary guidelines aimed at preserving the area's natural beauty and maintaining property values. It is advisable to check with the county's planning department for any specific requirements, including permitted building types, height restrictions, and allowable uses. Additionally, homeowners should consider the seasonal weather patterns when planning construction, as winter snow and spring rains can impact building schedules.
