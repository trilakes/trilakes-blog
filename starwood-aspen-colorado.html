About Starwood

Starwood is a prestigious residential neighborhood located just a few miles from the bustling town of Aspen, Colorado. Nestled in the serene foothills of the Elk Mountains, this area boasts stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, making it a prime location for those seeking tranquility combined with luxury. The appeal of Starwood lies not only in its breathtaking scenery but also in its exclusivity and proximity to world-class skiing, dining, and cultural experiences that Aspen offers. Property types in Starwood range from expansive estates and modern mountain retreats to cozy cabins, allowing for a diverse range of architectural styles and sizes suited to various tastes and lifestyles. This neighborhood is ideal for those looking to invest in a serene yet vibrant mountain community.

Water & Utilities

Starwood primarily relies on private wells for its water supply, with residents typically using cisterns for additional water storage. This self-sufficient approach to water sources means that buyers should be aware of the regulations and responsibilities that come with well ownership. The water infrastructure in the area is well-established, but potential homeowners should conduct thorough research, especially regarding well depth and water quality. Resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable insights into well water testing, maintenance, and regulations specific to the Starwood area. Additionally, residents have access to electricity and natural gas services, ensuring modern comforts in this mountainous setting.

Septic Considerations

In Starwood, most properties utilize septic systems as there is no municipal sewer service available. The types of septic systems commonly found in the area include conventional gravity systems and alternative treatment systems, which are chosen based on the specific terrain and soil conditions. Given the varied topography of Starwood, it is essential for prospective buyers to conduct a thorough assessment of the land prior to purchase. This includes understanding the soil percolation rates and any potential drainage concerns. Working with a knowledgeable local expert can help navigate these considerations and ensure that any septic system installed will function effectively and comply with local regulations.
