Where to Get a Perc Test in Colorado

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

To get a perc test (percolation test) in Colorado, contact a licensed soil testing professional or environmental consultant. Local health departments, such as the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment, can provide a list of certified testers. It's crucial to schedule the test well in advance of any construction project.

Detailed Explanation

A percolation test, commonly known as a perc test, is essential for determining soil absorption rates for septic systems and other wastewater management solutions. In Colorado, the test is typically required before installing a septic system, especially in rural areas where municipal services are unavailable. Here‚Äôs how to go about securing a perc test:

  • Contact Local Health Departments: Reach out to your county‚Äôs health department for guidance on local regulations, and they can often recommend certified professionals who conduct perc tests.
  • Hire a Certified Soil Tester: Look for licensed soil testers or environmental consultants in your area. Websites like the Colorado Association of Soil Conservation Districts can provide directories.
  • Understand Costs: Expect to pay between $300 and $1,000 for a perc test, depending on the location and complexity of the site.
  • Schedule the Test: Coordinate with your soil tester to determine the best time for the test, as weather conditions can impact results. Tests may take several days to complete.
  • Review Results: Once the test is complete, carefully review the findings, which will indicate your soil's suitability for a septic system and inform your construction plans.

What This Means for Your Project

Getting a perc test is a critical step in the planning phase of any construction project that involves wastewater management. The results will directly influence the design and installation of your septic system, which is essential for compliance with local zoning and health regulations. If your soil does not meet the necessary absorption rates, you may need to revise your plans or seek alternative wastewater solutions. Once you receive the results, consult with your contractor to address any implications on your project timeline and budget.
```
