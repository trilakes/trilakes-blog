About Vail Village

Nestled at the base of the majestic Vail Mountain, Vail Village is a charming European-style village that boasts a unique blend of outdoor adventure, luxury, and vibrant alpine culture. Established in the mid-1960s, the village features cobblestone streets lined with boutique shops, cafes, art galleries, and world-class dining options, making it an appealing destination for both tourists and residents alike. The stunning mountain views and access to outdoor activities, including skiing, hiking, and biking, draw many to consider building or buying a home here.

Property types in Vail Village range from cozy condominiums and townhouses to luxurious single-family homes and estates. Water & Utilities

Vail Village is served by municipal water, ensuring a consistent and reliable supply for residents. The water infrastructure is managed by the Eagle River Water and Sanitation District, which provides high-quality drinking water and wastewater services. Homeowners can expect modern plumbing systems and access to essential utilities that meet the needs of both full-time residents and vacationers. Septic Considerations

In Vail Village, most properties are connected to the municipal sewer system, making septic systems relatively uncommon. However, if you are considering building on a parcel of land that is not connected to the sewer system, you may need to install a septic system. Common types of septic systems in the area include conventional gravity systems and alternative systems for properties with challenging terrain. Given the mountainous landscape, be prepared for potential challenges related to soil type and drainage when evaluating septic options. Always consult with local health authorities to ensure compliance with regulations and to determine the best solution for your property.

Building & Permits

The Eagle County Community Development Department oversees building permits in Vail Village, ensuring that all constructions adhere to local zoning laws and building codes. It's essential to familiarize yourself with these regulations, especially if you plan to make significant changes or build new structures. Additionally, many properties in Vail Village are governed by Homeowners Associations (HOAs) that have their own set of guidelines and restrictions regarding design, landscaping, and property use. Before initiating any building project, it's crucial to engage with the HOA and ensure your plans align with their requirements, helping to maintain the aesthetic integrity of the neighborhood.
