Custer County Building Permits: Complete Guide

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed Contractor Updated January 2026

Building Permit Guide for Custer County, Colorado

Custer County, nestled in the heart of Colorado, is known for its stunning landscapes and outdoor recreational opportunities. Whether you're constructing a new home, adding an extension, or embarking on a commercial project, understanding the building permit process in Custer County is essential. This guide will provide you with an overview of the Custer County Building Department, the types of permits needed, how to apply, typical fees, timeline expectations, the inspection process, and some tips for a smooth permitting experience.

Overview of Custer County Building Department

The Custer County Building Department is responsible for ensuring that all construction within the county meets safety standards and complies with local codes and regulations. The department reviews building plans, issues permits, and conducts inspections to ensure that construction projects are safe and suitable for the environment. The department's mission is to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public while promoting responsible development throughout the county.

Types of Permits Needed

When planning a construction project in Custer County, you may need to obtain various permits, depending on the scope and nature of your project. Here are the primary types of permits you may require:

  • Building Permit: Required for the construction, alteration, or repair of structures, including residential homes, commercial buildings, and accessory structures like garages and sheds.
  • Septic Permit: Required for the installation or modification of septic systems. This is crucial for properties not connected to a municipal sewer system.
  • Well Permit: Necessary for drilling a new well or modifying an existing one. This permit ensures that the well complies with state regulations regarding water quality and usage.
  • Electrical Permit: Required for any electrical work, including new installations, upgrades, and repairs to existing electrical systems.

How to Apply for Permits

Applying for building permits in Custer County involves several steps:

  1. Gather Required Documentation: Before applying, ensure you have all necessary documentation, including construction plans, site plans, and any relevant reports (e.g., soil tests for septic systems).
  2. Complete the Application Forms: Download or request the appropriate application forms from the Custer County Building Department’s website or office.
  3. Submit Your Application: Submit your completed application along with the required documentation and any applicable fees to the Building Department. This can often be done online, via mail, or in person.
  4. Wait for Review: The Building Department will review your application to ensure it meets all local codes and regulations. This process may take several days to weeks, depending on the complexity of your project.

Typical Fees

Building permit fees in Custer County vary depending on the type of permit and the scope of work being performed. Here’s a general breakdown of what you can expect:

  • Building Permit Fee: Typically ranges from $100 to $1,000+, based on the estimated cost of construction.
  • Septic Permit Fee: Generally around $250, which may include the cost of inspections.
  • Well Permit Fee: Usually around $100 to $200.
  • Electrical Permit Fee: Typically ranges from $50 to $500, depending on the complexity of the electrical work.

It’s important to check with the Custer County Building Department for the most current fee schedule.

Timeline Expectations

The timeline for obtaining building permits in Custer County can vary widely based on the complexity of your project and the current workload of the Building Department. Here are some general timelines to expect:

  • Simple Projects: Projects such as small renovations or accessory structures may take 1-2 weeks to receive a permit.
  • Moderate Projects: Larger residential projects or more complex renovations can take 2-4 weeks.
  • Complex Projects: Commercial projects or those requiring extensive reviews may take 4-8 weeks or longer.

Keep in mind that these timelines can be affected by factors such as incomplete applications, required revisions, or additional inspections.

Inspection Process

Once your permit is issued, you will need to schedule inspections at various stages of your project. The inspection process ensures that all work complies with local building codes and regulations. Here’s how it typically works:

  1. Schedule Inspections: Contact the Custer County Building Department to schedule required inspections based on your project’s scope.
  2. Prepare for Inspections: Ensure that the work to be inspected is complete and accessible. Keep in mind that some inspections may need to occur before other work begins (e.g., foundation inspections before framing).
  3. Pass Inspections: The inspector will review your work and either approve it or provide a list of required corrections. If any issues are found, you’ll need to address them before scheduling a re-inspection.

Tips for Smooth Permitting

To ensure a smooth permitting process, consider the following tips:

  • Plan Ahead: Start the permitting process early, especially if you anticipate that your project may require extensive reviews or inspections.
  • Be Thorough: Ensure your applications are complete and all required documentation is included. Incomplete applications can lead to delays.
  • Communicate: Maintain open lines of communication with the Building Department. If you have questions or need clarification, don’t hesitate to reach out.
  • Stay Organized: Keep copies of all submitted documents, correspondence, and permits. This will help you track the progress of your application and address any issues that may arise.
  • Follow Local Guidelines: Familiarize yourself with local building codes and regulations to avoid issues that could delay your project.

By following this guide and being proactive in your approach, you can navigate the permitting process in Custer County with confidence. Good luck with your construction project!
