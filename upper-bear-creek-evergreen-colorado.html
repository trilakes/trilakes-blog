About Upper Bear Creek

Upper Bear Creek is a picturesque neighborhood nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, just a short drive from the charming town of Evergreen, Colorado. This area is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, characterized by towering pine trees, scenic mountain views, and the tranquil waters of Bear Creek winding through the landscape. What sets Upper Bear Creek apart is its unique blend of seclusion and accessibility: residents enjoy a peaceful, rural lifestyle while still being close to Evergreen's shops, restaurants, and recreational opportunities.

The neighborhood is appealing to homebuyers seeking a retreat from urban living, as well as those who appreciate outdoor activities such as hiking, fishing, and skiing. Property types in Upper Bear Creek vary widely, from cozy mountain cabins to luxurious custom homes, often situated on generous lots that provide ample privacy and space for outdoor enjoyment. The diverse real estate options cater to families, retirees, and individuals looking for a serene mountain escape.

Water & Utilities

In the Upper Bear Creek area, most homes are serviced by private wells rather than municipal water systems. This means that prospective buyers should conduct thorough research about the well water quality and availability. Homeowners typically rely on wells for their water supply, and it's crucial to understand the maintenance and regulations surrounding this. Resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable information regarding well installation, testing for water quality, and local regulations. Additionally, it's essential to consider the potential costs associated with drilling a new well or maintaining an existing one. In some cases, cisterns may be used in conjunction with wells, particularly in areas where water accessibility is limited. Be sure to inquire about the water infrastructure and any water rights associated with the property you are considering.

Septic Considerations

Upper Bear Creek is primarily served by septic systems rather than municipal sewage systems. When considering a property, it's important to be aware of the type of septic system in use, as there can be variations in design, including conventional systems and alternative systems designed for challenging terrains. The hilly and rugged terrain of Upper Bear Creek can present unique challenges for septic system installation and maintenance. Buyers should ensure that any existing system has been properly maintained and inspected for functionality. Additionally, if you plan on building, it's advisable to consult with local professionals to determine the best septic solution for your specific property and to comply with any county regulations.

Building & Permits

The building and permit process for homes in Upper Bear Creek is managed by Jefferson County. Before starting any construction, it is vital to familiarize yourself with the local building codes and land use regulations.
