About Woodland Hills

Woodland Hills is a picturesque residential community nestled in the foothills near Woodland Park, Colorado. This area is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, with sweeping views of Pikes Peak and easy access to the pristine wilderness of the surrounding national forests. The neighborhood appeals to those seeking a tranquil lifestyle, yet still wanting to be close to the amenities of Woodland Park, which is just a short drive away. Properties in Woodland Hills primarily consist of single-family homes and custom builds, often featuring spacious lots that provide ample privacy and room for outdoor activities. Many homes are designed to blend seamlessly with the natural landscape, showcasing log cabin aesthetics and modern mountain designs. The community's serene environment, combined with its outdoor recreational opportunities, makes Woodland Hills a desirable place for families, retirees, and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Water & Utilities

In Woodland Hills, residents typically rely on individual wells for their water supply, as the area is not served by municipal water systems. This means potential homeowners should be prepared to either drill a new well or inherit an existing one. The depth and flow rate of wells can vary, so it's advisable to research specific properties using resources like ColoradoWell.com to understand the water infrastructure better. Additionally, water quality testing is crucial, as some wells may contain minerals that require treatment. Residents should also consider the implications of well ownership, such as maintenance and potential regulations. While wells provide a degree of independence, prospective buyers should ensure they are informed and prepared for the responsibilities that come with this water source.

Septic Considerations

Most homes in Woodland Hills utilize septic systems, as the area is not connected to a public sewer system. Common types of septic systems include conventional gravity systems and alternative systems like mound or drip systems, depending on the property's soil and terrain characteristics. The mountainous landscape can present challenges such as rocky soil or steep slopes, which may necessitate specific system designs to ensure proper drainage and function. Before purchasing land or a home, it's essential to have any existing septic system inspected and certified, ensuring it meets state regulations and is in good working order. Understanding the maintenance needs and local septic regulations can save homeowners from future headaches and expenses.

Building & Permits

Building in Woodland Hills requires navigating the permitting process set forth by Teller County, which manages all construction permits in the area. Before starting any building project, it's essential to familiarize yourself with the county's zoning regulations and building codes. Additionally, it's worthwhile to check if there are any Homeowners Association (HOA) restrictions that may apply to your property, as these can influence the design and construction of your home. The HOA may have guidelines regarding architectural styles, landscaping, and even exterior colors. Understanding these regulations before you start designing your dream home can help streamline the building process and ensure compliance with community standards.
