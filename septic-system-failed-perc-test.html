Quick Answer

If your septic system has failed the perc test, you have several options to consider. These include installing an alternative septic system, relocating the system, or seeking a variance. Engaging with local experts like Tri-Lakes Contracting can guide you through the best choice for your property.

Detailed Explanation

A percolation (perc) test measures how quickly water drains through soil. A failure indicates that your soil may not adequately support a traditional septic system, which can complicate your construction or renovation project. Here are some options to consider: - **Alternative Septic Systems**: These include mound systems, sand filters, or aerobic treatment units. Each option has specific requirements and may be more suited to your land's conditions. - **Soil Remediation**: In some cases, it‚Äôs possible to improve the soil's drainage by adding organic materials or amending the soil composition. This route is often complex and requires a thorough understanding of local laws. - **Consulting Experts**: Engaging professionals who specialize in septic systems, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, can provide tailored solutions based on your specific situation and local regulations. Understanding the implications of a failed perc test can significantly impact your construction timeline and budget.

A failed perc test can delay your project and increase costs, as you may need to explore alternative solutions.
