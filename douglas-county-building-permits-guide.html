Building in Douglas County

Building in Douglas County, Colorado, is a rewarding yet complex process that requires careful navigation of local regulations and requirements. Known for its stunning landscapes and thriving communities, Douglas County has seen rapid growth, leading to an increase in construction projects ranging from residential homes to commercial establishments. When planning your build, expect to engage with various local agencies, adhere to zoning regulations, and obtain the necessary permits. The county emphasizes sustainable development, which means you'll likely encounter guidelines focused on environmental conservation and community aesthetics. Familiarizing yourself with these regulations early in your project will help streamline the process and prevent potential setbacks.

Septic System Permits (OWTS)

In Douglas County, septic system permits, also known as Onsite Wastewater Treatment Systems (OWTS), are handled separately from general building permits. This process is overseen by the Douglas County Health Department and is crucial for properties not connected to a municipal sewer system. Before you can begin construction, you must submit an application for a septic permit, which includes detailed site evaluations, soil tests, and system design plans. The Health Department will review these documents to ensure your proposed system complies with state and local health regulations.
