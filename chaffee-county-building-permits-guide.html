Building in Chaffee County

Chaffee County, nestled in the heart of Colorado, offers a stunning backdrop of mountains and rivers, making it a coveted destination for new builds. Whether you're planning a cozy cabin, a luxurious mountain home, or a commercial space, understanding the local building landscape is crucial. Expect a mix of rugged terrain and picturesque views, which can influence your design and construction process. It's essential to be aware of the unique local regulations and building codes that govern construction in this area. Chaffee County Building Department

The Chaffee County Building Department is your primary resource for all building-related permits and inspections. Their website, chaffeecounty.org/planning-zoning, provides a wealth of information regarding the permitting process, application forms, and guidelines. You can contact the department directly at (719) 539-2218, where knowledgeable staff are available to assist you. Expect a friendly and professional approach; they are accustomed to working with both seasoned contractors and first-time builders. Be prepared to provide detailed information about your project, as this will help streamline your permit application. The Building Department is dedicated to ensuring that all construction adheres to local codes and safety standards, making it a vital partner in your building journey.

Septic System Permits (OWTS)

If your property is not connected to a central sewer system, you will need to secure a permit for an On-site Wastewater Treatment System (OWTS), commonly known as a septic system. This process is separate from your building permit and is overseen by the Chaffee County Health Department. Begin by submitting an application along with a site plan that demonstrates the proposed location of your septic system in relation to your building and any water sources. The Health Department will review your plans to ensure compliance with state regulations designed to protect public health and the environment. Expect site inspections to verify soil suitability and system placement. It's advisable to engage a local septic system contractor familiar with the county's requirements, as they can guide you through the technical aspects of the permit process. Remember, obtaining your OWTS permit can take additional time, so plan accordingly to avoid delays in your overall project timeline.
