About Smeltertown

Smeltertown is a unique and charming neighborhood located just south of Salida, Colorado, nestled along the banks of the Arkansas River. This historic area was once home to a thriving mining community and has transformed into a vibrant residential locale that attracts outdoor enthusiasts and families alike. The stunning mountain views, proximity to recreational activities such as hiking, fishing, and whitewater rafting, make it an appealing choice for those seeking a serene lifestyle surrounded by nature. The real estate landscape in Smeltertown consists primarily of single-family homes, a mix of modern builds and charming older properties, as well as some vacant lots waiting for new construction. Water & Utilities

In Smeltertown, residents typically rely on a combination of municipal water supply and private wells. The Salida Water Department manages municipal services, which are generally reliable and well-maintained. For those considering building on vacant land, it is essential to research the availability of water connections or the feasibility of drilling a well. Resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide insights on well regulations and considerations specific to the area. Many properties may have existing wells, but it is wise to confirm their status, depth, and water quality before committing to any purchase. Additionally, utility services in the area generally include electricity, natural gas, and internet, ensuring that residents have access to modern conveniences while enjoying the beauty of rural living.

Septic Considerations

Most properties in Smeltertown are not connected to municipal sewer systems and instead utilize septic systems. The type of septic system commonly found in the area includes conventional systems and alternative systems like aerobic treatment units, depending on land size and soil conditions. When purchasing land or a home, it is critical to assess the terrain, as the topography can impact the design and function of septic systems. Conducting a soil test and consulting with local health departments will provide valuable information regarding suitability for septic installation. Understanding the regulations surrounding septic systems is essential for new builds and renovations to ensure compliance with local health codes.

Building & Permits

Building permits in Smeltertown are managed by Chaffee County, which has specific regulations and guidelines for construction in the area. It is crucial to familiarize yourself with these requirements before beginning any building project. There are no active homeowner association (HOA) restrictions in Smeltertown, providing homeowners with greater freedom in design and construction. However, it is advisable to check for any local zoning laws that may influence your project. Before breaking ground, ensure that you have the necessary permits and approvals in place. This will not only expedite the construction process but also ensure that your property adheres to safety and environmental standards.
