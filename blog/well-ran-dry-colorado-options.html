Your Options

Option 1: Well Rejuvenation

This process involves cleaning and reopening your existing well to restore its water flow. Techniques like hydrofracturing can help increase water yield by creating fractures in the surrounding rock to enhance water flow. The cost for this service typically ranges from $1,500 to $5,000, depending on the complexity of the job.

Pros: Well rejuvenation can be a less invasive and more cost-effective solution compared to drilling a new well. It also allows you to continue using your existing infrastructure.

Cons: This option may not work if the underlying groundwater issues are severe or permanent, and it may require ongoing maintenance.

When it makes sense: Well rejuvenation is ideal if you believe your well issues are temporary or if you want to avoid the costs and complications of drilling a new well.

Option 2: Drill a New Well

If your current well is beyond repair or if it has been determined that the groundwater in your area is permanently low, drilling a new well may be your best option. This process typically costs between $15,000 and $30,000 and can take several days to complete.

Pros: A new well can provide a reliable and long-term solution, tapping into deeper water sources that may not have been affected by current drought conditions.

Cons: This option can be expensive and may require permits and compliance with local regulations. Additionally, thereâ€™s no guarantee that a new well will yield the desired water flow.

When it makes sense: Drilling a new well is advisable if your current well has consistently underperformed or if youâ€™re looking to secure a more stable water supply for the long term.

As a temporary solution, you might consider hiring a water delivery service. These companies can provide you with potable water for drinking, cooking, and other household needs. Costs typically range from $100 to $200 per delivery, depending on the volume needed.

Pros: This option offers immediate relief and can help you manage your water needs until a more permanent solution is implemented. Itâ€™s also a hassle-free service, as the water is delivered directly to your location.

Cons: Relying on water delivery can become expensive over time, and it does not address the underlying issue of your dry well.

When it makes sense: Water delivery services are ideal for those who need a quick fix while exploring more sustainable, long-term solutions.
