Guide to Off-Grid Infrastructure in the Colorado Mountains

The Reality of Off-Grid Living

Living off the grid in the Colorado mountains is often romanticized as a return to simpler times, surrounded by breathtaking scenery and the sounds of nature. However, the practical reality can be quite different. While many properties in Teller, Park, and Chaffee counties are off-grid by choice or necessity, making this lifestyle sustainable requires careful planning and significant investment. Before diving into off-grid living, it's important to consider not just the charm of a secluded life but also the logistical challenges it presents. From access to water and power to waste management, each aspect demands attention and foresight. Fortunately, with the right resources and determination, off-grid living is absolutely doable. But itâ€™s essential to have a clear understanding of what youâ€™ll need to establish a functional and comfortable lifestyle in these beautiful yet rugged terrains.

Water: Your #1 Priority

Option 1: Well

One of the first considerations for any off-grid property is access to water. If you have the ability to drill a well and can find a reliable water source, that option can be ideal. However, drilling a well can be an expensive and uncertain venture, especially in elevated areas where water tables are often unpredictable. It's advisable to check resources like ColoradoWell.com to gauge the average depths of wells in your area, as this can give you a better understanding of your potential water source.

Option 2: Cistern (Recommended for Off-Grid)

For many off-grid residents, a cistern system serves as a more practical solution. Cisterns are predictable and can be installed virtually anywhere, making them ideal for mountainous terrains. Most mountain properties have access roads for delivery trucks, allowing for regular water delivery. A cistern can hold anywhere from 1,500 to 2,500 gallons of water, which typically suffices for a family's needs for two to four weeks. Companies like Tri-Lakes specialize in cistern installation, ensuring that you have a reliable and efficient water supply without the uncertainty of drilling a well.

Septic: No Grid Connection Anyway

One of the advantages of living off-grid is that standard septic systems can function independently of electricity. A conventional septic system, which relies on gravity, can be installed without any grid connection, making it suitable for off-grid homes. However, if you opt for an aerobic septic system, be aware that these systems require power to function properly. Regardless of your choice, itâ€™s crucial to plan for easy maintenance access to your septic system. Regular upkeep ensures that your waste management system operates efficiently and avoids costly repairs.

Power Options

When it comes to powering your off-grid home, there are several viable options to consider:

  • Solar + Battery: Currently the most common solution, solar panels paired with battery storage offer a sustainable way to harness energy from the sun.
  • Generator Backup: Having a generator fueled by propane or gasoline can provide backup power, especially during cloudy days or extended periods of low sunlight.
  • Micro-hydro: If you have access to a stream on your property, a micro-hydro system can generate continuous electricity, taking advantage of flowing water.
  • Wind: Wind energy can be an option, but it is highly site-dependent. Areas with consistent wind patterns may benefit from small wind turbines.

While this overview touches on the basics, it's worth consulting with experts to find the best power strategy for your specific property and lifestyle.

Heat: Propane is King

When it comes to heating your off-grid home in the Colorado mountains, propane reigns supreme. Most mountain areas have reliable propane delivery services, allowing you to have a consistent heat source. Installing a propane tank is generally straightforward, and it can be easily refilled as needed. Additionally, having a wood stove as a backup heating option can be very beneficial. Wood stoves not only provide warmth but also add a cozy atmosphere, making them a popular choice for many off-grid residents.

Internet & Communication

The landscape of off-grid communication has changed dramatically with advancements like Starlink, which offers high-speed internet access even in remote areas. This satellite service can significantly enhance your ability to work from home or stay connected with loved ones. Additionally, investing in cell phone boosters can help improve reception in areas that might otherwise experience weak signals.

Site Prep for Off-Grid Properties

Before any infrastructure can be installed, proper site preparation is essential. This includes excavation for your water systems, septic, and any other necessary installations. Additionally, ensuring that you have a well-maintained driveway is crucial for delivery access, especially for water deliveries and materials needed for construction. Companies like Tri-Lakes can assist with site preparation, ensuring that your property is ready for off-grid living and meets all necessary regulations.
