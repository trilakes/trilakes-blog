Common Causes

  • Seasonal Changes: Seasonal fluctuations can impact groundwater levels. Drought conditions, especially common in Colorado, can lower water tables significantly, leading to wells running dry.
  • Overuse: If your household's water consumption has increased, it may exceed the well's recharge rate. This is particularly common during the summer months when outdoor watering is frequent.
  • Well Depth Issues: If your well is shallow, it may be more susceptible to running dry, especially during periods of low rainfall or high demand.
  • Pump Problems: A malfunctioning pump can prevent water from being drawn from the well, leading to the appearance of a dry well even if there is still water present.
  • Nearby Development: Construction or drilling in your area can alter groundwater flow, potentially affecting your well‚Äôs water supply.

Immediate Steps to Take

  1. Stop Using Water: Immediately cease all water usage in your home to conserve any remaining supply.
  2. Check the Well Pump: Inspect your well pump to ensure it‚Äôs functioning correctly. Look for any signs of damage or malfunction.
  3. Inspect Your Water System: Check for leaks in your plumbing that could be contributing to water loss.
  4. Test Water Levels: If you have the equipment, check the water level in your well to see if there is still water present.
  5. Contact Neighbors: Talk to neighbors to see if they are experiencing similar issues, which may indicate a broader water supply problem.

When to Call a Professional

If you notice any of the following warning signs, it‚Äôs time to call a professional:

  • Your pump is running constantly without producing water.
  • You observe a significant drop in water pressure or quality.
  • There are unusual noises coming from your well pump.
  • Your neighbors are also experiencing dry wells, indicating a potential larger issue.
  • You are unsure about the cause of the dry well or how to proceed with troubleshooting.

Prevention for the Future

To prevent your well from running dry in the future, consider these proactive measures:

  • Regular Maintenance: Schedule regular maintenance checks for your well and pump to ensure everything is functioning properly.
  • Water Conservation: Implement water conservation practices in your home to reduce overall usage, especially during dry spells.
  • Monitor Usage: Keep track of your water usage patterns and adjust them based on seasonal changes.
  • Increase Well Depth: If feasible, consult with a professional about deepening your well to access more sustainable water sources.
  • Stay Informed: Keep an eye on local water table levels and drought conditions to prepare for potential issues.
