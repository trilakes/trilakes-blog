About Blue Mountain Estates

Nestled in the serene foothills of the Rocky Mountains, Blue Mountain Estates offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Located near Divide, Colorado, this neighborhood is renowned for its stunning mountain views, diverse wildlife, and an abundance of outdoor recreational opportunities. Residents enjoy a close-knit community feel while being just a short drive from larger towns like Woodland Park and Cripple Creek. The area is particularly appealing to those seeking a peaceful lifestyle, with a mix of year-round and seasonal residents. Property types in Blue Mountain Estates vary from cozy cabins and custom-built homes to vacant lots perfect for your dream build. The combination of natural beauty, friendly neighbors, and a wealth of outdoor activities makes this neighborhood a desirable place to call home.

Water & Utilities

In Blue Mountain Estates, most properties rely on individual wells or cisterns for their water supply, as the area does not have municipal water services. Homeowners are responsible for drilling their own wells, which can be a significant aspect of the buying or building process. It's essential to research well regulations and water availability through resources like ColoradoWell.com, where you can find information on well permits and water quality. Depending on the depth of the water table in your chosen location, well drilling costs can vary, so budgeting accordingly is important. It's also advisable to check the water rights associated with any property you're considering, as this can impact your access and use of water resources. Septic Considerations

Blue Mountain Estates does not have a central sewer system, meaning that most properties will utilize septic systems for waste management. Common types of septic systems in the area include conventional systems, which consist of a septic tank and a drain field, as well as alternative systems for properties with challenging terrain. The mountainous landscape can pose unique challenges for septic installations, so understanding soil conditions and drainage is crucial. Before purchasing land or building a home, it's advisable to have a soil test performed to determine its suitability for a septic system. Local regulations require that all septic systems be permitted and inspected, so working with a qualified contractor familiar with local codes will help streamline the process.
