About Bull Hill

The Bull Hill neighborhood, located just a few miles from the historic town of Cripple Creek, Colorado, offers a unique blend of rugged mountain beauty and rich historical significance. Nestled at an elevation of over 9,000 feet, this area features stunning views of the surrounding Colorado Rockies, making it a desirable location for both year-round residents and seasonal visitors. The charm of Bull Hill lies in its peaceful atmosphere, surrounded by nature, wildlife, and the remnants of the gold rush era.

What makes Bull Hill particularly appealing is its close-knit community feel, combined with the recreational opportunities available right outside your door. Residents and visitors can enjoy hiking, biking, and exploring nearby national forests, while the historic town of Cripple Creek offers entertainment, dining, and cultural experiences. The property types in Bull Hill range from cozy cabins and rustic homes to larger parcels of land perfect for building your dream mountain retreat, providing a variety of options for potential buyers and builders.

Septic Considerations

The Bull Hill neighborhood does not have municipal sewer services, which means most homes utilize septic systems. When purchasing land in this area, it‚Äôs crucial to understand the type of septic system that may be required. Aerobic, conventional, and sand filter septic systems are common, and the specific type will depend on factors such as soil conditions and the size of the property.

Terrain considerations are essential when planning for a septic system. The hilly and rocky landscape of Bull Hill can pose challenges for installation and maintenance, so a thorough site evaluation is necessary. Prospective buyers should also check for existing septic permits and any required inspections to avoid complications down the line.
