About Green Mountain Heights

Green Mountain Heights is a picturesque neighborhood located just a stone's throw away from the charming town of Green Mountain Falls, Colorado. Nestled at the foothills of the majestic Rocky Mountains, this area boasts breathtaking views, an abundance of outdoor recreational opportunities, and a serene mountain atmosphere that attracts nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike. The community is characterized by a mix of single-family homes, cabins, and vacant lots, providing ample opportunities for prospective buyers and builders. With its proximity to hiking trails, lakes, and parks, as well as easy access to Colorado Springs, Green Mountain Heights is an ideal place for those seeking a peaceful retreat without sacrificing modern conveniences.

Water & Utilities

In Green Mountain Heights, residents primarily rely on wells for their water supply. The area does not have a municipal water system, so homeowners will need to drill their own wells or utilize existing ones if available. Water quality and yield are essential considerations, and prospective buyers should research their options thoroughly. Websites like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable insights into local well regulations, water rights, and drilling services. It's advisable to conduct a water quality test to ensure that the water meets health standards. Additionally, residents should be prepared for the maintenance responsibilities that come with well ownership, such as regular inspections and possible water filtration systems.

Septic Considerations

In terms of waste disposal, Green Mountain Heights predominantly utilizes septic systems, as the area lacks a centralized sewer system. Most homes have conventional septic systems, which consist of a septic tank and a drain field. Terrain considerations are crucial when installing a septic system; the rocky and hilly landscape can present challenges for proper placement and drainage. Homeowners should consult with local septic system professionals to ensure compliance with county regulations and to evaluate the suitability of their property for septic installation. It's also important to understand the maintenance requirements of septic systems, which can help prevent costly repairs down the line.
