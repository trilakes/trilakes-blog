When to Winterize Your Cistern in Colorado

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

You should winterize your cistern in Colorado before the first hard freeze, typically between late September and mid-October, depending on your specific location and elevation. This ensures that the water system is protected from freezing temperatures, which can cause costly damage and potential water supply issues.

Detailed Explanation

Winterizing your cistern is crucial in Colorado's mountainous regions, where temperatures can plummet quickly. The ideal time to begin this process is when nighttime temperatures consistently dip below 32¬∞F, signaling the onset of winter. Factors influencing the timing include:

  • Geographical Variation: Higher elevations may experience freezing temperatures earlier than lower areas. Monitor local forecasts for your specific altitude.
  • Historical Weather Patterns: Review past weather data to inform your winterization schedule. Areas prone to early snow or frost should be prioritized.
  • Water Usage Needs: If you rely on your cistern year-round, consider the risk of freezing and adjust your winterization accordingly.

Acting too early can lead to unnecessary labor and costs, while waiting too long can risk damage to your cistern and plumbing. If you winterize before the temperatures drop, you may have to repeat some steps or monitor the system longer than necessary. Conversely, if you wait until after the first freeze, you risk:

  • Frozen Pipes: Water can expand when frozen, leading to ruptured pipes and costly repairs.
  • Water Contamination: Stagnant water in a frozen system can lead to bacterial growth, affecting water quality.
  • Increased Costs: Emergency repairs due to freezing damage can be significantly more expensive than preemptive winterization.

What This Means for Your Project

Properly winterizing your cistern protects your water supply and avoids costly repairs. Here are practical steps you should take:

  • Drain any excess water from the cistern and pipes to prevent freezing.
  • Insulate any exposed pipes and the cistern itself with materials designed for cold weather.
  • Consider installing a heating element or heat tape if your area is prone to extreme conditions.

Planning for winterization should be a part of your annual maintenance schedule. Mark your calendar for late September to assess your system and make necessary preparations.
```
