About Shining Mountain

Shining Mountain is a picturesque neighborhood located just a few miles from the vibrant town of Woodland Park, Colorado. Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, this area is known for its stunning views, tranquil atmosphere, and abundant outdoor recreational opportunities. With a mix of single-family homes, cabins, and vacant lots, Shining Mountain attracts a diverse community of nature lovers, retirees, and families seeking a peaceful lifestyle away from the hustle and bustle of urban life. The neighborhood's proximity to Pikes Peak and numerous hiking trails makes it particularly appealing to outdoor enthusiasts. Additionally, the charming local shops and restaurants in Woodland Park are only a short drive away, providing residents with essential amenities while still allowing them to enjoy the serenity of mountain living.

Water & Utilities

In the Shining Mountain area, most properties rely on wells for their water supply, as municipal water services are limited. Homebuyers should be prepared for the responsibility of maintaining their own water systems. For those considering drilling a new well, it's crucial to research the water table and quality. Websites like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable information on the area's well history and water availability. Additionally, many properties may utilize cisterns as a secondary water source for non-potable uses, like irrigation or fire protection. When building or buying in Shining Mountain, it's essential to evaluate the existing water infrastructure and consider potential costs for water system installation or maintenance, which can vary widely depending on the property and location.

Septic Considerations

Shining Mountain is not connected to a municipal sewer system, so homes in the area typically use septic systems for wastewater management. The most common types of septic systems in this region are conventional systems, which consist of a septic tank and a leach field. When purchasing a property, it is crucial to have the septic system inspected to ensure it is functioning correctly and complies with local regulations. Additionally, terrain plays a significant role in septic system placement and performance; the hilly landscape may require specialized system designs or more extensive permitting processes. Homebuyers should familiarize themselves with the local soil conditions and drainage patterns, as these factors can impact the feasibility and cost of septic installation.

Building & Permits

The Teller County government oversees building permits in Shining Mountain, and it is essential for potential builders to familiarize themselves with local regulations and requirements. Before starting any construction, you will need to obtain the necessary permits, which may include site plans, environmental assessments, and adherence to zoning laws. Additionally, some properties in Shining Mountain may be subject to Homeowners Association (HOA) rules, which can dictate architectural styles, landscaping, and other aspects of property development. It's recommended to check with the local HOA, if applicable, to ensure compliance. Understanding these factors prior to building can help streamline the process and prevent costly delays.
