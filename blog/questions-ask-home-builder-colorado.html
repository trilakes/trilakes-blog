The 25 Essential Questions

Licensing & Insurance (Questions 1-5)

  1. Are you licensed as a general contractor in Colorado?
    This question is fundamental as a valid license ensures that the builder has met the state's requirements for education, experience, and ethics in the construction industry.
  2. What's your license classification?
    Understanding the classification gives insight into the builderâ€™s area of expertise and the types of projects they are legally allowed to undertake.
  3. Can I see your insurance certificate?
    Insurance protects both you and the contractor from potential liabilities that may arise during the building process, ensuring peace of mind.
  4. What does your warranty cover?
    A comprehensive warranty indicates the builder's confidence in their work and provides protection for you against defects or issues after the home is completed.
  5. Are you bonded?
    Bonding means that the contractor has secured a bond to protect you from potential financial loss due to incomplete work or failure to meet contractual obligations.

Experience & Track Record (Questions 6-10)

  1. How long have you been building in this area?
    Local experience is crucial as it indicates familiarity with regional building codes, climate challenges, and community preferences.
  2. How many homes have you built at this elevation?
    Building at higher elevations presents unique challenges; a contractor with experience in this area will be better equipped to handle them effectively.
  3. Can I visit a current job site?
    Visiting a job site allows you to see the builder's work ethic, attention to detail, and the quality of materials being used firsthand.
  4. Can I talk to past clients?
    Speaking with previous clients offers valuable insights into the builderâ€™s communication style, reliability, and overall customer satisfaction.
  5. What's your specialty?
    Knowing a builder's specialty can help you determine if they are the right fit for your specific vision and project needs, whether it's luxury homes, eco-friendly designs, or another focus.

Process & Communication (Questions 11-15)

  1. Who will be my main point of contact?
    Clear communication is vital in any construction project; knowing who to reach out to ensures that your queries and concerns are addressed promptly.
  2. How often will we meet during construction?
    Regular meetings help keep you informed about the projectâ€™s progress, allowing for timely adjustments to plans or expectations as needed.
  3. How do you handle change orders?
    Understanding how change orders are managed can prevent misunderstandings and potential conflicts regarding costs and timelines.
  4. What project management tools do you use?
    Effective project management tools can enhance communication and streamline the building process, ultimately leading to a more efficient project.
  5. How do you handle delays?
    Knowing how a builder addresses potential delays can provide reassurance and help set realistic expectations for your project timeline.

Financial (Questions 16-20)

  1. What's your payment schedule?
    A clear payment schedule helps you manage your budget and ensures that you are only paying for work that has been completed to your satisfaction.
  2. Do you provide detailed cost breakdowns?
    Transparency in costs is essential; a detailed breakdown helps you understand where your money is going and can prevent unexpected expenses.
  3. How do you handle cost overruns?
    Understanding how the builder manages unforeseen costs can help you prepare for potential financial surprises during the project.
  4. Do you require a deposit?

  5. What's included vs. excluded in your bid?
    Clarity on inclusions and exclusions in the project bid can prevent misunderstandings later on and ensure all parties are on the same page.

Mountain-Specific (Questions 21-25)

  1. How do you handle the limited building season?
    Building in mountainous terrain often means dealing with short construction windows; a knowledgeable builder will have strategies to maximize efficiency during these times.
  2. What's your experience with rocky/difficult soil?
    Soil conditions can greatly affect the stability and cost of your home; a builder experienced in dealing with challenging terrain will have effective solutions to mitigate issues.
  3. How do you address fire mitigation requirements?
    Fire safety is particularly important in Colorado; understanding a builderâ€™s approach to fire mitigation shows their commitment to safety and compliance with local regulations.
  4. How do you handle access issues during winter?
    Snow and ice can complicate construction; a contractor who has strategies in place for winter access will help ensure the project remains on track.
  5. What energy efficiency features do you recommend for this elevation?
    Energy efficiency is crucial in mountainous areas; a knowledgeable builder can recommend features that will save you money and enhance comfort in your new home.
