About North Evergreen

North Evergreen is a picturesque neighborhood located just north of the charming town of Evergreen, Colorado. Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, this area boasts stunning views, abundant wildlife, and a strong sense of community. One of the key attractions of North Evergreen is its proximity to outdoor recreational opportunities, including hiking, fishing, and skiing, making it an ideal location for nature enthusiasts. The neighborhood features a mix of property types, including single-family homes, spacious estates, and cozy cabins, all designed to blend harmoniously with the natural landscape. With its serene ambiance and access to a variety of amenities in Evergreen, North Evergreen has become a desirable place to live for families, retirees, and anyone seeking a peaceful mountain lifestyle.

Water & Utilities

In North Evergreen, residents primarily rely on municipal water systems, with some properties utilizing wells or cisterns. The area's water infrastructure is well-regulated, ensuring that residents receive clean and safe drinking water. If you are considering a property that relies on a well, it is essential to conduct thorough research, including checking the flow rate and water quality. Resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable information on well regulations and performance for properties in the region. Additionally, be prepared for varying utility availability, as some locations may have limited access to certain services, particularly in more remote areas. Understanding the local water infrastructure is crucial for prospective homeowners and builders.

Septic Considerations

Most properties in North Evergreen are serviced by septic systems rather than a centralized sewer system. The type of septic system you may encounter includes conventional systems, alternative systems, and sometimes advanced treatment systems, depending on the size of the lot and the terrain. The hilly and rocky landscape can pose challenges for septic installation, so it's essential to conduct a percolation test and consult with a local expert to ensure that your system meets local health regulations. Buyers should also be aware that maintenance of septic systems is crucial; regular inspections and pumping can prevent costly repairs and environmental issues.
