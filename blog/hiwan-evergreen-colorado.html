About Hiwan

The Hiwan neighborhood, nestled in the picturesque foothills of Evergreen, Colorado, is a serene enclave known for its stunning mountain views, abundant pine forests, and proximity to outdoor recreational activities. This area is appealing for its blend of natural beauty and a strong sense of community, offering residents a peaceful lifestyle while being just a short drive from the vibrant amenities of Evergreen. The properties here range from charming single-family homes to more luxurious estates, often featuring sprawling lots that provide ample privacy and space to enjoy the great outdoors. Many homes boast architectural designs that harmonize with the natural landscape, creating a picturesque setting that is hard to resist. Whether you're seeking a weekend getaway or a permanent residence, Hiwan provides the perfect backdrop for mountain living.

Water & Utilities

In the Hiwan neighborhood, residents typically rely on individual wells for their water supply, rather than municipal water systems. Due to the area's mountainous terrain, it is essential to research the specifics of well installation and maintenance. You can visit ColoradoWell.com for valuable information regarding well permits, drilling requirements, and potential water quality issues. Depending on the property, some homes may also use cisterns as a supplementary water source, particularly for fire protection or irrigation. When purchasing or building a home in Hiwan, it is crucial to conduct thorough water testing and assess the reliability of the well, as well as understand the implications of water usage restrictions during drought periods. Ensuring you have a sustainable water source is a vital step in maintaining your home in this beautiful area.

Septic Considerations

Most properties in the Hiwan neighborhood are serviced by septic systems, as the area lacks centralized sewer infrastructure. Homeowners must ensure that their septic systems are properly designed and maintained, as the rocky terrain can pose unique challenges for installation. Common types of septic systems in the area include conventional gravity systems and alternative systems that may include advanced treatment options for better environmental compliance. When considering a property, it's essential to have a septic inspection performed to evaluate its condition and capacity, which can save you from costly repairs down the line. Additionally, understanding the terrain and local regulations regarding septic systems will help you navigate the building process smoothly and ensure a sustainable waste management solution for your home.

Building & Permits

The Hiwan neighborhood falls under the jurisdiction of Jefferson County, which manages the building permits for the area. Before embarking on any construction projects, it is essential to familiarize yourself with the local zoning regulations and building codes. Homeowners should also be aware of any Homeowners Association (HOA) restrictions that may apply, as these can influence building design, landscaping, and property maintenance. It's advisable to consult with the HOA to ensure compliance with their guidelines. Additionally, working with local contractors who understand the building landscape in Hiwan can be beneficial. They can guide you through the permitting process, helping you avoid common pitfalls and ensuring that your project adheres to all necessary requirements.
