Quick Answer: Cistern Costs in Colorado

Cistern installation in Colorado typically costs $5,000-$15,000 in Colorado mountain communities. The final price depends on several key factors, including the size and type of the cistern, site conditions, and accessibility to the installation area.

Cost Breakdown: What You're Paying For

When you're investing in a cistern, itâ€™s important to understand what your money is going towards. Hereâ€™s a detailed breakdown of the primary cost components:

  • Materials: The cost of the cistern itself is a significant portion of your total expense. Depending on the type (plastic, concrete, or fiberglass), prices can range from $1,000 to $10,000.
  • Labor: Labor costs can vary widely based on local rates and the complexity of the installation. Expect to pay $50 to $100 per hour for skilled labor. Total labor costs can range from $1,500 to $5,000.
  • Equipment: Equipment rentals or purchases for the installation process can add another $500 to $1,500, depending on whatâ€™s required for your specific site.
  • Permits: Depending on your local regulations, permits can cost anywhere from $100 to $1,000. Always check with your local building authority for specifics.

When you combine these costs, you can see how the total could easily range from $5,000 to $15,000, depending on your unique situation.

Factors That Affect Your Price

Several factors can influence the final cost of installing a cistern in Colorado:

  • Site Conditions: The condition of your land can significantly impact costs. Steep or rocky terrain may require additional excavation and stabilization, increasing labor and equipment costs.
  • Access: If your property is difficult to access, this can lead to higher transportation costs for materials and equipment. Installation on remote sites may require additional planning.
  • Soil Type: The type of soil can affect how easily the cistern can be installed. Sandy soils may require different approaches than clay soils, which may necessitate additional excavation or reinforcement.
  • Distance from Town: The further your property is from urban centers, the higher the transportation costs for materials. This can also affect the availability of skilled labor.
  • Complexity: The complexity of your installation can lead to higher costs. Custom installations or integrating the cistern with existing water systems may require specialized labor and materials.
  • Season: Prices can fluctuate with the seasons, especially in Colorado where winter conditions can halt construction. Installing during peak summer months may also carry a premium due to demand.

Assessing these factors will help you prepare a more accurate budget for your cistern installation.

Cost by Type/Option

There are various types of cisterns available, each with different price points and functionalities:

  • Plastic Cisterns: Generally the most affordable option, these can range from $1,000 to $3,000 depending on size.
  • Concrete Cisterns: More durable but also more expensive, concrete cisterns typically cost between $3,000 and $10,000.
  • Fiberglass Cisterns: These are lightweight and resistant to corrosion, costing around $2,500 to $5,000.

Consider the long-term benefits and durability of each type before making a decision, as the upfront cost may vary significantly.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

When getting quotes for cistern installation, be aware of potential hidden costs that may not be included in the initial estimate:

  • Additional Excavation: If unexpected obstacles arise during installation, you may incur extra costs for excavation or site preparation.
  • Water Testing: Before installation, you may need to test your water supply, which could add to your costs.
  • Maintenance Costs: Consider ongoing maintenance and cleaning, which can add to your long-term expenses.

Make sure to ask your contractor about these potential costs to avoid surprises later on.

How to Get Accurate Pricing

To ensure you get an accurate estimate for your cistern installation, follow these steps:

  • Get Multiple Quotes: Always gather estimates from several contractors to compare prices and services.
  • Ask What's Included: Clarify what the quote covers, such as labor, materials, and permits to avoid hidden fees.
  • Site Visit is Essential: A site visit allows contractors to assess the conditions and provide a more accurate estimate based on your specific needs.

Taking these steps will help you make an informed decision and ensure you stay within budget.
