What Is an Engineered Septic System?

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

An engineered septic system is a specialized wastewater treatment system designed to manage and treat sewage and wastewater, particularly in areas where traditional systems may not work effectively. These systems are meticulously planned and often include advanced technology to ensure proper function, making them ideal for challenging terrains or soil types commonly found in Colorado.

Detailed Explanation

Engineered septic systems differ from conventional septic systems in that they are tailored to meet specific site conditions and regulatory requirements. This makes them particularly useful in mountainous regions like Colorado, where factors such as soil composition, topography, and groundwater levels can complicate wastewater management. Here are some key features:

  • Site-Specific Design: An engineered system is designed based on a thorough assessment of the location, including soil tests and hydrological studies.
  • Advanced Treatment Technology: These systems may incorporate aeration, filtration, or other technologies to enhance the treatment process and reduce environmental impact.
  • Increased Capacity: Engineered systems can handle larger volumes of wastewater, making them suitable for larger homes or commercial properties.
  • Regulatory Compliance: They are often required to meet stringent state regulations, ensuring that the system functions effectively and safely.

Homeowners may require an engineered septic system for various reasons, such as:

  • Building on land with poor drainage or rocky soil.
  • Constructing a home in a remote location where municipal sewer services are unavailable.
  • Renovating or expanding an existing property that exceeds the capacity of a standard septic system.
```
