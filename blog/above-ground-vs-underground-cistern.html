Above Ground vs Underground Cistern: Pros and Cons

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

When deciding between an above ground and underground cistern, consider your specific needs and site conditions. Above ground cisterns are easier to install and maintain but may be less aesthetically pleasing and susceptible to temperature fluctuations. Conversely, underground cisterns save space and are insulated from extreme weather but are more expensive and complex to install.

Detailed Explanation

Choosing the right cistern for your Colorado property involves weighing the pros and cons of both above ground and underground options. Here‚Äôs a closer look:

Above Ground Cisterns

  • Pros:
    • Ease of Installation: These cisterns are generally easier and faster to install, requiring less excavation.
    • Maintenance Accessibility: Routine checks and maintenance can be performed without extensive digging.
    • Cost-Effective: Typically, they are cheaper to purchase and install compared to underground options.
    • Visibility: You can easily monitor water levels and detect leaks.
  • Cons:
    • Aesthetics: Above ground cisterns can detract from the visual appeal of your property.
    • Weather Vulnerability: They can be affected by extreme temperatures, leading to potential freezing in winter or algae growth in summer.
    • Space Requirements: They take up more surface area, which could be a concern if space is limited.

Underground Cisterns

  • Pros:
    • Space Saving: These systems are hidden underground, freeing up surface space for other uses.
    • Temperature Regulation: Being buried protects the water from freezing and keeps it cooler in summer.
    • Reduced Aesthetic Impact: They have minimal visual impact on your landscaping.
  • Cons:
    • Higher Installation Costs: The excavation and installation are more labor-intensive, leading to increased costs.
    • Complex Maintenance: Accessing the cistern for maintenance is more difficult and may require professional help.
    • Potential for Groundwater Contamination: If not properly managed, there can be risks of contamination from surrounding soils.

What This Means for Your Project

When deciding between an above ground or underground cistern, assess your specific circumstances. If you prioritize ease of installation and maintenance, an above ground cistern might be more suitable. However, if space is at a premium and you‚Äôre concerned about temperature fluctuations, an underground system could be the better choice. Consider factors such as your budget, site conditions, and water needs. Consulting with a local expert can provide tailored advice and help you make the best decision for your property in Colorado.
```
