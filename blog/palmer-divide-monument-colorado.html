About Palmer Divide

Palmer Divide is a picturesque neighborhood located near Monument, Colorado, nestled between the Front Range of the Rocky Mountains and the high plains. Known for its stunning panoramic views, expansive open spaces, and a strong sense of community, this area is perfect for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life while still enjoying access to modern amenities. The Palmer Divide is characterized by a mix of rural and suburban living, offering a variety of property types, including single-family homes, ranch-style properties, and spacious lots for custom builds. The sense of tranquility, coupled with the proximity to outdoor activities such as hiking, biking, and horseback riding, makes Palmer Divide an appealing choice for families, retirees, and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Septic Considerations

In the Palmer Divide area, most properties are equipped with septic systems, as public sewer services are not widely available. Common types of septic systems include conventional systems, aerobic treatment units, and alternative systems, depending on the specific site conditions and soil types. It's crucial to consider the terrain when evaluating septic options, as the rocky, hilly landscape can present unique challenges for installation and maintenance. Homebuyers should also review the existing septic system's condition and functionality during the inspection process. Understanding local regulations regarding septic installations and maintenance is essential to ensure compliance and prevent future issues.

Building & Permits

El Paso County manages building permits for the Palmer Divide area.
