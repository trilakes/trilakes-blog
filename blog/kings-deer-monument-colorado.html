About Kings Deer

Kings Deer is a serene, upscale community located just north of Monument, Colorado. Nestled against the backdrop of the majestic Rocky Mountains, this neighborhood is known for its spacious lots, stunning natural beauty, and a close-knit community feel. Kings Deer appeals to families, retirees, and nature lovers alike, thanks to its picturesque landscapes, abundant wildlife, and proximity to outdoor recreational activities. The area features a mix of custom-built single-family homes, ranch-style properties, and some equestrian estates, making it a versatile choice for potential buyers. Many homes boast large lots averaging one to five acres, providing ample space for privacy and outdoor activities. With its blend of luxury and tranquility, Kings Deer is an ideal location for those seeking a peaceful retreat without sacrificing access to modern amenities.

Water & Utilities

In Kings Deer, residents primarily rely on individual wells for their water supply. The neighborhood is not serviced by a municipal water system, which means homeowners need to drill their own wells to access groundwater. The water quality in this area is generally good, but it's crucial to conduct thorough testing before finalizing any purchase. You can research well specifications and requirements with resources like ColoradoWell.com, which provides valuable insights on well construction and maintenance. Additionally, homeowners should be prepared to manage their own water systems, including the routine testing and possible treatment of well water. Electricity is provided by local utility companies, and internet services are available through various providers, ensuring that residents have access to modern conveniences while enjoying the peaceful, rural lifestyle.

Septic Considerations

In Kings Deer, properties typically use septic systems for wastewater management, as the area is not connected to a municipal sewer system. Common types of septic systems include conventional gravity systems, pressure distribution systems, and advanced treatment systems, depending on the specific lot's size and soil conditions. Before purchasing a property, it is essential to have the septic system inspected to ensure it meets local health department regulations and is functioning correctly. Additionally, the varied terrain of Kings Deer can influence septic system design and installation; for example, rocky soil may require specialized systems. It's advisable to consult with local septic experts to assess your options and ensure compliance with all regulations, especially if you plan to build or modify existing systems.

Building & Permits

Building and zoning permits for Kings Deer are managed by El Paso County, which also oversees the regulatory framework for the area. If you plan to build a new home or make significant modifications to an existing structure, you will need to submit plans and obtain the necessary permits. It's also essential to check for any HOA restrictions that may apply in Kings Deer, as the community has specific guidelines to maintain the neighborhood's aesthetic and character. Before starting any construction, familiarize yourself with local zoning regulations, building codes, and environmental considerations, such as fire mitigation and wildlife protection. Engaging with a local architect or builder who understands the unique aspects of constructing in this area can streamline the process and help you comply with all requirements.
