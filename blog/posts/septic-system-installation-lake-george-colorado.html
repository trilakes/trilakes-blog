Local Challenges in Park County

Lake George's rocky granite terrain and varying soil types present significant challenges for septic system installations. Most properties require engineered septic systems due to the prevalent clay and decomposed granite in the area. Additionally, the water table is often deep, which may necessitate cisterns for water delivery, impacting your overall septic installation plans. With a frost line that can reach 36-48 inches, especially at higher elevations, proper winterization becomes critical to avoid freeze damage. Access can also be a concern; many properties have steep driveways that limit accessibility during winter months and may complicate excavations. Understanding these local challenges is key to a successful installation and maintaining a properly functioning onsite wastewater system.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Lake George

The cost of septic installation can vary widely based on several factors. Here‚Äôs a breakdown:

  • The initial soil test and engineering services usually range from $2,000-$4,000.
  • Permits can cost between $500-$2,000, depending on the requirements set by the Park County Building Department.
  • Excavation for the system ranges from $8,000-$20,000+, influenced by rocky terrain and site accessibility.
  • The tank and components typically add another $5,000-$15,000 to your total.
  • If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is necessary, expect costs between $8,000-$15,000.
  • Electrical and control systems can range from $2,000-$4,000.

In total, you can expect to pay at least $35,000-$50,000 for a basic engineered system and upwards of $55,000-$80,000+ for advanced systems, especially in challenging sites. Remember, the size of your cistern is based on the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of servicing six people, totaling 600 gallons per day.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000+ for advanced systems, especially in challenging terrains like those found in Lake George.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process for a septic system can take several weeks, depending on factors like soil testing, permitting, and site conditions. Generally, you can expect the entire process to last anywhere from a few weeks to a couple of months.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Lake George require engineered systems due to challenging soil conditions. A soils report will determine the specific type of system needed to ensure functionality and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. These tests involve digging deep holes to analyze soil percolation and composition, which helps determine the best septic system design for your property.
