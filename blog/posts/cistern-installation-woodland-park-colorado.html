Why Woodland Park Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living at an elevation of 8,465 feet in Woodland Park, known as the "City Above the Clouds," presents unique challenges for homeowners regarding water supply. Many properties in this picturesque mountain town lack sufficient access to a reliable well, making cistern installation an attractive solution. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting understands the intricacies involved in setting up a water storage tank that satisfies both local regulations and the specific needs of your household. With fluctuating temperatures and harsh winters, having a professionally installed underground water storage tank ensures you have a consistent and safe supply of potable water throughout the year.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Woodland Park's unique terrain and weather patterns can complicate cistern installation. The soil composition ranges from rocky granite to clay in the valleys, posing challenges for excavation and installation. The average water table in this area is often quite deep, which means many homeowners must rely on cisterns and water hauling to ensure they have enough usable water. During winter, the frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating special considerations for freeze protection when installing a potable water storage tank. Additionally, steep driveways and limited access during winter months can further complicate the installation process. Fire mitigation is also critical in this forested area, requiring adequate planning for fire suppression water storage.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Woodland Park

Understanding the costs involved in water storage tank installation is crucial for homeowners. The price of installing a water cistern can vary significantly depending on several factors:

  • Tank Size: The size of your cistern will depend on your daily water usage and how often you want to refill. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills. This equates to costs starting from $40,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.
  • Excavation Costs: Due to the rocky conditions in Woodland Park, excavation costs can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more. This is an essential consideration as it significantly impacts the overall project cost.
  • Pump System: A pump system and pressure tank can cost between $2,500 and $5,000, which is necessary for efficient water distribution.
  • Additional Expenses: Other costs include trenching to your house ($25-$50 per linear foot), electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500), and water filtration systems ($2,000-$5,000).

When you total these expenses, a basic 5,000-gallon cistern installation can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can go upwards of $75,000 depending on the specific requirements of your property.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of a cistern installation usually ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system, while larger installations can cost $40,000 to $120,000, depending on factors like excavation challenges and system complexity.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank will depend on your daily water usage, which averages about 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants may need at least an 18,000-gallon tank for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions, permits, and weather. Typically, the process can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to complete.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property‚Äôs water table and access. Cisterns are often more suitable for properties with deep water tables or unreliable well sources, providing a consistent water supply through water hauling or rainwater collection.
