Our Septic System Installation Process

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Parshall

The cost of septic installation in Parshall can vary widely based on several factors. On average, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000 for a basic engineered system. This includes the soils test and engineering costs, which range from $2,000 to $4,000, along with permits that may vary from $500 to $2,000. Excavation costs can add another $8,000 to $20,000, depending on access and terrain.

For homes that require advanced treatment units (ATU), the costs can escalate to between $55,000 and $80,000+. This higher expense is often justified by the benefits of reduced setbacks and a smaller required soil treatment area, which can save on excavation costs in the long run. If your property has rocky terrain or difficult access, you should also prepare for additional costs of $10,000 to $25,000.

For properties requiring water cisterns, determine the size based on bedroom count‚Äîtypically, you would allocate 100 gallons per day per two people per bedroom. This consideration is crucial in areas where the water table is deep, and you may need to haul water instead of relying on a well.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems. Advanced treatments can cost between $55,000 and $80,000+, depending on site conditions, including terrain and soil challenges.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it usually takes from a few days to a couple of weeks. Factors affecting the timeline include soil testing, permit acquisition, and installation conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Parshall require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A soils report will determine the best system type for your property, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil depth and characteristics, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design.
