Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Pine's unique terrain and environmental conditions present several challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation efforts, often leading to increased costs. Homeowners need to be aware that the water table is usually deep in this area, which is why many properties rely on cisterns or water hauling to meet their water needs. Additionally, the frost line in Pine ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning to prevent freezing and ensure the longevity of the underground water storage tank.

Winter access can also be problematic, as many properties feature steep driveways that may not be accessible during heavy snowfall. This makes it crucial to consider the size and capacity of your water storage tank to minimize the frequency of necessary refills. Defensible space requirements for fire mitigation add another layer of complexity, making it essential to choose a cistern company that understands both local regulations and environmental conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Pine

The cost of cistern installation in Pine can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and additional requirements. For instance, when calculating your required tank size, a common rule of thumb is to estimate daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. Each bedroom typically accommodates two people, with an average usage of 100 gallons per person per day. For a 3-bedroom home, this would translate to a daily requirement of approximately 600 gallons.

Given the refill frequency preferences, homeowners need to determine how much water they want to store. For example, if you wish to refill every 2 weeks, you would require at least 8,400 gallons, whereas a system designed to get through winter could need upwards of 72,000 gallons. The price for a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank starts around $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000, excluding additional excavation and installation costs.

Excavation can add another $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the difficulty of the terrain, and you should also consider costs for the pump system, trenching, electrical hookups, and water treatment systems, which can collectively push the total installed cost of a 5,000-gallon system to between $25,000 and $35,000. Rocky and difficult terrain can elevate costs by 20-40%, so it's essential to plan accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely based on several factors, including the size of the tank and site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000 when considering installation, excavation, and other associated costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and refill frequency. A general guideline is to calculate daily usage based on bedroom count (2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per person). For instance, a 3-bedroom home would require at least 36,000 gallons for a winter supply, considering the need to refill every few months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but generally, it takes about 2-4 weeks from consultation to completion. This includes obtaining permits, excavating the site, installing the tank, and setting up the necessary systems. Challenges like rocky terrain and winter weather can impact this timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific circumstances. While wells can provide a continuous water source, they may not be feasible in areas with a deep water table. Cisterns, on the other hand, are often a more reliable choice for mountain properties where water supply is inconsistent and can be tailored to meet your storage needs.
