Local Challenges in Custer County

Custer County presents several challenges for septic system installations, particularly in Querida's rocky terrain. The area's soil is predominantly rocky granite and clay, which significantly impacts the feasibility of traditional systems. Many properties require engineered septic solutions due to these challenging conditions, as indicated by the comprehensive soils testing we conduct. The high elevation also means the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, complicating installation further. Additionally, access to many homes may be limited, particularly in winter, where steep driveways can hinder transport and installation efforts. Understanding these local challenges is crucial for ensuring a successful septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is meticulous and designed to accommodate the unique needs of Querida's properties. The first step involves conducting a thorough soils test, where we bring a mini excavator to the site to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. Soil samples are collected and sent to a licensed engineer for analysis, determining the appropriate system based on the soil conditions. This step is often advisable for potential buyers pre-purchase, as a proper soils test can reveal crucial information about the land.

Once the soils report is available, we begin the septic design process tailored to the specific requirements of your property. We then apply for the necessary septic permit through the Custer County Building Department, which is vital for compliance with Regulation 43. After receiving approval, we move to the installation phase where excavation, tank placement, and connection to the drain field are conducted. Depending on the complexity of the system, installation can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, factoring in weather conditions and site accessibility.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Querida

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Querida is crucial for homeowners. The total installed cost for an engineered system typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000, while options like a sand filter system usually cost between $45,000 and $65,000. For advanced treatment units (ATUs), homeowners should expect to invest $55,000 to $80,000, especially in challenging terrains where additional excavation may be necessary. The size of the septic tank is determined based on the number of bedrooms in the home, calculated as follows: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day.

Additional factors influencing costs include the need for extensive excavation due to rocky soil, which can add $10,000 to $25,000 to the total. It's also essential to consider that permit fees can vary from $500 to $2,000, depending on specific county requirements. Overall, investing in a high-quality septic system is essential to protect your property and the environment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000+, depending on the type of system required and the specific soil conditions of your property.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary significantly, generally taking from a few days to several weeks. This duration is influenced by site accessibility, the complexity of the installation, and weather conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Querida require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A professional soil analysis will determine the best system for your property to ensure long-term functionality.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed; instead, we conduct soils testing to evaluate the suitability of the land for septic systems. This involves digging test holes to assess drainage and soil characteristics essential for system design.
