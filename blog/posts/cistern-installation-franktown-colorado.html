Why Franktown Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Franktown, Douglas County, with its breathtaking mountain scenery and unique challenges, can make traditional water supply solutions impractical. At elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, many homes are unable to access reliable well water, making cistern installation an attractive option for homeowners looking for a consistent water supply. A properly installed underground water storage tank can serve a variety of needs, from providing drinking water to supporting irrigation for lush landscapes. Working with an experienced cistern installer near you ensures that your system meets local regulations and is designed to withstand the challenges of our mountain environment.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

The unique geography of Douglas County presents several challenges when it comes to water supply. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation for an underground cistern difficult and expensive. Homeowners often find that the water table is deep, necessitating water hauling services to fill their potable water storage tanks. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches requires careful planning, especially during winter installations, to prevent freeze damage. Properties often feature steep driveways and limited winter access, making installation logistics tricky. These environmental factors highlight the importance of professional expertise in water storage tank installation to ensure that your system is efficient, reliable, and compliant with Douglas County regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on the size and specifics of the installation. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system will range from $40,000 to $55,000, including installation and necessary equipment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need is determined by your daily water usage and how often you want to refill. A general guideline is to calculate your daily usage based on bedroom count, using a formula of 2 people per bedroom times 100 gallons per person per day.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary, but it typically takes several weeks from the initial assessment to final installation. Factors like permitting, excavation conditions, and weather can influence the duration of the project.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors such as water table depth and soil conditions. Cisterns are often a better alternative if wells are impractical or too deep, providing reliable access to water, especially in rural and mountainous areas.
