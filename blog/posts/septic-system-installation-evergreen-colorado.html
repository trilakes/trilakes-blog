Why Evergreen Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

If you own property in Evergreen, Colorado, you understand the beauty and challenges of living at a high elevation of over 8,000 feet. With stunning mountain views comes the responsibility of managing your septic system, which is vital for maintaining healthy onsite wastewater treatment. Proper septic installation is crucial, as a poorly designed or installed septic system can lead to costly repairs, environmental contamination, and health hazards. Engaging a licensed and experienced septic contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your system meets the stringent standards established by Colorado's Regulation 43, providing peace of mind that your septic tank will function effectively for years to come.

Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Living in the Jefferson County mountain region, with its rocky granite and clay soils, presents unique challenges for septic installation. The elevation of over 8,000 feet complicates matters with a frost line that can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating deeper installations. Many properties require engineered septic systems due to the often deep water table and varying soil conditions. The steep driveways and limited access can further complicate excavation and transport, especially during winter months when snowfall can restrict access. In addition, fire mitigation efforts in this forested area must be considered in your septic design, ensuring defensible space around your property while adhering to local regulations.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process to ensure every septic installation is done right. It begins with a thorough soil testing phase, where we utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples for analysis. This is essential, particularly in Colorado, where we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. Once we have the results, we work closely with a licensed engineer to determine the best system type for your property. Following that, we assist you in obtaining the necessary permits through the Jefferson County Building Department.

Once the design is approved, our skilled team will handle the excavation and installation of your septic tank and associated components, which typically takes between one to three weeks, depending on site conditions. We consider factors such as the type of system installed, the complexity of the terrain, and seasonal weather patterns. By choosing a professional septic installer near me, you can be confident that your system will be expertly managed from start to finish, ensuring compliance with all local regulations and optimal performance.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Evergreen

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Evergreen is crucial for planning your project. The total costs can vary based on several factors, including the type of system required, site conditions, and necessary permits. Basic engineered systems start from $35,000 to $50,000, while more complex systems such as sand filter systems can range from $45,000 to $65,000. Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) can cost between $55,000 and $80,000, especially for properties with challenging rocky terrain that may add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to the total cost.

For properties requiring a cistern, the size is typically calculated as two people per bedroom, using a guideline of 100 gallons per day. This means, for example, a three-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of storing approximately 600 gallons. Additionally, remember that permits can range from $500 to $2,000 depending on Jefferson County's requirements, and soil testing and engineering can add another $2,000 to $4,000 to your overall budget. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions and system type. Basic engineered systems can start from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems typically range from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units can cost between $55,000 and $80,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically takes one to three weeks, depending on site conditions, type of system, and seasonal weather. Engaging a professional septic installer near me ensures a smooth process and adherence to local regulations.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains, including Evergreen, require engineered systems due to challenging soil and water table conditions. These systems are designed to perform effectively, ensuring compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed. Instead, we conduct soil testing, which involves digging test holes to analyze soil conditions. This information is crucial for determining the appropriate septic system for your property.
