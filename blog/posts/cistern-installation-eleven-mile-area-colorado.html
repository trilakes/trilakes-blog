Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for a cistern can range from approximately $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. This includes the underground water storage tank, excavation, and installation of necessary equipment.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's water usage. A common formula is: number of bedrooms x 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. For instance, a three-bedroom home would require at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes about one to two weeks, depending on site conditions and weather factors. This process includes excavation, tank installation, and setup of the pump and delivery systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. Cisterns are ideal for properties where the water table is deep or wells are unreliable. They provide a consistent source of water, especially for off-grid or rural properties.
