Why Wetmore Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Wetmore, Colorado, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners, especially regarding water supply. Many properties in this area lack direct access to wells, making cistern installation an essential service for reliable water access. A properly installed underground water storage tank ensures that you have a sufficient supply of potable water for your household needs, landscape irrigation, and even livestock if you have a ranch. Engaging a professional cistern installer minimizes risks associated with improper installation, such as contamination and structural issues, and ensures compliance with Custer County regulations.

Local Challenges in Custer County

Wetmore's terrain is characterized by rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay valleys, which can complicate not only excavation but also the overall installation process of a water cistern. The depth of the water table often necessitates using cisterns, as many properties require extensive water hauling. Additionally, the region's frost line, ranging from 36 to 48 inches, means that your buried water tank must be installed well below this line to avoid freezing in the harsh winter months. Access to properties can be limited during winter, making it imperative to consider the size of the system to ensure you have enough water through the colder months. As a homeowner, understanding these challenges will help you make informed decisions regarding your water storage solutions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Wetmore

When it comes to cistern installation in Wetmore, costs can vary significantly based on several factors, including the size of the underground water storage tank, excavation needs, and site accessibility. As a rule of thumb, tank sizing is calculated by the number of bedrooms in your home multiplied by two people per bedroom and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require approximately 600 gallons per day, which translates to needing a tank that can accommodate enough water for your usage and refill frequency.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of estimated costs:

  • 5,000-gallon system: Starts at $25,000-$35,000, including tank, installation, and necessary components.
  • 10,000-gallon system: Starts at $40,000-$55,000.
  • Dual tank systems: Ranging from $50,000-$75,000+.
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): Typically $75,000-$120,000.

Additional costs may include excavation ($3,000-$8,000+ depending on rocky terrain), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching ($25-$50 per linear foot), and water filtration ($2,000-$5,000). Keep in mind that rocky or difficult terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs. It‚Äôs essential to budget for these factors to avoid surprises.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on the size and site conditions. For a basic 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems may start around $40,000 and can go up to $120,000 or more depending on various factors.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be determined by your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. A general guideline is to calculate 100 gallons per day per person, multiplied by the number of residents in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would require a tank capable of holding at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process for a cistern typically takes around 2-3 weeks, depending on site access, soil conditions, and weather. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting works diligently to minimize disruptions while ensuring your water storage system is installed correctly.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. Cisterns are often a better alternative for areas with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions. They provide a reliable backup water supply and are well-suited for off-grid living, while wells may offer ongoing water access but can be expensive and impractical in some locations.
