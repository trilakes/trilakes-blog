Why Ellicott Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Building a custom home in Ellicott, Colorado, is an exciting endeavor that demands professional expertise. At an elevation of over 8,000 feet, the unique mountain terrain presents specific challenges that require the skills of a licensed custom home builder. From ensuring proper foundation engineering to navigating the complexities of building permits through the El Paso County Building Department, having an experienced general contractor by your side can make the difference between a dream home and a construction nightmare. With Tri-Lakes Contracting, you gain access to a dedicated team that understands the local landscape and regulations, ensuring your custom build is not only beautiful but also structurally sound and compliant.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Ellicott's charming mountain community is known for its stunning views, but building a home here comes with its set of challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil, coupled with clay found in the valleys, can complicate home construction. Builders must consider a deeper frost line of 36-48 inches at these elevations, which can significantly impact foundation types and overall design. Moreover, water availability is a key concern; many properties necessitate the installation of cisterns or water hauling due to a deep water table. Access issues, especially during winter months with heavy snow loads, can hinder construction timelines. Additionally, fire mitigation is critical in forested areas, requiring careful planning for defensible space. Understanding these challenges is crucial for any successful custom build in Ellicott.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

Construction progresses through various phases, including site preparation, foundation work, and finishing touches, culminating in a final inspection and the issuance of your certificate of occupancy.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Ellicott

Understanding the costs associated with custom home construction in Ellicott is crucial for planning your budget effectively. The cost per square foot can range significantly depending on various factors, such as site conditions and design complexity. For a basic build, expect to pay around $350-$450/sq ft, while standard quality homes typically range from $450-$550/sq ft. Custom and higher-end builds can escalate costs to $550-$700/sq ft or even beyond for luxury constructions, starting at $1,500,000.

For example, a typical 2,500 sq ft home could cost between $875,000 and $1,375,000. Additionally, if you're considering a cistern for water, the cost can range from $25,000 to $75,000, calculated based on the number of bedrooms (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). Other costs, such as septic systems and driveway construction, can also add significantly to your total expenses. Keep in mind that rocky terrain and challenging access issues may further increase these costs. Being aware of these factors from the beginning helps in making informed decisions and preparing for your exciting journey in new construction.
