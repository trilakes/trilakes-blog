Local Challenges in Summit County

Building in Summit County presents unique challenges, particularly in the Silverthorne area. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, making it essential to choose a knowledgeable cistern company familiar with the local terrain. The water table in this region is often quite deep, leading many homeowners to rely on water hauling and cistern installation as a viable alternative to traditional well drilling.

The extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and temperature fluctuations, necessitate thoughtful planning for your water storage solutions. The frost line in Silverthorne reaches depths of 36-48 inches, meaning your buried water tank must be installed with care to prevent freezing in the winter months. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can pose logistical challenges during installation, making professional assistance crucial for a successful project.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Silverthorne

The cost of water storage tank installation can vary significantly based on various factors. For homeowners in Silverthorne, the typical price for a 5,000 gallon Norwesco tank ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank may cost between $22,000 and $28,000. However, the total installed system, including excavation, plumbing, and necessary permits, often falls between $25,000 and $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system.

When determining the size of your underground water storage tank, consider the daily water usage based on your home's bedroom count. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires around 600 gallons per day (6 people x 100 gallons). If you want to refill your cistern quarterly, you would need a minimum capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons. Accessing your site can also add costs; rocky terrain may increase excavation expenses by 20-40%. Lastly, winter installations may incur higher costs due to challenging ground conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system. Prices can go up to $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system, depending on site conditions and additional features required.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is generally estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require at least a 36,000-gallon tank if refilling quarterly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take several weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and tank size. Planning ahead is crucial, especially to ensure winter readiness.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific location and water needs. Cisterns are a great alternative when wells are impractical due to depth or reliability issues, especially in mountainous regions like Silverthorne.
