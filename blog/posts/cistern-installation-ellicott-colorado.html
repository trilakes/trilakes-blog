Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Ellicott

Understanding the costs involved in water storage tank installation is crucial for planning your project. The total installation cost can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions, tank size, and any additional services required. For instance, the sizing of your potable water storage tank is based on your household's daily water usage:

  • Assuming 100 gallons per person per day, a 2-bedroom home (4 occupants) would need approximately 400 gallons daily.
  • A 3-bedroom home (6 occupants) would require around 600 gallons daily.
  • A 4-bedroom home (8 occupants) would need approximately 800 gallons daily.

Based on your refill frequency preference, the size of the tank can vary significantly. For example, if you wish to refill every 2-3 months for a 3-bedroom home, you would need a tank with a capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons, or even larger to "get through winter" reliably.

Typical costs for Norwesco tanks range from:

  • $13,000-$15,000 for a 5,000-gallon tank
  • $22,000-$28,000 for a 10,000-gallon tank

However, additional costs should be considered, such as:

  • Excavation: $3,000-$8,000+ depending on rock and access issues.
  • Pump systems: $2,500-$5,000.
  • Trenching and electrical hookups: $2,000-$5,000.

Overall, you can expect the total installed cost for a 5,000-gallon system to range from $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system could range from $40,000-$55,000. All these factors are vital to consider when budgeting for your cistern installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary based on several factors including tank size and site conditions. Typically, a 5,000-gallon cistern system can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system usually ranges from $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, which is typically estimated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would need a tank that accommodates at least 600 gallons daily, along with refill frequency considerations.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation timelines can vary based on the complexity of the site and weather conditions. Generally, the installation process can take several weeks from excavation to final inspection.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are an excellent alternative when well drilling is impractical due to deep water tables or rocky terrain, providing reliable backup water supply options.
