Why Tarryall Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In Tarryall, Colorado, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, the need for professional septic installation cannot be overstated. With challenging mountain terrain and unique soil types, a properly designed and installed septic system is essential for maintaining environmental safety and property value. Homeowners must navigate regulations from the Park County Building Department, which governs all septic permits and installations under Colorado's Regulation 43. Attempting to install a septic tank without the expertise of a licensed septic installer can lead to costly mistakes and system failures, particularly given the specific requirements for engineered septic solutions in our area.

Local Challenges in Park County

Installing a septic system in Tarryall presents several local challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils found in the area often require specialized engineered systems to ensure proper wastewater treatment. The water table is frequently deep, and many properties need cisterns for water supply, making the planning process critical. Furthermore, the frost line reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful consideration during installation to prevent freeze damage in winter months. Access to many properties can be limited, especially during snow events, complicating the installation process. These factors make it vital to engage a qualified septic contractor who understands the unique conditions of Park County.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we take a thorough approach to septic system installation, ensuring that every step is completed with precision:

  • Site Assessment: We begin with a comprehensive site assessment, including soil testing to determine the appropriate system design. Our team uses a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, collecting soil samples for analysis by a licensed engineer.
  • System Design: Based on the soils report, we will recommend the most suitable septic system, whether that be a pressure-dosed system, sand filter system, or an advanced treatment unit (ATU).
  • Permitting: We handle all necessary septic permits through the Park County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations.
  • Installation: The installation process typically takes one to three weeks, depending on site conditions and system complexity. Our team will excavate, install the septic tank, and connect it to the designated drain field.
  • Final Inspection: After installation, we schedule a septic inspection to verify that everything meets safety standards and operational guidelines.

This meticulous process ensures that your septic system operates efficiently and effectively, providing peace of mind for years to come.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Tarryall

The cost of septic installation in Tarryall varies significantly based on site conditions, but homeowners should anticipate the following:

  • Soil Testing and Engineering: Typically ranges from $2,000 to $4,000.
  • Permits: Costs can vary widely, generally falling between $500 and $2,000.
  • Excavation: This can cost anywhere from $8,000 to $20,000+ depending on site accessibility and soil conditions.
  • Tank and Components: Installation of the septic tank itself usually ranges from $5,000 to $15,000.
  • Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs): If required, expect to pay between $8,000 and $15,000 for the unit alone.
  • Electrical and Controls: This can add another $2,000 to $4,000 to the total costs.

For a basic engineered system, you can expect total costs to start from $35,000 to $50,000, with more complex systems like sand filter or ATU systems reaching $45,000 to $80,000 or more, particularly if the terrain poses challenges. For homes requiring cisterns, remember to size the cistern based on the formula of bedroom count multiplied by two people multiplied by 100 gallons per day.
