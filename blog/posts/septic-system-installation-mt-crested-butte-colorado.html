Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Building in Mt. Crested Butte comes with its set of challenges, primarily due to the rocky granite and clay soils that dominate the area. The water table tends to be deep, making many properties reliant on cisterns and water hauling. Moreover, the frost line in this region extends 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning during installation to avoid freeze damage. Access can be particularly tricky; steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate septic system installation. Additionally, the local building season is typically confined to May through October, so planning ahead is essential to avoid delays. Fire mitigation is also crucial in forested areas, requiring careful planning to ensure defensible space around your home.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic system installation to ensure your system is efficient and compliant with Colorado's regulations. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step overview:

  1. Site Evaluation: We start with a thorough site evaluation, including soil testing, where we dig two test holes up to eight feet deep to gather samples. This is crucial to determine the appropriate septic design.
  2. Soil Report: The samples are sent to a licensed engineer for analysis, producing a soil report that dictates what type of septic system is needed for your specific property.
  3. Permitting: We handle all the necessary paperwork for your septic permit through the Gunnison County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.
  4. Installation: Our experienced team will then proceed with the installation of your septic system, which may include a septic tank, leach field, or engineered system depending on your soil type.
  5. Inspection: After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to confirm that everything is functioning as it should.
  6. Finalization: Finally, we provide you with maintenance tips and guidelines to ensure your system operates optimally for years to come.

This entire process typically spans several weeks, depending on site conditions and permitting. Early planning is essential, particularly to prepare for winter.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Mt. Crested Butte

When considering septic system installation, understanding the costs involved is crucial. In Mt. Crested Butte, costs can vary significantly based on several factors:

  • Soils Test & Engineering: Expect to pay between $2,000 and $4,000 for soil testing and engineering services.
  • Permits: Gunnison County permits typically range from $500 to $2,000, depending on the complexity of your installation.
  • Excavation: Excavation costs can vary widely, with estimates ranging from $8,000 to $20,000+, particularly in rocky terrain or difficult access sites.
  • Septic Tank & Components: Depending on the type of septic system, tanks and components can cost between $5,000 and $15,000.
  • Advanced Systems: If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is required, this can add $8,000 to $15,000 to your overall costs.

Typically, the total cost for a basic engineered system will start from $35,000 to $50,000, while sand filter systems may range from $45,000 to $65,000. For ATU systems, you can expect to pay between $55,000 and $80,000+, especially on challenging sites with rocky soil or steep access.

For properties requiring a cistern, the size should be calculated as follows: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day, which will factor into your overall investment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of septic installation typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems and can go up to $80,000 or more for advanced systems like ATUs. Factors such as soil conditions, excavation needs, and permit requirements can influence the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes several weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, permitting processes, and the complexity of the system being installed. Early planning is crucial to accommodate the local building season.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains, including those in Mt. Crested Butte, require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. A soils report will determine the best system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are not performed; instead, soil testing is conducted. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which helps design the most effective septic system for your property.
