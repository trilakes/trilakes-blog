Local Challenges in Teller County

Crystola's rocky granite and clay soils present unique challenges for septic installation. With elevation ranging from 7,000 to 10,000 feet, the water table is often deep, necessitating engineered systems for most properties. Due to the frost line being between 36-48 inches, especially in higher elevations, systems must be carefully designed to prevent freeze damage. The heavy snow loads and extreme temperature swings common in Teller County can also complicate both the installation process and the long-term functionality of onsite wastewater systems. Furthermore, the steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder installation efforts, making it crucial for homeowners to engage experienced septic contractors who understand these local nuances.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic system installation process is thorough and designed to meet the unique needs of Crystola homeowners. It begins with a comprehensive soils test, where we utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. Soil samples are analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the appropriate type of system based on the soil report. Once the system design is approved, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Teller County Building Department, ensuring that all local regulations are adhered to. Typically, installation occurs during the building season, which runs from May to October, allowing for optimal conditions.

The actual installation process involves excavation, the placement of the septic tank, and the establishment of the leach or drain field. We pay special attention to the frost line depth to protect your system from winter damage. After installation, we conduct a comprehensive septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before finalizing the project. Our aim is to provide a seamless experience, allowing you to focus on enjoying your mountain home.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Crystola

The cost of septic installation in Crystola varies significantly based on site conditions, the complexity of the system needed, and local regulations. For most properties, a basic engineered system will range from $35,000 to $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, you can expect costs to fall between $45,000 and $65,000. For advanced treatment units (ATUs), which are often necessary due to the challenging soil conditions, the total installed price can start from $55,000 and exceed $80,000.

It‚Äôs also essential to consider additional costs such as soils testing and engineering fees, which typically range from $2,000 to $4,000, and permits that can vary between $500 and $2,000. If your property has rocky terrain or difficult access, it may add an extra $10,000 to $25,000 to your total costs. For homeowners needing a cistern, the size is calculated based on the number of bedrooms, estimating at 100 gallons per day per two occupants per bedroom. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system required and the specific conditions of your property. Basic engineered systems can start around $35,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) can exceed $80,000 due to their complexity and benefits.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but it generally takes about 1 to 2 weeks once all permits are secured. The process includes soils testing, design, installation, and inspections, ensuring every step meets local regulations and standards.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Crystola and the surrounding Teller County area will require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and deep water tables. These systems are designed to function efficiently in rocky or clay soils, which are prevalent in our region.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not perform traditional perc tests; instead, we conduct soils tests. These tests involve digging deep holes to analyze soil samples, which help determine the appropriate type of septic system needed for your property.
