Local Challenges in Chaffee County

Chaffee County presents a myriad of challenges for septic system installations due to its unique geological and climatic conditions. The rocky granite and clay soils often require engineered systems to ensure proper wastewater treatment. With the water table frequently located at significant depths, many properties necessitate additional solutions, such as cisterns or water hauling systems. The frost line reaching depths of 36 to 48 inches at higher elevations complicates the installation process further. Access to many homes is limited by steep driveways, making construction activities even more challenging. Additionally, homeowners must consider fire mitigation strategies, as forested areas require defensible space around structures. Understanding these local challenges is crucial to ensuring a successful and compliant septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation that begins with an initial consultation and site evaluation. First, we conduct soil testing, utilizing a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to assess the soil composition. This crucial step helps determine the appropriate septic design for your property. Once we have a soils report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permits through the Chaffee County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Following permit approval, our team will prepare the site, which may involve excavation and preparing the leach field or drain field. The installation process typically takes 1 to 2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. We specialize in various system types, including pressure-dosed systems, sand filter systems, and advanced treatment units (ATUs), each tailored to meet the specific needs of your property. After installation, we conduct thorough septic inspections to ensure everything is functioning correctly before finalizing the project. Our commitment is to deliver a reliable and efficient onsite wastewater treatment solution tailored for the challenges of Alpine living.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Alpine

The cost of septic installation in Alpine can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, access, and system complexity. On average, installing a basic engineered system starts from $35,000, while sand filter systems typically range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an advanced treatment unit (ATU), costs can reach $55,000 to $80,000 or more, particularly in challenging terrains.

When calculating costs, it's essential to remember that the size of your cistern (if needed) is based on the bedroom count‚Äîgenerally, it‚Äôs calculated as 100 gallons per day per bedroom, with two people per bedroom. Additionally, site conditions such as rock, steep access, and weather can increase installation expenses. Proper planning and consultation with a professional like Tri-Lakes Contracting can help you navigate these complexities and ensure a successful installation that fits within your budget.
