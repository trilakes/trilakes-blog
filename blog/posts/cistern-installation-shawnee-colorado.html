Local Challenges in Park County

Installing a water storage tank in Shawnee requires navigating several local challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils found in this area can significantly increase excavation costs, sometimes by as much as 20-40%. Additionally, properties here often experience limited access during the winter months, making timely water delivery and maintenance more complex. Heavy snowfall and the potential for extreme temperature fluctuations mean that your buried water tank must be equipped with frost protection to avoid freezing. Understanding these factors is essential for ensuring your underground cistern functions efficiently throughout the harsh Colorado winters.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on tank size and additional installation factors. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon system typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can go up to $120,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size you need depends on your daily water usage and how often you wish to refill. For a typical 3-bedroom home, you would need a tank that can hold between 36,000 and 54,000 gallons, allowing for convenient refills every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take several weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and permitting processes through the Park County Building Department. A thorough site assessment and excavation are essential steps that can affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's water table and accessibility. Cisterns are more suitable for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil conditions, while wells may be a viable option for those with reliable groundwater sources.
