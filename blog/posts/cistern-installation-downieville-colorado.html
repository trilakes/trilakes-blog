Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Living in Downieville means contending with various environmental challenges that can complicate the installation of a water cistern. The rocky granite and clay soils require careful excavation, often needing specialized equipment that can increase installation costs. The deep water table in many areas makes relying solely on wells impractical, making water hauling common among residents. Additionally, the region experiences extreme weather conditions, with significant temperature swings and heavy snowfall, necessitating a robust underground cistern that can handle the frost line, which can reach depths of 36-48 inches. Access to many properties can be limited, especially in winter, complicating both the installation and ongoing water delivery.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Downieville

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is crucial for budget planning. The total cost includes several components:

  • Tank Costs: A Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically starts at around $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000-$28,000.
  • Excavation and Installation: Excavation costs can vary significantly, generally between $3,000 and $8,000, depending on the rocky terrain and access to your site.
  • Pump and Plumbing: Installation of the necessary pump system and pressure tank can add another $2,500 to $5,000 to your overall costs.
  • Electrical and Filtration: Expect to budget $1,000 to $2,500 for electrical hookups and an additional $2,000 to $5,000 for water treatment solutions.

When sizing your cistern, it‚Äôs essential to consider your daily water usage. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an estimated usage of 600 gallons per day would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills. In more challenging climates like Downieville, larger systems are recommended to reduce the frequency of water deliveries and ensure a reliable supply through winter.

Overall, a standard 5,000-gallon system can range from $25,000 to $35,000, whereas a larger 10,000-gallon system may set you back between $40,000 and $55,000, factoring in all associated costs. Always remember that costs can vary based on site conditions, so it‚Äôs best to plan for the high end of these ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size of the tank and site conditions. For instance, a standard 5,000-gallon tank can start at around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank generally costs between $40,000 and $55,000 when factoring in excavation and additional system components.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your household's daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. For example, a typical calculation is 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of people in your home. Therefore, a 3-bedroom house with 6 occupants would require at least a 18,000-gallon tank if you opt for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration of cistern installation can vary significantly based on site conditions and accessibility. On average, installations take anywhere from several days to a couple of weeks to complete. Factors such as excavation complexity and the need for permits can also impact the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are advantageous where wells are impractical due to deep water tables or low yield. They provide a reliable backup water supply and can be integrated into off-grid water systems, making them ideal for many rural properties in Downieville.
