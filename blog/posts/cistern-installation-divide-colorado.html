Local Challenges in Teller County

Divide's high alpine environment presents specific challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite terrain and varying soil types, including clay in valleys, often complicate excavation efforts. Many properties require engineered septic systems due to these challenging soil conditions, which can also affect the installation of an underground cistern.

The water table in Teller County can be quite deep, making wells impractical for many homeowners. Instead, water hauling or installing a buried water tank becomes essential for reliable access to potable water. Additionally, with a frost line of 36-48 inches, ensuring that your cistern is properly installed for freeze protection is vital. The extreme weather conditions, including heavy snowfall and temperature swings, further emphasize the need for professional expertise to ensure your water storage solutions are robust and effective.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Divide

The cost of cistern installation in Divide can vary significantly based on several factors. The size of your cistern is primarily determined by your daily water usage, which is typically calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an assumed occupancy of six people would require approximately 600 gallons per day, leading to a minimum tank size of:

  • Refill every 2 weeks: 8,400 gallons minimum
  • Refill monthly: 18,000 gallons minimum
  • Refill every 2-3 months: 36,000-54,000 gallons
  • To get through winter (4-5 months): 72,000+ gallons (often requiring multiple tanks)

We typically install Norwesco tanks, known for their durability and reliability. A 5,000-gallon tank costs between $13,000-$15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000-$28,000. In addition to the tank cost, several other factors will influence your total installation expenses:

  • Excavation Costs: Depending on the terrain, excavation can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more due to rocky conditions.
  • Pumping System and Accessories: Expect to pay between $2,500 and $5,000 for a pump and pressure tank.
  • Trenching to the House: This costs approximately $25-$50 per linear foot.
  • Additional Components: Risers, access lids, and electrical work can add anywhere from $2,000 to $5,000 to your total costs.

Given these factors, the total installed system costs can range from $25,000 to over $120,000, depending on your specific needs and site conditions. Always keep in mind that rocky terrain and difficult access can add 20-40% to your overall costs.
