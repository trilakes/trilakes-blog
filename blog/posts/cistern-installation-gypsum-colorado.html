Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Gypsum

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is crucial for homeowners in Gypsum. The total installed cost of a cistern system typically ranges as follows:

  • 5,000-gallon basic system: $25,000-$35,000
  • 10,000-gallon system: $40,000-$55,000
  • Dual-tank systems (15,000-20,000 gallons): $50,000-$75,000+
  • Large capacity systems (30,000+ gallons): $75,000-$120,000+

This pricing includes the cost of the tank, excavation, pumps, trenching, electrical connections, and any necessary filtration systems. The tank size is determined based on daily water usage, which can be calculated by multiplying the number of bedrooms in your home by 100 gallons per day per two occupants. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants may require a tank with a capacity of at least 18,000 gallons if monthly refills are planned.

Several factors can increase costs, including rocky terrain that may raise excavation expenses by up to 40% and limited access that complicates the installation process. It‚Äôs essential to plan for these variables to avoid any unexpected financial burdens.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can range significantly based on the size and specific site conditions. Generally, expect to pay between $25,000 to $120,000 for the entire installed system, depending on factors such as the tank size and excavation needs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your household's daily water usage and refill preferences. For example, a typical calculation for a 3-bedroom home is approximately 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, whereas a system designed to last through winter may require 72,000 gallons or more.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a cistern can vary based on site conditions, but typically it takes about 2-4 weeks from excavation to completion. Weather conditions and access to your property can also influence the duration of the project.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs water table depth and access. Cisterns are an excellent alternative for homes where well drilling is impractical due to deep water tables or rocky conditions, providing reliable water storage for both residential and agricultural needs.
