Local Challenges in Teller County

In Teller County, the combination of high elevation, rocky granite soil, and varying weather conditions complicates cistern installation. Many properties here face challenges with deep water tables, necessitating the use of a buried water tank for reliable water supply. The local soil can be quite unforgiving, with decomposed granite and clay in the valleys making excavation more challenging and costly. Moreover, the frost line at 36-48 inches requires special considerations for winterizing cistern installations to prevent freezing. With steep driveways and limited access during winter, having a professional installer who understands these local challenges is vital for a successful installation and operation of your agricultural water storage or residential water cistern.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the size, site conditions, and additional installation factors. For example, a 5,000-gallon system can cost between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can cost $40,000 to $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage and desired refill frequency. Generally, we calculate the tank size based on the number of bedrooms in your home, with 2 people per bedroom and 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would ideally require a tank capable of holding at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the installation. Factors such as rocky terrain and the need for permits can affect the overall timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your specific circumstances. For homes in areas with deep water tables or unreliable wells, a cistern installation may be the better option. Cisterns also provide a reliable backup water supply and can be integrated into off-grid water systems.
