Why Kiowa Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In the unique and breathtaking landscape of Kiowa, Colorado, proper septic installation is essential for both environmental protection and homeowner convenience. With an elevation of over 8,000 feet, properties here face distinct challenges that require expert knowledge to navigate. As a licensed and insured general contractor, Tri-Lakes Contracting specializes in tailored solutions that comply with Colorado's stringent regulations governing onsite wastewater treatment systems (OWTS). From understanding the specific soil conditions to managing the complex permitting process through the Douglas County Building Department, our experienced team ensures that your septic system is installed efficiently and correctly.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

Kiowa's unique geographical and climatic conditions present several challenges for septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate standard installations, often necessitating engineered systems tailored to local conditions. At elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning to avoid freezing pipes and system failures during the harsh winters. Access is another concern, as many properties feature steep driveways that can hinder construction during heavy snowfall or rain. Additionally, a deep water table often leads to the need for cisterns and water hauling, emphasizing the importance of a thorough soil testing process before installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process to ensure your septic installation meets all necessary regulations and performs effectively. The first step is conducting a soil testing assessment, where we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to collect soil samples. This information is sent to a licensed engineer to determine the most suitable system for your property. Once the septic permit is secured through the Douglas County Building Department, we proceed with the installation, which typically occurs from May to October to avoid winter conditions.

The installation process includes excavating the area for the septic tank and leach field, followed by the installation of the necessary components like pumps and electrical controls. We also incorporate any additional features required for winterization and freeze protection, ensuring your system will function efficiently even in the harshest weather. Depending on the site conditions and the complexity of the installation, the entire process can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Kiowa

The cost of septic installation in Kiowa can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil condition, site access, and system type. For a basic engineered system, homeowners can expect to pay around $35,000 to $50,000. If a sand filter system is required, costs can range from $45,000 to $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATUs) typically start at $55,000 and can exceed $80,000, particularly on challenging sites with rocky terrain.

For properties that require cisterns, the size is generally calculated based on the number of bedrooms: 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. In high-elevation areas like Kiowa, the total installed costs can rise to reflect the increased complexity of the project and the deep frost line.

For homeowners in Kiowa, early planning is key. Conduct a soil test before purchasing land to ensure you understand the septic requirements and potential costs involved.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions, but homeowners can expect to pay high-end prices ranging from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system required.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but typically it takes anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on the complexity of the installation and the specific site conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Kiowa require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions typical of Colorado mountain terrain. These systems comply with Regulation 43 and are designed for long-term efficiency.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not perform traditional perc tests; instead, we conduct soil testing to evaluate soil suitability for septic systems. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples, which is crucial for determining the appropriate system.
