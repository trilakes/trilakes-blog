Local Challenges in Park County

Hartsel's remote location and unique geological conditions present several challenges for septic systems. The soil varies from rocky granite to clay, which complicates the design and installation of effective septic systems. Many properties will require an engineered system due to these challenging soil conditions, as conventional gravity systems are rare in this mountainous region. Furthermore, the depth of the water table often necessitates the use of cisterns or water hauling, making efficient septic installation even more crucial.

In winter, the ground freezes down to 36-48 inches, requiring careful planning to ensure that systems are designed to withstand harsh weather, including heavy snow loads and extreme temperature swings. Steep driveways can also limit access for installation, especially during winter months, underscoring the need for professional expertise.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to guarantee the best results for your property. Initially, we conduct soil testing to understand the specific conditions at your site. This involves bringing in a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, allowing us to gather samples for a licensed engineer to analyze. This important step helps determine the most suitable type of system for your property.

Once we receive the soils report, we will discuss the appropriate options with you, which may include engineered systems such as pressure-dosed systems, sand filter systems, or advanced treatment units. After selecting the best solution, we will assist you in securing a septic permit through the Park County Building Department. Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Hartsel

Understanding the financial aspects of septic installation is crucial for any homeowner in Hartsel. The total installed costs for a standard engineered system generally range from $35,000 to $50,000. However, specific site conditions, including rocky terrain and limited access, can elevate these costs significantly. For example, if your property presents challenges such as difficult excavation sites, you might see additional costs of $10,000 to $25,000.

For homeowners considering water needs, the size of a cistern is typically calculated based on the bedroom count: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. This can add to your overall project costs, especially if a larger system is required. A breakdown of potential costs includes:

  • Soils test + engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000 (varies by county)
  • Excavation for system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Septic tank + components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000-$4,000

Considering these factors will help ensure your septic installation is both efficient and compliant with local regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of septic installation typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the type of system and site conditions. Factors such as rocky terrain, access difficulties, and the need for engineered systems can significantly influence the final cost.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline for a septic system can vary based on complexity and permitting processes but generally takes between 1 to 2 weeks. This includes time for soil testing, permit acquisition, and the actual installation.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Hartsel and other mountainous areas require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. An engineered system is essential not only for compliance with local regulations but also for the long-term efficiency of your wastewater management.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests rather than traditional perc tests. These tests involve digging deep holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the best septic system design for your property, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.
