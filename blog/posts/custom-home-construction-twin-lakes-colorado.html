The soil here varies widely, including rocky granite and clay in the valleys, which can affect your foundation engineering and overall building plans. Many properties also have limited access, with steep driveways that can complicate construction efforts. Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our home construction process is designed to guide you through each step of creating your dream home. The journey begins with a Build Feasibility Report, which assesses the site's viability for construction. This vital step includes a site visit, setback analysis, and an evaluation of soil conditions. Once you're ready to move forward, we'll assist you with obtaining a well permit or cistern plan, ensuring that you have water access before building can begin.

Next, we develop a detailed Site Plan that outlines your home‚Äôs footprint, driveway, and utility routes, taking into account local topographic data. Architectural Plans follow, and you can either bring your own architect or select from our network or from resources like ArchitecturalDesigns.com. After securing your architectural plans, we proceed to Engineered Foundation and Framing Plans, which must be stamped by a licensed engineer.

Once your documentation is complete, we compile and submit all necessary materials for a building permit to the Lake County Building Department. The permitting process typically takes 2-8 weeks, depending on county backlog. Following approval, we enter the Pre-Construction Planning phase, where we finalize materials, schedule subcontractors, and establish a construction timeline. The construction phase typically lasts from 10 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of your project. Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Twin Lakes

The cost of building a custom home in Twin Lakes can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, you can expect to pay between $450 and $700 per square foot for a standard or custom build, with luxury options reaching upwards of $1,000 per square foot. For example, a 2,000 square foot home might cost between $700,000 and $1,100,000. It's important to note that challenging terrain, such as rocky soil and steep access, can increase costs significantly.

When planning for water, many homeowners opt for a cistern to avoid lengthy wait times associated with well drilling. The cistern tank size is generally based on bedroom count, calculated as follows: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. Installation costs for a cistern can range from $25,000 to $75,000, adding to your overall budget. Other additional costs include septic systems, which may run from $35,000 to $80,000, and driveway installation, which can range from $10,000 to $50,000. Given these factors, working closely with a general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting can help you navigate the complexities of your budget effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado typically ranges from $450 to $700 per square foot for standard construction, with luxury builds exceeding $1,000 per square foot. For example, a 2,000 square foot home may cost between $700,000 and $1,100,000, depending on site conditions and design features.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The construction timeline can range from 10 to 18 months for typical projects, but more complex builds may take up to 24 months. Pre-construction planning usually adds an additional 2-6 months to the overall timeline.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When choosing a custom home builder, look for experience in mountain construction, positive reviews, and a comprehensive understanding of local building codes. It's essential to ensure that they are licensed and insured and can provide a clear construction timeline and budget.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a Build Feasibility Report is crucial before purchasing land. This report assesses the site's buildability, taking into account soil conditions, access, and local regulations, helping you avoid costly mistakes and ensuring a smoother construction process.
