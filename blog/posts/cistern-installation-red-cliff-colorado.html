Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Red Cliff

The cost of a cistern installation in Red Cliff can vary significantly based on several factors. A key consideration is the size of the underground water storage tank, which is generally calculated based on your daily water usage. For instance, if you have a three-bedroom home, you can expect a total of approximately 600 gallons per day (based on 2 people per bedroom and 100 gallons per person). Depending on how often you want to refill the tank, sizes can range from 5,000 gallons to upwards of 72,000 gallons for larger systems.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can range between $40,000 to $120,000+, depending on site conditions and specific needs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is typically based on your household's daily water usage. For example, a three-bedroom home with a usage of about 600 gallons per day would need at least a 14,000-gallon tank for monthly refills, or larger for longer intervals.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can take a few weeks to complete, depending on factors such as excavation conditions, weather, and permit approvals. Our process includes site assessments, excavation, and installation of the tank and associated systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions. Cisterns provide reliable water storage, particularly in areas with deep water tables or limited access to well drilling. They also offer greater flexibility for off-grid systems.
