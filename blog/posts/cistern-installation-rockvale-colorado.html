Local Challenges in Fremont County

The geological and climatic conditions in Fremont County present unique challenges for water management. The rocky granite and clay soils complicate excavation, often increasing costs by 20-40%. Additionally, the frost line at depths of 36-48 inches means that proper installation techniques must be employed to prevent freezing, especially during the harsh winters characterized by heavy snowfall and extreme temperature swings. With access to many properties being limited, especially in winter, homeowners often find it difficult to maintain a reliable water supply through water hauling alone. This underlines the need for a well-planned and expertly installed cistern, which can provide a continuous supply of water throughout the year, including during the coldest months.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a detailed and structured process for cistern installation to ensure each project meets the highest standards of quality and compliance. Initially, we conduct a comprehensive site assessment, taking into account factors such as soil type, access points, and existing infrastructure. During this phase, we also assist homeowners in determining the ideal size for their water cistern, which is typically calculated based on the number of bedrooms in the home. For example, a three-bedroom home may require a minimum of 18,000 gallons to ensure sufficient water supply for a monthly refill.

Once the planning phase is complete, we secure the necessary permits through the Fremont County Building Department. This ensures that your installation complies with local regulations. With permits in hand, we proceed to excavation, which can take 1-3 days depending on site conditions. We then install the chosen underground water storage tank, often a Norwesco tank, followed by the necessary plumbing and electrical connections. The final steps include backfilling and ensuring proper access to the tank for maintenance. Overall, the complete installation process typically takes 2-4 weeks, factoring in weather conditions and site access.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Rockvale

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is critical for planning your project. The total installed cost can vary significantly based on several factors, including the type of tank, excavation complexity, and site accessibility. For instance, a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank, which is ideal for many residential applications, can range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000.

The size of your potable water storage tank is determined using a simple formula: multiply the number of bedrooms by two occupants per bedroom and by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons to accommodate monthly refills. Additional costs, such as excavation (typically $3,000-$8,000 depending on rocky terrain), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500), can add up quickly. Therefore, planning for a larger storage capacity can lead to fewer refills and long-term savings.

For homeowners in Rockvale, investing in a larger system now can save hassle and costs associated with frequent water deliveries later, especially during winter when access may be limited.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation complexity. A typical installed system can range from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the tank size and additional factors like excavation and site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank will depend on your household's daily water usage and refill frequency. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires at least an 18,000-gallon tank to accommodate monthly refills safely.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation usually takes about 2-4 weeks, depending on factors like site access and weather conditions. The process involves site assessment, permitting, excavation, tank installation, and connecting plumbing and electrical systems.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on site conditions and water availability. In areas like Rockvale with deep water tables, cisterns often serve as a more reliable and practical alternative, especially for homes where wells may be too costly or impractical to drill.
