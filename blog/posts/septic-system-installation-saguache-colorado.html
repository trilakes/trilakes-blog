Local Challenges in Saguache County

Saguache County presents numerous challenges for septic installation due to its unique geological and climatic conditions. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soils, often combined with clay in the valleys, can complicate the installation of a standard septic tank. Most properties require engineered systems, as conventional gravity systems are rarely feasible here.

The high elevation also means that the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating deeper installations to avoid freeze damage. Furthermore, heavy snow loads and extreme temperature swings can impact both access to your property and the installation process, making it vital to schedule work during the building season, typically from May to October. Many properties face limited winter access due to steep driveways, making professional experience essential for successful septic system installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process that ensures your septic installation meets all local and state requirements. Initially, we perform essential soil testing, where we utilize a mini excavator to create two test holes, each 8 feet deep. This allows us to gather samples at various depths, which are then analyzed by a licensed engineer. The resulting soils report is crucial as it determines the appropriate type of system for your property.

After obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Saguache County Building Department, we design your system according to Regulation 43. Installation can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the complexity and site conditions. During installation, we ensure proper excavation, placement of the septic tank, and setup of the drain field or leach field. We also implement necessary features like drain field insulation for freeze protection.

Winterizing your septic system is crucial in Saguache. Ensure that your system is properly insulated and that all lines are drained before the freezing temperatures arrive.

Post-installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly and complies with local standards.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Saguache

The cost of septic installation in Saguache can vary widely based on several factors, including soil condition, property access, and system type. A comprehensive breakdown reveals that the total installed cost for a basic engineered system typically starts at $35,000 and can go up to $50,000. For more complex installations, such as a sand filter system, you might be looking at costs between $45,000 to $65,000. If an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU) is necessary, the costs can rise to between $55,000 and $80,000 or more, particularly if the site is difficult due to rocky terrain.

When planning for a cistern, the size should be calculated based on your bedroom count, assuming 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern with a capacity of approximately 600 gallons. Including costs for soil testing and engineering, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, and excavation, which might add $8,000 to $20,000, it's essential to remember that rocky conditions will significantly increase your installation expenses.

Additionally, it's important to factor in permit fees, which in Saguache can range from $500 to $2,000, and remember to quote high-end estimates to avoid unexpected costs later on.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions, but you can expect prices for a basic engineered system to start at around $35,000 and potentially reach up to $80,000 for more complex systems, especially in challenging terrains like Saguache.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary depending on site conditions and system type, but generally, it ranges from a few days to a couple of weeks. This includes the time needed for soil testing, permitting, and the actual installation work.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In Saguache County, most properties require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions and regulations set forth in Regulation 43. This ensures that your system is both compliant and effective, protecting your investment and the environment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not perform traditional perc tests; instead, we conduct soils tests using a mini excavator to gather samples from various depths. This information helps determine the appropriate septic system for your property based on the specific soil conditions.
