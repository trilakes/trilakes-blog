Local Challenges in Douglas County

Perry Park's geographical and environmental characteristics present distinct challenges for septic system installation. The region is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which often necessitate engineered systems per Regulation 43. Many properties face a deep water table, leading to the need for cisterns or water hauling, further complicating the installation process. The frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, especially at higher elevations, making winter preparation a critical factor when planning your septic installation.

Additionally, the steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder equipment mobility, making it essential to choose a contractor with experience in navigating these conditions. With heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, it‚Äôs vital to have reliable drainage solutions, such as a properly designed leach field or drain field, to protect your property and maintain a functional system.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic system installation process is designed to ensure accuracy and compliance with all local regulations. The initial step involves performing soil testing at your property. Unlike traditional perc tests, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze the soil composition. This data is sent to a licensed engineer to determine the most suitable system for your property.

Once the soil report is complete, we move on to the septic design. Depending on the soil characteristics, your property may require a conventional gravity system or, more commonly, an engineered system like a pressure-dosed system or a sand filter system. After obtaining the necessary septic permit from Douglas County‚Äîan essential step that we guide you through‚Äîwe can proceed with excavation and installation.

Typically, the installation process takes between one to three weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Once the system is in place, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything functions as intended. Our team also provides winter preparation tips to help you winterize your septic system, ensuring it remains protected from freezing temperatures.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Perry Park

The cost of septic installation in Perry Park can vary significantly based on site conditions and system type. For an engineered system, you can expect costs to start around $35,000 for basic installations, with more complex systems like sand filter systems ranging from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), total costs can escalate to between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

When planning for cisterns, it's essential to factor in the size based on bedroom count‚Äîtypically calculated as two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day, which is crucial for determining your water storage needs. Additional expenses can arise from rocky terrain, which may add $10,000 to $25,000 to your total cost. Furthermore, excavation, tank installation, and electrical components can contribute significantly to the overall price, often leading to a final bill that reflects the challenging local conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of septic installation typically ranges from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the system type and site conditions. Factors such as rocky soil or difficult access can increase costs significantly.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary, but generally, it takes one to three weeks to complete a septic installation. This timeframe includes soil testing, obtaining permits, and the actual installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Perry Park require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions, as mandated by Regulation 43. This ensures proper functionality and compliance with local regulations.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves analyzing soil samples to determine the appropriate type of septic system for your property, ensuring compliance with local requirements.
