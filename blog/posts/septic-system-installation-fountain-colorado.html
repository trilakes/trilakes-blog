Why Fountain Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in Fountain, Colorado, at an elevation of 5,546 feet offers stunning views of the Front Range but comes with unique challenges, especially when it comes to septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils prevalent in the area often necessitate specialized knowledge and techniques to ensure a fully functional septic system. Attempting a DIY approach could lead to costly mistakes, potential regulatory violations, and long-term problems for your property. Therefore, hiring a licensed and insured septic installer near me like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures compliance with local regulations and provides peace of mind.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Fountain's unique terrain presents several challenges for homeowners considering a new septic system. The rocky granite and clay found in the valleys affect drainage and require thorough soil testing to determine the best type of system for your property. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep in many areas, often necessitating cisterns for water hauling. The frost line is typically between 36 to 48 inches, meaning that septic installation must account for winter conditions and adequate freeze protection. Moreover, access to many properties can be limited, particularly during the winter months, which complicates logistics for installation. Understanding these local challenges is essential for ensuring a successful and compliant septic installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we have a well-defined process for septic installation to ensure that every project runs smoothly. First, we conduct soil testing by bringing a mini excavator to your site, digging two test holes each eight feet deep, and sending samples to a licensed engineer for analysis. This soils report determines the type of system you'll need, whether it be a conventional gravity system or an engineered system, which is more common in our region.

Once we have the necessary information and your septic permit is approved through the El Paso County Building Department, we can schedule the excavation. This phase typically requires heavy machinery and could take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions. After excavation, we proceed with the installation of the septic tank and the associated drainage fields‚Äîeither a leach field or a drain field‚Äîbased on your approved system design.

Finally, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything meets the regulatory standards set forth by Regulation 43. This includes checking for proper setbacks from property lines and wells, ensuring compliance with local regulations. After the installation, we provide guidance on maintenance and tips for winterizing your septic system to protect it during Colorado's harsh winters.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Fountain

When considering septic installation in Fountain, it is essential to understand the cost factors involved. The total cost of an engineered system typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can escalate to $55,000 to $80,000 or more. Various factors can increase these costs, such as rocky terrain, which can add $10,000 to $25,000, and additional excavation requirements due to challenging site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems, which can go up to $80,000 or more for advanced treatment units, especially if challenging soil conditions are present.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically ranges from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions, permitting, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In most cases, yes. Due to the challenging soils found in Fountain and surrounding areas, an engineered septic system is often required by Regulation 43, ensuring proper drainage and longevity of the system.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we do not conduct traditional perc tests; instead, we perform soil testing, which involves digging test holes to analyze the soil structure and determine the best type of septic system suitable for your property.
