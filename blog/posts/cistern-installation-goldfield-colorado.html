Why Goldfield Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

As a resident of Goldfield in Teller County, Colorado, maintaining a reliable water supply is essential, especially given the unique challenges posed by our mountainous terrain at elevations over 8,000 feet. For many homeowners without access to a well, cistern installation becomes a critical solution. A professionally installed water cistern not only guarantees access to potable water but also ensures that your system is designed to withstand the specific environmental conditions, such as extreme temperature fluctuations and heavy snowfall. Proper installation is crucial to avoid costly repairs and ensure the longevity of your water storage system.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Goldfield's high elevation and rocky terrain present unique challenges for cistern installation. The soil here is primarily rocky granite and decomposed granite, making excavation labor-intensive and potentially costly. The water table is often deep, necessitating the use of cisterns and water hauling for many properties. Moreover, the frost line is between 36-48 inches, meaning that any underground water storage tank must be buried deep enough to avoid freezing, which can complicate installation in winter months. Additionally, many properties have steep driveways that can limit access during heavy snow, making it essential to plan installations carefully around seasonal weather patterns.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we pride ourselves on providing a comprehensive and efficient cistern installation process tailored to the needs of Goldfield homeowners. Our process begins with a thorough site assessment to evaluate soil conditions, access routes, and local regulations, including permits from the Teller County Building Department. Once we have gathered all necessary information, we will determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank based on daily water usage and refill preferences.

Next, we will prepare the site for excavation, ensuring that we minimize disruption to your property. Our team will handle the excavation process, taking into account the rocky soil and any potential challenges that may arise. After installing the buried water tank, we will set up the necessary pump systems and pressure tanks, followed by trenching to your home for easy access to the water supply. Finally, we will conduct a thorough inspection of the system to ensure everything is operational before completing the project.

The entire installation can typically be completed in a few weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Homeowners can rest assured that we are dedicated to providing a durable, efficient, and reliable water storage solution tailored to their specific needs.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Goldfield

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation in Goldfield is essential for planning your budget effectively. The price of an underground water storage tank can range significantly based on size and specifications. For instance, a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank typically costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000 to $28,000. When factoring in excavation, pump systems, trenching, and other necessary components, total installed costs can reach up to $35,000 for a basic system and can escalate to $55,000 or more for larger capacities.

To accurately size your water storage tank installation, consider your daily water usage and frequency of refills. A general guideline is to calculate daily water needs based on the number of bedrooms in your home‚Äîapproximately 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home might require a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, while a system meant to "get through winter" may need a capacity of 72,000+ gallons, which could involve multiple tanks.

Additionally, factors such as rocky terrain and limited access can increase excavation costs by 20-40%. Therefore, planning ahead and investing in a larger tank can lead to long-term savings on water hauling and maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation requirements. A basic 5,000-gallon cistern system typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can fall between $40,000 and $55,000. Additional costs for excavation, pumps, and trenching can further impact the total price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the underground water storage tank you need depends on your household's daily water usage and refill frequency. As a general rule, calculate 100 gallons per person per day, then multiply this by the number of bedrooms (assuming 2 people per bedroom). For example, a 3-bedroom home would require at least an 18,000-gallon tank if refilled monthly.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation can generally be completed in a few weeks, but this timeline may vary based on site conditions and local weather. Factors such as rocky terrain and winter access challenges can extend the installation timeframe, so early planning is crucial.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are ideal for locations with deep water tables, while wells are better suited for areas where groundwater is easily accessible.
