Local Challenges in Saguache County

Living in Moffat means you face a variety of local challenges that can complicate septic installation. The rocky terrain can make excavation difficult, while the water table is often deep, leading many properties to require cisterns for water hauling. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches means that any installations must be planned carefully to prevent freezing during harsh winters. Heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms can further complicate the installation process, making access to many properties limited during certain times of the year. Understanding these challenges is crucial in designing and installing a reliable septic system that meets your needs.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and designed to meet the specific needs of each property owner. We begin with a comprehensive soil testing procedure, where we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to analyze the soil composition. This information is crucial, as it dictates the type of septic system we will install. Following this, we prepare the necessary septic permit applications and submit them to the Saguache County Building Department.

Next, we design your system based on the soils report, which may include options like pressure-dosed systems or advanced treatment units (ATUs). After obtaining the appropriate permits, we schedule the installation, typically between May and October, when weather conditions are favorable. Our team ensures that the entire process is completed with minimal disruption to your property, providing you with a fully functional on-site wastewater system that adheres to Colorado's regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Moffat

The cost of septic installation in Moffat can vary significantly based on several factors. Typically, for an engineered system, you can expect a total installed cost starting from $35,000 to $50,000. This includes the necessary soil testing and engineering services, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, along with permits that can vary between $500 and $2,000 depending on local regulations.

Other costs include excavation for the system, usually priced between $8,000 and $20,000, and the tank and components, which can total anywhere from $5,000 to $15,000. If your property requires an ATU, expect to add an additional $8,000 to $15,000 to your budget. It's essential to keep in mind that rocky terrain and limited access can increase your costs further by $10,000 to $25,000. Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for septic installation typically starts from $35,000 to $50,000 for an engineered system. Factors like soil conditions, required permits, and access complications can influence the total cost, which may range significantly higher based on site-specific challenges.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary based on several factors, including soil testing, permitting, and site conditions. Generally, the installation can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, particularly if complications arise due to the challenging terrain.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Moffat and other Colorado mountain areas require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are designed to effectively manage onsite wastewater and comply with Colorado's OWTS regulations, ensuring long-term functionality and compliance.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with soil testing, which involves analyzing soil samples from various depths to determine the appropriate septic system type. This process is crucial in assessing how well the soil can absorb wastewater, guiding the design of your system.
