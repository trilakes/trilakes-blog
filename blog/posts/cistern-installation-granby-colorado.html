Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Granby

Understanding the cost factors associated with cistern installation is vital for Granby homeowners. The total installed system costs for a basic 5,000-gallon tank typically range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a larger 10,000-gallon system can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. The sizing of your underground water storage tank depends on daily water usage, generally calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with an estimated occupancy of 6 people would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, leading to a need for a system capable of holding between 18,000 to 54,000 gallons, depending on how often you want to refill.

Additional costs come into play due to site conditions. Rocky or difficult terrain can increase excavation costs by 20-40%, while access issues may add further expenses. Other considerations include the pump system, trenching to your home, electrical hookups, and water treatment systems, which can all contribute to the overall investment. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: A typical cistern installation in Colorado starts from around $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can go up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Various factors like site conditions and additional features can influence these costs significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is primarily determined by your household's daily water usage and how frequently you want to refill. For example, a household of 6 may need a tank that holds between 18,000 to 54,000 gallons based on refill preferences.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The entire process of cistern installation typically spans about a week, including a few days for excavation and another two to three days for tank installation and testing. Weather conditions and site access can affect this timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your property‚Äôs water table and access. For many Granby residents where wells may be impractical or too deep, a cistern offers a reliable alternative, ensuring a consistent water supply even in remote areas.
