Why Almont Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the stunning mountain community of Almont, Colorado, with its breathtaking landscapes and rugged terrain, comes with unique challenges, particularly when it comes to water supply. Many properties in this region, sitting at elevations above 8,000 feet, face difficulties accessing groundwater due to deep water tables and rocky soil. This makes cistern installation an essential solution for homeowners who require reliable access to potable water storage. Professional installation ensures that your water storage tank is properly sited, sized, and equipped to handle the variable weather conditions and the unique demands of mountain living.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Almont's mountainous terrain presents several challenges that necessitate the expertise of a qualified cistern installer near me. The area's soil composition, primarily rocky granite and clay in valleys, complicates excavation and can significantly increase costs. Additionally, the frost line in this region reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, requiring specialized techniques during water storage tank installation to protect your buried water tank from freezing. Extreme weather conditions, such as heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can also impact accessibility for water hauling and maintenance. As many properties have steep driveways, limited access during winter months makes planning for adequate water storage even more critical.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to cater specifically to the needs of Almont residents. The first step involves a thorough site assessment to determine the optimal location for your underground water storage tank. We analyze the soil type, assess the elevation, and consider factors such as access and local regulations, which are enforced by the Gunnison County Building Department.

Once the site is selected, we will help you determine the right cistern size based on your daily water usage and refill preferences. For example, a typical 3-bedroom home would require around 600 gallons per day, influencing your choice of tank size based on how often you want to refill. Our team will then coordinate the excavation, which typically takes 1-2 days, depending on the terrain and weather conditions. We install Norwesco tanks, renowned for their durability and reliability, and incorporate any necessary pump systems and filtration systems to ensure high-quality water supply.

Finally, the installation process includes backfilling, landscaping, and setting up access points for future maintenance. Typically, the entire process can take 2-4 weeks from start to finish, depending on site conditions and weather. This timeline allows us to ensure that your system is reliable and ready for the challenges of winter.

The cost of cistern installation in Almont varies significantly based on several factors, including tank size, excavation costs, and site conditions. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can range from $25,000 to $120,000, depending on the size and complexity of the system. For instance, a 5,000-gallon cistern typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while larger systems can reach upwards of $75,000 for capacities over 30,000 gallons.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your daily water usage and how often you wish to refill. For a 3-bedroom home, you would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, increasing to 72,000 gallons to last through the winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes 2-4 weeks from start to finish. The timeline can vary based on site conditions, excavation needs, and weather, but our team strives to ensure a timely and efficient installation process while adhering to local regulations.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

Cisterns serve as a reliable backup water supply when well drilling is impractical due to deep water tables, rocky terrain, or dry spells, making them a popular choice for many mountain properties in Almont.
