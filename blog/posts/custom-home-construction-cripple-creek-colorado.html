Local Challenges in Teller County

Building in Cripple Creek presents unique local challenges that must be addressed to ensure a smooth construction process. The region's rocky granite soil, coupled with clay in the valleys, can complicate foundation engineering. With a water table often located deep beneath the surface, many properties require cisterns for water supply and may involve water hauling. Additionally, the frost line at 36-48 inches can significantly impact construction timelines and methods, particularly during winter. The building season typically runs from May to October, but steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder progress. Fire mitigation is also critical in forested areas, necessitating defensible space requirements. Tri-Lakes Contracting understands these challenges and is prepared to navigate them efficiently, ensuring your custom build is both safe and durable.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

The custom home construction process with Tri-Lakes Contracting is designed to be straightforward and efficient, guiding you from conception to completion. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step breakdown:

  1. Pre-Purchase Build Feasibility Study: Before you buy land, it‚Äôs essential to obtain a Build Feasibility Report. This includes a site visit, setback analysis, utility availability, and an overall assessment of whether the land is buildable.
  2. Well or Cistern Permit: Most counties require proof of water before issuing a building permit. Filing a well permit early is crucial, even if you plan to use a cistern for immediate water access.
  3. Site Plan Development: A professional site plan outlines the footprint of your home, driveway, and utility routes, ensuring compliance with all local regulations.
  4. Architectural Plans: You can work with your architect or choose from a wide variety of plans available at ArchitecturalDesigns.com.
  5. Engineered Foundation & Framing Plans: These plans are critical for ensuring the safety and durability of your home, especially in challenging mountain conditions.
  6. Land Survey: A professional survey is required to verify property boundaries and identify any easements.
  7. Septic System Plans: If your property is not connected to a sewer system, engineered septic plans are necessary.
  8. Compile & Submit for Building Permit: We handle all documentation and submit your application to the Teller County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations as detailed on their website.
  9. Pre-Construction Planning: We finalize plans, order materials, and establish a detailed construction schedule.
  10. Construction Phase: This includes all stages from site preparation to final inspections, ensuring each phase meets quality standards.
  11. Certificate of Occupancy: After passing final inspections, we obtain your Certificate of Occupancy, allowing you to move into your new home!

This comprehensive approach typically spans 10-18 months for construction, depending on the complexity of your project and weather conditions.

Cost Factors for Custom Home Construction in Cripple Creek

When planning your custom home project, it‚Äôs important to consider various cost factors specific to the Cripple Creek area. The typical costs for custom home construction in Colorado mountain communities can range significantly, especially given the unique challenges posed by the terrain. On average, a basic build can start at $350-$450 per square foot, while standard quality homes range from $450-$550 per square foot. For custom or high-end luxury homes, costs can escalate to $700-$1,000+ per square foot.

For example, let‚Äôs say you plan to build a 2,500 square foot mountain home. You might expect costs to be in the range of $875,000 to $1,375,000, with additional expenses for land acquisition, cistern installation (ranging from $25,000-$75,000), and septic systems ($35,000-$80,000). For those considering a cistern, the size is typically based on the number of bedrooms; you would calculate it as two people per bedroom times 100 gallons per day. These factors can significantly influence your overall budget, especially if your land has rocky terrain or steep access.

It‚Äôs essential to quote the high end of these ranges, as costs can vary widely depending on site conditions and the complexity of your build.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies widely based on factors like location, design, and materials. In Cripple Creek, you can expect to pay between $350-$1,000 per square foot, depending on whether you opt for a standard or luxury home.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: Building a house in Cripple Creek typically takes 10-18 months once construction begins, depending on the complexity of the project and weather conditions, particularly during winter months.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When selecting a custom home builder, consider their experience with mountain construction, local knowledge of building codes, and their portfolio of completed projects. You want a general contractor who understands the unique challenges of building in elevated terrain.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a Build Feasibility Report before purchasing land is crucial. It helps assess site conditions, setback requirements, and utility availability, ensuring that you're making a sound investment in a buildable property.
