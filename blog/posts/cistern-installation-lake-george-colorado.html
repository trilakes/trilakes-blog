Local Challenges in Park County

Installing a water cistern in Lake George requires careful consideration of various local challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil found here can complicate excavation, often increasing costs by 20-40%. Furthermore, the water table in this region is frequently deep, meaning many properties must rely on water hauling or cisterns for their water supply.

Additionally, the frost line at elevations like ours can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating specialized installation techniques to avoid freeze damage during winter months. With extreme temperature swings, heavy snow loads, and afternoon thunderstorms, having a reliable potable water storage tank is not just a convenience; it's a necessity for long-term living in this beautiful yet challenging environment.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Lake George

Understanding the cost of water storage tank installation is crucial for planning your project. For a 5,000-gallon basic system, you can expect to pay between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000. The actual cost will depend on several variables:

  • Tank Size: Your tank size should be based on daily water usage, calculated with the formula of bedroom count multiplied by 2 people per bedroom and then multiplied by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require at least 600 gallons per day, leading to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly.
  • Excavation Challenges: Rocky terrain can significantly increase excavation costs, sometimes ranging from $3,000 to $8,000 or more.
  • Additional Components: The cost of pumps, electrical hookups, and filtration systems can add between $2,000 and $5,000 to your total.

It's important to quote the high end of these ranges to ensure you are prepared for potential expenses that may arise from unique site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can range from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size and complexity of the system. This includes excavation, installation, and any additional components like pumps or filtration systems.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your daily water usage. A general guideline is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of occupants in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a tank size of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes between two to four weeks, depending on various factors such as site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the installation process.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. If the well is impractical due to depth or reliability issues, a cistern provides a dependable alternative for water storage, especially in areas with challenging terrain like Lake George.
