Local Challenges in Lake County

Malta's challenging landscape is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils in the valleys, often requiring engineered septic systems to function effectively. The water table can be significantly deep, making it essential for many homes to have cisterns for water supply. Additionally, with the frost line in this region ranging from 36 to 48 inches, special consideration must be given to the installation depth of the septic tank and leach field. The building season is typically limited to the warmer months of May through October, and winterizing your septic system installation is critical to prevent freeze damage during the harsh Colorado winters. Access can also be limited due to steep driveways and snow accumulation, which can complicate the installation process.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive, step-by-step process for septic installation that ensures your system operates efficiently and meets all regulatory requirements:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with a consultation to understand your property's specific needs and circumstances.
  2. Soils Testing: Our team conducts soils tests, which involve digging two test holes, each eight feet deep, to collect soil samples. These samples are analyzed by a licensed engineer to determine the appropriate system type.
  3. Septic Design and Permitting: Based on the soils report, we will design your septic system and secure the necessary permits from the Lake County Building Department, a crucial step in compliance with local regulations.
  4. Installation: Installation typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Our team will excavate, install the tank, and create the leach field or drain field according to the engineered design.
  5. Final Inspection: After installation, we coordinate with local authorities for a final inspection to ensure everything meets the standards set by Regulation 43.
  6. Maintenance Guidance: We provide advice on how to maintain your system, ensuring longevity and compliance with local regulations.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Malta

The cost of septic system installation in Malta can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, system type, and accessibility. Generally, the total installed costs for an engineered septic system range from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic systems. For sand filter systems, expect costs between $45,000 and $65,000, and for advanced treatment units (ATUs), costs can soar between $55,000 and $80,000+.

For properties needing cisterns, sizing is generally calculated using the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons/day. This ensures you have sufficient water supply based on household needs. Factors that may increase costs include rocky terrain, challenging access, and the need for additional excavation, which can add $10,000 to $25,000 for difficult sites.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies widely based on soil conditions and system type. For engineered systems, you can expect costs to range from $35,000 to $50,000 on the low end, while advanced systems like ATUs can cost between $55,000 and $80,000+.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on factors such as site conditions, weather, and permitting timelines. Getting all necessary permits from the Lake County Building Department can affect the overall timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Colorado mountains require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions as mandated by Regulation 43. These systems are crucial for ensuring proper waste treatment and protecting groundwater.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils testing instead of traditional perc tests to evaluate the suitability of soil for septic systems. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples, which helps determine the appropriate septic design.
