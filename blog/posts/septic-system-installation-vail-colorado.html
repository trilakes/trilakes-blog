Local Challenges in Eagle County

Installing a septic system in Vail presents several unique challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils, combined with a water table that is often deep, necessitate careful planning and often the use of engineered systems. Properties in this mountainous area may require a cistern for water supply, which adds another layer to consider during installation. Additionally, the frost line ranges from 36-48 inches or more at higher elevations, meaning that the installation must account for potential freezing temperatures. Accessibility is also a vital factor, as many properties feature steep driveways and limited winter access, making it crucial to plan installations during the building season from May to October. Furthermore, fire mitigation strategies must be integrated into the design, especially in forested areas where defensible space is a requirement.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we have developed a comprehensive septic installation process to ensure that each project is executed efficiently and meets all local regulations. Our process begins with a thorough site evaluation, including soil testing to determine the type of system needed. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples that are sent to a licensed engineer for a detailed report. This report is essential for designing the appropriate system.

Once we receive the soils report, we work with you to create a septic design that suits your property‚Äôs specific requirements. After finalizing the design, we will assist you in obtaining the necessary permits from the Eagle County Building Department, a vital step that ensures compliance with Regulation 43 governing OWTS in Colorado.

Installation typically takes one to two weeks, depending on the system type and site conditions. We‚Äôll manage excavation, installation of the septic tank and components, and any necessary electrical work. After installation, we conduct a septic inspection to confirm everything is functioning correctly before you begin using your new system. Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Vail

The cost of septic installation in Vail can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, the type of system required, and site accessibility. Generally, the total installed cost for a basic engineered system starts at approximately $35,000 and can go as high as $50,000. For systems like a sand filter, you can expect costs between $45,000 and $65,000, while advanced treatment units (ATU) may range from $55,000 to $80,000 or more.

When considering cisterns, the size is typically determined by the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated at two people per bedroom with a daily usage of 100 gallons per person. For example, a three-bedroom home would need a cistern with a capacity of around 600 gallons. Additional costs may arise from challenging site conditions, such as rocky terrain or steep access, potentially increasing your total by $10,000 to $25,000.

It is essential to remember that while the initial costs may seem high, engineered systems are designed to last 30-50 years or more with proper maintenance, protecting both your property value and the environment.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can vary widely based on specific site conditions. On average, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000, depending on the type of system required and the complexity of the installation.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system typically takes one to two weeks, depending on factors such as site accessibility and the type of system being installed. Proper planning and execution by experienced professionals can streamline the process.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: In many cases, particularly in areas like Vail with challenging soils, an engineered septic system is required. These systems are designed to handle unique site conditions effectively, ensuring compliance with local regulations and long-term functionality.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing instead of traditional perc tests to determine soil suitability for a septic system. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps decide the type of system needed for your property.
