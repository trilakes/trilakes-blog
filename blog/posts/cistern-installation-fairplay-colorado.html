Local Challenges in Park County

Building in Park County comes with its own set of challenges. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soil can complicate installation, requiring thorough planning and expertise. Moreover, the deep water table often necessitates the installation of a water cistern instead of relying on well water. Many properties experience limited access due to steep driveways and snow in winter, making water hauling a common solution. The frost line in this region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, further complicating water storage tank installation. Understanding these factors is crucial when selecting a cistern installer near me.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Fairplay

The cost of cistern installation in Fairplay can vary widely based on several factors. For a typical installation, you can expect costs to start around:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: $25,000 - $35,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: $40,000 - $55,000

To determine the right size for your potable water storage tank, we use a simple formula: multiply your bedroom count by 2 people per bedroom, and then by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants will need approximately 600 gallons per day. If you wish to refill monthly, you‚Äôd need a tank that can hold at least 18,000 gallons.

Additional costs may arise from excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on rocky terrain and access issues. A pump system installation typically costs between $2,500 and $5,000, while trenching and electrical work can also add to the total. Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost of a cistern installation typically starts from $25,000 for a basic 5,000-gallon system and can range up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. Additional charges for excavation, pumps, and electrical work can increase the overall cost significantly.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by your daily water usage and how often you want to refill it. For example, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a tank of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, while a winter survival system might need over 72,000 gallons.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process usually takes between 2 to 5 days, depending on site conditions, permitting processes, and the complexity of the installation. Our team at Tri-Lakes Contracting is committed to efficiency while ensuring high-quality work.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on factors like water table depth and accessibility. In many rural areas of Colorado, including Fairplay, wells can be too deep or unreliable, making a water cistern the better option. A cistern also provides a consistent water supply during dry seasons.
