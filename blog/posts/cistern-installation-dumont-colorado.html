Local Challenges in Clear Creek County

Installing a water cistern in Dumont comes with its unique set of challenges. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation difficult, often increasing costs significantly. At elevations of 8,000 feet or higher, the frost line can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating proper planning to ensure the cistern remains frost-proof. Many properties also have steep driveways and limited winter access, making it imperative to consider the refill frequency of your underground water storage tank. During the winter months, heavy snowfall and extreme temperature swings can complicate both access and the installation process. Additionally, Clear Creek County requires permits for all cistern installations, which must be obtained through the local building department, ensuring compliance with safety and environmental regulations.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Dumont

The cost of water storage tank installation in Dumont can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions and tank size. For residential needs, a common sizing formula is based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For instance, if you have a 3-bedroom home, you can expect to use around 600 gallons of water per day (based on 2 people per bedroom). If you prefer a refill every month, you‚Äôll need a cistern with a minimum capacity of 18,000 gallons.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: $13,000 to $15,000 (tank only)
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: $22,000 to $28,000 (tank only)
  • Excavation for burial: $3,000 to $8,000+, depending on rock and access conditions

Total installed system costs for a 5,000-gallon cistern typically range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can run from $40,000 to $55,000. Keep in mind that factors such as rocky terrain or difficult access can add 20-40% to excavation costs, making it crucial to budget accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern in Colorado varies based on size and installation conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon cistern system typically costs between $25,000 and $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system can range from $40,000 to $55,000, including excavation and plumbing.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is influenced by your daily water usage and refill frequency. For instance, a 3-bedroom home typically requires a minimum of 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, and even more if you want to minimize winter refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation generally takes a few weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. This includes time for securing permits, excavation, and final system connections.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on various factors, including water table depth and property access. A cistern can be a better alternative when wells are impractical or unreliable due to deep water tables, making them essential for many Dumont properties.
