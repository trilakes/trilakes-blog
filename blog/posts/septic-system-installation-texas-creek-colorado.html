Local Challenges in Fremont County

Texas Creek's stunning mountain landscape comes with its unique set of challenges for septic system installation. The soil here varies greatly, often consisting of rocky granite and clay, which complicates the installation of a traditional septic system. Many properties require engineered systems due to the poor drainage characteristics of the soil, as outlined in Colorado's Regulation 43.

Moreover, the water table is typically deep, necessitating cisterns for water supply and adding to the overall complexity of wastewater management. Steep driveways can limit access for heavy machinery, particularly in winter when the weather can become extreme with heavy snow loads and freezing temperatures. All these factors make it essential to have a knowledgeable septic installer who understands the local terrain and can prepare for the challenges that come with high-elevation installations.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our process for septic installation begins with a thorough assessment of your property. We perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests to determine the best approach for your onsite wastewater system. This includes digging two test holes to a depth of eight feet and sending samples to a licensed engineer for analysis.

Once we receive the soils report, we can design an engineered septic system that meets local regulations and is tailored to your property‚Äôs specific conditions. The installation process typically takes place during the building season, which runs from May to October at higher elevations in Fremont County. After securing the necessary septic permit from the Fremont County Building Department, we proceed with excavation, installation of the septic tank, and the construction of the leach field or drain field.

This entire process usually spans several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Our goal is to ensure a smooth installation that minimizes disruption to your property while adhering to all regulatory requirements.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Texas Creek

The cost of septic installation in Texas Creek can vary widely based on several factors. For a basic engineered system, you can expect to pay around $35,000 to $50,000. Costs will increase if your property has challenging conditions, such as rocky terrain or steep access, which can add an additional $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall expense.

When planning for water supply, remember that a cistern's size is based on bedroom count‚Äîestimating two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This means if you have a three-bedroom home, your cistern should be sized for 600 gallons per day, which can influence your overall water management costs.

Specific cost breakdowns include:

  • Soils test + engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits (varies by county): $500-$2,000
  • Excavation for system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank + components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000-$4,000

By understanding these factors, you can better prepare for your septic system installation in this beautiful Colorado mountain community.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for a basic engineered system. However, factors such as challenging terrain and property access can increase costs significantly, sometimes adding $10,000 to $25,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation varies but generally spans several weeks, depending on site conditions and whether all necessary permits have been secured. Installation is best done during the building season from May to October.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in the Texas Creek area require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are designed based on soil testing and are critical for ensuring proper wastewater management in mountain terrains.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes and analyzing soil samples to determine the best septic system design for your property, as the results can vary significantly by location.
