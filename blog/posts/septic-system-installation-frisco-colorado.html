Local Challenges in Summit County

Frisco boasts stunning mountain views and a vibrant community, but its unique terrain poses significant challenges for septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils found in Summit County often necessitate engineered systems, as conventional gravity systems are rarely feasible. With a water table that can be deep, many properties require additional solutions, such as cisterns for water storage or hauling.

The frost line in Frisco can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, meaning that proper installation and winter preparation are essential to prevent freezing issues during the harsh winter months. Properties with steep driveways may also face limited access, complicating the installation process. Additionally, the need for fire mitigation in forested areas requires careful planning of defensible space, further complicating the overall septic system design and installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process to ensure that your septic system is designed and installed correctly. Our first step is to conduct soil testing, which is pivotal in determining the right system for your property. Unlike traditional perc tests, our soil testing involves digging two test holes that reach depths of eight feet, collecting samples from various layers, and sending them to a licensed engineer for analysis.

Once we receive the soils report, we can create a comprehensive septic design tailored to your site. This design will be submitted to the Summit County Building Department for the necessary septic permit. After securing the permit, we‚Äôll schedule excavation, which typically occurs between May and October due to weather conditions.

During installation, we will place the septic tank and establish the drain field or leach field according to the designed specifications. If an engineered system is needed, our team will implement the advanced treatment unit (ATU) or sand filter system, ensuring compliance with all local regulations. Finally, we will conduct a thorough septic inspection to confirm the system is functioning as intended, providing peace of mind for years to come.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Frisco

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Frisco is essential for planning your project. The total installed cost can vary significantly based on site conditions, but you can expect the following high-end ranges:

  • Basic engineered system: $35,000-$50,000
  • Sand filter system: $45,000-$65,000
  • ATU/Advanced system: $55,000-$80,000+
  • Difficult sites with rocky terrain: Add $10,000-$25,000

The first step in the process is a soils test, which typically costs between $2,000 and $4,000. Afterward, permits can range from $500 to $2,000 depending on the requirements set by the Summit County Building Department. Excavation costs can add an additional $8,000 to $20,000+, while the septic tank and components will range from $5,000 to $15,000. If an ATU is needed, expect an additional $8,000 to $15,000 for the unit itself.

For properties requiring cisterns, the size is determined by the number of bedrooms (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day). Consider all these factors carefully when budgeting for your septic system, and remember that rocky, challenging terrain will add to the overall cost.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can vary widely based on site conditions. Generally, you can expect a basic engineered system to cost between $35,000 and $50,000, while more complex systems like sand filters can range from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units may start from $55,000.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The duration of septic installation can vary based on the system type and site conditions. Typically, the entire process can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on factors like permitting, excavation, and weather conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Frisco require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions outlined in Regulation 43. Our soil testing will determine the exact system needed for your property to ensure proper functionality and compliance.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves analyzing the soil's capacity to absorb water, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design for your property.
