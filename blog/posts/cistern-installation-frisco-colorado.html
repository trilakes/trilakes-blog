For homeowners in this picturesque area, reliable access to water can be a challenge due to the often deep water table and rugged terrain. This is where cistern installation becomes essential. Local Challenges in Summit County

Summit County's unique topography and soil conditions present several challenges for homeowners considering a water storage tank installation. The rocky granite and decomposed granite soils can complicate excavation, potentially increasing costs by 20-40%. Furthermore, the frost line in this region typically lies 36-48 inches deep, requiring careful planning to ensure that your buried water tank is adequately protected from freezing conditions. Access to many properties can also be limited, especially in winter months, making it crucial to choose a cistern that accommodates easier refilling schedules. With these factors in mind, working with a reliable cistern company that understands local regulations‚Äîespecially the permit requirements through the Summit County Building Department‚Äîis vital.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, the cost for a potable water storage tank typically starts from $25,000 for a 5,000-gallon system and can reach up to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. These costs include excavation, installation, and necessary plumbing, but can vary based on site conditions and specific project requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground cistern is determined by daily water usage and refill frequency. A common calculation is to assume 100 gallons per person per day, with the number of bedrooms in your home helping to estimate occupancy. For example, a 3-bedroom home may need a tank capable of storing at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions but generally takes about one to two weeks from the initial site evaluation to completion. This includes excavation, tank placement, and plumbing installations, with additional time needed for any necessary permits from the Summit County Building Department.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs location and water table depth. Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or where wells are impractical. They provide a flexible and reliable water source, especially in areas with limited access during winter months.
