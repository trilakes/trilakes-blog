Local Challenges in Eagle County

Minturn presents distinct challenges for septic system installation due to its rocky granite soils and varying water table depths. Many properties require engineered systems to comply with Regulation 43, which governs Colorado's OWTS. The frost line here ranges from 36 to 48 inches, posing additional challenges during winter months. Properties often have steep driveways, making access difficult, especially during heavy snowfalls. The extreme temperature swings and afternoon thunderstorms further complicate site evaluations and installation timelines. Understanding these factors is key to successful installations, as they significantly influence system design and long-term performance.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation that begins with a thorough site evaluation. Initially, we conduct detailed soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves using a mini excavator to dig two 8-foot test holes to gather soil samples at various depths for analysis by a licensed engineer. Based on the soils report, we recommend the most suitable system type, which could range from a conventional gravity system to more advanced treatment options like an ATU system. Once the system type is determined, we guide you through the necessary septic permit application process with the Eagle County Building Department.

The installation process typically spans several weeks, dependent on factors such as weather, site access, and specific requirements for your property. We handle everything from excavation to tank installation and the setup of leach fields or drain fields. Our expertise ensures that your system is not only compliant with local regulations but also designed for optimal performance in Minturn's unique environmental conditions.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Minturn

When considering the costs of septic installation in Minturn, homeowners should anticipate a range of expenses based on specific site conditions. A basic engineered system can typically start from $35,000 to $50,000, while more complex systems like sand filters may range from $45,000 to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), the total cost can be between $55,000 and $80,000. Keep in mind that these figures can vary significantly depending on challenges such as rocky terrain, which may add $10,000 to $25,000 to the overall cost.

Additionally, if your property requires a cistern for water, the size should be calculated based on the number of bedrooms‚Äîtypically 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of holding 600 gallons daily. It's essential to factor in the costs of soil testing and engineering, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, and excavation, which may run between $8,000 and $20,000. By understanding these factors, you can better prepare for the financial commitment involved in your septic system installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado can range significantly based on site conditions. Typically, basic engineered systems start from $35,000 to $50,000, while more advanced systems, such as sand filters or ATUs, can range from $45,000 to $80,000 or more, especially in challenging terrains like those in Minturn.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic system installation can vary, but generally it takes several weeks to ensure proper site evaluation, permitting, and installation. Factors such as weather, access to the site, and the complexity of the system can all influence the duration.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Minturn require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations outlined in Regulation 43. An engineered system is designed to handle the unique terrain and ensure effective treatment of onsite wastewater.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, traditional perc tests are replaced with comprehensive soil tests. These tests involve digging test holes to analyze soil composition and determine the most suitable septic system for your property, ensuring compliance with state regulations.
