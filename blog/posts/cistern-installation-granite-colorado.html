Why Granite Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Granite, located at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for homeowners seeking reliable water solutions. With properties often lacking direct access to a well, cistern installation becomes a vital service for ensuring a consistent and safe water supply. A professional cistern installer near you, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, ensures that your underground water storage tank is not only installed correctly but is also tailored to your specific needs. This is crucial in a region where the water table can be deep and weather conditions fluctuate dramatically, making the right installation approach essential for long-term performance and peace of mind.

Local Challenges in Lake County

The mountain terrain of Lake County poses significant challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite soil, combined with clay in the valleys, often complicates excavation and requires specialized equipment. With the frost line reaching depths of 36 to 48 inches, it is imperative to ensure that your water cistern is properly buried to prevent freezing and other issues during harsh winters. Additionally, access to many properties can be limited due to steep driveways, particularly in winter when heavy snow can restrict movement. As a result, many homeowners in Granite rely on water hauling as a backup solution, making it crucial to have a robust water storage system in place.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is designed to ensure minimal disruption and maximum efficiency. We start with a comprehensive site assessment to identify the best location for your potable water storage tank based on your specific needs and property conditions. This includes evaluating soil composition, access points, and proximity to your home.

Once we finalize the site, we obtain necessary permits from the Lake County Building Department to ensure compliance with local regulations. Our team uses specialized excavation equipment to dig the necessary hole, taking into account the rocky terrain, which can add complexity to the project.

After installing the buried water tank, we connect it to the home's plumbing system, including pumps and pressure tanks. We also focus on winter preparation, ensuring the system is winterized and frost-proof, which is crucial for properties at this elevation. The entire installation process typically takes 1-2 weeks, depending on factors such as weather and site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can vary significantly based on its size and site conditions. For instance, a basic 5,000-gallon underground water storage tank typically starts around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may range up to $55,000. Additional installation costs can add thousands more depending on excavation and plumbing requirements.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: Your required tank size depends on daily water usage, calculated at about 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home (assuming 6 occupants) would need a minimum 18,000-gallon tank if refilled monthly, or even larger if you want to avoid frequent refills during winter.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Factors such as rocky terrain and access limitations can extend the timeline, making proper planning and professional service essential for timely completion.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on site-specific conditions. While wells can provide a continuous water supply, they may not be practical in areas with a deep water table. Cisterns offer a reliable alternative for water storage, especially in regions like Granite where accessibility and water hauling become significant concerns.
