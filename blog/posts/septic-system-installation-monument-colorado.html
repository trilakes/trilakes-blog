Why Monument Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in Monument, Colorado, at an elevation of 7,352 feet provides breathtaking views and a serene mountain lifestyle. However, building or renovating a home in this picturesque region comes with unique challenges, particularly concerning septic installation. As a homeowner, ensuring your septic system is installed correctly is crucial for maintaining property value and protecting the environment. A professional septic installer like Tri-Lakes Contracting has the experience and knowledge to navigate the complexities of Colorado's onsite wastewater regulations, especially in areas with difficult soil conditions and steep terrain.

Utilizing a licensed and insured contractor ensures that your septic system meets all local regulations, including the necessary El Paso County Permit Guide. Our experts will conduct thorough soil testing to determine the most suitable system for your property, guaranteeing that your investment is sound and compliant.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Monument's stunning landscape is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which can complicate septic installation. Many properties in the area require engineered septic systems due to the challenging soil conditions. The deep water table often necessitates cisterns for reliable water supply, adding another layer of complexity to your onsite wastewater management.

The frost line in our region is typically 36-48 inches deep, which means any septic tank installation must account for potential freezing. Additionally, steep driveways can limit access for heavy machinery, creating challenges for excavation and installation. Understanding these factors is critical for ensuring a successful septic system installation that meets all local codes and requirements.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure your system is tailored to your specific needs. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step overview:

  1. Site Consultation: Our team visits your property to assess its specific needs and challenges, including soil characteristics and access issues.
  2. Soil Testing: We perform detailed soil testing. Unlike traditional perc tests, we dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and send samples to a licensed engineer for evaluation. This report dictates the type of septic system required.
  3. Septic System Design: Based on the soil report, we design a custom septic system, which may include conventional, pressure-dosed, or sand filter systems.
  4. Permitting: All necessary permits are obtained through the El Paso County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43 for onsite wastewater treatment systems.
  5. Installation: Our professional team will conduct the installation, which typically occurs between May and October due to weather constraints. We prioritize safety and efficiency throughout the process.
  6. Final Inspection: After installation, we coordinate a final inspection to ensure everything meets local codes.

This process typically takes a few weeks from consultation to completion, depending on site conditions and permitting timelines.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Monument

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation is essential for budgeting your project. The total cost can vary significantly based on several factors, including soil conditions, the type of system installed, and site accessibility.

Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs for various systems:

  • Basic engineered system: $35,000-$50,000
  • Sand filter system: $45,000-$65,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU): $55,000-$80,000+
  • Difficult sites with rocky terrain: Add $10,000-$25,000

Additionally, the costs for initial soil testing and engineering can range from $2,000-$4,000, with permits costing between $500-$2,000. Remember, the installation of a cistern is determined by the number of bedrooms in your home, calculated as 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado varies widely depending on site conditions and system type. Basic engineered systems typically range from $35,000 to $50,000, while advanced treatment units can cost between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary, but on average, it takes a few weeks from initial consultation to completion, depending on permitting and site conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Monument will require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations as outlined in Regulation 43. A professional assessment will determine the best system for your needs.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil characteristics, which helps determine the most suitable septic system for your property.
