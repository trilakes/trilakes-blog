Why Mt. Crested Butte Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

In the stunning mountain community of Mt. Crested Butte, where the elevation exceeds 8,000 feet, having a reliable water supply is crucial. Many properties in this area lack a direct source of water, making cistern installation an essential service for homeowners. A properly installed underground water storage tank can serve various needs, from residential potable water storage to irrigation for lush mountain landscapes. Given the unique challenges of mountain living, including extreme weather conditions and rocky terrain, trusting a licensed and insured professional is vital to ensure your system is not only functional but also compliant with local regulations.

Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Building in Gunnison County presents unique challenges that require careful planning and expert knowledge. The area's rocky granite and decomposed granite soils, combined with a deep water table, often necessitate water hauling or the installation of a water cistern. Additionally, properties at this elevation face issues such as a frost line that can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, which complicates water storage tank installation. Many homeowners may be unaware that the local building department requires specific permits for cisterns, making professional guidance crucial. Extreme weather, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can also impact access and installation timelines, emphasizing the need for a well-prepared and knowledgeable cistern installer near me.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a systematic approach to ensure your cistern installation is seamless and efficient. Our process begins with a detailed site assessment, determining the best location for your buried water tank considering factors such as accessibility and soil conditions. We then assist in obtaining the necessary permits from the Gunnison County building department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

Once the permits are secured, we schedule the excavation phase, which can take anywhere from a few days to a week, depending on the complexity of the site. We typically install Norwesco tanks, known for their durability and reliability, but we also assess the specific needs of your property. This includes calculating the appropriate tank size based on the number of bedrooms and expected daily water usage.

After the tank is installed, we set up the required pump system and pressure tank, ensuring that your underground water storage is fully operational. Our team will also install necessary electrical hookups and conduct a final inspection to confirm everything meets safety standards. We pride ourselves on our customer service, providing ongoing support and advice for maintaining your cistern system, particularly through winter preparation tips and winterizing cistern installation.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Mt. Crested Butte

The cost of cistern installation in Mt. Crested Butte can vary significantly based on site conditions and specific project requirements. We typically quote on the higher end of the following ranges to account for the challenges presented by the mountain terrain. For instance, a standard Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank costs between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000. This pricing only reflects the tank itself; additional costs will arise from excavation, pump systems, trenching, and electrical setup, which can add another $25,000 to $55,000 to your total installed system cost.

For instance, if you have a 3-bedroom home, you might calculate your daily usage at approximately 600 gallons. Depending on how frequently you want to refill (monthly or quarterly), you may consider a larger system to avoid hassle during the winter months. Installing a cistern capable of holding 36,000 gallons or more could cost between $75,000 and $120,000, especially with rocky terrain that adds an additional 20-40% to excavation costs.

Keep in mind that the cost of water hauling for ongoing maintenance must also be factored into your budget, with typical delivery costing $200 to $350 for 3,000 gallons. Investing in a larger tank can lead to fewer deliveries, resulting in long-term savings and reduced hassle.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: In Colorado, particularly in the Mt. Crested Butte area, the installation of a cistern can range significantly based on various factors. For instance, a standard 5,000-gallon system typically starts at about $25,000, while larger systems can exceed $75,000, depending largely on site conditions and additional features.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's daily water usage, typically calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For a 3-bedroom home, assuming an occupancy of 6 people, you would need a tank that can hold at least 36,000 gallons if you plan to refill every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions but typically ranges from a few days to a week. Factors such as excavation challenges and permit approvals from the Gunnison County building department can influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well often depends on your location and water needs. In areas like Mt. Crested Butte, where the water table can be deep, a cistern may serve as a more practical and reliable solution, especially for homes lacking easy access to well water.
