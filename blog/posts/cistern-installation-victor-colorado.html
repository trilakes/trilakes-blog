Why Victor Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in Victor, Colorado, often referred to as the "City of Mines," brings unique challenges, especially concerning water supply. At an elevation of 9,708 feet, properties in this mountainous region frequently encounter difficulties related to water sourcing. For many homes here, traditional wells may not be a viable option due to depth or soil conditions, making professional cistern installation essential. A licensed and insured cistern company like Tri-Lakes Contracting ensures that your underground water storage tank is not only expertly installed but also meets local regulations, providing you with a reliable source of potable water and peace of mind.

Local Challenges in Teller County

Victor's high elevation and challenging terrain create several obstacles when it comes to water sourcing. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation for water storage tank installation. Properties often experience deep water tables, necessitating the use of cisterns for effective water management and water hauling. Moreover, the frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, which demands careful planning for freeze protection during installation. The extreme weather, including heavy snow and afternoon thunderstorms, further emphasizes the need for a robust cistern system that can withstand the elements while providing a reliable supply of water throughout the year.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Victor

Understanding the cost of cistern installation is crucial for planning your water storage solutions. The total installed costs for a typical system can vary significantly based on several factors:

  • Tank Size: The size of your underground water storage tank is primarily determined by your daily water usage and how often you prefer to refill. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an estimated daily usage of 600 gallons would require a tank of at least 18,000 gallons if refilled monthly, or 72,000 gallons if set to last through winter.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavation can range from $3,000 to $8,000 or more, depending on the difficulty of the terrain, particularly in rocky areas.
  • Additional Components: The installation includes costs for a pump system, pressure tank, trenching, electrical hookups, and any necessary water filtration systems, adding another $2,500 to $5,000 for pumps and $2,000 to $5,000 for filtration.
  • Tank Pricing: We typically install Norwesco tanks, which are industry-leading for underground potable water storage. A 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank can cost between $13,000 and $15,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank ranges from $22,000 to $28,000.

Considering these factors, the total installed cost for a basic 5,000-gallon system can start from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems could range from $40,000 to $120,000 based on your specific needs. Remember that rocky terrain and challenging access will likely increase your overall costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely based on the size and complexity of the project. For example, a basic 5,000-gallon system typically starts from $25,000 to $35,000, while larger systems can cost between $40,000 and $120,000, depending on site conditions and additional components required.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be determined by your daily water usage, which is generally calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six residents may require a tank size of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on site conditions and permitting processes. Typically, the entire process takes several weeks, with factors like excavation complexity and access challenges influencing the duration.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific circumstances. Cisterns are often a better option in areas with deep water tables or challenging drilling conditions, providing a reliable water supply without the complications of well maintenance.
