Why Colorado Springs Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In Colorado Springs, located at an elevation of 6,035 feet, the unique terrain and climate present distinct challenges for septic system installation. Properties often require specialized solutions due to the rocky granite and clay soils that are common in the region. Engaging a professional septic installer near you, like Tri-Lakes Contracting, ensures that your septic installation is not only compliant with Colorado's stringent regulations but also tailored to meet the specific needs of your property. A successful septic system installation involves understanding soil conditions, local regulations, and designing a system that will function effectively for years to come.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

El Paso County presents unique challenges for septic systems primarily due to its diverse soil types, which can vary dramatically even within a single property. The rocky and decomposed granite soils, coupled with clay in lower valleys, often necessitate engineered septic systems to ensure proper waste management. Additionally, the water table can be quite deep, meaning many properties require cisterns for adequate water supply. The frost line in this area is also significant, ranging from 36 to 48 inches, which can complicate the installation process. Heavy snowfall in winter months and steep driveways can limit access during certain times of the year, making it essential to plan your septic system installation well in advance. Understanding these local challenges is critical for homeowners looking to build or renovate in this beautiful mountain community.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process begins with a thorough soil test to understand the specific conditions of your property. We use a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, and collect samples from various depths. These samples are then analyzed by a licensed engineer to provide a comprehensive soils report that determines the type of septic system you need. This step is crucial, as it guides the design and ensures compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43 governing onsite wastewater treatment systems (OWTS).

Once we have the soils report, we will apply for the necessary septic permit through the El Paso County Building Department. This is an important step, as all permits must go through this office before any installation can commence. The installation timeline typically spans from May to October, aligning with the best weather conditions for construction.

During the installation, we will excavate the site, install the septic tank, and create the leach field or drain field as required. Our expert team will ensure that every aspect of the system is executed according to the highest standards, with a focus on long-term reliability and environmental safety. After installation, we recommend regular septic inspection and maintenance to extend the life of your system and prevent costly repairs.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Colorado Springs

The cost of septic installation in Colorado Springs can vary widely based on several factors, including soil conditions, site accessibility, and system type. Generally, the total installed cost for a basic engineered system starts from $35,000 to $50,000. If your property requires a sand filter system, expect costs to range from $45,000 to $65,000, while an advanced treatment unit (ATU) can range from $55,000 to over $80,000, especially in challenging terrains.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to over $80,000, depending on factors such as soil conditions, system type, and site accessibility. Most homeowners can expect to pay on the higher end of these ranges due to the challenging mountain terrain.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The septic installation process can take anywhere from a few days to several weeks, depending on factors like the complexity of the system and weather conditions. Generally, planning for installation in the warmer months of May to October is advisable for optimal conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Colorado, especially in mountain areas, require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. A soils report conducted by a licensed engineer will indicate the type of system necessary for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition and drainage capabilities, which is essential for determining the appropriate septic system for your property.
