Local Challenges in Eagle County

Edwards is not just a picturesque mountain community; it presents unique challenges for water storage solutions. The rocky granite and clay soils can make excavation difficult and costly. Many properties in this area require extensive grading and specialized equipment to install a buried water tank. Furthermore, the water table is often deep, necessitating the use of cisterns and frequent water hauling for those without wells.

Winter poses additional challenges, with a frost line that reaches 36-48 inches, making it vital to consider winter preparation tips during your installation. Property access is often limited due to steep driveways and heavy snowfall, complicating both installation and ongoing water delivery. Therefore, a frost-proof cistern is essential to prevent freezing and ensure a reliable water supply year-round.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Edwards

Understanding the cost of cistern installation in Edwards is crucial for homeowners. The total installed system price ranges significantly based on site conditions and specific requirements. For a 5,000-gallon system, expect costs starting from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system ranges from $40,000 to $55,000. This includes the tank, excavation, pumps, and all necessary installations.

To accurately size your potable water storage tank, consider the formula: bedroom count x 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a minimum of 600 gallons per day, which translates to a quarterly refill capacity of at least 18,000 gallons. Factors such as rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, and remote access can further increase expenses due to additional hauling and equipment requirements.

Being aware of these factors allows for better budgeting and ensures that you choose a system that meets your long-term water needs without frequent refills.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of installing a cistern in Colorado typically starts at around $25,000 for a 5,000-gallon system, reaching up to $55,000 for larger capacities. Factors like site conditions, tank size, and additional features can significantly affect the final price.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your household's daily water usage. A simple calculation is to multiply the number of bedrooms in your home by 2 (people per bedroom) and by 100 gallons per person. This will give you a solid estimate for your needs.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: A typical cistern installation can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the project. Factors such as obtaining permits, excavation, and weather conditions can influence the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific location and water needs. Cisterns are often a more reliable option when wells are impractical due to deep water tables or rocky terrain. They also provide flexibility in water sourcing, especially in remote areas.
