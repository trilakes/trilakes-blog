Why Evergreen Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Evergreen, nestled high in the Rocky Mountains at an elevation of over 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges when it comes to water access. With many homes lacking reliable well water due to deep water tables and rocky soil, cistern installation becomes an invaluable solution for homeowners. Utilizing a professionally installed underground water storage tank not only ensures a steady supply of potable water but also provides peace of mind, especially during the harsh Colorado winters. The icy temperatures and heavy snowfall can render unprotected systems useless, making it essential to rely on experienced cistern installers who understand the local terrain and climate.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Evergreen

Understanding the costs associated with water storage tank installation is essential for any Evergreen resident considering a cistern. The size of your water cistern will depend on your daily water usage and how frequently you wish to refill. A general rule of thumb is to calculate your water needs based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count: assume 2 people per bedroom and approximately 100 gallons of water per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require about 600 gallons per day.

Refill frequency also influences sizing; if you want to minimize refills, a larger cistern is advisable. For instance, refilling every two weeks would necessitate a minimum of 8,400 gallons, while a system designed to last through winter would require upwards of 72,000 gallons.

The cost of a Norwesco 5,000-gallon tank starts around $13,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can range from $22,000 to $28,000. Additional expenses can include excavation costs ($3,000-$8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and the electrical hookup ($1,000-$2,500). Therefore, the total installed price for a 5,000-gallon system can start from $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system might reach up to $55,000 or more, depending on site conditions and accessibility.

Keep in mind that rocky terrain can add another 20-40% to excavation costs, making it crucial to budget accordingly. For a comprehensive overview of costs, check out our Cistern Cost Guide.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The total cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary significantly based on size and site conditions. For a 5,000-gallon system, you can expect prices starting from $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may range from $40,000 to $55,000 or higher, considering excavation and additional installation costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is typically determined by your household's daily water usage and refill preferences. A 3-bedroom home, for instance, would require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or up to 72,000 gallons for a winter supply solution.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeframe for a cistern can range from a few weeks to a month, depending on factors such as site preparation, weather conditions, and local permit processing through Jefferson County. We keep you updated throughout the process for transparency.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on several factors, including water table depth and property access. Cisterns are often more reliable in areas with deep water tables or rocky soil, as they serve as excellent alternatives when wells are impractical or too costly to drill.
