Why Penrose Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

In the heart of Penrose, known as "Apple Valley," the picturesque landscape can be deceptive, especially when it comes to installing a septic system. With an elevation of 5,200 feet, properties here face unique challenges that highlight the importance of hiring a qualified septic installer. The rocky granite and clay soils, combined with the depth of the water table, often necessitate engineered systems rather than conventional installations. A professional septic contractor will ensure that the system is not only compliant with Colorado's Regulation 43 but also tailored to the specific conditions of your property, thus preventing potential failures and costly repairs down the line.

Local Challenges in Fremont County

Fremont County's distinctive terrain presents several challenges for septic installation. The soil here is a mix of rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay, which complicates the installation of standard systems. Many properties require engineered solutions due to these soil conditions, making the expertise of a seasoned septic contractor vital. Additionally, the area's elevation means a frost line that can reach depths of 36-48 inches, necessitating careful planning to avoid freeze-related damage. Access can often be difficult, especially in winter months, when steep driveways may limit construction activities. Understanding these local challenges is essential for a successful and compliant septic system installation.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation that ensures compliance and efficiency. The journey begins with a thorough soil testing phase, where we utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each reaching 8 feet deep. Samples are collected from varying depths and sent to a licensed engineer for analysis, which informs the type of system required for your property. This step is crucial, especially in the Penrose area, where challenging soil conditions often dictate the need for engineered systems.

Once the soils report is completed, we help you navigate the permit process through the Fremont County Building Department, which is essential for obtaining a septic permit. After securing approval, we move on to the installation phase, which typically occurs between May and October due to weather constraints. Our team performs the excavation, installation of the septic tank, and ensures that the drain field and leach field are correctly placed according to the specifications. Throughout this process, we prioritize clear communication, keeping you informed every step of the way, and ensuring your new onsite wastewater treatment system is set up for long-term success.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Penrose

The cost of septic installation in Penrose can vary significantly based on several factors. Generally, homeowners should expect to pay a minimum of $35,000 for a basic engineered system and up to $80,000 for advanced systems like the ATU. Costs are influenced by site-specific conditions such as rocky terrain, which can add between $10,000 and $25,000 to overall expenses. Additionally, the size of your system will depend on your household's water usage, typically calculated as two people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day. For example, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern sized for 600 gallons per day.

Breaking down the costs further, the initial soils test and engineering may range from $2,000 to $4,000, while permits can add another $500 to $2,000 based on the county's requirements. Excavation typically costs between $8,000 and $20,000, and the tank and components can range from $5,000 to $15,000. If you require an advanced treatment system, expect to pay an additional $8,000 to $15,000. It's important to budget appropriately and remember that the total costs can vary greatly based on your specific site conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for septic installation in Colorado typically starts at around $35,000 for a basic engineered system and can go up to $80,000 or more for advanced treatment systems, especially in challenging terrains like those found in Penrose.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process for a septic system generally spans several weeks, including the soils testing and permitting phases. Once permits are obtained, the actual installation can take a few days to a week, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Penrose require an engineered septic system due to the challenging soil conditions. A thorough soils test will determine the best system type for your property, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil testing rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to assess soil conditions, which helps in determining the appropriate type of septic system for your property.
