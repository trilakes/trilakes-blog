Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Kittredge

When considering septic installation in Kittredge, understanding the costs involved is paramount. The total cost for installation ranges widely, typically starting from:

  • Basic engineered system: $35,000-$50,000
  • Sand filter system: $45,000-$65,000
  • Advanced treatment unit (ATU): $55,000-$80,000+

These costs can be influenced by several factors, including site conditions such as rocky terrain, which can add an additional $10,000-$25,000 to the overall price. The size of your septic tank is also determined by your property‚Äôs bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). For example, a 3-bedroom home would typically require a cistern of at least 600 gallons per day.

Moreover, the costs for soil testing and engineering can range from $2,000-$4,000, while permits can cost anywhere between $500-$2,000. Excavation for the system may also vary from $8,000-$20,000+ depending on access and terrain challenges. It‚Äôs essential to account for these variables to get an accurate estimate for your unique situation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary greatly based on site conditions and system requirements. Generally, basic engineered systems start from $35,000 and can go up to $80,000+ for advanced treatment systems.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic system installation typically spans several weeks, depending on factors such as permitting and weather conditions. The installation itself usually occurs during the optimal building season from May to October.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Kittredge require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations. A soils report is essential to determine the best system for your property.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests to assess soil drainage capabilities. This involves collecting soil samples to inform the design of your septic system.
