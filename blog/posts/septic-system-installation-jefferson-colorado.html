Local Challenges in Park County

Park County, with elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, presents unique challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils typical of the area often require engineered systems to meet the stringent standards set by Colorado's Regulation 43. Additionally, the water table can be significantly deep, necessitating the use of cisterns for water storage in many properties.

During the winter months, properties can experience extreme temperature swings and heavy snow, which can compromise septic systems if they are not winterized properly. The frost line in this region reaches depths of 36 to 48 inches, making it essential to dig deeper for effective installation. Moreover, steep driveways and limited winter access can complicate the installation process. Understanding these local challenges allows us to design and install systems that are both durable and efficient, protecting your investment for years to come.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a meticulous process for septic installation to ensure that each system is tailored to your property‚Äôs unique needs:

  1. Initial Consultation: We begin with an onsite assessment to understand your property, discuss your needs, and outline the installation process.
  2. Soil Testing: Unlike traditional perc tests, we perform comprehensive soil testing to determine the soil type and its suitability for different system designs. This involves digging two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to collect samples for analysis by a licensed engineer.
  3. Design and Permitting: Based on the soils report, we create an engineered design for your septic system and assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Park County Building Department.
  4. Installation: Once permits are secured, we schedule the installation, typically between May and October when access is optimal. Our team handles everything from excavation to the final connection of the septic tank and leach field.
  5. Inspection and Maintenance: After installation, we conduct thorough inspections to ensure everything meets local regulations, providing guidance on ongoing maintenance to prolong your system‚Äôs lifespan.

Our detailed approach ensures that your septic system will function effectively, even in the demanding conditions of Jefferson.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Jefferson

Understanding the costs associated with septic installation in Jefferson is crucial for homeowners. The total cost typically starts from $35,000 for a basic engineered system and can rise to $80,000 or more for advanced treatment units, depending on site conditions.

The key components contributing to the overall cost include:

  • Soils Testing and Engineering: Expect to pay between $2,000 and $4,000 for soil testing and the engineering report.
  • Permits: These can vary significantly, ranging from $500 to $2,000, depending on the specifics of your project.
  • Excavation: Excavation costs typically range from $8,000 to $20,000, heavily influenced by terrain challenges, such as rocky soil.
  • Tank and Components: The septic tank and its components can cost between $5,000 and $15,000.
  • Advanced Treatment Units (if required): These units add approximately $8,000 to $15,000 to the overall cost.

For properties requiring cisterns, remember that the size is typically calculated based on the formula: bedroom count x 2 people x 100 gallons per day. With the unique challenges of Jefferson's terrain, including rocky soils and steep access, costs can increase significantly, often by $10,000 to $25,000.
