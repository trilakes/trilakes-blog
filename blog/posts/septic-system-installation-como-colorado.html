Local Challenges in Park County

The unique geology at 9,800 feet elevation, characterized by rocky granite, decomposed granite, and clay in the valleys, poses significant challenges for septic installation in Como. The soil often requires specialized engineered septic systems rather than conventional setups. Additionally, the frost line in this region can reach depths of 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning to avoid freezing issues during winter.

Weather conditions can also affect septic system performance. Como experiences extreme temperature fluctuations, heavy snow loads, and afternoon thunderstorms, all of which can impact access to properties. Many homes have steep driveways that may become inaccessible during winter months, making it essential to select a septic contractor who understands these local challenges and can devise solutions to ensure year-round functionality.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive process for septic installation to ensure compliance with local regulations and optimal performance. Our first step is to conduct a thorough soil testing assessment, where we utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep. This soil analysis helps us understand the soil's characteristics and informs the septic design needed for your property.

Once we have the soil report, we move on to obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Park County Building Department. Understanding the local permitting process is vital, as it affects the timeline for installation. The typical building season in higher elevations like Como runs from May to October, so timely permits can help streamline the project.

After securing the permit, we prepare the site, which may involve clearing access paths and ensuring that our excavation equipment can operate efficiently. The installation process generally takes about one to two weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. Finally, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning correctly before completing the project.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Como

The cost of septic installation in Como can vary significantly based on site conditions, soil type, and system requirements. For a complete engineered system, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000. If you require an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can start from $55,000 and go as high as $80,000, especially in rocky or difficult terrains where additional excavation may be necessary.

For homeowners considering cisterns, the size is typically based on bedroom count‚Äîcalculating 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. This means, for instance, a three-bedroom home would need a cistern with a capacity of approximately 600 gallons. Additionally, when budgeting for your septic system, keep in mind the costs for soil testing and engineering, which can range from $2,000 to $4,000, and permits that may vary from $500 to $2,000.

Other factors that may increase your costs include rocky terrain, steep access drives, and the necessity for extensive excavation work. Being prepared for these potential costs can help you make informed decisions and avoid surprises during the installation process.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems. For more complex systems like sand filters or ATUs, prices can rise to between $45,000 and $80,000, especially if site conditions are challenging.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process can generally take about one to two weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. Factors like soil testing, permitting, and weather can also influence the timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Many properties in Colorado, especially those with challenging soils, require engineered septic systems. These systems are designed to meet the specific conditions of your site and are often mandatory under Regulation 43.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soil testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging holes to assess soil composition, which informs the type of septic system suitable for your property.
