Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Perry Park

When considering water storage tank installation, understanding the costs involved is crucial for budgeting. The total installed cost for a cistern system can vary significantly based on various factors. For instance, a typical Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank ranges from $13,000 to $15,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank can cost between $22,000 and $28,000. However, it‚Äôs essential to remember that these figures only cover the tank itself. Additional expenses, including excavation (which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on rocky conditions), pump systems ($2,500 to $5,000), and trenching to your house (at $25 to $50 per linear foot), can increase the overall investment significantly.

To determine the appropriate tank size for your needs, estimate your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms. For example, in a 3-bedroom home with an average of 6 occupants, you‚Äôd require approximately 600 gallons per day. If you prefer a refill every 2-3 months, you‚Äôd need a cistern with a capacity of at least 36,000-54,000 gallons. Considering winter access challenges, many homeowners opt for larger systems to minimize the need for frequent refills, which can be a hassle during snowy months.

In total, the pricing for a complete installation can range from $25,000 to $35,000 for a 5,000-gallon system, up to $75,000 to $120,000 for large capacity systems exceeding 30,000 gallons. Always prepare for the higher end of the pricing spectrum and remember that factors like rocky terrain or limited access can add 20-40% to your costs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado can vary greatly based on the tank size and site conditions. For a typical 5,000-gallon system, expect costs to range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may run between $40,000 and $55,000, especially in challenging terrains like those found in Perry Park.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household‚Äôs daily water usage and refill preferences. The general formula is to multiply the number of bedrooms by 2 people per bedroom and then multiply by 100 gallons per day. For a 3-bedroom home, this would suggest a minimum of 36,000 gallons for quarterly refills, while larger systems are recommended for less frequent access.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions, weather, and the complexity of the installation. This process includes everything from site assessment and permitting to excavation and final testing of your new water system.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on your specific property conditions. Many mountainous areas like Perry Park have deep water tables, making wells impractical or unreliable. In such cases, cistern installation offers a viable alternative, providing a consistent water supply without the challenges of well drilling.
