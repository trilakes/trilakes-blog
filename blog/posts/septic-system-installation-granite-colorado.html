Local Challenges in Lake County

Lake County's mountainous landscape poses several challenges for septic system installations. The rocky granite and clay soils found in the valleys often require engineered systems, as traditional methods may not suffice. Additionally, the deep water table can complicate drainage solutions, often necessitating cisterns for water storage. The frost line here is typically between 36 to 48 inches, which means that your septic installation must be deep enough to avoid winter freeze damage. Furthermore, heavy snowfall and afternoon thunderstorms can hinder access to many properties, particularly those with steep driveways, making timely installation crucial during the building season from May to October.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a streamlined process for septic installation to ensure efficiency and compliance with local regulations:

  1. Site Assessment: We begin with a thorough site assessment, including soil testing, where we dig test holes to understand the soil composition and its capacity for wastewater treatment.
  2. Soils Report: The soil samples are sent to a licensed engineer who generates a detailed soils report, which guides our septic design.
  3. Permitting: Next, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Lake County Building Department, ensuring all regulations under Regulation 43 are met.
  4. System Design: Based on the soils report, we design an appropriate engineered septic solution tailored to your property.
  5. Installation: Our team then proceeds with the installation. This includes excavation, laying down the drain field, and connecting the septic tank.
  6. Final Inspection: Finally, we arrange for a septic inspection to confirm that the system meets all regulatory requirements.

This comprehensive approach ensures a reliable and durable septic system that will serve your household for years to come, particularly in the challenging conditions of Granite.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Granite

Understanding the costs involved in septic installation is crucial for homeowners in Granite, where the rugged terrain and unique soil conditions can impact pricing. Here‚Äôs a breakdown of typical costs:

  • Soils test + engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits: $500-$2,000, depending on Lake County requirements.
  • Excavation: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank + components: $5,000-$15,000
  • Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU): $8,000-$15,000, if needed.
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000-$4,000

In general, the total installed costs for a basic engineered system will range from $35,000 to $50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000 to $65,000. For advanced ATU systems, expect costs between $55,000 and $80,000. Keep in mind that challenging access and rocky terrain can increase costs significantly, sometimes adding an additional $10,000 to $25,000.

For homeowners considering a cistern for water needs, the size is typically calculated based on the number of bedrooms: 2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons per day.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically ranges from $35,000 to $50,000 for basic engineered systems, while advanced systems can cost between $55,000 and $80,000. Factors such as site conditions, terrain, and access can significantly impact the final price.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation varies but generally takes about 1 to 2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the project, permitting processes, and weather conditions. Proper planning and scheduling are vital for timely installations, especially in the mountain regions.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Due to the challenging soil conditions in Granite, most properties require an engineered septic system to comply with Colorado regulations. A professional assessment will determine the best system for your specific site conditions.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils testing instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which informs the design and installation of an appropriate septic system.
