Local Challenges in Fremont County

Cotopaxi is not just a picturesque mountain community; its unique geographical features pose significant challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite soils, coupled with decomposed granite and clay found in valley areas, often require engineered systems to ensure effective waste treatment. Given the elevation and varying water table depths, many properties may need cisterns for water supply, complicating the planning process further.

Moreover, the frost line in the region can reach depths of 36-48 inches, which necessitates careful planning to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months. Seasonal weather patterns, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, can delay projects, emphasizing the importance of choosing a reliable septic contractor. Additionally, properties with steep driveways may face limited access during winter, further complicating the installation process.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our approach to septic installation is comprehensive and tailored to meet the unique needs of each property. The process begins with a thorough soil testing to determine the appropriate solution for your land. Using a mini excavator, we dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, and gather soil samples from different depths. These samples are then sent to a licensed engineer, who provides a detailed soils report that is crucial for determining the right type of system for your property.

Once we have the soils report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Fremont County Building Department. This step is critical as it ensures compliance with local regulations governed by Regulation 43. After securing permits, we move forward with the installation of your septic tank and the associated drainage systems, whether that involves a pressure-dosed system, sand filter, or an advanced treatment unit (ATU).

Our typical timeline for installation is from May to October, taking into account the accessibility of your property and any specific challenges posed by the terrain. We ensure that your system is installed with precision, maintaining high standards of quality that will last for decades. Following installation, we also provide guidance on how to properly maintain your system, especially during the winter months to prevent freeze-related issues.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Cotopaxi

Understanding the financial aspects of septic installation is essential for planning your project. The costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, but for most engineered systems, you can expect total installed costs to start from:

  • Basic engineered system: $35,000-$50,000
  • Sand filter system: $45,000-$65,000
  • Advanced treatment unit (ATU): $55,000-$80,000+

In addition to these estimates, you should also account for the costs associated with soil testing and engineering, which typically range from $2,000-$4,000, and permits that may vary from $500-$2,000 based on Fremont County requirements. Excavation, tank components, and electrical setups also contribute to the overall budget, which can increase further if your property has rocky terrain or difficult access. For properties requiring cisterns, remember the size is based on the formula of bedroom count multiplied by two people per bedroom, multiplied by 100 gallons per day.

Regular maintenance and winterizing your septic system can prevent costly repairs, especially in Cotopaxi's harsh winters. Examine your system every year, and consider a professional inspection to ensure optimal performance.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado varies significantly based on site conditions. For most residential properties, expect to pay between $35,000 and $80,000+, depending on the type of system required and the complexity of installation.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: Typically, septic installation can take anywhere from a few days to a couple of weeks. This timeline can be affected by factors such as permit approvals, weather conditions, and site accessibility, especially in mountainous areas like Cotopaxi.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Cotopaxi require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and depth of the frost line. An engineered system ensures compliance with Colorado's Regulation 43 and provides a reliable solution for onsite wastewater treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests instead of traditional perc tests. These tests help determine the appropriate type of septic system based on the soil's drainage capabilities, which is crucial for effective onsite wastewater management.
