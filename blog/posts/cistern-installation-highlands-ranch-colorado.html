Local Challenges in Douglas County

Building in the Highlands Ranch area presents specific challenges that require an experienced approach to cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils in the region can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs and time delays. Additionally, with an elevation of 8,000+ feet, the water table is frequently deeper, making water hauling a common necessity for many homeowners. Many properties require effective underground cistern systems to ensure a steady supply of water.

Winter can be particularly harsh, with heavy snowfall and freezing temperatures that can complicate access and installation. The frost line ranges between 36 to 48 inches, which must be accounted for when installing a buried water tank. Furthermore, steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder installation efforts, emphasizing the importance of working with a reputable cistern company that understands these local conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Highlands Ranch

Understanding the cost of cistern installation is crucial for planning your water storage solution. In Highlands Ranch, the total installation costs typically range from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. This estimate includes the water storage tank installation, excavation ($3,000-$8,000+), pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), and additional costs such as electrical hookups and filtration systems. Size is determined by the number of bedrooms in your home, with the formula being:

  • Daily water usage: 75-100 gallons per person per day.
  • Bedrooms: Assume 2 people per bedroom.

For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would require approximately 600 gallons per day, indicating that a system capable of holding at least 18,000 gallons is advisable for monthly refills. However, for properties in rural areas with challenging winter access, larger systems holding 36,000-54,000 gallons or more may be preferable to minimize refills during the snowy months.

It's essential to keep in mind that factors like rocky terrain can increase costs by 20-40%. Thus, planning for the higher end of the cost spectrum is advisable. Understanding these nuances will help you navigate the investment in a cistern effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $40,000 to $55,000 for a 10,000-gallon system. This price includes the tank, excavation, pump system, and necessary plumbing connections, which can vary based on specific site conditions.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on daily water usage and how often you plan to refill it. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants may need a minimum of a 10,000-gallon tank to ensure sufficient water supply, especially during winter months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on factors such as weather conditions and site accessibility. Our team conducts a thorough site assessment to ensure that the installation proceeds smoothly.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are often recommended when wells are impractical or too deep, providing a reliable backup water supply while also accommodating properties in areas with challenging soil and water table issues.
