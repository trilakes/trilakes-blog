Local Challenges in Fremont County

Fremont County presents a variety of challenges when it comes to water storage solutions. The soil here primarily consists of rocky granite and clay in the valleys, complicating excavation and installation processes. Additionally, the water table is often deep, which makes relying on a well impractical for many residents. Many properties require water hauling to meet their daily needs, making a potable water storage tank a smart investment. The frost line in this region ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating careful planning for winter installations. With heavy snowfall and extreme temperature fluctuations, ensuring your cistern is properly winterized is essential to prevent freezing and maintain functionality throughout the year.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Penrose

Understanding the cost of cistern installation is essential for budgeting effectively. The total cost will vary based on several factors, including the size of the system, excavation challenges, and any additional installation requirements. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an estimated water usage of 600 gallons per day would ideally need a cistern that holds at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, translating to an installation cost ranging from $40,000 to $55,000.

Here are some key factors affecting costs:

  • Tank Size: Our preferred Norwesco tanks range from 5,000 gallons ($13,000 to $15,000) to 10,000 gallons ($22,000 to $28,000). For larger systems, multiple tanks can be connected.
  • Excavation Costs: Excavation can vary widely from $3,000 to $8,000+, especially in rocky or hard-to-reach locations.
  • Additional Components: Installation of pump systems, trenching, electrical work, and any required water treatment systems can add $2,500 to $5,000 or more to the total.

In summary, the total installed cost of a basic 5,000-gallon system generally starts at around $25,000 and can go up to $35,000, while larger systems can significantly increase that figure depending on specific site conditions and requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size of the tank, excavation conditions, and additional components needed for installation.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: Your needed tank size is based on your daily water usage, which is approximately 100 gallons per person per day, multiplied by the number of bedrooms in your home (assuming 2 people per bedroom).

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation timeline can vary but generally takes several days to a few weeks, depending on site conditions and the specific requirements of your installation.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on several factors, including water table depth and availability. Cisterns provide a reliable alternative for properties where wells are impractical due to deep water tables or challenging soil conditions.
