Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Minturn

The cost of cistern installation in Minturn can vary significantly based on several factors, including site conditions and system size. Generally, the starting costs for a 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank range from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000-gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. To determine the appropriate tank size, consider your household's daily water usage, which is typically calculated at 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with an estimated occupancy of six people would require around 600 gallons per day, translating to a minimum tank size of 36,000-54,000 gallons if refilled every 2-3 months.

Additional costs can also add up quickly, especially in challenging terrain. Excavation can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rockiness of the ground and access to your property. When considering the entire installed system, including pumps and trenching, you might expect to reach a total of $75,000 to $120,000 for larger systems of 30,000+ gallons. Given these factors, it's wise to consult with a professional like Tri-Lakes to get an accurate estimate tailored to your specific needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern in Colorado can vary widely based on size and installation factors, typically ranging from $25,000 to $120,000. A 5,000-gallon cistern installation generally starts at $25,000, while larger systems can exceed $75,000, especially in rocky terrain.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: To determine the appropriate size for your underground water storage tank, you should calculate daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For instance, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would ideally need at least 36,000-54,000 gallons if refilled every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on site conditions and the complexity of the installation. Factors such as soil composition and accessibility can affect the timeline.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are often a better option for properties with a deep water table or unreliable well sources, providing a consistent supply of water when properly sized.
