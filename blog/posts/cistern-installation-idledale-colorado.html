Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Idledale's unique terrain presents specific challenges when it comes to cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil prevalent in the area can complicate excavation, often requiring an engineered approach to ensure stability and functionality. Additionally, the water table in Jefferson County tends to be deep, making a water cistern a necessary alternative for many homes that do not have access to a well.

The harsh winters, with frost lines reaching 36-48 inches, further complicate the installation process. To mitigate freezing issues, it is essential to install a frost-proof cistern, ensuring that your system remains operational even during the coldest months. Furthermore, steep driveways and limited winter access can hinder construction efforts, making it crucial to work with a local expert that understands the specifics of mountain property water logistics.

Our Cistern Installation Process

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Idledale

The cost of cistern installation in Idledale can vary widely and is influenced by several factors, including the size of the cistern, site conditions, and any additional features needed for your setup. For example, the general sizing formula we use is based on your home's bedroom count, with the assumption of two people per bedroom using approximately 100 gallons per day. This means a 3-bedroom home would need around 600 gallons per day, translating to a minimum tank size of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Typical costs for a Norwesco underground water storage tank are:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

In addition to the tank, homeowners should consider excavation costs, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rocky terrain and access issues. Other expenses may include pump systems ($2,500-$5,000), trenching to the house ($25-$50 per linear foot), and electrical hookups ($1,000-$2,500). The total installed cost for a basic system usually starts from around $25,000 and can go up to $120,000 for larger setups.

Understanding these cost factors helps in budgeting effectively for your water storage solutions. It‚Äôs essential to remember that investing in a larger tank can lead to fewer refills and long-term savings, especially in challenging winter conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado can range significantly depending on size and site conditions. For example, a 5,000-gallon system typically starts from $25,000-$35,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may cost between $40,000 and $55,000.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank should be based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count and daily water consumption. For instance, a 3-bedroom home would typically require a minimum of 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, which can be calculated as 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The duration of cistern installation can vary based on site conditions and weather. Typically, a project can take a few days to a couple of weeks, depending on the complexity of the installation and whether winter conditions apply.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on site conditions and water availability. Cisterns are a great alternative where wells are impractical, too deep, or unreliable, providing a reliable backup water supply while also allowing for larger storage options.
