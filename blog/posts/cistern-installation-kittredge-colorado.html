Local Challenges in Jefferson County

Kittredge's location in Jefferson County presents unique challenges for cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate excavation, often requiring specialized equipment and techniques to ensure proper burial of your buried water tank. Furthermore, with the water table often lying deep beneath the surface, many properties are unable to rely on well water, making an underground cistern an essential alternative.

Additional challenges arise from the area's steep driveways and limited winter access, which can make installing systems difficult during the colder months. The frost line reaching depths of 36-48 inches means your water storage solutions must be designed with frost protection in mind. Understanding these local conditions is critical for a successful installation that can withstand the elements and deliver consistent water supply for your residential or agricultural needs.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Kittredge

When considering cistern installation in Kittredge, it‚Äôs crucial to understand the various cost factors involved. The overall pricing is influenced by tank size, excavation challenges, and additional equipment. Cisterns are typically sized based on daily usage, calculated as follows: the number of bedrooms multiplied by 2 people per bedroom, multiplied by 100 gallons per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 occupants would need approximately 600 gallons per day.

Refill frequency also plays a significant role in determining the size of your water storage tank. If you prefer to refill every 2-3 months, you might require a tank capacity of 36,000-54,000 gallons or more to ensure sufficient supply during winter months.

Here are some approximate costs for Norwesco tanks:

  • 5,000-gallon tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000-gallon tank: $22,000-$28,000

In addition to the tank, other costs include:

  • Excavation: $3,000-$8,000+
  • Pump system: $2,500-$5,000
  • Trenching: $25-$50 per linear foot
  • Electrical hookup: $1,000-$2,500

Therefore, the total installed system costs can range significantly, with a 5,000-gallon system starting from around $25,000 and potentially reaching up to $35,000 or more based on site conditions. It‚Äôs important to note that rocky terrain can add 20-40% to excavation costs, making it advisable to budget for the high end of these ranges.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of installing a cistern in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on tank size and site conditions. For a 5,000-gallon system, costs can start around $25,000, while a 10,000-gallon system may reach $40,000 or more.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the tank you need is determined by your daily water usage, which is generally calculated as 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home with 6 residents would require a tank capacity of at least 36,000 gallons to refill every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation process for a cistern can take several weeks, depending on factors like weather conditions and site accessibility. Our thorough approach ensures that every aspect, from excavation to testing, is completed accurately and efficiently.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs local water table and access to reliable water sources. Cisterns are often preferred in areas where well water is too deep or unreliable, providing a consistent backup water supply.
