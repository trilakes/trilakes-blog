Why Franktown Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the stunning Franktown area of Douglas County, Colorado, offers breathtaking views and a unique lifestyle. However, with these mountains comes the responsibility of maintaining an efficient and compliant septic system. Proper septic installation is critical, especially at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet, where unique soil compositions and challenging terrain can complicate the process. Hiring a licensed and insured septic installer near me ensures that your septic tank is installed correctly, adhering to local regulations set by Regulation 43, while also considering the climatic challenges of the region. This professional approach not only protects your investment but also safeguards the environment, ensuring that onsite wastewater is effectively treated before it re-enters the ecosystem.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

Franktown's mountain terrain presents several challenges when it comes to septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils often necessitate the use of engineered systems, as traditional systems may not function properly in these conditions. With a water table that is frequently deep, many properties require additional water solutions like cisterns or water hauling. The frost line, extending 36-48 inches deep, complicates septic installation even further, especially during the winter months. Additionally, steep driveways and limited access can hinder the installation process, making it crucial to work with experienced professionals who understand the local landscape. Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive and efficient process for septic installation. Initially, we conduct thorough soil testing to evaluate the land's suitability for a septic system. This involves using a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each eight feet deep, from which we collect soil samples and send them to a licensed engineer for analysis. The resulting soils report determines the best type of system for your property, whether it be a conventional gravity system‚Äîrare in mountain areas‚Äîor an engineered system designed for the unique conditions of Franktown.

Once the soils report is complete, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the Douglas County Building Department. With permits in hand, installation typically occurs during the building season from May to October. Our skilled team will then excavate the site, install the septic tank, and create the required leach field or drain field as dictated by the soils report. We also ensure that your system is winterized for freeze protection, which is critical in our extreme climate. Finally, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to guarantee everything is functioning correctly before leaving the site.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Franktown

The cost of septic installation in Franktown varies based on several factors, including soil conditions, terrain, and access. On average, the total installed cost for basic engineered systems starts at around $35,000, while more complex systems, such as sand filter systems, can range up to $65,000. If your property requires an Advanced Treatment Unit (ATU), costs can escalate to $80,000 or more. It's essential to consider that rocky or difficult terrain typically adds an additional $10,000-$25,000 to the total expense.

For properties with a need for cisterns, the tank size should be calculated based on the number of bedrooms in the home; typically, you should plan for 2 people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. The costs for soil testing and engineering typically range from $2,000-$4,000, and permits can vary from $500 to $2,000 depending on the specific requirements set by Douglas County. Our team is here to help you navigate these costs and ensure you receive a system that meets your needs and complies with local regulations.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can vary widely, but you can expect to pay between $35,000 to $80,000 depending on the type of system required, soil conditions, and any additional site challenges. Engineered systems, which are common in mountain areas like Franktown, will generally fall on the higher end of this range.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation process typically takes about 1 to 2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. Factors such as weather and accessibility can also influence the timeline.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Franktown will require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations under Regulation 43. These systems are designed to function effectively in rocky and clay-rich soils, making them essential for proper onsite wastewater treatment.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. These soil tests involve digging deep holes to analyze soil composition and determine the most suitable septic system for your property, a crucial step before installation.
