Local Challenges in Gunnison County

Gunnison County's unique topography presents several challenges for septic system installation. The rocky granite and clay soils found at high elevations require specialized engineered septic systems to ensure proper function. Many properties are affected by deep water tables, often necessitating cisterns or water hauling, which adds another layer of complexity to your wastewater treatment needs.

Moreover, the frost line in this region typically ranges from 36 to 48 inches, demanding careful planning and execution to prevent freeze damage. The limited building season from May to October means that timely installation is critical. Steep driveways and limited winter access can also hinder construction efforts, making it essential to work with a contractor familiar with these challenges. Finally, the extreme weather, including heavy snow loads and afternoon thunderstorms, calls for durable solutions that protect your investment.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive, four-step process for septic installation:

  1. Site Evaluation: Our team begins by conducting a thorough site evaluation, including soil testing. We utilize a mini excavator to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep, to gather soil samples for an engineered report.
  2. Design and Permitting: Based on the soils report, we design an appropriate septic system tailored to your property. We assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit through the Gunnison County Building Department, ensuring compliance with Regulation 43.
  3. Installation: Our experienced crew will handle all aspects of your installation, from excavation to system setup. We prioritize implementing features like leach fields and drain fields that are compatible with the local soil conditions.
  4. Final Inspection: After installation, we ensure the system passes all inspections required by the county, guaranteeing that your septic system is fully operational and compliant.

This process typically spans several weeks, depending on weather conditions and the site‚Äôs unique challenges. By adhering to this systematic approach, we ensure that your system is not only compliant but also built to last.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Gunnison

The cost of septic installation in Gunnison County can vary widely based on several factors. Generally, you can expect to pay between $35,000 and $50,000 for a basic engineered system, with more complex designs such as sand filter systems ranging from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units (ATU) costing between $55,000 and $80,000 or more.

Key factors influencing the overall cost include:

  • Soil Conditions: Rocky or difficult terrain can add $10,000 to $25,000 to your project.
  • Site Access: Steep driveways and limited access points can complicate installation and increase labor costs.
  • System Type: The type of system recommended based on the soils report will heavily influence the budget. Engineered solutions are often required due to challenging conditions.

When planning for water needs, remember that the size of your cistern is based on your bedroom count, calculated at 2 people per bedroom multiplying by 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would typically require a cistern with a capacity of 600 gallons.

Understanding these costs upfront helps you budget effectively for your septic installation project and ensures your system will function optimally for decades to come.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can range from $35,000 to $80,000, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. Basic engineered systems start around $35,000, while advanced treatment units can exceed $80,000, especially in challenging terrain.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation can vary but typically spans several weeks. Factors such as weather conditions, permitting processes, and site accessibility play crucial roles in determining the exact duration.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Gunnison County require an engineered septic system due to challenging soil conditions. A soils report will determine the best solution tailored to your site's unique characteristics.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil composition, which helps determine the appropriate septic system design for your property.
