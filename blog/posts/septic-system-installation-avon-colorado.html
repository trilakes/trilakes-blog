Local Challenges in Eagle County

Avon‚Äôs mountainous terrain and high elevation present several challenges for septic installation. The rocky granite and clay soils can complicate the installation of traditional septic systems. In many cases, properties require engineered systems due to these challenging soil conditions, which are a direct result of the area's unique geology. Additionally, the water table tends to be deep, necessitating the use of cisterns or water hauling for many homes.

The frost line in Avon typically reaches depths of 36-48 inches, which means that any septic tank or system must be installed below this line to prevent freezing during the harsh winter months. This consideration is vital to ensure longevity and functionality. Moreover, steep driveways and limited winter access make it essential to plan your installation carefully to avoid delays and complications.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a systematic approach to ensure that your septic system installation is seamless and efficient. Here‚Äôs how our process works:

  1. Consultation and Site Assessment: We start with an initial consultation to understand your specific needs and evaluate your property‚Äôs unique challenges.
  2. Soils Testing: Before installation, we conduct thorough soils testing to determine the best system for your property. This involves digging two test holes to a depth of eight feet and sending samples to a licensed engineer for analysis.
  3. Septic Design: Based on the soils report, our team will design an engineered septic system that meets both your needs and Regulation 43 standards.
  4. Permitting: We handle all necessary permits through the Eagle County Building Department, ensuring compliance with local regulations.

  6. Final Inspection: Once installation is complete, we conduct a final inspection to ensure everything meets the necessary standards.

This entire process typically takes several weeks, depending on weather conditions and site accessibility. Preparing for your septic installation during the building season, which usually runs from May to October, is advised to avoid winter complications.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Avon

When considering the cost of septic installation in Avon, several factors come into play. The total cost is influenced by soil conditions, the type of system installed, and site-specific challenges. For an engineered system, costs typically start from $35,000-$50,000, while a sand filter system may range from $45,000-$65,000, and advanced treatment units (ATUs) often fall between $55,000-$80,000+. Keep in mind that rocky terrain can add an additional $10,000-$25,000 to these estimates.

If your property requires a cistern, remember that the size is determined by the bedroom count (2 people per bedroom x 100 gallons/day). For example, a 3-bedroom home would need a cistern capable of holding at least 600 gallons to meet daily needs.

The costs for essential elements break down as follows:

  • Soils test + engineering: $2,000-$4,000
  • Permits (varies by county): $500-$2,000
  • Excavation for system: $8,000-$20,000+
  • Tank + components: $5,000-$15,000
  • ATU unit (if needed): $8,000-$15,000
  • Electrical/controls: $2,000-$4,000

While costs can vary significantly based on site conditions, planning and budgeting for potential challenges will help ensure a successful installation.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado can range significantly based on system type and site conditions. For engineered systems, the typical range is $35,000-$50,000, while sand filter systems may run $45,000-$65,000. Advanced treatment units (ATUs) can range from $55,000-$80,000+, especially in challenging terrains.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The timeline for septic installation varies depending on several factors, including weather conditions and site accessibility. Generally, the process can take several weeks, particularly in mountainous areas where access may be limited.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Avon require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions and local regulations. These systems are designed to handle specific site conditions effectively, ensuring optimal performance and longevity.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we conduct soils tests instead of traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to determine soil composition and drainage capabilities, which are crucial for designing an effective septic system.
