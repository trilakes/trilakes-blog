Why Falcon Properties Need Professional Custom Home Construction

Building a custom home in Falcon, Colorado, means embracing the majestic mountain views and unique terrain at elevations over 8,000 feet. However, this picturesque setting comes with specific challenges that make professional expertise essential. The rocky granite and clay soils, combined with the steep driveways and limited access during winter months, require a skilled custom home builder who understands the intricacies of mountain home construction. A licensed and insured general contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting will help navigate local regulations, secure necessary permits, and ensure your home is designed to withstand extreme weather conditions.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

El Paso County presents multiple challenges for home construction, particularly in Falcon where the elevation can reach 10,000 feet. The soil composition, often rocky granite or decomposed granite, necessitates careful foundation engineering to ensure stability. Furthermore, the water table can be quite deep, often requiring property owners to invest in cisterns for water supply, particularly when planning for new construction. During winter, the area experiences heavy snowfall and freezing temperatures, meaning that proper winterization techniques are vital for a durable custom home. Understanding the setback requirements and fire mitigation strategies in forested areas is also crucial to ensure safety and compliance with local regulations.

Our Custom Home Construction Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a comprehensive 11-step process for custom home construction to ensure your project runs smoothly. First, we conduct a pre-purchase build feasibility report to evaluate the land's suitability for your dream home. This includes assessing soil conditions, utility availability, and potential water flow issues.

Once you've secured a suitable lot, the next steps involve obtaining a well permit or planning for a cistern if water is scarce. Our team then develops a professional site plan, detailing the house footprint, utility routes, and driveway access. Following this, we create architectural plans tailored to your vision, either using your architect or one from our trusted network, including options from ArchitecturalDesigns.com.

Next, we prepare engineered foundation and framing plans that adhere to Colorado's stringent building codes. With these plans in hand, we facilitate the land survey and septic system design if needed, before compiling everything required for a building permit submission through the El Paso County Building Department.

Finally, we initiate the construction phase, which includes a detailed timeline and careful oversight of each construction stage, from site preparation to final inspection. Our goal is to provide a seamless experience, making your custom build a reality.

Frequently Asked Questions About Custom Home Construction

Q: How much does it cost to build a custom home in Colorado?

A: The cost to build a custom home in Colorado varies based on several factors. For basic builds, expect to pay around $350-$450 per square foot, while standard builds can range from $450-$550 per square foot. Custom and luxury homes typically start at $550 per square foot and can exceed $1,000 per square foot for high-end builds.

Q: How long does it take to build a house?

A: The timeline for building a custom home typically ranges from 10 to 18 months, depending on various factors such as the complexity of the design and local weather conditions. Pre-construction planning can take an additional 2-6 months, making it crucial to account for these timelines during your planning.

Q: What should I look for in a custom home builder?

A: When searching for a custom home builder, consider their experience, reputation, and whether they are licensed and insured. Look for a builder who is familiar with local building codes and has a proven track record of successfully navigating the unique challenges of mountain home construction.

Q: Should I get a feasibility study before buying land?

A: Yes, obtaining a feasibility study before buying land is crucial. This helps assess soil conditions, water availability, and overall buildability, ensuring that you don't purchase property that poses significant challenges or costs.
