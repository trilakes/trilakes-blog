Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we follow a systematic approach to water storage tank installation that guarantees safety, efficiency, and satisfaction. Here‚Äôs a step-by-step breakdown of our process:

  1. Consultation and Site Assessment: We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your specific needs and assess your property. Factors like daily water usage and refill frequency will guide our recommendations for tank size.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Bailey

Understanding the costs associated with cistern installation is essential for homeowners in Bailey. The price can vary significantly based on several factors, including excavation difficulty, tank size, and the specific requirements of your property.

For instance, to determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank, we use a straightforward formula based on your home's bedroom count. Each bedroom typically accommodates two people, with an average of 100 gallons of water usage per person per day. Therefore, a 3-bedroom home with six occupants would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day. If you choose to refill every two months, you would need a tank capacity of at least 36,000 gallons, emphasizing the importance of planning.

Our pricing for tanks includes:

  • Norwesco 5,000 gallon tank: starting at $13,000-$15,000
  • Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank: starting at $22,000-$28,000
  • Total installed systems can range from $25,000 to $120,000+ depending on size and site conditions.

Keep in mind that rocky terrain can increase excavation costs by 20-40%, while winter installations may also incur additional expenses. A larger capacity tank not only provides long-term savings on water hauling costs but also reduces the frequency of refills, which is critical in areas with limited access during winter months.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost for a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to over $120,000, depending on the size of the tank and specific site conditions. For example, a Norwesco 10,000 gallon tank can cost between $22,000 and $28,000, plus excavation and installation costs.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank is determined by daily water usage and how often you plan to refill it. For instance, a 3-bedroom home might require a tank with a capacity of 36,000 gallons if refilled every two months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern can take several weeks, depending on site conditions and weather. Factors such as rocky terrain and permitting processes can influence the timeline, but we strive for efficiency without compromising quality.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well largely depends on your property‚Äôs specific conditions. Wells may not be feasible if the water table is deep or the soil is unsuitable for drilling. In such cases, a cistern installation can be a better option, providing reliable access to water.
