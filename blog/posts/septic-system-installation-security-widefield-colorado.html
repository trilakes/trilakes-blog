Why Security-Widefield Properties Need Professional Septic System Installation

Living in the scenic mountain community of Security-Widefield, El Paso County, presents unique challenges when it comes to septic installation. With elevations soaring above 8,000 feet and rocky terrain, it is crucial to work with a licensed and insured septic installer near me to ensure your septic system is designed, installed, and maintained correctly. An improperly installed septic system can lead to costly repairs, environmental hazards, and potential legal issues. At Tri-Lakes Contracting, we understand the complexities of your local soil types and the specific requirements outlined in Colorado's Regulation 43, which governs all onsite wastewater systems (OWTS).

Local Challenges in El Paso County

Security-Widefield's unique geographical features create several challenges for septic system installation. The region is characterized by rocky granite and clay soils, which often necessitate engineered systems due to their poor drainage capabilities. Additionally, the water table in this area is generally deep, making many properties reliant on cisterns or regular water hauling. The frost line reaches depths of 36-48 inches, creating further complications during installation, especially in the winter months. The extreme temperature swings and heavy snow loads require thoughtful winter preparation tips to protect your system from freezing. Limited winter access due to steep driveways can also delay installation, making it essential to plan ahead.

Our Septic System Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our septic installation process is thorough and efficient, ensuring that your system meets all local regulations and your property‚Äôs needs. The first step involves soil testing, where we bring a mini excavator to your site to dig two test holes, each 8 feet deep. This allows us to collect soil samples from various depths and send them to a licensed engineer for analysis. The resulting soils report will dictate the type of system required for your property.

Once we have the report, we assist you in obtaining the necessary septic permit from the El Paso County Building Department. This permit is crucial as it complies with Colorado's OWTS regulations under Regulation 43. The installation process usually takes one to two weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and site conditions. Our team will excavate the site, install the septic tank, and set up the drain field as per the engineered designs. After installation, we conduct a thorough septic inspection to ensure everything is functioning as intended.

Cost Factors for Septic System Installation in Security-Widefield

The cost of septic installation in Security-Widefield can vary significantly based on site conditions and the type of system required. Typically, you can expect to invest starting from $35,000 for basic engineered systems, with sand filter systems ranging from $45,000 to $65,000, and advanced treatment units (ATU) costing between $55,000 and $80,000+. Factors that can increase these costs include rocky terrain, difficult access, and specific site requirements. For instance, if your property encounters substantial rock, you may need to budget an additional $10,000 to $25,000 for excavation.

When planning for a water supply, remember that the size of your cistern should be based on bedroom count, calculated at two people per bedroom at 100 gallons per day. For example, a three-bedroom home would require a cistern capable of holding at least 600 gallons. This consideration is vital to ensure your onsite wastewater system operates effectively, especially in the winter months when water levels may fluctuate.

Frequently Asked Questions About Septic System Installation

Q: How much does septic installation cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of septic installation in Colorado typically starts from $35,000 for basic engineered systems. More complex systems, such as sand filters and advanced treatment units, can range from $45,000 to $80,000+, depending on site conditions and specific requirements.

Q: How long does septic installation take?

A: The installation of a septic system usually takes one to two weeks, depending on the complexity of the system and the site conditions. Delays may occur due to the need for additional excavation or obtaining the necessary permits from local authorities.

Q: Do I need an engineered septic system?

A: Most properties in Security-Widefield require engineered septic systems due to challenging soil conditions. These systems are designed to comply with Colorado's Regulation 43, ensuring that your septic system functions efficiently and meets safety standards.

Q: What is a perc test?

A: In Colorado, we perform soil tests rather than traditional perc tests. This involves digging test holes to analyze soil drainage capabilities, which helps determine the appropriate type of septic system for your property.
