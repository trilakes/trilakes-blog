Why Cimarron Hills Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

Living in the scenic Cimarron Hills, a mountain community in El Paso County, presents unique challenges, particularly when it comes to water supply. With an elevation exceeding 8,000 feet, many properties require cistern installation to ensure reliable access to water. Whether you are constructing a new home or looking to optimize your existing water supply, professional installation from a licensed contractor like Tri-Lakes Contracting is essential. Our expertise in underground water storage tanks guarantees compliance with local regulations and the proper mitigation of terrain-related challenges, providing peace of mind and long-term sustainability.

Local Challenges in El Paso County

The stunning but rugged terrain of Cimarron Hills poses several challenges for homeowners considering cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, often requiring engineered systems to navigate the depth of the water table, which is typically much deeper in this area. Furthermore, the frost line here ranges from 36 to 48 inches, necessitating proper winterization strategies to prevent freezing. Weather conditions can also be extreme, with heavy snow loads and turbulent afternoon thunderstorms, making access to properties particularly difficult during winter months. Many homeowners find that these local challenges make a water cistern an ideal solution for a reliable and consistent water supply.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Cimarron Hills

The total cost for water storage tank installation can vary widely based on several factors. For instance, a 5,000 gallon underground water storage tank typically ranges from $25,000 to $35,000, while a 10,000 gallon tank can cost between $40,000 and $55,000. To determine the appropriate tank size, you'll want to calculate your daily water usage based on the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home would require approximately 600 gallons per day, meaning you would need a tank capacity of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills or up to 72,000 gallons to last through the winter.

Additional costs may arise from excavation, which can range from $3,000 to $8,000 depending on the rocky terrain and access difficulties. Other expenses include pump systems, trenching, electrical hookups, and water filtration systems, contributing to the overall investment in your cistern installation. Given these considerations, it‚Äôs essential to account for potential increases due to challenging conditions, ensuring you budget accordingly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern in Colorado can range significantly, with a 5,000 gallon tank typically costing between $25,000 and $35,000 and a 10,000 gallon tank costing between $40,000 and $55,000. Additional costs for excavation, plumbing, and other necessary installations can add several thousand dollars to the total project cost.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your household's water usage. A general rule is to calculate 100 gallons per person per day. For example, a 3-bedroom home (estimated occupancy of 6 people) would require a minimum of 600 gallons per day, necessitating a tank of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The installation of a cistern typically takes between 2 to 4 weeks from initial consultation to final setup. This timeline may vary based on site conditions, weather, and the necessary excavation work involved.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well often depends on individual circumstances. Cisterns are an excellent alternative for properties where wells are impractical due to depth or reliability issues. They offer flexibility for water storage and can be tailored to meet the specific needs of your mountain property.
