Why Como Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For residents of Como, Colorado, nestled at an elevation of 9,800 feet, having a reliable water source is crucial, especially given the remote nature of this historic railroad town. Many properties in this area lack direct access to municipal water supplies, making cistern installation a viable solution for both potable and agricultural needs. A professionally installed underground water storage tank not only ensures a consistent supply of water but also safeguards against harsh winter conditions. Proper installation is essential to avoid issues like freezing or water contamination, especially with Como's extreme temperature fluctuations and limited winter access.

Local Challenges in Park County

Como's mountainous terrain presents unique challenges that influence cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soil can complicate excavation, often requiring engineered septic systems and adding to installation costs. With an average frost line of 36-48 inches at this elevation, it's vital to consider freeze protection during installation. Additionally, the water table can be quite deep, necessitating water hauling for many residents who rely on underground cisterns as a well alternative. Access to properties can be limited, especially in winter months, making larger capacity water storage tanks a practical choice to minimize the need for frequent refills.

Our Cistern Installation Process

At Tri-Lakes Contracting, our cistern installation process is thorough and designed to meet the unique needs of Como residents. We begin with an initial consultation to assess your property's specific water requirements, taking into account your daily usage and refill preferences. Once we determine the appropriate size for your potable water storage tank, we handle all necessary permits through the Park County Building Department. Our team will then excavate the site, which may take longer if rocky terrain is encountered. After digging, we install the tank, connecting it to your home and ensuring that all systems are in place for efficient operation.

We also provide guidance on winterizing your system to prepare for Como's harsh winters, ensuring ongoing functionality and protection against the elements.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically starts from $40,000 for a 10,000-gallon system and can go up to $120,000 for larger capacities, depending on site conditions and additional features required.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of your underground water storage tank depends on your daily water usage, calculated by the number of bedrooms in your home. For example, a 3-bedroom home might need a cistern that holds 36,000-54,000 gallons to accommodate refills every 2-3 months.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: Cistern installation generally takes 2-4 weeks, depending on site conditions and excavation challenges. Weather can also impact the timeline, especially in winter when access may be limited.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: The choice between a cistern and a well depends on your property's specific conditions. Cisterns are often a better option for properties with deep water tables or challenging soil, providing a reliable alternative for rural water supply.
