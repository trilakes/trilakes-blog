Why Parker Properties Need Professional Cistern Installation

For homeowners in Parker, Douglas County, cistern installation is an essential consideration, especially for properties at elevations exceeding 8,000 feet. With the harsh winters and the challenges of maintaining a reliable water supply, opting for a professional cistern installer can ensure that your underground water storage tank is installed correctly, efficiently, and in compliance with local regulations. A professional installation minimizes the risks associated with improper setup, which can lead to leaks, contamination, or costly repairs. By choosing Tri-Lakes Contracting, you gain peace of mind knowing that your potable water storage tank will be reliable and durable, essential for your mountain lifestyle.

Local Challenges in Douglas County

The unique terrain of Parker presents several challenges that affect cistern installation. The rocky granite and clay soils common in the area can complicate excavation, often leading to increased costs and extended timelines. Moreover, many properties have a deep water table, making wells impractical and necessitating effective water storage solutions like underground cisterns. With frost lines extending 36-48 inches deep, ensuring your buried water tank is adequately insulated and winterized is critical to prevent freezing. Additionally, steep driveways and limited winter access can make installation more difficult, emphasizing the need for an experienced cistern company that understands these local conditions.

Cost Factors for Cistern Installation in Parker

Understanding the cost of cistern installation is vital for budgeting your project. The total cost will depend on various factors, including the size of the tank, excavation needs, and site conditions. Generally, a typical formula for sizing your underground water storage tank is based on your home‚Äôs bedroom count. For example, if you have a three-bedroom home, you can expect to use around 600 gallons per day (3 bedrooms x 2 people/bedroom x 100 gallons/day).

For a comprehensive system, here are typical cost estimates:

  • 5,000-gallon Norwesco tank: $13,000-$15,000
  • 10,000-gallon Norwesco tank: $22,000-$28,000
  • Total installed costs for a 5,000-gallon system: $25,000-$35,000
  • Total installed costs for a 10,000-gallon system: $40,000-$55,000

Factors that can increase costs include rocky terrain (which can add 20-40% to excavation costs), remote access challenges, and additional features like filtration systems. Always consider that a larger upfront investment could lead to fewer refills and long-term savings in water hauling costs, especially given that typical delivery rates range from $200-$350 for 3,000 gallons.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cistern Installation

Q: How much does a cistern cost in Colorado?

A: The cost of a cistern installation in Colorado typically ranges from $25,000 to $55,000, depending on the size of the underground water storage tank and the complexities of the installation site. For instance, larger systems, such as a 10,000-gallon tank, can cost between $40,000 and $55,000 when fully installed.

Q: What size underground water storage tank do I need?

A: The size of the cistern you need is determined by your household's daily water usage and your preferred refill frequency. For example, a three-bedroom home, which averages about 600 gallons per day, would typically require a cistern of at least 18,000 gallons for monthly refills, or larger for less frequent refills.

Q: How long does cistern installation take?

A: The timeline for cistern installation can vary but generally takes several weeks. Factors such as site evaluation, permitting, excavation, and weather conditions all influence the total installation time.

Q: Cistern vs well - which is better?

A: Choosing between a cistern and a well depends on site conditions. In many areas of Parker, wells may be impractical due to deep water tables, making cisterns a reliable alternative. Cisterns are especially beneficial for off-grid water systems, providing consistent water storage without the need for drilling.
