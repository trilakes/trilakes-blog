About Vollmer Road Area

The Vollmer Road Area is a charming and picturesque neighborhood located in the northern part of El Paso County, just a stone's throw from Black Forest, Colorado. This serene enclave offers a perfect blend of rural tranquility and close-knit community spirit, making it an ideal spot for families, retirees, and nature enthusiasts alike. What sets this area apart is its stunning natural surroundings, featuring sprawling pine forests, open meadows, and breathtaking views of the Rocky Mountains.

Homes in the Vollmer Road Area typically consist of single-family residences, ranging from cozy cabins to sprawling custom builds, often set on large lots that provide ample space for outdoor activities. The appeal of this neighborhood lies not only in its natural beauty but also in its proximity to outdoor recreation, including hiking, biking, and horseback riding, making it a haven for those who appreciate an active lifestyle. Additionally, the local community hosts various events throughout the year, fostering a sense of belonging and camaraderie among residents.

Water & Utilities

Homeowners typically drill private wells, which can provide a reliable source of water if properly maintained. Septic Considerations

The Vollmer Road Area does not have access to a municipal sewer system, so homeowners will need to install septic systems for waste disposal. The most common types of septic systems found in the area are conventional septic tanks and alternative systems for properties with challenging terrains. Understanding the soil and terrain is crucial, as the area features various landscapes that can affect the type of septic system that is appropriate for your property.

When planning on building or buying, it's essential to have a percolation test done to determine the soil's ability to absorb effluent, which will also help in designing an adequate septic system. Additionally, ensure that any existing septic systems comply with local regulations, as improper systems can lead to environmental issues and costly repairs down the line.
