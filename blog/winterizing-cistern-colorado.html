Why Maintenance Matters

Proper maintenance of your cistern is essential, especially in Colorado where winter temperatures can plunge to extreme lows. Regular maintenance extends the life of your system, ensuring that it functions optimally through the cold months. A well-maintained cistern can prevent costly repairs caused by freezing, leaks, or other issues that may arise when temperatures drop. By investing time in maintenance, you can have peace of mind knowing that your water supply will remain reliable and safe throughout the winter season. Additionally, a proactive maintenance routine can help you detect potential problems early, allowing you to address them before they escalate into more significant and expensive issues.

Regular Maintenance Schedule

Establishing a clear maintenance schedule can help ensure that your cistern remains in peak condition, especially as winter approaches. Here's a suggested maintenance schedule:

  • Weekly Tasks:
    • Check the water levels in your cistern to ensure they are adequate.
    • Inspect the filters and screens for debris or clogs, cleaning them as necessary.
    • Monitor the surrounding area for signs of leaks or water pooling.
  • Monthly Tasks:
    • Inspect the cisternâ€™s exterior for cracks or damage and seal any openings.
    • Test the water quality for contaminants, ensuring it remains safe for use.
    • Check all valves and connections for signs of wear or leaks.
  • Seasonal Tasks:
    • Before winter, insulate all exposed pipes to prevent freezing.
    • Flush the cistern to remove sediment that can accumulate over time.
    • Review your emergency procedures for water supply disruptions.
  • Annual Tasks:
    • Schedule a professional inspection of the cistern and its components.
    • Service the pump and check electrical connections for safety.
    • Replace any aging parts that may lead to future issues.

Signs of Problems

Being aware of the warning signs can help you catch issues before they become serious. Look out for the following:

  • Unusual noises coming from the cistern or pump, such as grinding or rattling.
  • Changes in water pressure, which may indicate blockages or leaks.
  • Water discoloration or unusual odors, which can signal contamination.
  • Visible cracks or bulges in the cisternâ€™s structure, suggesting structural damage.
  • Increased water bills which may indicate leaks or overconsumption.
