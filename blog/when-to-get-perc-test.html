When to Get a Perc Test Before Buying Land

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

It's essential to get a perc test before buying land if you plan to build a home or install a septic system. Ideally, you should conduct the test during the due diligence phase of your land purchase, as it determines soil suitability for wastewater disposal.

Detailed Explanation

A percolation test, or perc test, measures how well soil absorbs water, which is critical for designing septic systems. In Colorado, where many properties are not connected to municipal sewer systems, knowing the soil's percolation rate can significantly impact your building plans. Here are key points to consider:

  • When to Test: Conduct the perc test after your offer is accepted but before finalizing the purchase. This timing is crucial to ensure that the property can support a septic system.
  • Soil Variability: Soil types can vary widely even within small areas. A perc test can uncover hidden issues that might not be apparent during a visual inspection.
  • Permitting Process: In Colorado, obtaining permits for septic systems requires perc test results. Without them, you may face delays or complications in the building process.
  • Financing Considerations: Lenders may require perc test results before approving a loan for land that will be developed.

Acting too early (such as testing before your offer is accepted) can lead to wasted time and money if your offer isn't accepted. Conversely, waiting too long may result in losing the property, especially in competitive markets where multiple buyers are interested.

What This Means for Your Project

Understanding your land's septic potential through a perc test can save you from significant headaches down the line. If the perc test results are unfavorable, you may need to explore alternative waste disposal options or reconsider your purchase entirely. It's advisable to work with a qualified soil scientist or engineer to interpret the results and guide you through the next steps in the permitting process.
```
