About Forest Lakes

Forest Lakes is a picturesque neighborhood located just south of Monument, Colorado, offering residents a serene environment amidst stunning landscapes. Nestled in the foothills of the Rocky Mountains, this area features a harmonious blend of natural beauty and modern living. Its appeal lies in its abundant outdoor recreational opportunities, including hiking, fishing, and biking, all while being just a short drive from the amenities of downtown Monument. The community is characterized by its diverse property types, from spacious single-family homes to custom-built luxury estates, often situated on larger lots that provide a sense of privacy and tranquility. The charming, rustic feel of the area is complemented by its friendly community vibe, making it an ideal place for families and nature lovers alike.

Water & Utilities

In the Forest Lakes area, residents typically rely on a combination of municipal water and private wells. The neighborhood is serviced by the Tri-Lakes Water District, which provides reliable water supply to homeowners. For those considering building or buying, it's essential to evaluate the specific water infrastructure in place. If you're looking at properties with wells, resources like ColoradoWell.com can provide valuable information regarding well permits and regulations. It's also worth noting that water quality and availability can vary by location, so potential buyers should conduct thorough testing and research to ensure they meet their water needs. Understanding the local water rights and any associated fees is crucial for new residents to anticipate their monthly utility costs effectively.

Septic Considerations

Forest Lakes primarily utilizes septic systems for wastewater management, as many homes are not connected to a municipal sewer line. The most common types of septic systems in the area include conventional gravity-fed systems as well as alternative systems designed for specific terrain challenges. Given the hilly landscape of the neighborhood, it's important to consider the topography when evaluating properties, as this can affect the design and functionality of a septic system. Homebuyers should have a thorough inspection of existing systems or consult with a local septic service provider to ensure compliance with health regulations and proper maintenance. Additionally, understanding soil types and drainage characteristics can help in making informed decisions regarding future home construction.
