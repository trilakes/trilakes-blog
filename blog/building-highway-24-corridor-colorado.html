The Highway 24 Corridor

The Highway 24 corridor is a major route through the stunning Colorado mountains, connecting travelers and residents alike from Woodland Park to Buena Vista. This scenic drive takes you through diverse terrain, presenting unique challenges and opportunities for building and development. Encompassing picturesque landscapes, rich wildlife, and vibrant communities, the corridor passes through Woodland Park, Divide, Florissant, Lake George, and Buena Vista, each offering its own character and charm. Whether you're looking to build a vacation home, a permanent residence, or a commercial space, understanding the specific conditions along this route is crucial for a successful project.

Communities Along Highway 24

Woodland Park (Mile ~280)

Located at an elevation of approximately 8,500 feet, Woodland Park is the most developed community along the Highway 24 corridor and is conveniently close to Colorado Springs. With a blend of residential, commercial, and recreational opportunities, it attracts both year-round residents and seasonal visitors. Water sources in this area are primarily a mix of wells and cisterns, necessitating thorough research and planning for any building project. The building regulations fall under Teller County jurisdiction, which means that builders need to familiarize themselves with local codes and zoning laws. Woodland Park boasts a vibrant community atmosphere, complete with parks, schools, and shopping facilities, making it an ideal location for families and those seeking a mountain lifestyle.

Divide (Mile ~275)

As you drive further along Highway 24, you reach Divide, situated at an elevation of about 9,200 feet. This quaint community serves as a gateway to the beautiful Mueller State Park, famed for its hiking trails and stunning vistas. Water sources here predominantly consist of cisterns, which are very common due to the elevation and geological conditions. Building at higher altitudes presents unique challenges, including the potential for increased snowfall and the need for specific construction techniques to withstand harsh weather conditions. Florissant (Mile ~268)

Florissant offers a rural escape with larger properties ideal for those seeking space and tranquility. Known for its proximity to the Florissant Fossil Beds National Monument, this area is rich in natural beauty and historical significance. The community is characterized by its peaceful atmosphere and scenic surroundings, making it perfect for those interested in building homes that blend into the landscape. The larger property sizes provide opportunities for custom builds and unique architectural designs.

Lake George (Mile ~260)

Transitioning into Park County, Lake George is a small community known for its access to the breathtaking Pike National Forest. At this location, you can experience a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, fishing, and camping. Water sources may vary, and builders should take note of the surrounding environment when planning their projects. The community offers a serene escape from the busier areas along the corridor, making it an appealing choice for those looking to build a getaway or permanent residence in nature.

Buena Vista (Mile ~210)

Buena Vista, located in Chaffee County, is renowned for its majestic views of the Collegiate Peaks and offers a vibrant outdoor lifestyle. This charming town is a hub for adventure enthusiasts, providing access to rafting, hiking, and mountain biking. Builders in Buena Vista will find ample opportunities to create homes that take advantage of the stunning surroundings. Water Along the Corridor

Water accessibility is a critical factor when building along the Highway 24 corridor. Well depths can vary dramatically depending on the specific location and geological conditions, with some areas requiring deeper wells than others to access reliable water sources. Prospective builders should utilize resources like ColoradoWell.com to conduct thorough research on well depths and water quality in their chosen area. In many communities along the corridor, cisterns are a common water solution, especially in regions where well drilling is not feasible or practical. Understanding the nuances of water availability will help ensure that your building project meets local regulations and provides a sustainable water supply.

County Boundaries to Know

The Highway 24 corridor stretches across three counties: Teller, Park, and Chaffee. Each county has its own set of building regulations, zoning laws, and permitting processes that builders must navigate. It's essential to familiarize yourself with the specific requirements for the county in which you plan to build, as these can significantly affect your project's timeline and feasibility. For instance, Teller County may have different regulations than Park County regarding land use and construction permits, so consulting with local authorities or a knowledgeable contractor can help smooth the way for your project.

Building Season

The building season along the Highway 24 corridor can vary significantly based on elevation and geographic conditions. Generally, the higher you go, the shorter the building season becomes due to increased snowfall and colder temperatures. In lower elevations, like Woodland Park, construction can often occur from late spring into early fall, whereas in Divide and higher elevations, the window may be limited to just a few months. Builders should plan their projects accordingly, considering weather patterns and local conditions to optimize their construction timelines.

The Tri-Lakes area serves the entire corridor, providing resources and connections to skilled contractors, builders, and local officials who understand the unique challenges of mountain construction. Leveraging local knowledge will not only expedite your project but also help you navigate the complexities of building in the Colorado mountains. Whether you need assistance with permits, site selection, or construction techniques, reaching out to local experts can make a significant difference in the success of your building endeavor.
