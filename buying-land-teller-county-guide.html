Why People Love Teller County

Teller County, nestled in the heart of the Colorado Rockies, offers an unmatched lifestyle that combines the tranquility of mountain living with the convenience of being close to Colorado Springs. With charming communities such as Woodland Park, Divide, Cripple Creek, Victor, Florissant, and Green Mountain Falls, residents enjoy stunning views, diverse wildlife, and a plethora of outdoor activities. The elevation ranges from 8,000 to 10,000 feet, providing not only breathtaking panoramas but also a unique climate that fosters a vibrant ecosystem. Whether you're looking for a peaceful retreat or an adventurous lifestyle, Teller County offers the perfect balance, making it a beloved destination for both newcomers and long-time residents alike.

Water in Teller County

When buying land in Teller County, understanding the water situation is critical. Well depths can vary significantly depending on the location, and many areas face challenging water tables. It's not uncommon for wells to require deeper drilling, which can become costly. As a result, many residents opt for cisterns as a practical solution for water storage, especially in areas where accessing groundwater is difficult. Before finalizing any property purchase, it is essential to research neighboring wells to ascertain water availability and quality. Connecting with local well drilling companies or water resources can provide valuable insights into the water situation in your desired area.

Septic in Teller County

Most properties in Teller County require an On-Site Wastewater Treatment System (OWTS), commonly known as a septic system. The rocky soil in the area often necessitates engineered systems, which can be more complex and expensive than traditional septic systems. Building Season & Weather

The primary building season in Teller County typically runs from May to October, providing a window for construction activities before the snow begins to fall. By October or November, snowfall at higher elevations can hinder building progress, making it crucial to plan your construction projects around these weather windows. Additionally, mountain weather can be unpredictable; therefore, it is wise to account for potential delays due to rain or early snow. Understanding these seasonal patterns can help you schedule your building timeline effectively and avoid unexpected challenges.

Areas & Communities

Several unique communities in Teller County offer distinct lifestyles and amenities:

  • Woodland Park: The largest and most accessible community, Woodland Park provides a range of services, shopping, and restaurants, making it a great base for families and retirees alike.
  • Divide: Situated at a higher elevation, Divide is a gateway to Mueller State Park, known for its stunning natural beauty and outdoor recreation opportunities.
  • Cripple Creek/Victor: Rich in history and the highest elevation town in the state, these towns offer a glimpse into Colorado's gold mining past, complete with unique attractions and festivals.
  • Florissant: A rural area with larger lots, Florissant provides a peaceful environment that is ideal for those seeking solitude amidst nature.
  • Green Mountain Falls: This small village exudes charm, with a picturesque setting that includes a lake and hiking trails, perfect for those who appreciate a close-knit community feel.
