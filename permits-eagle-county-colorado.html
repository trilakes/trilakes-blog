Eagle County Building Permits: Complete Guide

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed Contractor Updated January 2026

Building Permit Guide for Eagle County, Colorado

Building a new home or undertaking a renovation project in Eagle County, Colorado, is an exciting venture. However, before you embark on this journey, it's crucial to familiarize yourself with the building permit process. This guide will provide you with essential information about the Eagle County Building Department, the types of permits available, application procedures, fees, timelines, inspections, and tips for a smooth permitting experience.

Overview of Eagle County Building Department

The Eagle County Building Department is responsible for ensuring that all construction projects within the county comply with local building codes, zoning regulations, and safety standards. The department's mission is to promote safe and sustainable building practices while providing excellent service to residents and contractors. They offer guidance throughout the permitting process, ensuring that your project meets all requirements.

Types of Permits Needed

Before starting your construction project, it’s essential to understand the various types of permits you may need. Here are the primary permits issued by the Eagle County Building Department:

  • Building Permit: Required for new construction, additions, and significant renovations.
  • Septic Permit: Necessary if your property is not connected to a municipal sewer system and requires a septic system installation or modification.
  • Well Permit: Required for drilling a new well, modifying an existing well, or for any work related to water access on your property.
  • Electrical Permit: Needed for any electrical work, including installations, upgrades, or repairs.

How to Apply for Permits

The application process for building permits in Eagle County can be straightforward if you follow these steps:

  1. Determine the Required Permits: Assess the nature of your project to identify which permits are needed.
  2. Prepare Documentation: Gather all necessary documents, which may include site plans, construction drawings, and specifications. For septic and well permits, additional documentation regarding soil tests and water quality may be required.
  3. Complete the Application: Fill out the relevant application forms available on the Eagle County Building Department's website.
  4. Submit the Application: Submit your application along with all supporting documents and applicable fees to the Eagle County Building Department, either online or in person.

Typical Fees

Permit fees can vary based on the type of permit and the scope of your project. Here’s a general overview:

  • Building Permit: Fees typically range from $100 to several thousand dollars, depending on the project's size and complexity.
  • Septic Permit: Usually ranges from $250 to $500.
  • Well Permit: Typically costs around $200.
  • Electrical Permit: Fees are generally based on the total value of the electrical work and can range from $50 to $500.

Always check the Eagle County Building Department’s website for the most current fee schedule, as these amounts can change.

Timeline Expectations

The timeline for obtaining a building permit can vary significantly depending on the complexity of your project and the current workload of the Building Department. Here’s a general timeline:

  • Initial Review: 1-2 weeks after submission.
  • Corrections and Resubmissions: If corrections are needed, this may add an additional 1-3 weeks.
  • Final Approval: Once all corrections are made, final approval can take another 1-2 weeks.

In general, you should expect the entire permitting process to take anywhere from 4 to 12 weeks. To avoid delays, ensure that all documentation is complete and accurate before submission.

Inspection Process

Once you receive your building permit, you must schedule inspections at various stages of your project to ensure compliance with building codes. Here’s a brief overview of the inspection process:

  • Scheduling Inspections: Inspections should be scheduled through the Eagle County Building Department, typically at least 24 hours in advance.
  • Types of Inspections: Common inspections include foundation, framing, electrical, plumbing, and final inspections.
  • Passing Inspections: Each inspection must be passed before proceeding to the next phase of construction. If an inspection fails, you will need to make necessary corrections and reschedule.

Tips for Smooth Permitting

To ensure a smooth permitting process, consider the following tips:

  • Be Prepared: Gather all necessary documents and ensure your plans meet local codes before submitting your application.
  • Consult Professionals: Consider hiring an architect or a contractor familiar with Eagle County’s permitting process to assist you.
  • Stay in Communication: Maintain regular communication with the Building Department to stay updated on your application status and any requirements.
  • Be Patient: Understand that some delays may be out of your control, and allow extra time in your project timeline for potential hiccups in the permitting process.
  • Follow Up: If you haven't received a response within the expected timeline, don’t hesitate to follow up with the Building Department.

By understanding the building permit process in Eagle County and following these guidelines, you can navigate this essential step more effectively and get one step closer to realizing your construction project.
