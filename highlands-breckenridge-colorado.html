About Highlands

The Highlands neighborhood, located just a short drive from Breckenridge, Colorado, is renowned for its stunning mountain views and serene alpine atmosphere. Nestled among towering pines and aspens, this area offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and convenience. The Highlands is appealing to those seeking a peaceful mountain retreat while still being close to the vibrant culture and amenities of Breckenridge. Properties in this neighborhood typically range from cozy mountain cabins to luxurious custom-built homes, allowing potential buyers a variety of choices to suit their lifestyle. The community is characterized by spacious lots, providing ample privacy and room for outdoor activities, making it a favorite among nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts.

Water & Utilities

The Highlands neighborhood primarily relies on wells for water supply, as municipal water services are not available in most areas. Homeowners typically obtain their water from individual wells, which can be drilled on their property. It's essential to research well water quality and flow rates before making a purchase. Resources such as ColoradoWell.com are invaluable for understanding the specifics of well water in this region, including testing and maintenance requirements. In terms of utilities, residents can expect electric services from Xcel Energy, and natural gas is available in some areas. Internet and cable services are provided by various local companies, so checking availability is crucial for those who work remotely or require reliable connectivity.

Septic Considerations

The Highlands does not have a centralized sewer system, so most homes rely on septic systems for waste management. The type of septic system commonly used in this area is a conventional gravity system, although alternative systems may be necessary depending on the specific lot's layout and soil composition. When considering building or buying a home here, it's essential to understand the terrain, as the topography can impact septic system design and installation. Additionally, obtaining a permit for the septic system is required, and a soil test may be necessary to determine the appropriate system for your property. Consulting with local experts can offer insights into the best practices for maintaining a septic system in the Highlands.

Building & Permits

Building in the Highlands neighborhood falls under the jurisdiction of Summit County, which manages the permitting process. When planning to build, it's crucial to familiarize yourself with local regulations and zoning laws that may impact your project. The Highlands is part of a homeowners association (HOA) that enforces specific restrictions to maintain the aesthetic and environmental integrity of the community. These may include guidelines on building materials, height restrictions, and landscaping requirements. Before commencing any construction, ensure that you have obtained all necessary permits and approvals from both the county and the HOA to avoid potential legal issues down the line.
