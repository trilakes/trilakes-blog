Do I Need an Engineered Septic System?

By Kyle Rossignol, Licensed GC
```html

Quick Answer

Yes, you may need an engineered septic system, especially if your property has challenging soil conditions, is in a high groundwater area, or does not meet local health department regulations. An engineered system is designed to handle specific site conditions, ensuring efficient waste treatment and compliance with state and local codes.

Detailed Explanation

An engineered septic system is a customized solution designed to address unique site challenges that a traditional septic system cannot handle. In Colorado, certain factors can necessitate the installation of an engineered system:

  • Poor Soil Conditions: If your land has clay-heavy, sandy, or rocky soil, an engineered system may be necessary to ensure proper drainage and waste treatment.
  • High Groundwater Levels: Properties near water bodies or in flood-prone areas may require an engineered system to avoid contaminating groundwater.
  • Small Lot Size: If your property is smaller than standard lot sizes, an engineered system can help maximize the available space while meeting legal requirements.
  • Proximity to Water Sources: Homes near wells or streams may need engineered systems to prevent pollution of these water sources.
  • Existing System Failure: If you are replacing a failing septic system, an engineered solution may be required to prevent future issues.

In Colorado, local health departments assess the need for engineered systems based on site evaluations. Always consult local regulations to understand specific requirements.

What This Means for Your Project

Deciding whether you need an engineered septic system impacts your construction timeline and budget. If your property requires an engineered solution, expect additional costs for design and installation. You will also need to engage with a licensed engineer or septic designer who understands local regulations and can provide a tailored solution.
```
