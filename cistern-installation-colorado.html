What is Cistern Installation?

Cistern installation involves the creation of an underground water storage tank designed to collect and store water for various uses including potable water, irrigation, and fire suppression. In Colorado's mountain communities, cisterns are vital for homeowners who either lack access to a municipal water supply or wish to supplement their existing water sources. These tanks can be made from various materials like polyethylene, fiberglass, or concrete, and are typically buried underground to protect them from environmental elements and freezing temperatures.

Cisterns can also serve as backup water supplies in emergencies, making them an invaluable resource for rural and off-grid homeowners.

How Much Does Cistern Installation Cost in Colorado?

The cost of cistern installation in Colorado can vary widely based on several factors, including the size of the tank, the type of materials used, site conditions, and additional features such as filtration systems or pumps. Generally, homeowners can expect to pay between $5,000 and $15,000 for complete installation, with the average cost hovering around $10,000.

Below is a breakdown of potential costs associated with cistern installation:

  • Tank Cost: The cost of the cistern tank itself can range from $1,500 to $5,000, depending on size and material.
  • Excavation and Site Preparation: Excavation costs can vary based on soil type and access issues, typically ranging from $1,000 to $3,000.
  • Installation Labor: Labor costs for installation can range from $2,000 to $5,000, based on the complexity of the job.
  • Additional Features: Optional features like filtration systems, pumps, and monitoring equipment can add $500 to $3,000 to the total cost.

The Cistern Installation Process: Step by Step

Installing a cistern is a detailed process that requires careful planning and execution. Below are the key steps involved in the cistern installation process:

  1. Initial Consultation: This stage involves discussing your water needs, budget, and potential site locations with your contractor. They will help you determine the appropriate size and type of cistern.
  2. Site Assessment: A professional will evaluate the proposed installation site for soil type, accessibility, and proximity to existing structures or utilities.
  3. Permitting: Your contractor will handle obtaining the necessary permits from local authorities, ensuring compliance with regulations.
  4. Excavation: Once permits are secured, the site will be excavated to create space for the cistern. This step involves heavy machinery and may take several days, depending on site conditions.
  5. Cistern Installation: The tank is installed in the excavated space. This phase requires precision to ensure the tank is level and secure.
  6. Backfilling: After installation, the area around the tank is backfilled with soil to provide support and protection.
  7. Connection to Water Sources: The cistern is connected to your water sources, which may include water hauling or other supply methods.
  8. Testing: The system is tested for leaks and functionality, ensuring everything operates as intended.
  9. Final Inspection: A final inspection may be conducted by local officials to verify compliance with safety and health standards.
  10. Project Completion: Once all inspections are passed, the project is officially completed, and your cistern is ready for use.

Throughout this process, homeowners can expect regular updates and communication from their contractor, ensuring all aspects of the installation are transparent and understood.

Colorado Mountain Construction Challenges

Installing a cistern in Coloradoâ€™s mountainous regions presents unique challenges that require specialized knowledge and expertise. Below are some of the critical challenges to consider:

  • Elevation Considerations: Mountain properties often sit at elevations between 7,000 and 12,000 feet. This can impact excavation work and require adjustments in installation techniques due to thinner air and potential for altitude sickness among workers.
  • Soil Types: The soil in Colorado can vary significantly, including granite, clay, and rocky terrain. Understanding the type of soil can affect excavation and tank stability.
  • Weather Windows: The construction season is often limited to a narrow window from May to October, which can delay projects if weather conditions are not favorable.
  • Frost Line Depth: The depth of the frost line in Colorado can range from 36 to 48 inches, making it essential to install cisterns below this line to prevent freezing.
  • Access Issues: Many mountain properties may have limited access, which can complicate transportation of materials and equipment. Proper planning and logistics are crucial to ensure a smooth installation process.

Expert Tip: Choose a contractor experienced in mountain construction who can navigate these challenges effectively and ensure a successful installation.

Permits & Regulations by County

Obtaining the necessary permits for cistern installation is a critical step in the process. Each county in Colorado has specific regulations governing water storage systems.

  • Teller County: Requires permits for all cisterns, including site plans and potential water usage assessments.
  • Park County: Similar regulations, focusing on environmental impact and water quality.
  • El Paso County: Has specific guidelines for cisterns used for potable water, requiring health department approval.
  • Chaffee County: Mandates permits for all water storage installations, with a focus on sustainable practices.
  • Summit County: Requires detailed plans and may involve additional inspections for water quality and environmental impact.
  • Fremont County: Offers additional regulations focusing on water conservation and management.
  • Douglas County: Requires compliance with local building codes and water management regulations.

How Long Does Cistern Installation Take?

The timeline for cistern installation can vary based on the complexity of the project and local weather conditions. Generally, homeowners can expect the installation process to take between 2 to 6 weeks from initial consultation to project completion.

Factors that can cause delays include:

  • Weather Conditions: Adverse weather can halt excavation and installation work, especially during the spring and fall.
  • Permitting Delays: The time taken to secure permits can vary, and any complications in the approval process can extend your timeline.
  • Site Conditions: Difficult access or challenging soil conditions can lengthen excavation and installation times.

Planning ahead and allowing for flexibility in your schedule can help accommodate any unforeseen delays, ensuring your cistern installation proceeds as smoothly as possible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size cistern do I need for my property?

The size of the cistern you need depends on your water usage and the number of people in your household. A typical range is between 1,500 to 5,000 gallons for residential properties.

Can I use a cistern for drinking water?

Yes, cisterns can be used for potable water, but they must be installed and maintained according to health regulations, including proper filtration and disinfection systems.

How often do I need to maintain my cistern?

Regular maintenance is essential and should be performed at least once a year. This includes checking for leaks, cleaning the tank, and testing the water quality.

Can I install a cistern myself?

While some homeowners may attempt DIY installation, it is highly recommended to hire a licensed contractor. Proper installation is critical for safety and compliance with local regulations.

What happens if my cistern freezes?

To prevent freezing, cisterns should be buried below the frost line. If a cistern does freeze, it may require thawing techniques, which should be handled by a professional.

Do I need a permit to install a cistern?

Yes, most counties in Colorado require permits for cistern installation. Itâ€™s essential to work with a licensed contractor who can navigate the permitting process for you.

What is the lifespan of a cistern?

The lifespan of a well-maintained cistern can range from 20 to 50 years, depending on the materials used and the environment in which it is installed.

How does a cistern work with my existing water supply?

A cistern can serve as a supplementary water source, collecting rainwater or water from hauling services. It can also be integrated with existing well systems for a more reliable water supply.
